# TEXAS CHRONICLES — Development Notes

Shorts spinoff of the Ancapistan world. **Lead** = a master "hand" (the agency's fact-staking
virtuoso, a Holmes). **Watson** = a Union newcomer who inherits in, and who carries the
*transformation* arc across the series (James Scott Bell's 14-signpost superstructure, distributed
across shorts; each short is a complete case + one concept + one beat of his turn).

**Authority for the world is `stories/ancapistan/ARENA.md`.** This file captures series-specific
*directives and staging only* — not canon already in the bible.

Premise every short must carry (for cold viewers entering anywhere): *Texas left the United States;
there is no central government; all law and order is private.* Carried on three layers — structural
(the case is a private-law case), a fixed cold-open signature, and the Watson as walking orientation
(possibly the "letters home to the Union" device — TBD).

---

## Open directives to handle (captured 2026-06-15)

### 1. Dial UP the Union ↔ Free-States tension  *(new directive)*
Free Ross got its intensity elsewhere and left the geopolitics mostly offstage. Texas Chronicles
should make the Union/Free-States dynamic **present and charged** — the frozen war is in the air the
characters breathe. Series-level tonal dial, not a single episode.
- **Story consequence to exploit:** the Watson is a Union citizen — a son of the *enemy* nation.
  Raising the tension makes him not just a fish out of water but a suspect/outsider with skin in a
  live conflict, and his transformation becomes a **change of allegiance** — which raises the stakes
  of the entire arc, not just one short.

### 2. The "no internet" reveal, staged through the no-fly zone  *(canon underneath; staging is new)*
A dramatized orientation to the information regime (ARENA Part I supremacy/blockade, Part II inverse
firewall) — show it, don't explain it. Beat sketch:
- Watson: "Why don't we just look it up on the internet?"
- Local: "What's that?"
- Someone walks him outside. Union fighter jets cross the sky over Dallas.
- "What *is* that?" → the no-fly zone is the **whole** of the Union's reach here. They own the sky and
  nothing under it. No-fly zone + satellites shot down on launch + undersea cables cut = no internet.
Strong centerpiece for the information-regime / "the war that never ended" concept short.

### 3. Tech texture — keep consistent across every short  *(canon, confirmed/refined)*
- Backbone is landline + buried copper.
- **Cell phones DO work** — local towers, no satellite needed; coverage is regional only. The Watson's
  Union smartphone is a brick for data; a local handset is fine for calls.
- No internet, no satellites, no modern chips. The Union has all of it. The Watson losing the net is
  part of his ordinary-world dislocation (signpost 1–2 material).

### 4. Pro-Texas / secessionist swagger  *(new directive + craft frame)*
Deliberately seed defiant, pro-secession Texan voice so the show lands for a Texan audience.
Example: *"They couldn't win us over — we got guns. They got fighter jets."*
- **Grounded in canon:** the Union holds air/naval supremacy (the jets) but can't take the ground or
  the people — a frozen stalemate. The Texan reads it as *they couldn't beat us.* The line is swagger
  **and** accurate.
- **Craft frame (how it hits instead of preaches):** keep it in characters' mouths, deadpan, lived-in.
  Earned defiance travels; an author soapbox reads as a tract and dies. Never the narrator's thesis.
  (Holds the clear-pane + genre-guardrail laws.)
