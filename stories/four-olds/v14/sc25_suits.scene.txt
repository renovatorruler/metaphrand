@@SCENE@@
ACTION: A roll-up door climbs and morning light falls across forty feet of ranked steel shelving.
ACTION: Orange seizure stickers run the length of every shelf.
ACTION: MACK BOONE, 55, a clipboard against his hip and his reading glasses shoved up in his hair, walks the main aisle and doesn't slow for any of it.
ACTION: A cockpit trainer sits up on blocks with a sticker on its nose. Crates of tooling behind it. A glass case holds a town's Rotary banners and a Little League trophy, all of it tagged.
ACTION: At the end of the aisle stand four rolling racks, and on them hang the SUITS, white and bulky, museum tags wired to the wrists.
ACTION: Mack flips the clipboard and thumbs down to the lot sheet.
ACTION: ON THE SHEET -- LOT 114. DEGRADED TEXTILE ARTIFACTS, NON-FLIGHT-RATED. SOLD AS SCRAP.
MACK: That's the whole thing, one line. Hundred and forty for all four, and I had to take the chairs and a dishwasher along to get it.
ACTION: A forklift noses in, lifts the first rack, then swings a pallet of dead office chairs up alongside it.
ACTION: Out at the far end of the surplus lot the chain gate stands open in the daylight.
ACTION: A rented flatbed idles at the gate with the four racks strapped down between the chairs and a dishwasher laid on its side.
ACTION: A GUARD leans off his stool, takes the sheet, runs a finger partway down it, and never once looks at the load.
GUARD: Yeah, that's your scrap lot. Go ahead.
ACTION: Mack signs, hands it back, and pulls the flatbed out slow onto the county road.
ACTION: Inside Stitch's hangar at the county airstrip, a work lamp burns over a bench that runs the length of one wall.
ACTION: A single white suit lies open on the bench under the lamp.
ACTION: STITCH, Elmore McGrew, 74, reading glasses down his nose and a coil of new hose over one shoulder, feeds a fresh line into the suit and clamps it.
ACTION: GUNNY TIBBS, 71, his sleeves squared and a rag folded through his belt, lifts one glove off the bench with both hands and sets it down on a clean towel.
ACTION: Stitch dips a brush in a bucket of soapy water and paints it along the seam across the chest.
ACTION: Bubbles come up out of the seam and keep coming.
STITCH: There she goes. Every one of those little ones is air getting out where it shouldn't.
ACTION: He runs a hot sealing iron down the seam, holds it a while, then brushes the soapy water back across.
ACTION: The seam sits quiet this time. No bubbles.
STITCH: (dry) There, she quit bubbling. That one's holding now.
ACTION: On the next suit he cuts a patch to size and works it down over a worn place on the knee, pressing it flat with his thumb.
ACTION: He turns the museum tag right-side up on the wrist, wires it back where it hung, and leaves it there.
GUNNY: (to Stitch) The gloves go on last. Nobody sets a coffee cup on this bench till they're back in the box.
STITCH: (not looking up) You've wiped that same glove down four times now, Gunny.
GUNNY: I'll do it again anyway.
ACTION: Mack comes in the side door with the lot sheet still in his hand and looks down the row of racks.
MACK: Paper says four textile artifacts, non-flight-rated. That's all that's on it.
STITCH: Fifty years old, every one of them, and they'll hold air by Sunday.
ACTION: By the far wall the four suits stand upright on their racks now, filled out, and the tags turn slow on their wires in the draft off the door.
