@@SCENE@@
ACTION: Six flags stand in a row on the gray flat, stiff on their wire frames, no wind to move them.
ACTION: Two suited figures from the Harmony lander come up over the near rise and stop. Commander Brandt of the ESA, forties, checks the plaque numbers against a card strapped to his wrist. Behind him Observer Shen waits and says nothing.
ACTION: Between Brandt and the flags stand four old men in white suits. EARL 'CRICKET' DAWES, 79, holds the center, his visor level, his hands at his sides.
BRANDT: (into the loop) Geneva, Harmony One. We're on the surface. I have all six in front of me.
GENEVA (RADIO): Harmony One, this is Accord Coordination, Geneva. You are directed to proceed with retrieval of all six artifacts. Immediately, please.
BRANDT: (into the loop) Copy, Geneva. Proceeding.
ACTION: Brandt walks to the first staff. He plants his boots and reaches for it.
ACTION: The four old men step together into one line, Cricket at the center. Brandt's glove stops a foot short of the wire.
ACTION: Nobody moves. Brandt looks at the four visors. He looks at the flag.
ACTION: GUNNY TIBBS, 71, keys his loop, slow and even, and his voice goes out to every living room on Earth.
GUNNY: (into the loop) You want 'em? Come through us.
ACTION: Brandt lowers his arm. He takes one step back.
BRANDT: (into the loop) Geneva, Harmony One. The site is... occupied by personnel asserting jurisdiction. Requesting political guidance.
ACTION: Down in Accord Mission Control in Geneva, a controller holds a slip of paper out to the Flight Director at the front console. Washington wants one word carried up. The slip reads ACKNOWLEDGE.
ACTION: The Flight Director looks at the slip. He does not take it. He lifts his headset off, all the way off, and sets it down on the desk.
ACTION: Back on the surface, the loop hisses and nothing comes down it.
ACTION: Gunny turns to a card table standing in the lunar dust. He deals four hands onto it, one card at a time, in the quiet.
CRICKET: (off the table) Deal 'em face down. Wind'll take 'em.
GUNNY: There's no wind up here.
CRICKET: Force of habit.
ACTION: The loop clicks. A new voice comes on, warm and unhurried.
MARWANI (RADIO): Gentlemen. It's Zoran. The whole planet is standing still for you right now, and every one of them wants the same thing, which is a whole day, a good day, a day we can all share. So let me help you get there.
MARWANI (RADIO): (then) As your Commander in Chief, I am ordering you to stand down, Commander.
ACTION: A beat of static.
CRICKET: (into the loop) You're not my commander, sir. NASA's civilian. Always was. That was the whole point of it.
ACTION: The loop hangs open. Nothing answers, not from the moon, not from Geneva.
ACTION: Inside Roy's Diner back in Nebraska, Buck, seventies, feed cap, sits at the counter with the TV going over the pie case. He watches it a second and doesn't say anything.
ACTION: His fist comes down on the counter, once.
BUCK: U-S-A!
ACTION: The whole diner takes it up. Coffee cups rattle on their saucers.
ACTION: Out at the county fairgrounds the fair is mid-swing. The restored carousel keeps turning while every grown-up in the crowd stands up and chants at a phone.
ACTION: On a highway overpass a bedsheet flag hangs off the railing. A hundred horns go off underneath it at once.
ACTION: Inside a Brooklyn loft half the UNITY DAY shirts in the room catch themselves chanting it too. Some of them stop. Some of them don't.
