@@SCENE@@
ACTION: A pull-cord CLICKS and a work light comes up over the APOLLO PROCEDURES TRAINER.
ACTION: Hand-machined gauges sit in a plywood panel, every needle painted white at the zero mark.
ACTION: A crew couch rides in a welded steel cradle, bolted to the barn floor.
ACTION: The triangular window looks out on nothing but a Moon painted on the barn wall, gray and cratered and eight feet across.
ACTION: Along the back wall the spiral notebooks climb floor to roof, every spine dated in the same block hand, the bottom shelf starting in 1972.
ACTION: CRICKET lowers himself into the couch, seventy-nine, coveralls, reading glasses pushed up on his forehead.
ACTION: He sets a stopwatch on the panel ledge and squares a clipboard on his knee.
ACTION: A radio speaker on the shelf hisses, then a voice comes through it.
GUNNY (RADIO): Net's open. Tuesday, nineteen-oh-two local.
GUNNY (RADIO): Apollo Eighteen. Lunar descent rehearsal. Number two thousand, eight hundred and six.
ACTION: Cricket clicks the transmit key twice and lets it go.
CRICKET: Copy. Panel's up.
STITCH (RADIO): Copy, copy. You hear about Roy Alderson's place, they had the auction Saturday.
DUTCH (RADIO): Stand by on the auction. I show us thirty seconds to ignition.
STITCH (RADIO): They had a crowd out there, Dutch, I'm telling you, three hundred people easy on that lawn.
DUTCH (RADIO): There were not three hundred people at Roy Alderson's.
STITCH (RADIO): Well it was a lot.
CRICKET: Twenty seconds.
GUNNY (RADIO): Everybody clean it up. We're descending.
STITCH (RADIO): So they get the tractor out front, the good green one, and a fella from over in Beatrice buys it sight unseen.
CRICKET: Ignition. Throttle up.
ACTION: Cricket's thumb walks a hand-machined lever forward and holds it.
DUTCH (RADIO): Good ignition. Rates are nominal.
STITCH (RADIO): Sight unseen, Gunny. Never turned it over. Man's got money to burn.
GUNNY (RADIO): The auction is not on the net, Stitch.
STITCH (RADIO): Everything goes on the net, Gunny. Always did.
CRICKET: Seven thousand feet. Coming down.
DUTCH (RADIO): I want it on the record I'm watching Bus B.
GUNNY (RADIO): Dutch, we are aware.
DUTCH (RADIO): It's a forty-year-old relay. I wrote it up in the binder, June of '09.
STITCH (RADIO): Yeah, you wrote it up. We all read the binder.
CRICKET: Five thousand.
DUTCH (RADIO): I'm allowed to say it. It's my relay to say.
STITCH (RADIO): So anyway the widow Alderson, she's stood there in the driveway and she says to me, Elmore, she says.
GUNNY (RADIO): You did not talk to the widow.
STITCH (RADIO): I talked to the widow.
CRICKET: Three thousand feet. Rate of descent good.
DUTCH (RADIO): Which is another thing. You missed the net that whole month, October two years back.
STITCH (RADIO): I had my hip done.
DUTCH (RADIO): October the fourth through the twenty-fifth. I got it logged.
STITCH (RADIO): I had my hip done, Dutch, they cut the whole thing out and put a new one in.
GUNNY (RADIO): The hip is not on the net either.
CRICKET: Two thousand.
STITCH (RADIO): Point is a man gets a new hip and the Moon waits right where it was. Fifty years of Tuesdays. It is not going to jump out at anybody.
DUTCH (RADIO): It could. That's the whole reason for the drill.
STITCH (RADIO): In fifty years it never once jumped out.
CRICKET: One thousand feet.
ACTION: A needle on the left panel twitches, slides half a mark toward zero, and hangs there.
CRICKET: Got a walk on Bus B.
ACTION: Dutch's voice comes back fast, then catches itself and slows.
DUTCH (RADIO): Steady or does it stutter?
ACTION: Cricket leans to the gauge, watches the needle tremble at the low edge.
CRICKET: Stutters.
DUTCH (RADIO): Kick it. Low left corner. Heel of your hand.
ACTION: Cricket raises a boot and raps the plywood panel down in the left corner, one flat knock.
ACTION: The needle jumps, climbs back up the dial, and settles white on the zero.
CRICKET: Back up.
ACTION: A long hiss on the speaker. Nobody says anything for a moment.
DUTCH (RADIO): Well.
GUNNY (RADIO): Don't.
DUTCH (RADIO): June of '09.
GUNNY (RADIO): Dutch.
STITCH (RADIO): Aw, let him have it. He's been waiting on that a long while.
CRICKET: Five hundred feet.
STITCH (RADIO): So the casket, that's the part. They open it up at the front and it's veneer. The whole box. Veneer.
DUTCH (RADIO): You cannot know it was veneer.
STITCH (RADIO): My cousin does the finish work over there. It's veneer, I'm telling you, it's a quarter inch of walnut over the cheap stuff.
CRICKET: Four hundred feet. Coming down nice.
ACTION: The speaker goes quiet. No one calls for it. The three voices simply stop.
ACTION: Cricket's thumb rides the throttle down, easy, watching the painted Moon fill the triangular window.
CRICKET: Three hundred.
ACTION: The barn is silent except for the hiss of the open channel.
ACTION: A small green bulb on the panel lights.
CRICKET: Contact light. Engine stop.
ACTION: Cricket lifts his thumb off the lever and sets both hands flat on the clipboard.
ACTION: One beat. The work light hums. Nobody keys a mic.
STITCH (RADIO): You came in hot on the throttle there at the end.
CRICKET: I did not.
GUNNY (RADIO): The man is on the ground, Colonel.
DUTCH (RADIO): Contact, nineteen forty-one local. I've got it down.
GUNNY (RADIO): Net closed. Same time Tuesday.
STITCH (RADIO): God willing.
ACTION: Cricket clicks the transmit key twice.
ACTION: Two clicks answer back from the speaker. Then two more. Then two more, fainter, from farther off.
ACTION: The speaker cuts to dead air.
ACTION: Cricket pulls a spiral notebook off the shelf and opens it flat to a fresh page.
ACTION: He writes in ballpoint block letters, slow, and the words fill the top line. T+2,806. BUS B CORRECTED. CONTACT 19:41. CREW PRESENT BY RADIO.
ACTION: He runs his thumb down the dated spines on his way to the door, top shelf to bottom.
ACTION: He kills the work light and steps out. A padlock hangs on the hasp.
ACTION: He tests it with one pull, and it holds.
ACTION: Beyond the barn stands the farmhouse, and no lights are on inside it.
