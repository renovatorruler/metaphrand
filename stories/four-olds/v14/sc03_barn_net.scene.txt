@@SCENE@@
ACTION: In the dark of the barn a hand finds a starter cord and pulls it hard.
ACTION: A sharp CLICK carries across the dark barn.
ACTION: A single work light warms up over the APOLLO PROCEDURES TRAINER, a plywood-and-steel cockpit standing on blocks in the middle of the barn.
ACTION: The gauges are hand-machined, every dial cut and inked and numbered by somebody at a bench.
ACTION: A crew couch sits bolted into a welded cradle, tipped back the way a man lies down to land.
ACTION: A triangular window looks out on a Moon painted straight onto the barn wall, gray and cratered and flaking a little at the edges.
ACTION: ALONG THE BACK WALL -- spiral notebooks stand floor to roof, hundreds of them, each spine dated in the same block hand.
ACTION: The bottom shelf starts in 1972.
ACTION: CRICKET -- Earl Dawes, 79, a barn coat pulled over his pajamas, lowers himself into the couch and settles his back against it without looking.
ACTION: He thumbs a microphone clipped to the frame and clears his throat.
CRICKET: Net's open. Tuesday, nineteen-oh-two local.
ACTION: Static rolls up out of an old field radio wired into the trainer's frame.
ACTION: A drawl comes back first, slow and easy, off somewhere in East Texas.
STITCH (RADIO): Evenin', Earl.
ACTION: A dry, flat voice follows it, a man reading off a page.
DUTCH (RADIO): Present.
ACTION: The third voice lands square and clipped, an old Navy voice.
GUNNY (RADIO): Stand by for roll.
ACTION: In the barn Cricket reaches up and starts setting a row of toggle switches, one at a time.
GUNNY (RADIO): Apollo Eighteen. Lunar descent rehearsal. Number two thousand, eight hundred and six.
ACTION: Nobody laughs. Cricket just finishes his switches.
CRICKET: Crew's aboard. Comm check, three by three.
STITCH (RADIO): Loud and ugly, same as you.
DUTCH (RADIO): Loud and clear.
CRICKET: Coming up on the burn. Program's running.
DUTCH (RADIO): Confirm, guidance is nominal on my copy.
STITCH (RADIO): Y'all hear they put Harlan Voss in the ground Saturday.
GUNNY (RADIO): Can the estate sale, Stitch. We're descending.
STITCH (RADIO): I'm gettin' to it. They had damn near two hundred people out at that church, Dutch, and every last one of 'em stood up for the box, and that box — I want you to hear me — that box was veneer.
CRICKET: Ten thousand feet. Rate looks good.
DUTCH (RADIO): There were not two hundred. The church on County Road nine seats a hundred and ten. I measured it in oh-four for the fire panel.
STITCH (RADIO): Hundred and ten seated. They was standin' in the vestibule and hangin' off the steps.
DUTCH (RADIO): Then say a hundred and ten and standing room. Don't say two hundred.
CRICKET: Three thousand. Throttle down on schedule.
STITCH (RADIO): Man's serious his whole life, and they lay him in a box that's mostly glue anyhow. I want oak. Real oak. Put it in your book, Dutch.
DUTCH (RADIO): Noted. Oak.
DUTCH (RADIO): While I've got the book open. That Bus B relay is going to open on somebody one of these Tuesdays. I logged it November of eighty-nine and I have logged it every November since.
STITCH (RADIO): Here he goes.
DUTCH (RADIO): I'm allowed to say it. It is written down thirty-some times.
GUNNY (RADIO): Nobody's stopping you, Dutch. We're at three thousand feet.
ACTION: On the panel a needle sags off its peg and starts to drift down.
CRICKET: Bus B's moving.
DUTCH (RADIO): Say again.
CRICKET: Voltage. Walking down.
DUTCH (RADIO): (off it) Steady, or does it stutter?
ACTION: Cricket watches the needle bounce and fall, bounce and fall.
CRICKET: Stutters.
DUTCH (RADIO): Kick it. Low left corner, up under the panel.
ACTION: Cricket brings his heel up and raps the underside of the panel, hard, low on the left.
ACTION: The needle jumps, hangs a second, and climbs back up to its peg.
CRICKET: Back up.
ACTION: The radio holds quiet a beat.
DUTCH (RADIO): November of eighty-nine.
GUNNY (RADIO): Let it go, Dutch.
DUTCH (RADIO): I did. I let it go thirty-seven years.
CRICKET: Two thousand feet. Looking good.
STITCH (RADIO): Anyhow. Oak. And I'll tell you what else, they had the wrong hymn. Had the funeral hymn for a Methodist and Harlan was Baptist his whole —
DUTCH (RADIO): You'd know about missing a service. You missed a net. March of last year. I have you down as an absence.
STITCH (RADIO): I had a hip put in me, Dutch. They had me on the good drugs. I called Earl from the bed.
CRICKET: You did. You sang.
STITCH (RADIO): (dry) So take the absence off, Dutch. I was right there on the line.
CRICKET: One thousand feet.
GUNNY (RADIO): Quiet down, both of you, we're coming in.
STITCH (RADIO): Comin' in where, Gunny. It's the same Moon it was last Tuesday. After fifty years of Tuesdays that Moon is not gonna jump out from behind nothin'.
CRICKET: Five hundred.
CRICKET: Three hundred.
ACTION: The chatter stops. Nobody calls for it and nobody says why.
ACTION: Cricket's eyes stay on the painted Moon in the window.
CRICKET: A hundred feet. Kicking up a little dust.
CRICKET: Forty feet.
CRICKET: Drifting forward a hair.
ACTION: A small blue bulb on the panel comes on.
CRICKET: Contact light. Engine stop.
ACTION: The barn holds dead still. The work light hums over the couch.
STITCH (RADIO): You come in a touch hot on the throttle there at the end, Earl.
GUNNY (RADIO): The man is on the ground, Colonel.
CRICKET: Crew's down. Safe and sound.
DUTCH (RADIO): Logged. Contact, nineteen forty-one.
GUNNY (RADIO): Net closed. Same time Tuesday.
CRICKET: God willing.
ACTION: Two clicks of dead-key static come across the radio.
ACTION: Cricket keys the mic twice in answer, and one after another the others key back, two clicks each, until the field radio drops to hiss.
ACTION: He reaches over to the workbench, lifts down a fresh spiral notebook, and clicks a ballpoint.
ACTION: He prints it out slow in block letters that read T+2,806. BUS B CORRECTED. CONTACT 19:41. CREW PRESENT BY RADIO.
ACTION: He slides the notebook onto the back-wall shelf and runs his thumb down the dated spines, from this year back toward the bottom.
ACTION: He kills the work light and hooks a padlock through the door hasp, then gives it one pull to be sure.
ACTION: Across the yard the farmhouse stands dark, not one light on inside it.
