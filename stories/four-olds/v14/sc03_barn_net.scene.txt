@@SCENE@@
ACTION: A pull-cord CLICKS.
ACTION: Light drops over the APOLLO PROCEDURES TRAINER. Hand-machined gauges sit crooked in a welded panel, each one filed and fitted by hand. A crew couch is bolted into a steel cradle below a triangular window.
ACTION: The window looks out at a Moon painted on the barn boards, gray and cratered, taller than a man.
ACTION: ALONG THE BACK WALL -- spiral notebooks stand floor to roof. Every spine is dated in the same block hand. The bottom shelf starts in 1972.
ACTION: Folding chairs lean on a card table. A percolator sits cold on an upturned crate. The room is half flight facility and half clubhouse, and nobody who built it ever noticed the difference.
ACTION: Headlights swing across the painted Moon and hold there.
ACTION: Outside, a pickup rolls up the dirt drive and parks by the fence. A second set of lights follows it up. A third pulls in behind.
ACTION: CRICKET DAWES -- 79, seed-corn cap, a wedding band he still turns with his thumb -- stands at the panel already, going down a checklist with a golf pencil.
ACTION: The barn door rolls back. GUNNY TIBBS -- 71, Navy ballcap, a folded flag carried flat in both arms -- comes in first and sets the flag on the card table before he says a word.
GUNNY: Percolator's cold, Cricket.
CRICKET: It'll come up.
ACTION: STITCH McGREW -- 74, a paper sack of something warm under one arm -- holds the door for the last man.
STITCH: Brought kolaches. Sausage and the other kind.
ACTION: DUTCH HAAS -- 76, a black ledger and a mechanical pencil, nothing else in his hands -- steps around him without looking at the sack.
DUTCH: You bring 'em every week and I tell you every week I don't eat before a descent.
STITCH: Yeah, I know. Bring 'em anyway.
GUNNY: Headsets. We're eight minutes down.
ACTION: The four of them take their chairs in a loose ring near the panel. They pull headsets on over their caps. Nobody moves more than a few feet apart.
ACTION: Gunny keys his loop. His voice goes flat and formal.
GUNNY (RADIO): Apollo Eighteen. Lunar descent rehearsal. Number two thousand, eight hundred and six.
ACTION: He waits. He can hear Stitch breathing through eight feet of open air, and he waits for it on the loop anyway.
GUNNY (RADIO): Stitch. Comm check.
STITCH (RADIO): Loud and clear, Gun.
GUNNY (RADIO): Say again.
STITCH (RADIO): Loud and clear.
ACTION: Cricket sets his hand on the throttle. The needles come alive on the panel.
CRICKET (RADIO): Program's in. Coming up on high gate.
STITCH (RADIO): You watch that thing last night? The landing?
DUTCH (RADIO): We are on the loop.
STITCH (RADIO): I know where we are. I'm asking did you watch it.
CRICKET (RADIO): Four thousand feet.
STITCH (RADIO): 'Cause I watched it twice. Computer flew those two boys down, held both their hands the whole way, and he still come in long. Rolled right on past his own mark.
DUTCH (RADIO): Nine hundred feet long. Not that it matters to your point.
STITCH (RADIO): It don't matter to my point.
DUTCH (RADIO): I know it don't. I'm putting the number right.
CRICKET (RADIO): Three thousand. Coming down.
STITCH (RADIO): And that fella's got a patch on his arm and a parade in Houston.
GUNNY (RADIO): Loop discipline. Both of you.
ACTION: A needle low on the panel takes a small drop. It comes back. Then it dips again and starts a slow walk downward.
DUTCH (RADIO): (not looking up) Bus B.
CRICKET (RADIO): Confirm.
DUTCH (RADIO): (reading his ledger) It's that relay again. I wrote it up back in eighty-four. It's all in the book.
GUNNY (RADIO): Steady or does it stutter?
DUTCH (RADIO): It stutters. Give it a second and it'll drop again.
ACTION: The needle jumps, hangs, jumps again.
DUTCH (RADIO): There. Kick it. Low left corner.
ACTION: Cricket brings the heel of his boot up under the panel and raps the low left corner once, without looking at it.
ACTION: The needle climbs and settles home.
DUTCH (RADIO): (a beat) I've said for years it wants a new relay.
STITCH (RADIO): You have said that.
DUTCH (RADIO): I'm allowed to say it. It's my write-up.
CRICKET (RADIO): Fifteen hundred feet.
STITCH (RADIO): All I'm saying is, put either one of those boys from last night in that seat with a stuttering bus and no computer, and where do they set down.
DUTCH (RADIO): They don't set down. Hell, they don't even clear the physical to get in the seat.
STITCH (RADIO): Now that is the truth.
GUNNY (RADIO): Seven hundred feet. Quiet on the loop.
CRICKET (RADIO): Seven hundred. Down at twenty.
ACTION: Cricket eases the throttle back. The barn is loud with three men who have opinions.
CRICKET (RADIO): Three hundred feet.
ACTION: Nobody says anything. Nobody reaches for a kolache. The room goes still on its own, and no one asked it to.
ACTION: A blue CONTACT LIGHT blinks on the panel.
CRICKET (RADIO): Contact light. Engine stop.
ACTION: For one beat the barn holds nothing but the tick of the cooling percolator.
STITCH (RADIO): (off the gauge) Half a foot a second at the deck.
DUTCH (RADIO): Little left of center.
CRICKET (RADIO): On the line.
ACTION: They pull the headsets down around their necks. The room comes back to room volume.
GUNNY: Net closed. Same time Tuesday.
CRICKET: God willing.
ACTION: Stitch is already up, tearing the sack open on the card table. The percolator starts to knock.
STITCH: Sausage or the other kind, Gun.
GUNNY: The other kind. And leave the flag be, you've got grease on you.
DUTCH: He flew it long and they gave him a parade.
STITCH: Let it go, Dutch.
DUTCH: I let it go. I'm just saying it once more and then I'm done with it.
ACTION: Cricket pours four cups and does not sit down with them. He carries his to the panel and opens a fresh notebook.
ACTION: He writes in ballpoint block letters, pressing hard. T+2,806. BUS B CORRECTED. CONTACT 19:41. CREW PRESENT.
ACTION: One truck cranks outside. Then another.
GUNNY: You want the flag inside or on the porch pole tonight.
CRICKET: Porch is fine.
ACTION: The three of them go out. Cricket runs a thumb down the dated spines on the back wall on his way to the door, slow, the whole shelf, and does not stop on any one of them.
ACTION: Taillights swing around in the yard and shrink down the drive, one behind the other, and turn onto the section road.
ACTION: Cricket rolls the barn door shut and tests the padlock with one pull.
ACTION: Past the yard the farmhouse stands square against the sky with no lights on inside it.
ACTION: He stands in the dark a moment, then starts across the grass toward it.
