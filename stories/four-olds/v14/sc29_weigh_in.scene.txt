@@SCENE@@
ACTION: Six crates sit on their cradles down the center of the integration hall, each one taller than a man.
ACTION: Stencils run huge along the flanks. LOT A, OLD IDEAS. LOT B, OLD CULTURE. LOT C, OLD CUSTOMS. LOT D, OLD HABITS.
ACTION: The last two crates carry the same words. LOT E and LOT F, both stenciled SUPPLEMENTARY CONTAINMENT.
ACTION: PELL, 50s, his lanyard squared dead center on his chest, walks the row with a paper cup the size of a shot glass.
ACTION: A PHOTOGRAPHER walks backward ahead of him with the camera already up.
PELL: Random re-weigh, folks. Compliance is a rhythm. Random means random.
ACTION: MACK BOONE, 55, stands off the first cradle with the manifest folded small in one hand.
ACTION: At the floor scale a SCALE TECH, 40s, cracks the lid on a thermos and watches his readout.
ACTION: Pell lifts the little cup toward the crane cab overhead.
PELL: (calling up) Morning up there. Nice smooth lifts today, thank you.
ACTION: Up in the cab, JOSS REED, 26, keys the comm without taking his eyes off the hook.
JOSS (RADIO): Copy.
ACTION: The first crate settles onto the scale and the number climbs and holds.
SCALE TECH: (reading) Lot A. Declared three-eighty. Reads four hundred eleven and two.
PELL: (savoring) Four hundred and eleven. Kilograms. (then) People here still weigh in pounds. I did a year over in Rotterdam, you don't go back from that.
ACTION: Three more crates ride across the scale, one after the other.
SCALE TECH: (reading) B, declared three-ninety, reads four twenty-one. (then) C, three-seventy, reads four-oh-one. (then) D, three-eighty, four eleven again.
ACTION: Pell stops walking. He looks at the readout, then at the manifest in Mack's hand.
PELL: Thirty kilos over. Times the four of them. (then) That's a hundred and twenty kilos of something, Mr. Boone.
ACTION: The hall goes quiet enough to hear the air handlers.
MACK: Could be the couch upgrade. Archival spec revision came through in March, they put mass into the—
PELL: Re-run it. Cell check first.
ACTION: The tech comes off his stool and raps the scale housing under the crate with two knuckles.
ACTION: The readout jumps, drops, jumps again.
ACTION: ON THE READOUT -- 411.9. 408.2. 413.5.
ACTION: In the crane cab, Joss sits with his hands off the levers and does not move.
ACTION: The tech looks at the crate on the scale a long moment. LOT A, OLD IDEAS, painted big across the flank at his eye line.
ACTION: He looks down at his clipboard and holds there.
ACTION: His thermos steams on the console beside his hand.
ACTION: He clicks the pen out and starts writing, reading it off low as he goes.
SCALE TECH: (reading) Calibration drift, cell three. All four reads invalidated. Corrected tare applied. Within tolerance after correction.
ACTION: He initials the bottom, tears the copy loose, and holds it up over his shoulder without turning.
ACTION: Mack takes it. The tech is already back on his stool with the thermos, and he never once looks at Mack.
ACTION: In the cab, Joss lowers his hands back onto the levers, one finger at a time.
ACTION: Pell takes the sheet from Mack and reads it. Then he reads it again.
PELL: (reading) Hm.
ACTION: He squares the sheet to the corner of his clipboard and slides it under the clip.
PELL: (to Mack) Eleven years I've closed these clean. (then) You keep a tidy load, Mr. Boone. They'd like you over there.
MACK: Long as the paperwork's square.
ACTION: The photographer steps in close and raises the camera.
PELL: (to the photographer) Get the D crate behind me. Light's better on the D.
ACTION: Pell uncaps his pen and signs the inspection tag wired to LOT D, then feeds it into the holder on the flank.
ACTION: ON THE TAG -- INSPECTED, signed in Pell's own hand.
ACTION: The shutter clicks.
