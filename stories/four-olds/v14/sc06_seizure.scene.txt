ACTION: Two black SUVs and a county flatbed climb the gravel drive toward the Dawes farmhouse and barn.
ACTION: In the lead SUV a phone sits in a dashboard mount, a voice coming off the speaker, tinny and bored.
BOSS (RADIO): One verified recovery on the books by Saturday, or your office rolls into Lincoln's. That's the whole conversation, Pell.
ACTION: In the passenger seat rides PELL, 50s, his lanyard squared on a pressed shirt and a clipboard flat on his knee.
PELL: You'll have it.
ACTION: He taps the phone off and checks his hair in the visor mirror, turning his chin one way, then the other.
ACTION: He steps out of the SUV already smiling.
ACTION: CRICKET stands square in the open barn doorway, Earl Dawes, 79, the painted Moon on the wall behind him.
ACTION: Two movers in gloves wait at the flatbed with a dolly.
ACTION: WADE hangs back by the porch steps, a county deputy in his forties, his hat already off and held in both hands.
ACTION: Pell walks the length of the barn with the clipboard up, looking at the joints in the roof beams.
PELL: Somebody framed this to last. You don't see the old peg work anymore.
ACTION: He clicks his pen and starts down the inventory.
PELL: (reading) One crew couch. One cradle assembly, welded. One altitude indicator—
CRICKET: Attitude.
ACTION: Pell stops, pen up.
ACTION: He draws a neat line through the word and prints above it.
PELL: Noted.
CRICKET: It was surplus. Nineteen seventy-five. Told it'd be scrapped otherwise.
PELL: (not looking up) Paperwork says otherwise.
ACTION: A mover presses an orange sticker onto the trainer's frame and Pell smooths it flat with his thumb.
ACTION: The sticker reads LUNAR HERITAGE NEUTRALIZATION ACCORD -- EX-NASA ASSET RECOVERY.
ACTION: Pell drifts to the back wall and pulls down one spiral notebook, thumbs it open.
ACTION: Three pages of grid-ruled block printing pass under his thumb.
PELL: Personal papers.
PELL: (then) No salvage value. Leave them.
ACTION: He pushes the notebook back onto the shelf crooked and moves on.
ACTION: At the dolly a mover cracks his knuckle on a bolt head and shakes out his hand.
ACTION: Cricket reaches to the pegboard, takes down a socket, and holds it out.
ACTION: The mover takes it without looking at him and fits it to the bolt.
ACTION: The two movers track back and forth across the porch, their boots on boards painted only a few days back.
ACTION: Cricket watches the dolly take the weight of the couch.
CRICKET: (quiet, hard) Not one of you ever built a thing out here. Now it's all yours.
ACTION: Pell stops writing and looks at him for the first time.
PELL: You didn't build this either, Mr. Dawes. Nineteen seventy-five it should've gone back to NASA, and it never did, and nobody asked why for fifty years. It was never yours. Paperwork just took its time getting here.
ACTION: Cricket says nothing.
ACTION: The dolly turns for the open doors, and Cricket steps into the gap and sets his feet.
CRICKET: (to Wade) You'll have to move me, Wade.
ACTION: Wade sets his hat on the porch rail and comes down the steps slow.
WADE: Earl.
ACTION: He takes Cricket by both arms, gentle, and walks him three steps to the side, and Cricket lets him.
ACTION: The dolly rolls past the two of them and out into the daylight.
ACTION: Wade stays in close at his shoulder, voice down, while the movers load.
WADE: (low) Federal list came down Tuesday. County don't see a dime off it. Nobody does.
CRICKET: Where's it go.
WADE: Warehouse outside Lincoln, is what I hear. Same one as the rest of it.
WADE: (then) There's a window to put in an appeal. It's open till four.
ACTION: Inside the barn a clean rectangle of floor shows where the trainer stood, four bolt holes at its corners.
ACTION: Cricket steps to the edge of the rectangle and stops.
ACTION: He presses one hand hard to the back of his neck, takes one breath, and straightens.
ACTION: Pell pulls the SUV door open and turns back with a smile for the whole yard.
PELL: Folks, I know change feels sudden. But as the Accord reminds us -- harmony is a verb.
ACTION: The SUVs and the flatbed pull out and head down the drive.
ACTION: On the workbench the old field radio sits where they left it, still wired in.
ACTION: Cricket crosses to it, lowers himself onto the stool, and thumbs the microphone.
CRICKET: Net's open. Wednesday.
ACTION: Three voices come back fast, one climbing over the other.
STITCH (RADIO): Here.
DUTCH (RADIO): Present.
GUNNY (RADIO): Standing by.
ACTION: Cricket reaches up and pulls the work light on over the empty rectangle.
