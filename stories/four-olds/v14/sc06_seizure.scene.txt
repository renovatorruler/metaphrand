ACTION: Two black SUVs and a county flatbed climb the gravel drive and stop short of the barn.
ACTION: In the lead SUV, PELL rides shotgun, fifties, pressed shirt, a laminated badge on a lanyard, a phone propped in the dashboard mount.
ACTION: A voice comes off the phone, tinny and bored.
HOLLOWAY (RADIO): Saturday. And a real one, something that actually verifies, not some old shed you wrote up to pad your numbers. You miss it, they put your office under Lincoln's, and I got no reason left to pick up when you call.
PELL: You'll have it.
ACTION: Pell taps the phone dark, flips down the visor, and checks his hair in the little mirror. He steps out already smiling.
ACTION: CRICKET stands in the open barn doorway, seventy-nine, coveralls, both boots planted on porch boards still bright with new paint.
ACTION: Off by the flatbed waits WADE, mid-forties, county tan, his hat in both hands.
PELL: Morning. Real pretty piece of ground you got up here.
ACTION: Cricket says nothing. Pell keeps coming, a tablet in the crook of his arm.
PELL: Appreciate everybody's patience this morning. Truly. We'll be light on our feet, in and gone.
ACTION: Two movers in company polos wheel a dolly up the ramp and past Cricket into the barn.
PELL: Look at that framing. They don't put a building together like this anymore.
ACTION: Pell thumbs the tablet and reads down a list.
PELL: All right. One crew couch, one panel assembly, one altitude indicator--
CRICKET: Attitude.
ACTION: Pell clicks a pen and marks the line.
PELL: Noted.
ACTION: Inside, a mover cracks his knuckle on the steel cradle and hisses through his teeth.
ACTION: Cricket lifts a socket off the pegboard and holds it out. The mover takes it and can't look at him.
CRICKET: It was surplus. Nineteen seventy-five. Told it'd be scrapped otherwise.
ACTION: Pell doesn't look up from the tablet.
PELL: Paperwork says otherwise.
ACTION: He peels an orange sticker off a sheet and smooths it flat to the panel with his thumb. The sticker reads LUNAR HERITAGE NEUTRALIZATION ACCORD, EX-NASA ASSET RECOVERY.
ACTION: Pell drifts to the back wall and pulls one spiral notebook down. He fans three pages of grid-ruled block printing.
PELL: Personal papers.
ACTION: He looks another second, then drops it back on the shelf crooked.
PELL: No salvage value. Leave them.
ACTION: The movers get the dolly under the trainer and take up the weight.
CRICKET: Not one of you ever built a thing out here. Now it's all yours.
ACTION: Pell stops. He sets the tablet down at his side and looks at Cricket, and the warmth goes out of his voice without any heat coming into it.
PELL: You didn't build this either, Mr. Dawes. Nineteen seventy-five, it should've gone back to NASA and it never did, and nobody asked why for fifty years. You kept it. That's all. Long enough that theft starts to feel like inheritance.
ACTION: Cricket doesn't answer that.
ACTION: The dolly rolls the trainer down the ramp and out into the daylight. A clean rectangle shows on the floor where it stood, four bolt holes at the corners.
ACTION: Cricket steps into the empty doorway and sets his boots.
CRICKET: You'll have to move me, Wade.
ACTION: Wade comes up the ramp, hat still in one hand.
WADE: Earl.
ACTION: He takes Cricket by both arms, gentle, and walks him a few steps to the side. Cricket lets him. Neither says anything more.
ACTION: Wade keeps his voice low, close to Cricket, his back to Pell.
WADE: Federal list came down Tuesday. County don't see a dime off it. Nobody does.
CRICKET: Where's it go.
WADE: Warehouse outside Lincoln, is what I hear. Same one as the rest of it.
ACTION: Wade turns the hat over in his hands.
WADE: There's a window to put in an appeal on it. It's open till four, if you want the number.
ACTION: The movers slide the trainer onto the flatbed and throw a strap over it. Pell signs the tablet and tucks his pen away.
PELL: Folks, I know change feels sudden. But like the Accord reminds us, harmony is a verb.
ACTION: He gives the doorway a small nod, climbs into the SUV, and the two trucks pull off down the drive with the flatbed behind.
ACTION: The dust settles over the yard. Cricket walks back in and stops at the edge of the bare rectangle.
ACTION: He presses one hand hard to the back of his neck, takes one breath, and straightens.
ACTION: On the workbench sits a small radio the movers left behind. Cricket thumbs it on.
CRICKET: Net's open. Wednesday.
ACTION: The speaker crackles and three voices come back over each other, fast.
STITCH (RADIO): We're here, Earl.
GUNNY (RADIO): Standing by.
DUTCH (RADIO): Copy. Go ahead when you're set.
ACTION: Cricket reaches up and pulls the work light on over the empty floor.
