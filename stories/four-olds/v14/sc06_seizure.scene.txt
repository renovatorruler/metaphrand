@@SCENE@@
ACTION: Two black SUVs and a county flatbed climb the dirt drive toward the barn.
ACTION: In the lead SUV a phone sits in a dashboard mount, a call running on speaker. ADMINISTRATOR J. PELL -- 50s, pressed collar, a lanyard he keeps touching -- rides in the passenger seat.
BOSS (RADIO): I need one on the books by Saturday, Pell. A real one, serial number and all. You miss it, they fold your office into Lincoln and I quit taking your calls.
PELL: You'll have it.
ACTION: Pell taps the phone dark. He flips the visor down and checks his hair in the little mirror.
ACTION: He steps out of the SUV already smiling.
ACTION: Movers in gray coveralls drop the flatbed ramp. A steel dolly rolls off it.
ACTION: WADE HALVERSEN -- mid-40s, county deputy, hat already off and in his hand -- hangs back by the second truck and doesn't start toward the barn.
ACTION: At the barn door stands CRICKET DAWES -- 79, seed-corn cap, hands loose at his sides. He doesn't come out to meet them.
ACTION: Behind him in the dim wait three old men among the folding chairs. DUTCH, STITCH, and GUNNY. None of them sit down.
PELL: Mr. Dawes. Morning. And thank you, real sincerely, for your patience with all this.
ACTION: Cricket says nothing. Pell tips his head back at the rafters.
PELL: They built these to last, didn't they. You'd pay a fortune for joinery like this over on the continent.
ACTION: Pell clicks his pen and starts down the sheet on his clipboard.
PELL: (reading) Alright. One altitude indicator—
CRICKET: Attitude.
ACTION: Pell marks the correction without a word.
PELL: Noted.
ACTION: A mover presses an orange sticker to the panel and smooths it flat with his thumb. LUNAR HERITAGE NEUTRALIZATION ACCORD -- EX-NASA ASSET RECOVERY.
CRICKET: It was surplus. Nineteen seventy-five. Told it'd be scrapped otherwise.
PELL: (not looking up) Paperwork says otherwise.
ACTION: A mover carries a strap across the farmhouse porch for a shortcut. His boots leave gray scuffs in paint that went on this week.
ACTION: Pell drifts to the back wall and slides one notebook off the shelf. He fans three pages of grid-ruled block printing.
PELL: Personal papers.
ACTION: He turns a page, then sets the notebook back crooked on the shelf.
PELL: No salvage value. Leave them.
ACTION: A mover works a bolt at the base of the cradle and skins his knuckle. He shakes his hand and looks over at the pegboard.
ACTION: Cricket lifts the right socket off the board and holds it out. The man takes it and can't bring himself to look up.
CRICKET: (quiet and hard) Not one of you ever built a thing out here. Now it's all yours.
ACTION: Pell stops writing. He looks at Cricket for the first time.
PELL: You didn't build this either, Mr. Dawes. Nineteen seventy-five it should've gone back to NASA, and it never did, and for fifty years nobody so much as asked. You just had it a long time. Long enough it started to feel like yours.
ACTION: Cricket doesn't answer him.
ACTION: The dolly rolls toward the door with the crew couch strapped down on it.
ACTION: Cricket steps into the doorway and plants himself square in it, feet set, hands at his sides.
ACTION: The movers stop. Nobody moves.
PELL: (to Wade) Deputy.
ACTION: Wade comes across the yard slow, hat still in his hand.
CRICKET: You'll have to move me, Wade.
WADE: (low) Earl. Federal list came down Tuesday. County don't see a dime off any of it. Nobody does.
ACTION: Behind Cricket, Stitch shifts a half step forward.
STITCH: Where's it headed.
WADE: (to Stitch) Warehouse outside Lincoln, is what I hear. Same one as the rest of it.
WADE: (to Cricket) There's a window to appeal it. It's open till four.
ACTION: Wade takes Cricket's arm with both hands. He doesn't say anything but the name.
WADE: Earl.
ACTION: He walks Cricket three steps to the side. Cricket lets him. His eyes never leave the couch.
ACTION: The dolly rolls past the two of them, through the door, and down the ramp.
ACTION: Pell tucks the clipboard under his arm and turns to the men left standing in the yard.
PELL: Folks, I know change feels sudden. But as the Accord reminds us -- harmony is a verb.
ACTION: He gets in the SUV. The two trucks and the flatbed pull down the drive, and the dust hangs a while and settles.
ACTION: Inside the barn, where the trainer stood, a clean rectangle shows on the concrete, four bright bolt holes at its corners.
ACTION: Cricket walks to the edge of it and stops.
ACTION: He presses one hand hard to the back of his neck. One breath. Then he straightens up.
ACTION: On the workbench sits an old two-way radio the movers never listed.
ACTION: Dutch, Stitch, and Gunny come in from the yard and stand along the edge of the dust with him.
CRICKET: Net's open. Wednesday.
GUNNY: We'll be here.
STITCH: I'll bring the pot.
DUTCH: Nineteen hundred, then.
ACTION: Cricket reaches up and pulls the cord on the work light. It drops a circle of light on the bare concrete.
