@@SCENE@@
ACTION: The barn stove ticks with heat. Three lawn chairs and a nail keg crowd up close to it, and behind them the bare rectangle on the concrete still shows its four bolt holes.
ACTION: GUNNY TIBBS -- 71, Navy ballcap, a wooden crate open at his feet -- lifts out a cardboard tube and stands it on the floor. More of them sit packed in rows inside.
GUNNY: Fourth of July stock. Bought it two years before they banned the good ones. Nobody wrote it down anyplace.
ACTION: CRICKET DAWES -- 79, at the coffee pot with his back to the crate -- doesn't turn around.
CRICKET: For what.
ACTION: Gunny sets the tube down harder than he has to.
GUNNY: I did two years on a missile submarine off Alaska, listening for Soviet propellers, so a man like Earl could keep a barn out here no Russian could touch. And they send a fella in a zip-up vest with a sticker gun.
GUNNY: (then) Commies. I don't care what flag they fly over the building.
ACTION: STITCH McGREW -- 74, feet up near the stove -- turns a tube in his hand and reads the side of it.
STITCH: These'll go four, five hundred feet, easy.
ACTION: DUTCH HAAS -- 76, a mug he hasn't touched -- doesn't look up.
DUTCH: Three hundred. It's printed on the box.
STITCH: They always look higher than that once it's good and dark.
ACTION: Dutch sets the mug down on the floor by his boot.
DUTCH: I hauled that trainer up from Houston in a horse trailer. Seventy-five. Signed it off a supply captain who wanted it gone off his books.
DUTCH: (then) Fifty years I kept the government's property safe for the government. And they sent a man to take it back like I stole it.
ACTION: Gunny nods at the crate on the floor.
GUNNY: Office locks up at five. That gravel lot out back is empty by dark. Four cases of these up over the parking lot, and we're home before the news.
CRICKET: That's a federal building.
GUNNY: It's a strip mall with a flagpole, Earl.
CRICKET: I've got a grandson who still thinks I hung the moon. I'm not explaining a courtroom to him. Count me out.
GUNNY: Rest of us, then. Friday.
ACTION: Cricket sets the pot back on the burner. He looks at the crate a second, then at the three of them.
CRICKET: Do what you're going to do. Just don't tell me about it after.
ACTION: That night the compliance office sits dark behind its flagpole. Around the back there's gravel, a dumpster, and nothing else, and the three of them stand out in the middle of it in no hurry at all.
ACTION: Stitch braces a tube in a length of pipe against a cinder block and touches a lighter to the fuse.
ACTION: A shell climbs and cracks red over the lot. Then green. Then a long gold that just hangs there.
ACTION: A county truck rolls in slow, gravel popping under the tires. WADE HALVERSEN -- mid-40s, deputy, hat already in his hand -- climbs out and looks up at the sky before he looks at them.
WADE: Y'all want to tell me whose idea this was, or should I guess?
GUNNY: Mine.
DUTCH: His, but I brought the truck.
STITCH: I just held the lighter. Following orders.
ACTION: Above them a last shell lifts and pops gold. None of the three so much as flinches. Wade watches it burn down to nothing.
WADE: Anything left in that pipe?
GUNNY: She's cold.
ACTION: The next morning the three of them stand at a table in the county courtroom in their pressed shirts. JUDGE BENNING -- 70s, reading glasses low on his nose -- looks over a single sheet of paper.
ACTION: On the table in front of him lies one photograph of a scorched patch of gravel. Nobody was hurt.
BENNING: How do you gentlemen plead.
GUNNY: Guilty.
DUTCH: Guilty on all of it.
STITCH: Guilty, Your Honor.
ACTION: A young prosecutor starts to stand with a finger raised.
BENNING: I was nine years old in my daddy's living room the night men from this state walked on the Moon. Watched it on a television the size of a bread box. Time served. Four hundred dollars each, plus the cost of the gravel. I've got real criminals on this morning's docket, gentlemen. Get on home.
ACTION: The gavel cracks down before the prosecutor is all the way up out of his chair.
ACTION: Outside, Cricket leans against the fender of his pickup at the bottom of the courthouse steps. The three come down slow, blinking in the daylight.
GUNNY: (to Cricket) Come to gloat?
CRICKET: Came to drive you home.
ACTION: Nobody moves for a second. Cricket reaches back and opens the passenger door.
GUNNY: You could've come.
CRICKET: I know it.
ACTION: Cricket claps him once, hard, on the shoulder, then gets in behind the wheel.
