@@SCENE@@
ACTION: Two SUVs and a county flatbed grind up the dirt drive toward the barn.
ACTION: In the lead SUV a phone runs on speaker in the dashboard mount, the voice on it tinny and close.
ACTION: The man in the passenger seat is Administrator Pell, fifties, a lanyard he keeps touching.
HOLLOWAY (RADIO): I need one on the books by Saturday, Pell. A real one, serial number and all. You miss it, they fold your office into Lincoln and I quit taking your calls.
PELL: You'll have it.
ACTION: The phone taps dark and the visor snaps down. For a second there's nothing.
ACTION: The SUV door opens and Pell steps out onto the dirt.
ACTION: The movers drop the flatbed ramp and a steel dolly rolls off it onto the dirt.
ACTION: At the barn door Cricket stands with his boots set on the boards and doesn't come out to meet them.
ACTION: Back in the barn behind him the floor creaks under three men who stay on their feet, Dutch and Stitch and Gunny.
PELL: (warm) Mr. Dawes. Morning. And thank you, real sincerely, for your patience with all this.
ACTION: Cricket doesn't answer.
PELL: They built these to last, didn't they. You'd pay a fortune for joinery like this over on the continent.
ACTION: Pell clicks his pen and runs it down the clipboard.
PELL: (reading) Alright. One altitude indicator—
CRICKET: Attitude.
ACTION: Pell's pen scratches the correction in.
PELL: Noted.
ACTION: A mover peels a sticker and presses it flat onto the panel.
CRICKET: It was surplus. Nineteen seventy-five. Told it'd be scrapped otherwise.
PELL: (not looking up) Paperwork says otherwise.
ACTION: A mover cuts across the farmhouse porch and his boots knock over the fresh-painted boards.
ACTION: Pell slides a notebook off the back shelf and fans the pages.
PELL: Personal papers.
ACTION: He turns a page and drops the notebook back crooked on the shelf.
PELL: No salvage value. Leave them.
ACTION: Down at the cradle a wrench works a bolt loose, and the mover on it hisses and shakes his hand.
ACTION: Cricket lifts a socket off the pegboard and holds it out, and the man takes it without a word.
CRICKET: (quiet and hard) Not one of you ever built a thing out here. Now it's all yours.
ACTION: Pell's pen stops.
PELL: You didn't build this either, Mr. Dawes. Nineteen seventy-five it should've gone back to NASA, and it never did, and for fifty years nobody so much as asked. You just had it a long time. Long enough it started to feel like yours.
ACTION: Cricket doesn't answer him.
ACTION: The dolly rolls toward the door with the crew couch strapped down on it.
ACTION: Cricket steps into the doorway and plants his boots square on the boards.
ACTION: The dolly stops. Nobody moves.
PELL: (to Wade) Deputy.
ACTION: Wade Halversen, the county deputy, comes across the yard slow with his hat in his hand.
CRICKET: You'll have to move me, Wade.
WADE: (low) Earl. Federal list came down Tuesday. County don't see a dime off any of it. Nobody does.
ACTION: Behind Cricket, Stitch shifts a half step and a board creaks.
STITCH: Where's it headed.
WADE: (to Stitch) Warehouse outside Lincoln, is what I hear. Same one as the rest of it.
WADE: (to Cricket) There's a window to appeal it. It's open till four.
ACTION: Wade takes Cricket's arm in both hands, and the sleeve cloth bunches under them.
WADE: Earl.
ACTION: He walks Cricket three steps to the side, and Cricket lets him.
ACTION: The dolly rolls past the two of them, out the door, and down the ramp.
ACTION: Pell tucks the clipboard under his arm and turns to the men left in the yard.
PELL: Folks, I know change feels sudden. But as the Accord reminds us — harmony is a verb.
ACTION: Pell gets in the SUV. The trucks and the flatbed pull down the drive, and the engines fade off until the yard goes quiet.
ACTION: Cricket's boots cross the concrete and stop where the trainer stood, and the empty barn throws the sound back at him.
ACTION: He lets out one long breath.
ACTION: He reaches over the workbench and clicks on an old two-way radio the movers never listed, and it comes up in a low hiss.
ACTION: Dutch, Stitch, and Gunny come in from the yard and stand along the edge of the bare floor with him.
CRICKET: Net's open. Wednesday.
GUNNY: We'll be here.
STITCH: I'll bring the pot.
DUTCH: Nineteen hundred, then.
ACTION: Cricket reaches up and the work light's pull-chain clicks.
