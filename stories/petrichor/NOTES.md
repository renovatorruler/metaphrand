# PETRICHOR — working notes (premise locked, development open)

**Logline:** A girl who believes she inherited the worst of her feuding parents' two bloodlines — her mother's storms without the control, her father's wild growth without the patience — discovers, when the valley is dying and neither side can save it, that rain held by roots is the only cure: she has the best of both, and the proof is the smell of rain on dry earth, which neither family can make alone.

**Transformation (the spine):** "I am the worst combination of my parents" → "I am the best combination of my parents." The lie is tested out of her, never talked out of her. THE HEIR's mirror, pointed at the sun.

## Locked
- **Tone/genre:** Disney/Pixar animated feature, ~11-year-old protagonist.
- **The two folk:** Storm folk (sky, rain, big feelings = weather; blues/silvers) and Garden folk (rooted, patient, growth; greens/ochres), one valley split by an ancient hedge-wall with one gap.
- **DESIGN RULE (user, 2026-06-09): one people, two cultures.** Storm and Garden folk are the SAME ethnicity everywhere; the divide is dress, color, and way of life only. The protagonist must NOT read as mixed-race — the bloodline blend lives exclusively in fantasy traits (weather-hair with sprouts + one stubborn flower, micro-storms when she feels, wild growth where she lingers, patchwork storm/garden clothes). Every art prompt carries this rule.
- **Her parents:** the scandal marriage that "made peace" by suppression — mom hasn't stormed in years, dad prunes everything square. The household models the lie before anyone says it. (Load-bearing per the weave law.)
- **The grandmothers:** mirror matriarchs, one per side; each claims her with a side-name (Squall / Sprout); the "fix her by un-mixing" rite is theirs — love-as-erasure (the antagonist shape).
- **The engine beats:** promise up front · the clock = the drought · doorway 1 = she chooses the un-mixing rite · all-is-lost = the rite WORKS (perfect clear sky / perfect topiary, applause, and she feels nothing — cured into emptiness) · the test = the drought's break needs storm + roots in one body · the realization image = PETRICHOR — the smell after rain, made only when rain meets earth; both crowds breathe it in together.
- **Sidekick:** a tumbleweed — the plant that travels like wind; a weed to one side, "too slow" to the other; the answer rolling at her feet all film.
- **The naming device:** each side calls her by its own name; her true name is barely used until the end. (Name TBD — NOT "Mira," collides with THE HEIR's nurse.)

## Locked (cont.)
- **PIXAR MODE — no songs (user, 2026-06-09).** Interiority carried by world-rules and physical business, not numbers. Consequences: (a) the suppression arc lives in ritual/props — she braids her hair flat to pin the weather down, wears dad's pruning gloves to not-touch the ground, carries the grandmother's "calm stone"; (b) the world needs tight Pixar rules: storm-folk feelings leak as weather; garden-folk growth answers only their settledness; HER double mechanism is self-defeating — her rain drowns her own sprouts, her vines snag her own wind — the "worst of both" is a working machine, comedy and thesis in one; (c) the petrichor must be PLANTED as a sensory ritual early — the parents met at the hedge-gap after a rain; "that smell is why I spoke to your mother" — the family's private joy, paid off valley-wide at the end.

## Open forks
- **Her name.** Leading pitch: **WREN** — the bird that nests INSIDE hedges: she's named for the bird that nested in the wall the year she was born; the creature of the sky that makes its home in the thing dividing the families; folklore says wrens sing before rain. Each side still calls her Squall/Sprout; "Wren" is barely used until the end.
- The parents' own un-suppression beats (mom storms again / dad lets a corner go wild) — placement.
