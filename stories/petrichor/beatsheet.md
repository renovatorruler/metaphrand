# PETRICHOR — story spine (the seven-step pass)

Pixar mode, no songs. Canon: `NOTES.md`. Engine seed: `examples/petrichor.py`.

**1 · Transformation:** "I am the worst combination of my parents" → "I am the best combination of my parents." Tested, never declared.
**2 · Two worlds:** Storm side / Garden side of one valley, split by the hedge-wall; one people, two cultures; she belongs to neither → she is the gap where they meet.
**3 · Logline as for-loop:** *For every crisis, Wren tries to be less of one half of herself; each try works and costs her more of herself — until the drought demands the one thing only the whole of her can do.* Exception clause: roots first, then rain.
**4 · Two doorways:** D1 — she volunteers for the Settling (chooses self-erasure, wants it). D2 — after the family shatters, she demands the rite NOW, publicly (no return: it works).
**5 · Midpoint mirror:** she discovers her parents' secret night garden — mom storming softly, dad letting ground go wild, together — the only green left in the drought. Truth shown; kid-misread ("even they hide it → it IS shameful"): lie reinforced with evidence, stakes raised.
**6 · The test:** the dust-front and the water-war; roots first (gloves off, garden-stillness inside terror), then the banked storm (the braid undone) — the lattice holds the rain. Petrichor rises; both crowds breathe in.
**7 · Pacing:** valleys at 10 (Burr adopts her), 12 (the night garden, hushed), 16 (cured-empty quiet devastation), 21 (resolution warmth). Comedy front-loaded in the machine beats; act 2B runs nearly silent.

## The heart pass (2026-06-10 — bonds as ledgers; engine: metaphrand/heart.py, seed gate-green)
- **The master discovery: the drought IS the frozen bank.** In a world where feelings are weather, the clock and the heart layer fuse — no rain means the valley's one communal deposit (the smell; every adult's courtship and childhood) physically cannot be withdrawn. The feud isn't just old; it's *illiquid*. The climax isn't only water arriving — it's the first possible withdrawal in years, valley-wide.
- **Wren↔her parents:** deposit = the bedtime story (banked in the prologue, as a love story, about nothing). It pays TWICE: privately at the dark night (dad's words re-heard — "roots first, then rain" — the deposit unlocking the mechanism) and publicly at the riverbed (the smell itself). The SOUR = the midpoint: the night garden, the parents' own secret deposit-place, re-keyed by child logic into evidence of shame.
- **The tender wounds:** the suppression kit is administered as love — if MOM braids the hair flat each morning, and the gloves are DAD'S old gloves, then every morning's muzzling is also an embrace. Deposits and wounds in the same objects; this is why the kit can become the sequencer later instead of just a cage.
- **The begging that bounces (pre-D2):** after the split, each parent comes to her separately — mom explains, dad pleads — and it cannot land; arguments don't thaw. And the deposit that COULD reach her is drought-locked: there is no rain to smell. That's why "Settle me now" is inevitable rather than stubborn.
- **Cured-empty = the closed account:** she reaches for the bedtime story and can't feel it (the bank refuses her own withdrawals). **Burr's exit is the unanswered knock** — the inverse almost-thaw: the deposit works, circles twice, keeps time at her door, and the account doesn't answer. (Burr bond wired in the seed: deposit "kept time" at adoption; wound "the unanswered knock" at cured.)
- **The parents' thaw is their own deposit:** at the riverbed, as Wren's roots take and the first smell rises, it ambushes Tessa and Alder with *their own courtship* — they met at the hedge-gap after rain. Mom doesn't break ranks because anyone argued; she's thawed by the thing she banked before Wren existed. Same mechanism, one generation up.
- **The communal thaw:** both mobs stopping mid-shove is the heart law at village scale — nobody is persuaded; an entire valley is ambushed by its own banked past at once. The grandmothers' bench at the end is the feud's first interest payment.

## Cast ledger (weave law — every principal ≥2 edges, no cost on an unbuilt edge)
- **WREN, 11** — weather-hair + sprouts; her two traits FEED each other (her rain carries seeds; her calm grows storm-fast), which is why "worst" and, run in the right order, "best."
- **TESSA (mom, Storm)** — hasn't stormed publicly in years; secret night-rain. Edges: suppression model → night garden → family split → storms openly at the climax.
- **ALDER (dad, Garden)** — prunes everything square; secret wild corner; the courtship line: "that smell is why I spoke to your mother." Edges: the story that plants petrichor → night garden → split → roots beside her at the climax.
- **GRANDMA BORAH (Storm matriarch)** & **GRANDMA HEDDA (Garden matriarch)** — mirror snobs; the namers (Squall/Sprout); keepers of the Settling (love-as-erasure); custody of a parent each after the split; forced to interlock their tools (catchers + furrows) at the climax; share a bench at the end.
- **BURR the tumbleweed** — wind-traveling plant; a weed to one side, "too slow" to the other. Edges: adopts her → abandons her when Settled (reads as neither) → found nested in the hedge (the wren's spot) → returns carrying her seeds. The burr: the part of her that won't be removed.
- **The hidden garden** (a place as character) — where the parents met after rain; where the wren nested (her name); the midpoint discovery; the dark-night site; dead by D2, first green again at the end.
- Deliberately unwired texture: the mayor (comedy), the schoolteacher.

## Beats

### Act 1 — the machine of "worst" (the lie-world)
1. **Prologue / the promise.** Rain ending at the hedge-gap years ago; two crowds dispersing; a young storm-woman and garden-man linger, breathing in. Frame: ALDER telling little Wren the bedtime story — "that smell is why I spoke to your mother." The wren that nested in the wall that year. (The film's whole thesis, planted as a love story.)
2. **The morning ritual.** Wren, 11: braids the hair flat (pins the weather), dad's old gloves (so nothing grows), Grandma Hedda's calm stone in her pocket. The suppression kit.
3. **The machine, demonstrated.** School: embarrassment → micro-storm → it waters the seedlings her fidgeting sprouted → her own gust tangles in her own vines. Laughter. The names: storm kids call her Sprout, garden kids call her Squall — each side disowns her TO the other side. [comedy that is also the thesis]
4. **The two worlds tour (texture).** Storm terraces: rain-catchers, kites, big-feeling people — running low. Garden terraces: rows, topiary, ration ledgers. The river thin. THE CLOCK: the drought, already pinching both.
5. **Home.** The performed peace: Tessa's elaborate calm routines; Alder's apologetic square garden; dinner where nobody feels anything loudly. Wren learned the kit here.
6. **Inciting: the Grafting Fair.** The one truce day, at the hedge-gap. Wren's moment of hope (she brings a thing she grew/made) → public humiliation → the machine fires at scale: storm + bramble wreck the fair. Both sides see their own worst in her, out loud. The grandmothers collide over her: "Squall—" "Sprout—".
7. **The Settling offered.** The old rite that "settles the blood to one line." The parents can't argue against it — their whole marriage is the quiet version of it. Wren overhears the village verdict: *worst of both.*
8. **DOORWAY 1 — she volunteers.** Wants it. Chooses it. The deal: she'll live a stretch with each grandmother, then the rite settles her to one side at the Equinox. [doorway 1: chosen self-erasure]

### Act 2A — failing at halves
9. **Storm school (Borah).** Be ALL weather, bottle nothing — but her rain carries seeds; she rains a meadow onto the clean stone terraces. Storm folk horrified. Fails at pure storm.
10. **Garden school (Hedda) + BURR.** Stillness, rows, pruning — but her true calm grows thunder-fast, bursting the rows. Fails at pure garden. A tumbleweed blows over the wall, gets called a weed, follows her home. She names it Burr. [valley; the first creature that's also both/neither]
11. **The drought turns political.** The river dies; storm folk hoard catcher-water, garden folk dam the stream; the truce-gap is chained shut. The elders' superstition: "unsettled blood unsettles the sky." Wren becomes the symbol of the feud. Pressure on the rite.
12. **MIDPOINT MIRROR — the night garden.** Sleepless, following a sound (soft thunder), Wren finds it: mom raining gently in the dark over the one hidden unpruned corner — dad's wild patch — the two of them together, secret, practiced, tender. The only green in the valley. The wren's old nest in that hedge. *Her parents never stopped being both; they hid it.* And the kid-misread: if even they must hide it, it really is shameful. [lie reinforced with evidence; the audience sees the truth over her shoulder]
13. **The secret detonates.** Her sprout-trail (or Burr) leads the grandmothers to the night garden. The marriage's "peace" exposed as performance — in front of both sides. Each grandmother takes her own child home. The family splits along the hedge. Wren: *my both did this.*
14. **DOORWAY 2 — "Settle me now."** At the dead fairground, before both crowds, Wren demands the rite immediately — and surrenders the choice of side: "whichever is stronger. I don't care. Take one." [no return]

### Act 2B — cured
15. **The Settling works.** The braid, the stone, the rite. One half goes quiet. She produces, on command, a perfect small clear sky. Applause — the first applause of her life. Both grandmothers, for once, agree: fixed.
16. **Cured-empty.** [near-silent sequence] The flower gone from her hair. Food without savor. The hedge-gap just a gap. She reaches for the bedtime story and can't feel it. And Burr — who tracks wind and root — circles her twice, reads *neither*, and rolls away. She watches it go and feels... nothing, and knows the nothing is wrong. (Parents, separated, each see her and wake.)
17. **ALL IS LOST — the clock detonates.** The deep well fails. Dry lightning fires the last orchard (storm folk blamed); the dust-front rises off the dead fields, rolling toward the valley; both sides meet at the hedge with tools held like weapons over the final water.
18. **The dark night / the mechanism.** Wren at the dead night-garden. Burr found wedged in the hedge — nested where the wren nested. Dad's story replays — and lands differently: *the smell happens when rain meets earth that can hold it. Roots first. Then rain.* She always ran it backward — feelings first, drowning her own sprouts. And the Settling didn't delete her half: it taught her to BANK it. The suppression kit, re-read: not a muzzle — a sequencer. She unbraids nothing yet. She runs.
19. **THE TEST.** The dust-front hits; the water-war starts. Wren walks into the dried riverbed between the two mobs. Gloves off — palms to the dead ground — garden-stillness inside terror (the hardest beat in the film: a child choosing calm while a wall of dust comes on). The lattice spreads, gripping soil and hedge. THEN the braid comes undone: the banked storm of the whole film, all of it, let go — and the roots hold the rain. Tessa breaks ranks and storms openly; Alder kneels and roots beside her; Borah's catchers and Hedda's furrows interlock the two technologies for the first time.
20. **PETRICHOR.** The rain meets held earth. The smell rises. Both mobs stop mid-shove and breathe — every adult ambushed by their own courtship, their own childhood. Green rings out from the riverbed. The hedge-wall blooms; the chained gap becomes a gate.
21. **Resolution.** The fair re-held on green ground. The grandmothers on one bench, grumbling in stereo. Mom's small public rain; dad's gone-wild corner now the show garden. Burr rolls back trailing sprouts — it was carrying her seeds all along. The grandmothers start in: "Squall—" "Sprout—" and the parents, together: **"Her name is Wren."** A wren lands on the blooming gate. Mist off the warm earth. [the cycle re-armed, inverted: the next "wrong blend" kid in the valley has a road now]
