THE SEEING — BEAT SHEET (Save the Cat!)

LOGLINE: A man who dissolved himself into his function until there was no self left to leak -- a professional nobody -- is handed a self by people who are lying to him, and has to decide, with the law screaming in his ear, whether the truest thing he ever made was a con or a confession

Opening Image (p.1): Danny runs a con to its clean end, shakes the mark's hand under a name that isn't his, then sits alone in a sublet that isn't his either, with nothing left to be
Theme Stated (p.6): Reyes slides the cult file across the desk and tells him he's perfect for it: there's nothing of him to get in the way
Set-Up (p.1-10): The eaten life on the counter: an ex-wife's number he won't dial, a roll of film he never develops, a letter from a kid he put away that he leaves unanswered
Catalyst (p.13): Danny comes inside the Collective; they circle him for the Seeing and name, one by one, the man they say is in there, and he stays in the chair
Debate (p.13-25): He tells himself it's the job; he reports a little less each week, stays a little later, and starts sleeping at the loft
Break into Two (p.28): The cover puts a pencil in his hand for the first time in thirty years; he makes one true thing, they hang it on the wall, and he doesn't go home
B Story (p.33): Nora corners him outside the gallery with her brother's unmailed letters; she's after the truth, and she'll take nothing performed, which no one has ever asked of him before
Fun and Games (p.33-55): Deeper in: the work, the table they keep for him, Marsh walking him through the loft with a hand on his shoulder, the badge's calls going to voicemail, the cover no longer coming off at the door
Midpoint (p.55): At the Collective's rebirth rite, told to burn the man who couldn't and make the truest thing he has in front of them all, the cop who came to perform sincerity makes something real, and cannot tell afterward whether he conned them or confessed.
Bad Guys Close In (p.61-75): A buyer shakes Eli's photograph and calls him Marsh's assistant; Danny pulls the thread and finds the dead boy's paintings sold for years under Marsh's name, and Priya already in the chair to be next
All Is Lost (p.83): Reyes tapes the wire to his chest: walk in, give her Marsh, burn it down -- and to keep a badge that was never any realer, he has to torch the only self that was ever his
Dark Night of the Soul (p.83-85): He stands at the rite with the wire live under his shirt and cannot say which of the two men it is wired to; the gift pulling him under is the exact gift that put Eli in the river
Break into Three (p.94): The one question that always stopped him cold, and standing in the rite with the wire live he opens his mouth and answers it out loud for the first time in his life
Finale (p.94-109): Not for the badge and not for Marsh: Danny pulls Priya out of the loft before the machine closes on her and burns the whole con down with his own name on the record
Final Image (p.110): Months on, Danny develops the roll of film at last; in the prints stands a man who is finally somewhere, and across a diner table Nora looks at him and sees him plain

CAST: DANNY (hero), MARSH (shadow), NORA (ally), ELI (herald), REYES (threshold-guardian), PRIYA (shapeshifter), BRENNAN (mentor), COWAN (trickster), AUGUST (ally)

TO THE WRITER: this is the structure, not the prose. Expand each beat into scenes around its page mark; keep the beats and what they mean; you own the continuity and the words.
