# THE HEIR — plotline v4 (the siege, the break, the baptism, the lie)

> **STATUS: approved direction, NOT yet executed in the screenplay.** Supersedes plotline-v3 (whose two-layer engine survives inside this as texture). Co-written 2026-06-09: the resist-ladder spine, the Bonasera ledger, New York, the named square, the full load-bearing weave.

## The spine, in one breath

A man spends the whole film **resisting** becoming his father — really resisting, cleverly, at mounting cost — and *winning*, every time, until the one resistance that costs him his savior. Then he **breaks**: stops resisting in a single night, and authors **the lawyer's baptism** — every enemy, one night, entirely on paper. He seals it with **the lie** to the one person still holding the seat warm. And only after — in his father's old room — the realization that even the resisting was the inheritance: *the arguing was the keeping.* People don't slide into the dark. They hold the door, and hold it, and hold it, and then the door holds them.

## The engine rules (carried forward + new)

- **Two layers (from v3):** Peshat = visible anti-father resistance at every beat. Derash = the resistance's grammar is the father's (total, one-man, absolute). Narrator never states the underside; three rationed naming lines (Bayar ×2, Lena ×1) plus Sevda's prophecy.
- **Voice plan:** Nadir warm/reasonable/hedging ALL the way down — through the break, through the baptism, through **the lie (told in Nate's warmest voice)**. The flat register arrives only after the mirror realization, permanently. The cold open's flat "How many" is that end-state.
- **The resist-ladder:** every Act-2 crisis runs the same figure — *crisis → the iron solution offered → he refuses it → a clever, costly, lawyer's third way → it works → the cost compounds on someone he loves.* By rung three the audience should be half wanting him to break. That's the Godfather trick: you want Michael to handle it.
- **The weave law (the new pass):** no cost may arrive along an unbuilt edge; every principal carries ≥2 plot-causal edges (introduced → changes the plot → pays off). Deliberately unwired for contrast: Adak (a clean machine who belongs to nobody) and the basement clerk (anonymity is the point). This becomes a metaphrand gate after execution.
- **The naming-tell:** the square's two names do silent work everywhere (below).

## World changes

- **New York, not London.** Nadir: Oxford year (where he met Lena), then NYU Law, then a Manhattan firm — corporate practice that pays for the pro-bono asylum files. The American reflex — *everything is a deal* — is his anti-father method, and its failure (bought men get re-bought) is the film's lesson. **Lena stays English**: an English doctor transplanted to New York — a double foreigner, foreign in America, foreign in Karash, never once home in this marriage; her exit speech inherits that.
- **The square has names.** Old name, the one it had in 1989: **AZAT SQUARE** (*azat* — free). The father renamed it after the founding: **FOUNDER'S SQUARE**. The 1989 flashback — young Aslan preaching freedom from a crate — happens in Azat Square; the man who preached there renamed it after himself. Officials say Founder's; the crowd says Azat; which name a character uses is the loyalty-tell. Hamid: "You can tell which way a country leans by what people call the square." Nobody ever explains it.

## The ledger (load-bearing map — nobody new)

- **OSMAN (the Bonasera):** Scene 1, New York — Nadir wins his asylum; "your son's family, when we can get them out." → **Coup night:** the checkpoint sergeant studying faces is **Osman's son**; he recognizes the man who got his father out and waves the car through — Nadir saved by the one currency his father never minted. → **After the bomb:** the sweeps take "the men he let out"; the son goes into the net; Nadir finds the name, signs the release personally — **already dead in custody ("his heart")**. THE BREAK.
- **CLAIRE VOSS (the banker of the original sin):** coup night, 3 a.m., the battalion money — one call to New York, a bridge advance against the pipeline. The West is in his books from night one. Her late line — "a forty-year man, not a forty-year hope" — lands on a compounding account.
- **COLONEL VURAL (the Carlo):** the face of the coup-night phone call ("you haven't been paid since March") — bought, and the proof the method works. → Mid-film, **Anatoly re-buys him** ("You solved it like an American. Now watch an old empire raise the bid."). → The baptism's audit catches him — Anatoly's money in his accounts — so at the square he obeys the lit window **because he is caught, not paid.** In the end fear holds the army: the father's lesson, proven by committee.
- **RUSTAM (the spider):** spared in pyjamas, Act 1. From his cell, through the pensioned believers, **he is the market bomb.** The baptism closes his file the father's way wearing the son's clothes: "transferred to a facility in the east" — the bullet refused in Act 1, fired in Act 3 as paperwork.
- **SEVDA:** the town hall; the date delivered to her door in person. → **Bayar wrote that date** ("You knew it." / "I wrote it."). → Her grandson stands in Azat Square; after the clearing the notification reaches her as **a form letter from an office** — her own prophecy ("there's a version of you coming that sends an office"), fulfilled to the letter. Nobody comments.
- **FERIDA:** enters on coup night — the one judge in Astan who'll sign the 4 a.m. pyjama warrant. → Her courthouse triumph is her spending that credit. → Her fall is the machine eating his first ally.
- **MIRA / KERIM:** freed by his amnesty → in Azat Square the night of the clearing → Kerim dies under the order of the man who freed him → **Mira's call to London is what prompts Lena's question.** Hamid's bookend empty chair is Kerim's.
- **AYGÜN:** media-trained Yusuf in the reform spring (one teasing line) → reads the framing of his confession broadcast: the coach delivering the eulogy.
- **YUSUF:** the institutionalist (the commas scene stays) → the scapegoat the one-man state requires → in the cell he gets the truth as silence; **Lena gets the lie.**
- **BAYAR:** the tempter; the keeper of dates; the chequebook and committees naming lines; the "File it" witness.
- **TURAN:** the honest refusal of the chair; executes the pyjama arrest; takes Eskin's surrender.

### Act 3 — the baptism, the lie, the realization
17. **The lawyer's baptism.** One night, authored, simultaneous, entirely on paper — each order dated, headed, filed: clear *Founder's* Square (Adak's phone rings at last; Kerim under the water-trucks by dawn) · the believers' network rolled up by warrant · **the audit that catches Vural** (he obeys the window because he's caught) · Anatoly's men expelled with notes verbale · the east nationalized by compliance review · **Rustam "transferred to a facility in the east."** Intercut like the font: the calm office, the pen, the stamps — against the square, the trucks, the cell doors. "I don't want the number. Just tell me when it's quiet."
18. **The morning after.** The empty square; "It held." The world accepts the wall: Voss signs Thursday ("a forty-year man"); Anatoly: "he didn't get eaten — he got promoted." **Sevda receives a form letter from an office.** Hamid's shop has an empty chair.
19. **THE LIE (the Kay scene), reached through the figs.** Mira calls London with the news of Kerim — the begging that bounces: Lena, cold, will not dial the palace; the cold has blocked the one call the story needs. Days later a market stall has figs in, and she buys a stupid amount *without deciding to*, stands holding the bag on the pavement — and dials. *(The thaw spends the deposit; nothing and no one argued her into it.)* The only question left: "Yusuf. Tell me you didn't know." And in Nate's own warm voice, the last performance of Nate: **"I didn't know."** She believes him, or chooses to, which is worse. The thaw unlocked the scene that kills the marriage — the Good Wife mechanism with the current run backward through it. Nate's death scene. *(His side of the same deposit: the saved figs voicemail, re-encountered alone at the desk, earlier — one deposit, paid from both ends of a dead marriage.)*
20. **The mirror.** Aslan's old room; the notebooks; the short laugh of a man getting a joke twenty years late. The asylum files taken *at* him, the phone unanswered *at* him, the committees built *at* him. "You didn't need a son who'd keep it. You needed one who'd argue. The arguing was the keeping." — "Okay." **The flat voice from here, permanently.** *(HEART, the design's cruelest symmetry: every living bond in the film freezes — Lena, Yusuf, the country. The only thaw that completes is with the dead man. The notebooks are his father's banked deposit, found posthumously — the young reformer, the laugh Bayar watched go "a chair at a time" — and the one relationship Nadir finally feels his way back into belongs to a corpse.)*

## The heart pass (added 2026-06-10 — the emotional layer, Good Wife anatomy)
> **STATUS: folded into the beatline above (beats 1, 2, 16, 19, 20) — this section is the rationale; the beats are the law.** Engine: `metaphrand/heart.py`, opt-out gate; the heir seed runs it green.

The engine now carries bonds as ledgers (`metaphrand/heart.py`; the heir seed demonstrates it gate-green). The rules: deposits are banked early, in passing, in scenes about something else; wounds set temperature; a cold bond BLOCKS a plot action; only a re-encounter with a banked deposit can thaw it — *you cannot argue someone warm*; deposits can also SOUR (new knowledge re-keys them). Applied to this screenplay:

- **The figs, split three ways (one deposit, paid from both ends of the marriage).** PLANT live in the Act-1 flat scene: he comes home at a reasonable hour for once, too many figs, appetites ruined on the rug — an ordinary-joy scene about nothing. HER thaw: in London, after Mira's call (the begging that bounces — Lena cold, refusing to dial), a market stall has figs in; she buys a stupid amount *without deciding to*, stands holding the bag, and dials the palace. The thaw unlocks the Kay scene — and the truth it earns is the lie. The Good Wife mechanism, run inside a tragedy.
- **The sour (the dark inversion):** Lena's exit speech already re-keys the deposits — the asylum files, the unanswered phone, "even Nate was him." Name it in the staging: she handles the registry-office photo while she says it. The same objects that banked the love now testify for the prosecution.
- **The almost-thaw (the cell):** Yusuf's "say you didn't know" beat gains a deposit-encounter — the radiator/preamble banked in the whiskey scene surfaces (he starts the old joke out of habit, both feel it pull) and for one beat the audience must believe it will work. - **Cross-traffic audit:** Lena's cold must block something the plot needs (the call; earlier, the embassy paperwork only she can sign?); Nadir's frozen state post-break is never argued at — Bayar never persuades, he hands him objects (the folder, the sheet, the file).
- **Balance note (the cold/masculine detector):** the seed now reports tide-traffic in the heart gate; the screenplay rewrite should hold ≥1 deposit scene per act — scenes of ordinary joy with nothing presidential in them, planted before anything needs them.
