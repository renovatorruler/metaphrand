## Voice fix — per-character syntactic ARCHITECTURE (the real lesson)
Plain/complete is the DEFAULT. Break plain only when something inside the character breaks it (motivated, with real temporality — not decorative texture). Ration the good line (≤1 per character per act). KEEP the metaphors and indirection; do not sand them off. The fix is the *lungs*, not the ideas.
- **Nadir — the audible arc.** EARLY: hedges, qualifies, asks instead of states, complete-but-soft ("can we maybe—", "I just think—", "I mean—"). LATE: flat complete declaratives, the father's economy. His syntax HARDENS scene by scene; the descent is something you can hear.
- **Lena:** dry, complete sentences, British filler ("honestly", "the thing is"); says the true thing plainly; never fragments for effect.
- **Bayar:** the one earned control — patient, near-paragraphs that arrive at a quiet point; circles; never staccato.
- **Yusuf:** runs on, over-talks, comma-splices, quotes things, can't stop; the opposite of clipped.
- **The Lion / Aslan:** very few words, but COMPLETE and final; never explains.
- **General Rustam:** blunt, imperative, a soldier's contempt; short complete commands, not poetic fragments.
- **Turan:** blunt soldier, repeats himself, complete.
- **Anatoly:** long, subordinated, periodic; buries the point at the end.
- **Sevda / Mira / ordinary people:** plain; motivated mess only (hesitate because genuinely unsure; can't reach the painful word). Never the writer's fragment-stack.
- **Narrator / action lines:** plain present-tense action that SHOWS. No summarizing ("a question about school funding"), no labeling a beat instead of playing it. The line should read cleanly aloud (it's also the TTS narrator).
- **BANNED house tics (now flags):** the three-fragment stack, "Not X, not Y", dropped-article terseness, the statement-then-stapled-postscript ("He's out. Kerim. The amnesty. Your husband's."), the buttoned mic-drop closer.
- **TEST:** read aloud — does anyone sound *written*? If a line is quotable, suspect it. Could a stranger sort the lines by architecture alone?
