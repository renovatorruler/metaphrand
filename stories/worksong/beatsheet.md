# WORKSONG — story spine (seven-step pass)

Canon: `NOTES.md`. Seed: `examples/worksong.py`.

**1 · Transformation:** "I'm the worst of both" → "I'm the best of both." Tested, never declared.
**2 · Two worlds:** the Colony (tunnels, granaries, the Tally, winter as religion) / the Meadow (bands, jams, the season provides) — one valley, once one economy, divorced by an ancient swarm-year; the fable is the divorce decree.
**3 · Logline as for-loop:** *for each season's task, Tala mutes one half of herself to pass as the other; every pass is praised and costs her more — until the flood-and-swarm winter demands the one craft only both halves make: the work song.* Exception clause: count first, then swing.
**4 · Doorways:** D1 — she petitions for the Sealing (deadline: Frost Tally). D2 — post-flood, exiles underway, she demands it now, publicly.
**5 · Midpoint mirror:** the foundation chamber — tally-marks that are notation; the ants used to sing; her name is carved in the wall. Truth shown, kid-misread ("the song caused the swarm"), lie reinforced.
**6 · The test:** wall vs swarm at the valley mouth; the count struck on the drum-boards, then the lilt — the first work song; crews instead of swarm; the shimmer recedes.
**7 · Pacing valleys:** the aphid-dairy dawn (world-flesh) · Tick's adoption · the chamber hush · cured-empty silence · the finale's warmth.

## Beats
**Act 1:** 1 Pageant prologue (dad in the costume; the applause; small Tala watching) · 2 The courtship told (three notes through a wall = her name) · 3 The kit (bindings, wax, the mute) · 4 The machine misfires (syncopated tally → grain avalanche; "ONE-two-three-four" at the jam) · 5 World-flesh (aphid dairy dawn; meadow dew-harvest) · 6 Inciting cruelty: the pageant casts HER as the grasshopper ("you're half — you'll be convincing") · 7 **D1: she petitions for the Sealing** · 8 The drought-of-rain autumn; stores anxious; refugee bands arriving from the burned south meadow.
**Act 2A:** 9 Meadow trial with Brio (fails the jam) · 10 Colony trial (the metronome heart syncopates under stress) · 11 TICK adopts her · 12 **MIRROR: the foundation chamber** (notation; her name in the wall; Quern: "this room is why we have the fable") · 13 The storm; the sluice her avalanche weakened gives way; the granaries flood · 14 Blame: the refugee bands exiled; Reed walks out with his people; Millet stays with famine duty; the family splits · 15 **D2: "Seal me now."**
**Act 2B:** 16 The Sealing works — the perfect unmusical tally, the first applause · 17 Cured-empty (the mute unneeded; Tick cocks his head, hops away) · 18 ALL IS LOST: winter bites; the camp at the valley mouth crowds; the first wing-shimmer; the colony walls up with half-stores.
**Act 3:** 19 Dark night at the carved wall (Tick keeping time against it; the mechanism: a song is a tally you can feel; count first, then swing) · 20 **THE TEST:** drum-boards between wall and swarm; count → lilt → the work song; haul-lines march, the band riffs, the swarm becomes crews; Reed fiddles, Millet calls verses; the shimmer recedes · 21 PETRICHOR-equivalent: the whole valley working in one time — the sound both sides recognize from somewhere older than the fable · 22 Resolution: the pageant re-staged with the carved ending; Quern + Brio on one bench; the colony hums three notes — *her name is a tune.*
