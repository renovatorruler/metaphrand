# WORKSONG — working notes (the Ant & Grasshopper's kid)

Sibling to PETRICHOR: the same mirror (worst-of-both → best-of-both), new world. Pixar mode, no songs *performed at camera* — but this one is ABOUT music, so music lives diegetically (work songs, jams, the pageant). Working title: **WORKSONG** (alt: *The Grasshopper's Daughter*).

**Logline:** In a valley where the ants perform "The Ant and the Grasshopper" every harvest as scripture, the daughter of an ant quartermaster and the grasshopper who plays the starving fool in the pageant believes she got the worst of both — an ant with a backbeat, a grasshopper with a clipboard — until a flood and a swarm-year prove that the thing the fable cut in half was one craft all along: the work song.

## The two anchors (the petrichor-facts)
1. **Work songs are real and universal** — shanties, field hollers, railroad songs: every human culture coordinated labor with rhythm. Music and work were never opposites; the fable's dichotomy is the lie.
2. **Locusts are not a species** — they're grasshoppers that transform when crowded. The monster the fable warns about is a *phase*, not a nature — and pressure is what makes it.

## Locked
- **The fable as in-world scripture:** the Colony performs the Aesop pageant every harvest. The antagonist is not a person; it is *the story itself* — institutionalized, taught to every child, including her.
- **Her father plays the grasshopper.** Every year REED, the fiddler who wintered-in for love, puts on the costume of his own people's humiliation, begs on stage, "dies" in the snow, and the colony applauds. His daughter watches. That is the wound the film opens with.
- **The courtship (the promise, planted):** Reed was the hired pageant-grasshopper one winter; MILLET was the granary counter on the other side of the wall who heard the fiddle and started counting in 6/8. She tapped three notes back through the wall. **Those three notes are the kid's name.** The colony uses her tally-number; the meadow uses song-names she can't hold; her true name is a tune almost nobody plays. (End: the colony hums it.)
- **TALA, ~11** (tala = a rhythmic cycle; reads as a girl's name). The self-defeating machine: her tallies come out syncopated (grain-pyramid avalanches), her jamming comes out counted ("ONE-two-three-four" — the meadow winces), her leaps land metrically (no swing), her marching swings (no discipline). An ant with a backbeat; a grasshopper with a clipboard.
- **Suppression kit (props):** leg-bindings under the worker's apron (kill the spring), wax to chew (kill the hum), and her father's **fiddle mute** carried like a talisman — she mutes herself with the literal device.
- **The Sealing of the Tally:** the Colony rite — your number becomes your whole name; the other half struck from the rolls. Love-as-erasure, administered by **QUERN**, her granary-keeper grandmother. Doorway 1: Tala petitions for it herself, deadline the Frost Tally (the fable's own clock: winter). Doorway 2: post-flood, she demands it immediately, publicly.
- **The deep secret (midpoint mirror):** the flooded lower vault opens the foundation chamber — tally-carvings that are *musical notation*. The first granary was raised to work songs; the valley was once ONE economy (grasshopper bands + ant granaries, coordinated in rhythm) until an ancient swarm-year broke it. **The fable is the divorce decree, written by the winners.** Kid-misread: "so the song really did cause the swarm" — lie reinforced; she asks to be Sealed faster. Quern knew. Her three-note name is carved in that wall — the courtship tune was an old work song resurfacing.
- **The crisis (clock):** an autumn storm floods the granaries (through a sluice her own avalanche weakened — "my both did this"), refugee grasshopper bands press into the valley mouth, and crowding begins the locust-shimmer. The colony walls up with half-stores. Famine inside, swarm outside, winter coming.
- **Cured-empty:** the Sealing works. Her first perfect, unmusical tally gets the first applause of her life. The inner metronome goes silent; **TICK the cricket** (the hearth-pest who adopted her; the creature that is both house and song) cocks his head at her chirp-less pulse and hops away.
- **The mechanism (dark night):** the Sealing didn't delete the music — it banked the count. Her lifelong discipline IS the bandmaster's gift: *a song is a tally you can feel.* She always swung first and counted second; the order is count first, **then** swing.
- **The test:** on the granary drum-boards between the wall and the massing swarm, she strikes the count — then lets it lilt: the first work song in living memory. The haul-lines can't help marching; the band can't help riffing; the swarm breaks into crews — flooded grain hauled, the sluice rebuilt, the meadow's seedheads threshed into shared stores. Reed fiddles; Millet calls the tally as verses (call-and-response = the parents' two languages, fused, public). The locust-shimmer recedes from the crowd's wings.
- **Resolution:** the pageant re-staged with the carved old ending — one valley, two crafts, one song. Quern and Brio (the meadow's old bandleader, Reed's estranged father) share the conductor's bench, grumbling. The colony hums three notes. *Her name is a tune.*
- **Design rule carried over:** the blend must never read as a body-diagram; Tala reads as a kid (spring legs hidden under the apron is a costume beat, not anatomy charting). One valley, two crafts.

## Cast ledger (weave law)
- **TALA** — the machine above; the mute; the carved name.
- **REED (dad)** — pageant grasshopper → exiled with the refugee bands post-flood → fiddles the climax. Edges: the costume wound · the courtship · exile · the song.
- **MILLET (mom)** — quartermaster; counted 6/8 through a wall once; famine-duty splits her from Reed; calls the verses at the climax.
- **QUERN (grandmother, Colony)** — keeper of the granary AND the Sealing AND the sealed chamber (she knew). Edges: rite · secret · forced to open the stores to the song.
- **OLD BRIO (grandfather, Meadow)** — bandleader who never forgave Reed for "wintering in a hole"; runs the Jam that fails her; brings the band at the climax.
- **TICK the cricket** — adopts her → abandons the Sealed her → found at the carved wall keeping time → visible metronome of the finale. (The Burr role: the creature that is also both.)
- **The refugee band** — the crowding; one young hothead whose wings shimmer first.
- Deliberately unwired: the pageant director (comedy), the aphid-dairy hands (world-flesh).

## Production notes
- Pixar visual register: tunnels = warm grain-golds and lantern light; meadow = green-gold stadium of grass; the swarm = a weather system of wings; the locust-shimmer = a color phase creeping across wings (real biology, readable to kids).
- Music is diegetic only. The work song at the climax must be buildable from sounds established earlier (tally-taps, haul-chants, Reed's three-note phrase).
