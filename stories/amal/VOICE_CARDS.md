# अमल — Voice Cards (read from the charts)

*Per-work pass #7 (docs/05) · read from `CHARTS.md` onto the six axes of `docs/04`. Write to the card; a
line is in voice only if it could sit on these axes. **The gate is blind attribution:** strip the cue,
read the Hindi cold, name the speaker — any line that fits two people gets re-voiced. Enforced by
`metaphrand/preflight.py`.*

---

### Ratan — `ratan` · *the headless detective under the tombstone*
- **Sentence:** short, clipped, controlled (Budh–Shani). Often one beat. No subordinate clauses.
- **Emotion/reason:** reasoned, sealed; feeling shows only as a stoppage, never as words.
- **Vocabulary:** plain, exact, procedural; the file, the form, the fact.
- **Precision:** sharp — names the exact thing, or nothing.
- **Directness:** withholds; says less than he knows; the answer is often a silence or a bare fact about
  the world/the case (he looks *out*).
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** slow, dry, cold; a flat declarative; lets a question die unanswered.
- **Never:** explains or **defends** himself — pressed, he goes **silent**, never argues; reaches for a
  feeling-word; uses two sentences where one will do. *(Twin-tell: pressed, Ratan seals; Bhanwar talks more.)*
- **Sample:** «गिर गई। केस बंद।»

### Deva — `deva` · *the young light, asking*
- **Sentence:** complete, eager, a touch long when he believes something (Surya–Budh, Guru).
- **Emotion/reason:** warm and reasoned at once; idealism stated plainly.
- **Vocabulary:** clear, articulate, a little bookish-new; the words of someone who still trusts them.
- **Precision:** precise; he wants the *why*.
- **Directness:** **ASKS** — the question everyone else swallows; deferential (साहब / आप, **never तू**,
  never Ratan's first name); forward-looking.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** quick, warm; ends on a question; "साहब…" before the thing he shouldn't say.
- **Never:** withholds to survive; takes the money without flinching; lets an injustice pass unspoken;
  **pronounces** who Ratan is — he *asks*, he doesn't tell (that's Govind).
- **Sample:** «साहब, पोस्टमॉर्टम क्यों नहीं? लड़की मरी है।»

### Sugna — `sugna` · *the stone*
- **Sentence:** fragments, or nothing. Two or three words land like a verdict.
- **Emotion/reason:** feeling calcified to flat fact; no self-pity, ever.
- **Vocabulary:** bare, hard, concrete.
- **Precision:** exact and final; she does not soften.
- **Directness:** withholds to the floor; says the unbearable quiet and plain, or keeps her hands busy.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** slow, cold; long silence; the sentence that ends a conversation.
- **Never:** explains, pleads, raises her voice, fills a silence.
- **Sample:** «अब आए हो।»

### Bherulal — `bherulal` · *the insatiable head*
- **Sentence:** rolling, angling, never quite landing; every clause turns toward gain (Budh–Rahu).
- **Emotion/reason:** appetite wearing the costume of reason; grandiose.
- **Vocabulary:** standing, alliance, the boy, the patta, "the future"; the trade's euphemisms.
- **Precision:** slippery — gestures past the ugly thing.
- **Directness:** indirect; charm and threat in one breath.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** pushy-warm, then cold; invokes God and the son in the same sentence as the sale.
- **Never:** says the plain shameful fact; lets a silence sit; speaks without an angle.
- **Sample:** «लड़के का पट्टा बन जाए, बस। बाक़ी ऊपरवाला देख लेगा।»

### Kanta — `kanta` · *the keeper of the house*
- **Sentence:** plain, short-to-medium, concrete (Budh in Vrishabh).
- **Emotion/reason:** warm, balanced; care expressed as practicality.
- **Vocabulary:** food, papers, the body, the day; few syllables.
- **Precision:** concrete; the cooling roti, the pension date.
- **Directness:** gently corrective, indirect about the big thing — respectful **आप / -इए** always.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** steady, warm, resigned; nudges him to eat instead of naming the grief.
- **Never:** uses तू to him; raises her voice; speaks the buried thing directly.
- **Sample:** «मक्खन मत लगाईए, खाना खाईए। ठंडा हो रहा है।»

### Amma — `amma` · *the warm mother (Sugna's opposite)*
- **Sentence:** runs on, chatty, blessing (strong Chandra, Guru).
- **Emotion/reason:** all warmth; worry and love spilling.
- **Vocabulary:** food, neighbours, the match, God's name; homely.
- **Precision:** concrete-domestic, a little scattered.
- **Directness:** direct in fussing, indirect about money/fear.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** fast, warm; "अरे बेटा…"; feeds before she'll let anyone talk.
- **Never:** goes cold or silent; the stone is Sugna, not her.
- **Sample:** «अरे बेटा, खा ले पहले, बात बाद में।»

### Manju — `manju` · *the yielding bride*
- **Sentence:** few words, soft, trailing (Budh–Chandra).
- **Emotion/reason:** feeling held under; swallowed.
- **Vocabulary:** plain, small, deferential.
- **Precision:** vague where her own want is; she won't claim it.
- **Directness:** indirect, yielding; agrees rather than says.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** slow, downcast; "जैसा आप ठीक समझें."
- **Never:** states her own desire; argues; raises her eyes to refuse (that's Leela).
- **Sample:** «जैसा आप ठीक समझें।»

### Govind — `govind` · *the conscience (proposed name)*
- **Sentence:** plain, warm, sometimes a long true one after a tease (Budh–Guru, earthed).
- **Emotion/reason:** warm wisdom; the long view, plainly.
- **Vocabulary:** ordinary, lived; NOT the bard's archaisms — he's the Chuckie, not the Charan.
- **Precision:** concrete; names the old days, the Chambal, the boy Ratan was.
- **Directness:** **TELLS** — lands a statement, not a question; intimate (**the ONLY one who says तू and
  "रतन"** to him); backward-looking.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** unhurried, warm; teases, then lands it; uses Ratan's name like a hook.
- **Never:** **asks permission or defers** (that's Deva's साहब); lectures in legend (that's Charan);
  flatters; gives up on him.
- **Sample:** «तू हमेशा से अलग था, रतन। भूल गया?»

### Mishra — `mishra` · *the velvet argument*
- **Sentence:** measured, balanced, reasonable (Budh–Shukra).
- **Emotion/reason:** all reason — corruption as common sense.
- **Vocabulary:** procedure, years, "the way it works"; warmth as a tool ("भाई", "बेटा").
- **Precision:** smooth; rounds the hard edges off.
- **Directness:** indirect; never an order, always a reasonable suggestion.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** calm, unbothered; never raises his voice; a small tired smile in the words.
- **Never:** threatens openly; loses composure; says the ugly thing without a cushion.
- **Sample:** «पच्चीस साल से चल रहा है, अब अंकगणित सीखोगे? साइन कर दो, भाई।»

### Bhanwar — `bhanwar` · *Dr. Bhanwar Singh — the mirror that says go home (proposed name)*
- **Sentence:** clipped, dry, clinical (Budh–Shani — Ratan's twin, but he never lifts the sword).
- **Emotion/reason:** cold reason; fatigue as armour.
- **Vocabulary:** medical, procedural, curt; "report", "clean", "fall".
- **Precision:** exact, then defensively over-built when pressed.
- **Directness:** blunt with Ratan (peer to peer): drop it. Pressed, he reaches for the **report and the
  procedure** (he looks *down at the paper*).
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** flat, weary; "you of all people…"; cold formal **आप** + clan-address **पँवार साहब** (NEVER सिंह — Singh is the shared honorific, not the surname) — fake respect, the wall between two finished Rajputs.
- **Never:** goes **silent** under pressure (that's Ratan) — he **talks more**, cites the rules; pleads;
  pretends not to understand what Ratan understands.
- **Sample:** «रिपोर्ट साफ़ है। गिरने से मौत। घर जाइये, पँवार साहब।»

### Dhanraj — `dhanraj` · *money that never raises its voice*
- **Sentence:** soft, slow, complacent (Shukra–Shani).
- **Emotion/reason:** entitled ease; appetite under courtesy.
- **Vocabulary:** standing, position, comfort; the obscene said politely.
- **Precision:** smooth, euphemistic.
- **Directness:** indirect, oily-courteous.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** unhurried; the small cold smile; "उम्र नहीं, हैसियत."
- **Never:** hurries; pleads; drops the courtesy that makes the obscenity worse.
- **Sample:** «उम्र नहीं देखते, हैसियत देखते हैं।»

### Rana — `rana` · *the laundered king*
- **Sentence:** expansive, populist, owns-the-room (Surya–Rahu, strong Budh).
- **Emotion/reason:** warm charisma; everything and nothing said.
- **Vocabulary:** big smiling generalities; "apna ghar", "the people", "we'll see to it".
- **Precision:** deliberately diffuse — the velvet over the ravine.
- **Directness:** charming-indirect; power needs no threat.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** easy, broad, warm; never rattled; the host who owns what he offers.
- **Never:** grasps (that's Bherulal); shows the ravine under the smile.
- **Sample:** «अरे इंस्पेक्टर साहब! आइए, बैठिए — अपना ही घर है।»

### Leela — `leela` · *the fire Ratan signed away*
- **Sentence:** quick, short, sharp; a tongue that talks back (Mangal–Budh).
- **Emotion/reason:** alive, hot, defiant.
- **Vocabulary:** plain, young, vivid, cheeky.
- **Precision:** sharp and immediate.
- **Directness:** blunt — says the refusal out loud, which the older women can't.
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** fast, spirited; the eye-roll, the back-talk; the spine.
- **Never:** yields quietly (that's Manju); softens to survive (that's everyone older).
- **Sample:** «मैं नहीं जाऊँगी उस बूढ़े के पास।»

### Charan — `charan` · *the bard (the ONLY earned eloquence)*
- **Sentence:** long, rolling, mythic; the legend's cadence (Budh–Guru).
- **Emotion/reason:** exalted; feeling raised to lineage.
- **Vocabulary:** archaic, Rajput, sung; रकत, तलवार, साका, पँवार.
- **Precision:** image-rich, declamatory.
- **Directness:** oblique through legend — he tells the past so you'll see the present (never names it).
- **Pace/warmth/tics:** slow, incantatory; the blind eye; recites and half-sings.
- **Never:** speaks plainly or briefly; *and no one else is allowed his eloquence.*
- **Sample:** «सिर कटा, पर तलवार न रुकी — वो पँवार का रकत है, बेटा।»
