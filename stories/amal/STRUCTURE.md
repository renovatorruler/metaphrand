# अमल — Episodic Structure

*The TV layer over `PLOT.md`. Plot is what happens; this is how the season is parcelled into hours so
each one has its own shape and still pulls to the next.*

---

## The form

A **serialized limited series: one case, six chapters, ~50 minutes each**, built to be watched as a
season. Not a case-of-the-week procedural; the season is the story, and each episode is a chapter that
turns, the way Aranyak, Delhi Crime, and Paatal Lok are built. Season one is the whole of Leela's case,
complete in itself. (Franchise note at the bottom.)

---

## The two clocks

Every episode runs two clocks at once, and the craft is keeping both honest:

- **The season clock (serialized):** the mystery, Ratan's descent into his own complicity, and a hard
  deadline ticking under all of it: *the alliance must still close.* The groom's people want the match
  sealed, with another girl if not Leela, so the machine that killed her is visibly still running and
  still hungry all season. That deadline is the pressure that won't let the case rest.
- **The episode clock (chapter):** each hour opens one sharp question and turns on it by the end, so
  the episode is satisfying as a unit even though the case isn't closed.

---

## The repeating episode engine

Each chapter is built from the same six parts, which is what makes it feel like a series and not a
chopped-up film:

1. **Cold open:** usually a train fragment (see below), or a hard present-tense hook.
2. **The episode question:** the one specific thing this hour is about, opened in the first act.
3. **One layer peeled:** the case exposes a single layer of the system this hour — the family, then the
   diversion, then the network, then the debt, then the pattern, then the truth — so the world deepens
   as the plot advances.
4. **Ratan's step down:** the case moves him a notch deeper into his wound and his complicity. The
   personal arc and the investigation are the same line.
5. **The B-beat:** Deva and Sunita advance in parallel, rhyming the hour's theme in a gentle key.
6. **The turn:** the question gets answered in a way that recontextualises what came before and hooks
   the next hour, on a doorway, a discovery, or a gut-punch.

---

## The train as the structural spine

The unexplained train fragment is doled out across the six, so the audience's understanding *dawns*
rather than gets revealed. This is not a clean whodunit and not a clean twist: the fragments seed the
viewer's spine, so by the time Ratan lands on the truth it feels both impossible and inevitable.

- **Ep1 and 2:** the fragment is pure and baffling, a woman and a bundle and the flies and a bust, with
  no place in time. We do not know what it is.
- **Ep3 and 4:** it recurs, and the present begins to rhyme with it: a cloth, a way of holding still,
  the flies around Leela's body answering the flies on the train.
- **Ep5 and 6:** the rhyme becomes undeniable, and at the moment of the truth the fragment completes,
  felt and never captioned. The boy and Leela, the two opium-children, bracket Ratan's whole life.

The dial for you: how early the audience *consciously* lands on Sugna. The default is that they feel
the wrongness early but only name it at Ep5, alongside Ratan, so the gut-punch is one hindsight proves
was fair.

## Per-episode shape (the chapter turn)

1. **तौल** opens on the field at dawn and the harvest that won't stop, asks whether Ratan will look, and
   turns when he halts the lancing and refuses to close it; the first train fragment tags the end.
2. **The Viewing** asks whether the death breaks the match, and turns when the groom's people come
   anyway, the doctor finds opium in her, and a tidy suicide is offered to bury it.
3. **Short Weight** asks where the family's trouble really comes from, and turns when Rana leans on
   Ratan from above to call it suicide, which tells him the death is being protected.
4. **The Debt** asks why this marriage, and turns when it proves to be a debt settled with a daughter,
   his own family's story running again, with the case still pointing hard at the father.
5. **The First Wife** asks whether the father killed her, and turns when the thread runs instead toward
   the mother, and Ratan finds his own name in the chain.
6. **अमल** asks what Ratan will do with the truth, and turns when he refuses to give them the mother,
   burns the machine, and walks out clean with nothing, while Deva stops Sunita's viewing.

---

## The franchise question

Season one is a complete limited series. If it continues, there are two roads: **anthology**, a new
keyhole case in the belt each season the way the belt grinds a new family every year, with Ratan or a
new investigator; or **continue Ratan**, the fallout of burning the machine. The anthology fits the
world's thesis best, since the machine is permanent and only the bodies change, but that is a
season-two decision, not a season-one constraint.
