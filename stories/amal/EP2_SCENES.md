`VOICE_CARDS.md`, `MALWA.md`, `EP1_PAGES_v2.md` (continuity). Blueprint, not prose — each scene names its

## Runtime — the real math (measured, not guessed)
Ep1 = **30.0 min**, 23 scenes, ~5,330 script words — but **~75% narration / ~25% dialogue** (only ~1,430
Hindi dialogue words in the whole episode: an audiobook ratio). 50 min = **1.67×** ≈ ~9,000 words ≈
**~38–42 scenes** at natural crime-drama pacing. So Ep2 = **~40 scenes, DIALOGUE-FORWARD** — shift the
ratio toward speech (this both adds runtime and fixes the narrator-heavy feel). Mix of **long anchors**
(Rana offer, the cut, Kanta — 3–5 min) and **short cross-cut beats** (the machine, the belt, the weave —
1–2 min). **I render M1 and measure the actual clock before committing to the rest** — no more estimates
from the air.

## Held cards (buried — audience does NOT learn these here)
- **WHO killed Leela = Sugna** (mercy). The incision says *murder*, never the hand. The witness's pieces
  point (wrongly) at **Bherulal**, never at her.
- The **train / Ratan–Sugna wound** (Ep4): in Ep2 they're visibly estranged; the *why* is withheld.
- **The saka = Ratan's death** — untouched. Audience already knows (Ep1): Leela was his niece, signed away.

## MOVEMENT 1 — THE ORDER vs THE MACHINE (the siege of the held body)

**S1 — THE HELD GIRL** *(morgue, dawn — cold open).* Leela on ice; the family at the gate to burn her; a
brick of notes slid to make her vanish; the order on the wall holds her. *Want:* the family — a pyre
today (a quick fire = a fall). *Wall:* his order. *Turn:* it holds; the clock starts. *Hook.*
**S2 — TITLE SEQUENCE: अमल · चीरा.** The झूझार song over the title imagery.
**S3 — BROTHER & SISTER** *(morgue side-room).* Sugna asks her brother to let her burn her girl; he
refuses. *Turn:* his crossing turns personally cruel. *Held:* the wound + her hand, submerged.
**S4 — THE PEN STILL WET** *(thana, morning).* The world re-set: the daily skim crossing his desk, Deva
green beside it, the signed order from last night. *Turn:* he doesn't take the easy thing today.
**S5 — THE VELVET WALL** *(Mishra).* Withdraw it, it's a fall, big house, save yourself. *Turn:* he won't;
"then it goes up." *(→ Rana.)* Voiced: Mishra velvet, Ratan stone.
**S6 — BHERULAL COMES** *(the empire, in person).* Velvet pressure: let her rest, the boy's patta needs a
clean name, you won't even let us burn her. *Wall:* the family's power. *Turn:* Ratan unmoved.
*Bherulal on-page (Ep3 suspect seed); held: patta-for-son light.*
**S7 — NOTHING IS AVAILABLE** *(transport wall).* No ambulance, no van, the HQ mortuary "renovating." The
machine refuses without refusing. *Change:* Deva learns how no is said here.
**S8 — THE CREMATION GROUND** *(the town).* Pyre wood stacked, no body; the women wail; the town watches;
the held girl a public wound. *Wall:* shame and pressure mount.
**S9 — HE ARRANGES IT.** Ratan stops asking the system — a hired tempo, an old marker, he'll ride with the
body. *Turn (M1):* resolve hardens; the siege becomes a journey.

## MOVEMENT 2 — THE VELVET OFFER (RANA)

**S10 — SUMMONED.** Rana's people send for him; Ratan and Deva drive out to the farmhouse. *Dread under
courtesy.*
**S11 — THE COURT.** Rana holding court — supplicants, populist warmth, "अपना ही घर है." Deva watches
power. *Build Rana the man.*
**S12 — THE AMAL MANUHAR.** Opium-water of courtesy poured for the man he's buying. *The Malwa custom as
the velvet over the ravine.*
**S13 — THE OFFER** *(centerpiece).* The Pawar patta back, the signet redeemed, the standing returned —
for one look away. The breaker offers to rebuild the fort. *Want:* Ratan — out without taking it. *Wall:*
it's everything he's wanted, offered warmly. *Voiced:* Rana expansive/velvet, Ratan sealed.
**S14 — THE RAVINE.** He refuses — silence, one flat line. Rana's warmth shows its edge: "सोच लो, पँवार
साहब." *Turn:* the apex is now a personal enemy.
**S15 — THE JEEP.** Deva grasps it — Ratan refused his own family's patta for a dead girl's postmortem.
*Weave:* the fork watches a man refuse to look away.

## The Rana scene — spec (S11–S14, the season's apex built into a person)
- **Warmth, not threat.** He offers a *gift* — the exact thing his bloodline took. The breaker offering to
  rebuild is the whole scene.
- **The amal manuhar is the staging** — courtesy as the weapon (`MALWA.md`).
- **He never owns his family's hand** in the Pawar ruin; he mourns it *with* Ratan, generous, as if both
  were wronged by weather. (Sentimental about the man he destroyed — that's the cruelty.)
- **Cards:** Rana = expansive, populist, "अपना ही घर है," never grasps, shows the ravine only at the
  refusal. Ratan = sealed; no defence, no speech — a silence and one flat line.
- **Must NOT:** name the niece, hint Sugna's hand, let Ratan monologue his meaning.
