# अमल — TITLE TRACK (the show's main-title anthem)

*Distinct from the [झूझार/Jhujhar leitmotif](CHARAN_SONG.md). This is the show's world-anthem over the
main titles. **NOT a song about opium or addiction** — the show is not a drug story. It is about the
**moral economy**: a belt where everything human is weighed on the same scale that weighs the opium, and
the **पट्टा (licence) outweighs the child**. अमल = *the way it is all done* — corruption as inherited
custom. The drug (अफ़ीम) is only the first thing on the scale; the song is about what gets weighed after
it — conscience, the daughter, the son, the man. Central image: the **तौल / the scale** (Ep1 = "The
Weighing").*

## The way it's supposed to be (style / intent)

**Dark Malwi folk-noir**, not a celebratory march. A weathered, knowing male voice — the belt's own
voice — half-spoken then sung. Brooding and fatalistic, with a hypnotic, driving pulse so it still hooks.
The register of a prestige crime-drama main title (Paatal Lok / Sacred Games), folk-rooted and dark, a
catchy refrain that lands अमल. Contrast with the Jhujhar song is the point: Jhujhar = the hero who won't
fall; the title track = the world that weighs and sells him.

**Suno style box:**
```
Dark Malwi folk-noir title theme; a weathered, knowing male voice, half-spoken then sung; sarangi, harmonium and algoza over a tanpura drone and a low hypnotic dholak pulse; brooding, fatalistic, atmospheric, cinematic, building; the haunted register of a prestige crime-drama opening title, not celebratory; mid-tempo with a dark catchy hook
```

## Why these lines (the show, compressed)
- *तराज़ू पे अफ़ीम तुले, साथ तुले ईमान* — the scale that weighs the opium weighs the conscience next; human worth is priced like the crop. (Ep1 is literally *The Weighing*.)
- *पट्टा भारी, हल्का बेटा* / *लड़के का पट्टा हो, तो बेटी चढ़ा दो दान में* — the spine, stated plain: the licence outweighs the son; the daughter is sold to fund it.
- *कोई नोटों में, कोई वर्दी में, कोई चुप्पी में* — bought with cash, the uniform, silence (the trader, the police, the witnesses).
- *पुश्तों से यही रीत है / बाप ने जो किया, वही बेटा करता है* — the corruption as inherited custom: that is अमल, the way it is all done — not a drug, a *way of life*.
- *ये अमल का जहान* — the title: the world where this is simply how things are.
