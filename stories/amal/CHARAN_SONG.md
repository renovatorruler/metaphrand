# अमल — झूझार (Jhujhar) — Charan's Song (the show's leitmotif)

*Official title: **झूझार / Jhujhar** ("Charan's Song"). Canonical lyrics = the **Rajasthani** version
(preferred — this is the one to render). The MP3 is generated by the **user in Suno** (Rajasthani lyrics +
style below) and used as the **title-credit song and/or the ending song**. Every episode video opens with
a **generated title sequence** built around it.*

*Charan is the **only earned eloquence** in अमल (`VOICE_CARDS.md`): archaic, Rajput, sung; image-rich,
declamatory; oblique through legend — *he tells the past so you see the present, and never names it.*
This register is **quarantined to Charan** — it must not leak into any other mouth (the One Law /
clear-pane rules are deliberately suspended here, and only here).*

## What it is and where it plays

A bard's **veer-ras** ballad of the 1305 saka — the Parmar fort falling to Khilji, the Jhujhar (the
headless warrior whose body fights on). It is the **musical spine of the series**: it underscores the
Ep1 cold open (S1, the saka), surfaces when Charan tells Deva the legend (S11, the signet), and pays off
at the finale (Ratan's own saka). One song, returning.

## The dual level (held — for us, never on the page)

On the surface it is **pure 1305 legend** — it names no policeman, no Ratan, nothing modern. But it is
built so the audience (and Ratan) joins it to him without help: *गिरकर भी जो न गिरे* (he who, even
fallen, does not fall), *झूझार आज खड़ो* (the Jhujhar stands today), *बहे रग-रग में* (it flows in these
very veins), *जो सुणे सो जाणे* (he who hears, knows). The bard invokes **हरसिद्ध** — the Paramara
kuldevi whose rites Ratan performs without believing a word. The blade that does not stop after the head
is off **is** the saka-form we locked (the signature that keeps swinging). None of this is stated; the
spine joins it.

## Lyrics (canonical — Malwi-Rajasthani / Devanagari)

**आह्वान — Invocation** *(Charan, half-sung, the blind eye lifted)*
> सुण ले ओ दुनिया, झूझार रो नाम —
> जो मर'र भी ना मरे, वो अमर सूरमा।

**टेक — Refrain (the hook)**
> सिर कट गिरयो रण में, पण तेग न रुकी,
> धड़ लड़तो रह्यो — ये वीरों रो रकत है।
> गिरकर भी जो न गिरे — वो झूझार,
> सिर देकर भी जो जीते — वो साका।

**अंतरा १ — the host at the gate**
> सात सौ बरस पहले, इण ही माटी पे,
> खिलजी रो लश्कर चढ़्यो, काल रो रूप ले।
> कोट री दीवारां काँपी, पण पाग न झुकी,
> वीरों रो माथो — भूख से भी ऊँचो रह्यो।

*(टेक)*

**अंतरा २ — the saka**
> रानी हँसती ज्वाला में उतरी उस भोर,
> मरदां ने केसरिया बाना पैर लियो।
> दरवाज़ा खुद खोल्यो — भाग्या नी, लड़्या,
> जद जीत नी रही, तद मौत ने परणन चल्या।

**अंतरा ३ — the Jhujhar**
> बैरी री तेग चली, सिर धड़ से जुदा,
> पण वो धड़ रुक्यो नी — तलवार घूमती रही।
> आँख बिन, सिर बिन, वो लड़तो रह्यो,
> जद तक बैरी रो रकत धरती नी पी लियो।

*(टेक)*

**कोडा — Coda (the bridge to now; names no one)**
> सिर तो कद रो गिरयो — झूझार आज खड़ो,
> रकत वो थम्यो नी अजहूँ, बहे रग-रग में।
> जो सुणे सो जाणे — कुण गिरकर भी न गिरे,
> जद तक माटी है, झूझार जीवतो रहे।

## Archaic / dialect gloss (single words, for the read)

पाग = turban (honour) · बाना = the warrior's death-robe (केसरिया = saffron, shroud *and* wedding) ·
धड़ = the headless trunk · तेग = sword · रकत = रक्त, blood · बैरी = enemy · लश्कर = host/army ·
कोट = fort · साका = the last-stand death-ride · झूझार = the headless warrior who fights on (venerated) ·
परणन (परणना) = to wed · अजहूँ = even now (archaic) · रो/री = Rajasthani का/की · पण = but · नी = नहीं ·
जद/तद = when/then · कुण = कौन · हरसिद्ध = the Paramara kuldevi (Ujjain).

## Production plan (ElevenLabs Music API — only after approval)

- **Key:** `~/.elevenlabs_api_key` (music_generation enabled). **No named artists** — describe by tradition.
- **Style prompt:** solo male charan/bhat voice, Rajasthani–Malwi folk martial ballad (veer ras); sparse
  and building — harmonium drone, dholak, sarangi/kamaicha; raw, devotional-defiant; no Western kit.
- **Pronunciation:** ship the API a **romanised** transliteration of the approved lyrics (Devanagari stays
  canonical here) for cleaner singing; generate **2–3 takes**, pick the best.
- **Deliverables:** full ballad + a ~45s cold-open cut (टेक + अंतरा ३ + coda tag). **MP3, never WAV.**
- **v1 asset (2026-06-21):** user's Suno take stored at
  `stories/amal/audio/jhujhar_charan-song_rajasthani_v1_2026-06-21.mp3` (4:18, MP3 48k stereo) — the
  working title-credit / ending track. Cut a ~45–60s section for the Ep2 title sequence.
- **Backend:** the sung version needs **ElevenLabs Music** (`force_instrumental: false`, `model_id:
  music_v1`); MusicGen is instrumental-only and **cannot sing lyrics**.
- **Honest risk + fallback:** Eleven Music's sung Rajasthani and exact-lyric adherence are unproven. If it
  mangles the dialect, the fallback is **Charan's own TTS voice half-singing/chanting the lines** (the
  card: "recites and half-sings") over an instrumental bed (MusicGen/AudioLDM harmonium–sarangi–dholak) —
  more authentic to a bard than a clean sung take anyway.
