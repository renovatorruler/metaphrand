# अमल — Episode 1: तौल ("The Weighing")

*Malwa opium belt. English sluglines and action; Devanagari dialogue, light Malwi register. A
licensee's daughter found dead in the family's poppy field at dawn; the investigation opens. Ground in
`BIBLE.md`.*

---

## NEW-COLD-OPEN

**EXT. CBN OPIUM WEIGHMENT CENTRE, AMARGARH — DAY**

A government shed at the edge of the fields. Over the door, a board painted by hand: केंद्रीय नारकोटिक्स ब्यूरो — अफ़ीम तौल केंद्र. A line of farmers waits in the dust, each holding a steel lota against his chest.

Inside, at a brass scale, sits KHARE — fifty, neat, a thick register open at his elbow. A FARMER sets his lota on the table. Khare lifts the lid, scrapes a little of the dark gum onto the pan, and watches the needle settle.

KHARE: कम है। पिछली बार भी कम थी।

The farmer brings his hands together at his chest, the way you fold them in a temple.

FARMER: साहब, इस बार बारिश कम हुई। अगली बार पूरा कर दूँगा, कसम से।

KHARE: (writing, not looking up) पूरा तौल लाया कर। वरना पट्टा कट जाएगा, फिर दफ़्तर-दफ़्तर घूमता रहेगा।

FARMER: (hands still folded) नी साहब, पट्टा मत कटवाना। बस इस एक बार देख लो।

Khare stamps the page, tears off a receipt, holds it out without raising his eyes. The farmer takes it in both hands.

KHARE: कैशियर से ले ले। अगला।

At a barred window down the wall, a clerk counts a single bundle of notes against the receipt. The farmer folds it small and pushes it deep into an inside pocket, the way you carry what you can't afford to lose.

**EXT. EDGE OF THE FIELDS — DUSK**

The same farmer, off the road by a culvert, where a MAN waits beside a motorcycle of his own. The farmer pulls out a second bundle — cloth-wrapped gum, the lot that never went near the government scale. The man takes it, weighs it once in his hand — no scale, no register, no receipt — and counts notes into the farmer's palm: a fat brick of them, many times the bundle from the cashier.

The farmer pushes it into his trouser pocket without folding it, without counting. He works a beedi loose from behind his ear and lights it.

FARMER: अगली बार भी इतना ही निकलेगा। तू बस आ जाना।

THE BUYER: और हो तो भेज देना।

The farmer throws a leg over his Enfield and kicks it down — that heavy thud rolling out across the fields — and rides off in no hurry, the cash sitting fat in his pocket.

**TITLE CARD: अमल**

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य एक

**EXT. POPPY FIELD, OUTSKIRTS OF AMARGARH — DAWN**

A poppy field before sunrise, cold enough to show breath. The pods were scored the evening before and
have wept their gum overnight; a line of scrapers moves down the rows, lifting the grey beads off each
pod before the dew burns away. The work does not stop.

At the edge, where the rows meet the earthen bund, lies a girl on her side in the dirt: LEELA, sixteen,
a printed kameez gone stiff with dew, one slipper on and one in the furrow beside her. An old woman
scrapes the row nearest the body without looking at it, her lips going राम राम, राम राम.

BHERULAL, the girl's father, stands a few rows off — not near her, not far — with his brother MOHAN.
Neither of them has sent for anyone. A CBN jeep turns onto the field road. INSPECTOR RATAN SINGH PANWAR
— fifties, heavy — gets out with a hand on the door. Constable DEVA follows. Bherulal sees the uniforms
and goes still.

BHERULAL: साहब? आप... आपको किसने बुलाया?

RATAN: थाने ख़बर आई। एक छोरी खेत में पड़ी है।

BHERULAL: ख़बर? किसने दी? (he looks past Ratan at the empty road, as if the caller might still be on it)

Ratan walks to the body and crouches. Bherulal does not. He stays up, eyes going to the rows, the sky,
his brother — everywhere but the ground.

BHERULAL: गिर गई बेचारी, साहब। रात को अँधेरे में पाँव फिसल गया होगा। आप काग़ज़ बना दो, मैं दस्तख़त कर दूँगा। घर की बात है।

RATAN: पहले देखी किसने?

BHERULAL: लुआई वाले, मुँह अँधेरे। (already moving past it, half to himself) लुआई रोकी तो सारा माल ख़राब... और परसों लड़के वाले आ रहे थे, तारीख़ पक्की करने...

MOHAN: (low, practical) बर्तन तो बन के आ गए होंगे। नाम खुदा होगा उन पर।

The problem lands on Bherulal as a real one. He turns to his brother, away from the body.

BHERULAL: हाँ... लीला खुदा होगा उन पर।

MOHAN: शीला करवा दो। लीला-शीला, ज़्यादा फ़रक़ नी है। सुनार एक दिन में ठीक कर देगा। छोटी तो है ही।

Bherulal nods, slow, holding onto the solvable thing. Deva has heard all of it. He comes to Ratan's
shoulder, low, so the family won't catch it.

DEVA: (low) साहब। छोरी मरी पड़ी है, और इनको बर्तन की पड़ी है।

RATAN: (low, his eyes on the girl) सदमा है, देवा। ऐसे में आदमी काम में घुस जाता है — फ़सल, ब्याह, बर्तन। मानना नी चाहता कि बेटी गई। (a beat) मैंने बहुत देखे हैं ऐसे बाप।

Deva looks at the father with something new working in his face. Ratan stands, slow on his knees, and
looks at the scrapers still bent over the rows around the dead girl.

RATAN: (to Deva) क्यारी ख़ाली करवा। और बॉडी ज़िला अस्पताल भेज — पोस्टमॉर्टम होगा।

The word reaches Bherulal and the denial cracks open.

BHERULAL: पोस्टमॉर्टम? नी साहब, नी! गिर के मरी है — चीर-फाड़ क्यों? (his voice goes) रहने दो उसे... बस रहने दो...

RATAN: (quiet, not moving) नियम है, भेरूलाल।

Bherulal keeps at him — साहब, घर की इज़्ज़त है, एक बार और सोच लो — but Ratan has turned to Deva.

RATAN: चल, उसका कमरा देखते हैं।

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य दो

**INT. BHERULAL'S HOUSE — LEELA'S ROOM — DAY**

A small room off the women's side, thin light through one barred window. The whole house smells of the
gum, in the walls and in the dead girl's clothes. A string cot, a steel almirah, Leela's Class 10
guidebooks tied with string for the boards three weeks off. In the next room a woman's voice goes up — हाय लाडो, हाय मेरी लाडो — and the others take it up.

SUGNA sits on the floor with her daughter's clothes in her lap, her shoulders shaking, a fold of the
girl's clothes pressed to her mouth.

RATAN fills the doorway. He steps in and goes to the almirah. Sugna's head comes up.

SUGNA: क्या कर रहे हो आप... ये मेरी बच्ची का सामान है।

An AUNT comes in behind him.

AUNT: साहब, बाहर चलो। ये जनाना है।

Ratan does not stop. He works the lid off a steel tin, and under a fold of cloth finds a smaller cloth
knotted tight: a thin roll of tens and twenties and a fist of one-rupee coins, and a coaching-class handbill
from Ratlam, soft at the folds from handling.

Sugna has got to her feet and reached him, pulling at his arm, at the clothes in his hands.

SUGNA: (to the clothes) मेरी लाडो... मेरी बच्ची...

Ratan looks at the handbill a moment, then folds the cloth back over the money and puts it in his
pocket. The aunt comes and takes Sugna by
the arm, and Sugna folds against her and lets herself be held.

He goes, out through the keening.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-FIELD-DUSK

**EXT. POPPY FIELD — DUSK**

The field at the end of the day, the lancing stopped on Ratan's order, the gum drying grey and useless
on the pods. Ratan walks the bund where the body lay, Deva a step behind with a torch he does not need
yet. At the field's edge, a well. The house two hundred metres off, its one light on.

RATAN: घर वहाँ। मिली यहाँ, क्यारी के इस सिरे पर।

DEVA: रात को इस सिरे पर क्या करने आती?

Ratan looks past the well, to a footpath running off into the dark.

RATAN: ये रास्ता कहाँ जाता है?

DEVA: सड़क पे। आगे बस अड्डा है, मनासा वाला।

Ratan stands a while where the path leaves the field.

RATAN: रात को आख़िरी बार किसने देखा उसे — घर में, खेत में, रास्ते में। सबसे पूछ। नाम लिख।

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-WITNESS

**EXT. A WATCHMAN'S CHARPAI BY THE FIELDS — DAY**

An old NIGHT-WATCHMAN sits on a charpai outside a hut, a stick across his knees. Deva has brought him
to Ratan. He is careful, an old man who has kept his job by not seeing things.

RATAN: उस रात तू चौकी पर था?

WATCHMAN: था साहब। (a beat) आँख लग गई थी बीच में थोड़ी।

RATAN: लीला को देखा? भेरूलाल की छोरी।

The old man looks at the stick across his knees.

WATCHMAN: ...देखी थी। रात गए। अकेली, तेज़ चल के, सड़क की तरफ़।

DEVA: रोती हुई? डरी हुई?

WATCHMAN: इतना अँधेरा था साहब। बस जल्दी में थी। (and then he stops himself) पीछे कोई था कि नी, वो मैंने नी देखा। मैं फिर सो गया।

RATAN: किसी और ने देखा होगा?

WATCHMAN: (shuts, afraid) मुझे नी पता। साहब, मेरा नाम किसी काग़ज़ में मत डालना। मुझे यहीं रहना है।

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य चार

**INT. DEVA'S HOUSE — EVENING**

A small farming household — a kitchen open to the courtyard, a hand-pump, the smell of frying onion.
DEVA's mother, AMMA, works the dough. His father, BAPU, sits on a charpai. MANJU, eighteen, Deva's
sister, moves between them with the food. DEVA comes in from the day still in uniform.

AMMA: आ गया। हाथ-मुँह धो ले। (then) सुन — गिरधारी का लड़का, विजय। उसके घरवाले मंजू के लिए बात चला रहे हैं।

BAPU: अच्छा घर है। दो बीघा ज़मीन, पट्टा भी है। लड़का शहर में नौकरी देख रहा है।

Sunita sets a bowl down and does not look up.

AMMA: (to Deva) तू एक बार जा के मिल आ उनसे। वर्दी वाला है तू, तेरी बात का वज़न है।

DEVA: अभी एक केस में फँसा हूँ अम्मा।

BAPU: केस तो चलते रहेंगे। लड़की की उम्र निकली जा रही है। (to Sunita) क्यों? तू बोल।

MANJU: (low) जैसा आप ठीक समझें।

Deva looks at his sister. The day is still on him.

DEVA: मंजू से किसी ने पूछा?

AMMA: (a beat, not unkindly) पूछने की क्या बात है, बेटा। अच्छा रिश्ता है। हम उसका बुरा थोड़ी सोचेंगे।

Sunita's eyes come up to Deva's for a second — and go back down to the food.

BAPU: तो जा मिल आ। परसों।

DEVA: (a beat) ...देख लूँगा।

Sunita carries the empty pot to the pump and works the handle, and the water covers whatever she does
not say.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-MANJU

**INT. DEVA'S HOUSE — NIGHT**

The house asleep, one low bulb still on in the corner where MANJU has her bedding. She sits up with a
school notebook open in her lap and a pen in her hand, not writing. From the parents' room, the murmur
of the match goes on — गिरधारी का लड़का, दो बीघा, पट्टा. She listens to it the way you listen to weather.

Deva comes in for water, sees her awake.

DEVA: सो जा।

MANJU: (a beat) तू मिलने जाएगा? परसों? उनसे?

DEVA: देखूँगा।

Sunita closes the notebook and lies down, her face to the wall.

MANJU: रहने दे। (quieter) सब ठीक है।

Deva stands with the glass in his hand and does not drink it.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य पाँच

**EXT. CBN WEIGHMENT SHED, AMARGARH — DAY**

The same shed, later in the day. The queue has thinned. Khare stamps a slip and a grower carries it
off, folding it small. Ratan has come up at the side, in no hurry. Khare sees him, and a practised
warmth comes on.

KHARE: अरे, रतन साहब। (a beat) सुना भेरूलाल की लड़की का।

RATAN: भेरूलाल तौल पूरी देता है तेरे यहाँ?

The pen stops for half a second.

KHARE: लड़की गिर के मरी, साहब। उसमें भेरूलाल के पट्टे का क्या काम?

RATAN: पूछ रहा हूँ बस।

Khare leans in, the practised warmth gone, his voice dropped.

KHARE: रतन। भेरूलाल पर उधार है, और किसका उधार है ये तू भी जानता है। सबका हिसाब बराबर चल रहा है इधर। बीच में टाँग मत अड़ा।

RATAN: लड़की मरी है, खरे।

Khare goes back to his register and the next man's lota.

KHARE: (not looking up) लड़कियाँ मरती रहती हैं इधर।

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-TEASTALL

**EXT. TEA STALL, AMARGARH BAZAAR — DAY**

A roadside stall, a kettle, men on a bench with glasses of tea, the bazaar behind. The talk is the
dead girl. They do not lower their voices much.

MAN ONE: सुना, भेरूलाल की छोरी। रात को खेत में।

MAN TWO: छोरी रात को खेत में क्या कर रही थी, ये कोई नी पूछता। (he sips) आजकल की लड़कियाँ।

MAN ONE: सगाई हो गई थी ना उसकी? अच्छे घर में?

MAN TWO: हो गई थी। (a shrug) भगवान की मर्ज़ी।

A third man, older, has been quiet.

MAN THREE: सी.बी.एन. वाला घुमा रहा है इधर-उधर। पोस्टमॉर्टम करवा दिया।

MAN TWO: (lowering his voice at last) रहने देगा क्या? ऊपर तक पहुँच है इन लोगों की। (he tips his head, vaguely, toward somewhere bigger) रतन साहब समझदार है। करवा देगा बंद।

They drink their tea.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य छह

**INT. DISTRICT HOSPITAL — POSTMORTEM ROOM — DAY**

Tiles, a steel table, a drain. Leela's body under a sheet. The medical officer, DOCTOR VERMA, fills a
form standing up, wanting to be elsewhere. Ratan and Deva at the door.

VERMA: सिर पर चोट, गिरने से। साफ़ बात है। (he ticks a box) ब्रॉट डेड लिख रहा हूँ।

RATAN: विसरा भेजा?

VERMA: किसलिए? चोट सामने है।

RATAN: फेफड़े, पेट, ख़ून — सब भेजो।

VERMA: इंस्पेक्टर, यहाँ रोज़ चार बॉडी आती हैं। हर एक का विसरा भेजूँ तो लैब बैठ जाए। ऊपर से कुछ कहा गया है क्या?

RATAN: कहा गया होता तो रोकने को कहा जाता, भेजने को नी।

Verma looks at him, then at the small shape under the sheet. He folds the sheet back from her face and
leans in, and something stops him a moment.

VERMA: (half to himself) चोट गिरने की ही है, पर... (he lets it go) ठीक है, भेज देता हूँ। रिपोर्ट में हफ़्ता-दस दिन लगेंगे।

RATAN: जल्दी करवाओ।

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य तीन

**INT. CBN OFFICE, AMARGARH — DAY**

A government room gone yellow at the edges — steel almirahs of files, a ceiling fan turning slow, a
framed Gandhi over the door. SUPERINTENDENT MISHRA sits behind his desk with Leela's thin file open in
front of him. Ratan stands. DEVA waits by the door.

MISHRA: (not looking up) क्यारी में गिर के मरी, और पट्टे वाली ज़मीन है। बाप दस्तख़त करने को तैयार है। (he turns a page) इसमें इतना क्या है, रतन।

RATAN: बॉडी पोस्टमॉर्टम के लिए भेज दी है।

Mishra looks up.

MISHRA: किसने भेजी?

RATAN: मैंने।

Mishra sits back, the tiredness of a man who has settled a hundred of these.

MISHRA: गाँव की बच्ची है। घर में मौत है, इज़्ज़त का सवाल है। चीर-फाड़ से तुझे क्या मिलेगा? रपट बना, बंद कर। मैं अभी साइन कर देता हूँ।

RATAN: बाप माल छोड़ने को तैयार नी था साहब। बच्ची पड़ी थी सामने, और वो लुआई की सोच रहा था।

MISHRA: (a beat) हर बाप जल्दी में होता है। (quieter) रतन। इस इलाके में किसके पट्टे हैं, किसके पीछे कौन है — तुझसे ज़्यादा कौन जानता है। बेकार में किसी के घर में हाथ मत डाल।

Ratan does not answer that.

RATAN: रपट पोस्टमॉर्टम के बाद बनेगी।

Mishra holds his eye a moment, then drops it back to the file.

MISHRA: ठीक है। तेरी रपट, तेरी ज़िम्मेदारी।

Ratan turns to go. Deva follows him out.

**INT. CBN OFFICE — RATAN'S DESK — CONTINUOUS**

Ratan lowers himself into his chair. Deva hovers, the file still in his hands.

DEVA: (low) साहब... मिश्रा साहब ग़लत तो नी कह रहे। फिसल के गिरी होगी।

RATAN: तूने कभी किसी को क्यारी में गिर के मरते देखा है? पाँव फिसल के?

DEVA: ...नी।

RATAN: मैंने भी नी देखा।

A beat. Ratan does not go on with it.

RATAN: चाय बोल दे, देवा।

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-RATAN-ROUTINE

**EXT. A LICENSED FIELD, EDGE OF AMARGARH — DAY**

Another grower's plot, another lancing crew. The grower, HUKMA, walks Ratan along his rows, easy, the
two of them old at this. At the end of the row Hukma folds a few notes into Ratan's hand the way you'd
pass a relative something for the children, and Ratan takes them the way you take it, without looking.

HUKMA: तौल इस बार थोड़ी ऊपर-नीचे हो जाए तो... आप तो हैं ही साहब।

RATAN: देख लेंगे।

HUKMA: (about the other thing, carefully) भेरूलाल वाला... सब ठीक हो जाएगा ना?

Ratan does not answer that. He pockets the notes and walks back to the jeep, and for a second he
stands at the door with his hand on it, not getting in.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-RATAN-WOUND

**EXT. A ROAD PAST AN OLD FIELD — DUSK**

The jeep pulled over on an empty country road. Ratan stands at the edge of a field that is not part of
his case — a good plot, well worked, someone's gum drying on the racks. He looks at it the way a man
looks at a house he used to live in. A YOUNG FARMER slows his motorcycle, sees the uniform, folds his
hands.

FARMER: राम राम साहब।

RATAN: राम राम।

Ratan looks at the field a moment longer, then gets back in the jeep, and it goes dark behind him.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य सात

**EXT. BHERULAL'S HOUSE — COURTYARD — DAY**

The condolence crowd has thinned. DAULATRAM, sixty, the groom's uncle, prosperous and unhurried,
sits where the mourners sat. Behind him stands PRATAP, fifty-five, the groom, saying nothing. Bherulal
receives them with both hands.

DAULATRAM: बहुत बुरा हुआ भेरूलाल। होनी को कौन टाल सकता है। (a beat) पर आगे की बात भी करनी है।

BHERULAL: (grasping) हाँ जी, ज़रूर। रिश्ता तो अपना बना ही रहेगा दौलतराम जी।

DAULATRAM: लड़की तो रही नी। और जो हिसाब था — उधार, ज़मीन — वो रिश्ते के साथ बँधा था।

BHERULAL: उधार... मैं चुका दूँगा जी। बस थोड़ा वक़्त।

Daulatram lets that sit. Then, easily:

DAULATRAM: तेरी छोटी वाली कितने की है?

Something goes across Bherulal's face. His elder girl is not yet in the ground.

BHERULAL: (low) ...बारह की।

DAULATRAM: (rising) बड़ी हो जाए तो बात करेंगे। (a hand on Bherulal's shoulder) हिसाब बराबर रखना।

Pratap looks at the house a moment — the dead girl's house — with flat eyes, and follows his uncle
out. Bherulal stands where they left him.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-WOMENS-HOUSE

**INT. BHERULAL'S HOUSE — INNER ROOM — DAY**

The women's side. An older relative, the AUNT, sorts through Leela's things into a cloth bundle to be
given away, the way it is done. Bherulal's younger daughter, SHEELA, twelve, sits against the wall
watching. SUGNA is on the floor, not helping, her hands in her lap.

The aunt holds up a kameez, decides, folds it into the bundle.

AUNT: (not unkindly) रख के क्या करेगी इन्हें। किसी ज़रूरतमंद के काम आएँगी।

Sugna says nothing. Nanhi watches a green dupatta go into the bundle.

SHEELA: वो दीदी का है।

AUNT: हाँ बेटा। दीदी अब नी है ना।

Nanhi looks at her mother. Sugna does not look back at her, or at the bundle, or at anything. Her hands
stay folded in her lap as the last of the girl goes into the cloth.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-PRESSURE

**INT. CBN OFFICE — MISHRA'S ROOM — DAY**

Mishra is on the phone, listening far more than he speaks, his face gone careful. He puts it down. Ratan
is at the door.

MISHRA: रतन। अंदर आ। (Ratan comes in) अभी फ़ोन आया था। ऊपर से।

RATAN: किसका?

MISHRA: (does not say) पूछ रहे थे भेरूलाल वाला मामला अभी तक बंद क्यों नी हुआ।

RATAN: रिपोर्ट आने दो साहब।

Mishra comes around the desk, and what is in his voice now is almost kindness.

MISHRA: पच्चीस साल हो गए तेरी नौकरी को। दो साल बचे हैं, पेंशन सामने है। एक मरी हुई छोरी के लिए जो ऊपर बैठे हैं उनसे पंगा लेगा? वो तुझे एक झटके में कहीं का नी छोड़ेंगे। समझ रहा है?

RATAN: समझ रहा हूँ।

MISHRA: तो बंद कर दे।

RATAN: रिपोर्ट आने दो।

Mishra looks at him a long moment, the way you look at a man walking into something, and lets him go.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य आठ

**EXT. A LANE BEHIND THE SCHOOL — DAY**

A narrow lane, a wall, the back of the government school. RESHMA, sixteen, in school uniform, walking
fast with her bag. Ratan falls in beside her. She does not stop.

RATAN: तू लीला की सहेली थी। रेशमा।

RESHMA: मुझे कुछ नी पता साहब।

He holds out the Ratlam handbill, folded soft.

RATAN: ये उसके पास था। कोचिंग का, रतलाम का। (she keeps walking) मैं उसे फँसाने नी आया, बेटा। जो हुआ, वो जानने आया हूँ।

Reshma slows. She looks back down the empty lane once.

RESHMA: वो पढ़ना चाहती थी। आगे। रतलाम जा के। पैसे जमा कर रही थी, चुपके से।

RATAN: किससे चुपके?

RESHMA: घरवालों से। (lower) शादी तय हो गई थी ना उसकी, उस आदमी से जो बहुत बड़ा था। लीला बोलती थी — मैं वहाँ नी जाऊँगी। भाग जाऊँगी, पढ़ूँगी।

The lane is quiet. Reshma's eyes fill, and she wipes them with the back of her wrist, angry at it.

RESHMA: साहब, मेरा नाम किसी को मत बताना। मुझे जाना है।

RATAN: उसकी मदद किसी ने की? भागने में?

But she has already pulled her bag up and gone, fast, the way you go when you have said one word too
many.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य नौ

**INT. CBN JEEP — NIGHT**

The jeep on a dark road, the fields black on both sides. Deva drives. Ratan beside him, the day behind
his eyes.

RATAN: वो भाग रही थी। उसी आदमी से जिससे शादी तय थी। पचपन का।

DEVA: पचपन। और लीला सोलह की।

They drive a while.

DEVA: साहब... मेरी बहन की भी बात चल रही है। अच्छा लड़का है, ज़मीन है, पट्टा है। मैं परसों मिलने जाने वाला हूँ उनके घर।

RATAN: तेरी बहन ने हाँ बोली?

Deva opens his mouth to answer, and finds he does not have one.

DEVA: ...पता नी।

Ratan looks out at the dark fields and does not say anything, and lets the boy sit with it.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## NEW-DEVA-ACTS

**INT. DEVA'S HOUSE — MORNING**

Morning, the family at tea. BAPU is dressed to go — the visit to the boy's family is today. AMMA
presses a clean shirt on Deva. Sunita pours, eyes down.

BAPU: चल देवा, देर हो रही है। गिरधारी इंतज़ार कर रहा होगा।

Deva does not get up.

DEVA: बापू। (a beat) उससे पूछा हमने? मंजू से?

The room stops a little. Bapu turns.

BAPU: ये कैसी बात कर रहा है तू।

DEVA: बस पूछ लेते हैं। एक बार। (to Sunita, gently) तू बोल। तुझे जाना है?

Sunita's hand stays on the kettle. The whole house waits on her, and for a moment it is not certain what she will
say.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य दस

**INT. RATAN'S HOUSE — NIGHT**

A small house. Ratan eats alone at the table. His wife, KANTA, clears a pot in the back, the two of
them long past needing to speak. A knock. At the door, A MAN — pressed shirt, calm, the kind that is
never quite introduced.

THE MAN: रतन साहब। माफ़ करना इतनी रात। दो मिनट।

He steps in only as far as he needs to, and sets a thick envelope on the edge of the table.

THE MAN: भेरूलाल वाला मामला। ऊपर इच्छा है जल्दी बंद हो जाए। दुर्घटना, बस इतना। (a small push toward the envelope) और ये आपकी तकलीफ़ के लिए।

Ratan looks at the envelope. He has taken a hundred of these. Kanta, in the back, does not turn around.

RATAN: किसकी इच्छा?

THE MAN: (a smile that names no one) आप समझदार आदमी हैं।

The envelope sits between them. It would be the easiest thing in the world. Then Ratan slides it back
across the table.

RATAN: इस बार नी।

The smile goes.

THE MAN: सोच लो साहब। ये रास्ता आपके लिए अच्छा नी रहेगा।

RATAN: रात बहुत हो गई।

The man takes his envelope and goes. Ratan sits. After a while Kanta speaks from the back, not unkind,
not hopeful.

KANTA: किस बात का पंगा ले रहे हो इस उम्र में।

Ratan does not answer her. He looks at the door the man left by.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## दृश्य ग्यारह

**INT. CBN OFFICE — RATAN'S DESK — NIGHT**

Late, the office empty. Ratan sits with Leela's thin file open under the lamp. Her school photo is
clipped to the corner — a girl grinning crooked at a camera, a board exam three weeks off. He looks at
it for a long time.

**EXT. BHERULAL'S HOUSE — COURTYARD — NIGHT**

The mourners are gone. The house is dark. SUGNA sits alone against the wall where the women keened all
day, her daughter's folded clothes in her lap, her hands still on them. The camera holds on her, on her
hands on the folded clothes, and does not move either.

**CUT TO BLACK. END OF EPISODE ONE.**
