# अमल — EP1: तौल / The Weighing — SCENE BREAKDOWN (v2)

*Built against `EP1_STRUCTURE_v2.md` (the authority). ~23 scenes, ~50 min. Every scene carries a
**WANT / WALL / TURN** (the Mamet gate at blueprint level — the want-less texture scene is what failed
before, #12). The investigation is the engine; the Malwa world surfaces only when the case moves through
it. Held cards in [HELD] brackets — they stay buried in the eventual prose (no spoilers, #14). Chiastic
marks: **A** = the Jhujhar planted, **A′** = Ratan enacts it. Approve before any line is written.*

## MOVEMENT 1 — PROMISE (~1 min)

**S1 · The saka (cold open).** 1305. A bloodied old hand on a Parmar talwar, the ancestral ring on it,
mid-saka as Paramara Malwa falls to Khilji — a headless warrior fighting on. *Promise beat (image, not a
want-scene): this ends in blood.* [HELD: reads as the ancestor; it is Ratan's own eventual death.]

**S2 · Title — अमल.**

## MOVEMENT 2 — THE CAGE (~12 min)
*Meet the bought man at full corruption; plant the Jhujhar (A); the body enters; he signs it unseen.*

**S3 · Deva arrives.** Constable Deva — Itarsi outsider, no idea of the opium trade — reports to the
Amargarh post; he moved the family here because his sister **Manju** is marrying across into Rajasthan.
- WANT: report in, place himself. WALL: a sleepy, bought backwater that doesn't match a real posting.
  TURN: the first thing he sees is the chai-paani economy running the desk.

**S4 · Ratan, working.** We meet Ratan properly — the bought cop at full corruption: a death waved through
as a tractor accident, the envelope pocketed, the obscene cash flashing (thin govt slip vs the brick). He
teaches Deva the only lesson that keeps a man alive here.
- WANT (Ratan): clear the day's files, take the take, go home. WALL: the dead man's kin / a petitioner
  wanting more than a stamp. TURN: Deva watches the lawman debased; a flinch he can't hide. [#10 — uglier
  than any stand.]

**S5 · The pawnbroker (chiastic A).** Ratan goes to hold off the interest on the **pawned ancestral ring** —
the Parmar Jhujhar's own ring, in a drawer. The pawnbroker (or the old bhat there) names the legend: the
warrior who fell headless at the Khilji conquest, fighting on.
- WANT (Ratan): keep the ancestor from being sold off — pay, or stall. WALL: the debt, the humiliation of
  begging time. TURN: the bare finger; the headless warrior's ring in hock to a moneylender. [A planted +
  the ring marker, on a real want — not a texture scene. The descendant has pawned his ancestor.]

**S6 · The body.** Dawn. A girl dead at a field's edge — reported in thin, name-light, for a quick stamp.
- WANT (the reporter — constable/villager): get it signed "fall," fast. WALL: it's a death, it needs the
  SHO's pen. TURN: the file lands on Ratan's desk. **The case enters (~min 8–10).**

**S7 · He signs it a fall — from his desk.** Reflex. Twenty-five years of reflex. He never goes, never
looks, never reads the name.
- WANT: clear it, take the chai-paani, go home. WALL: (the horror — there is none; that's the point) only
  Deva's silence. TURN: stamped, unseen. **The promise goes live: a girl is dead and the cop just buried
  her.** [HELD/recognition: the name doesn't register — see Open Q1.]

**S8 · Home / Kanta.** The pension papers; two years to retirement. Her respectful आप/-इए register (#4).
- WANT (Kanta): get him to eat, a normal evening, the papers signed. WALL: his deadness, the day on him.
  TURN: a glimpse of the buried man — and the clock is set (two years, then the pyre). [The mortality-clock
  planted, pays off at S9–10.]

## MOVEMENT 3 — THE CATALYST (~10 min)
*Mortality + memory, not family. The corpse stirs.*

**S9 · The cremation.** A third friend of the old circle is dead — retired, dead of nothing, a small pyre.
Nirgun/Kabir bhajan. The guide-friend (alive — the conscience) at Ratan's shoulder.
- WANT (Ratan): get through it, pay respects, stay numb, leave. WALL: the guide-friend won't let him stay
  numb. TURN: *"Who'll remember us when we're gone?"* — and the same fire is two years off for Ratan.

**S10 · After the pyre.** Ratan and the guide-friend, alone.
- WANT (guide-friend): wake him — *"you were always different. You walked into the Chambal ravines and
  brought the dacoits out. You were someone."* WALL: twenty-five years of surrender; Ratan refuses it.
  TURN: a crack — the dead man stirs. **The catalyst.** [#13 — the friend carries the memory + the Jhujhar
  blood, deepening A; #25 — mortality + memory, NOT the niece. The refusal-of-the-call beat.]

## MOVEMENT 4 — THE FIRST CROSSING (~12 min)
*The body lifts the sword. He pulls the thread; the world surfaces through the case.*

**S11 · The field.** The catalyst drives him to the spot he signed away. Deva shadows him (the Watson).
- WANT: see it himself — the detective waking. WALL: the scene's been walked over; villagers all say
  "fall." TURN: his eye catches the wrong — **this is no fall.** The Chambal detective is back.

**S12 · He amends his own document.** Back at the post, he strikes his own stamp and writes an order for a
postmortem.
- WANT: get her to autopsy. WALL: the munshi — *"you already signed it, sir; it's closed."* TURN: he
  overrides his own signature. **First crossing.** A bought man does not amend his own paperwork.

**S13 · The machine notices.** Mishra (the local machine) leans in.
- WANT (Mishra): put it back to sleep — *"why are you stirring a closed file?"* WALL: Ratan's new, fragile
  resolve. TURN: Ratan deflects, doesn't fold. **Pressure established.**

**S14 · Kaal Bhairav.** The bought *kotwal* before the god-*kotwal* of Avanti (Ujjain — same city as his
kuldevi Harsiddhi and Mahakal). The deity you offer liquor.
- WANT (Ratan): steady his nerve / a kind of absolution before the one authority he can't pay off. WALL:
  the god-kotwal can't be bribed — the liquor-offering is the mirror (the one god you hand your vice). TURN:
  he sees himself in it. [Mirror beat, short + charged — #12 watch: the WANT (absolution he can't buy) is
  what keeps it from being the want-less scene that failed before. Flag for the drama pass.]

**S15 · The thread (the world via the case).** Off-books begins. Pulling at "who was she, why was she
there" surfaces the belt: the debt, old **Dhanraj**, the selling. Deva — the outsider — learns the trade
here, now case-driven (the teaching earns itself). Obscene cash flashes again.
- WANT: who she was, why the field. WALL: the belt closes ranks; nobody talks. TURN: the selling surfaces —
  she was being married off. [#8 satisfied through the investigation, not as standalone tourism.]

**S16 · Deva & Manju.** The B-story weave (lean — one beat). Deva's home; Manju's Rajasthan match talked up
warm and loving; Deva swallows his unease.
- WANT (Deva): protect/place his sister. WALL: the match is already moving. TURN: he goes along. [The
  marriage-machine smiling — rhymes, unspoken, with the girl who was sold. The weave under the case.]

## MOVEMENT 5 — THE TRAP (~10 min)
*His corruption is the lens. The apparatus convicts itself. The knife.*

**S17 · The postmortem — clean, a fall, SAME-DAY.** The report lands fast and official: accidental fall.
The system hands him the exit — *you did your due diligence, it's signed, go home.*
- WANT: the truth. WALL: the clean official report; a lesser cop would exhale and take the out. TURN: he
  reads it the way only the man who forged a hundred can — *the speed is the tell; I've handed this doctor a
  dozen accidents and he's never once disappointed me.* **He KNOWS.** The clean PM doesn't close the case —
  it convicts the whole apparatus. [KEYSTONE — his own sin is the proof.]

**S18 · The doctor.** A fellow Rajput, an old hand at this; he signed it.
- WANT (Ratan): make him admit it / redo it. WALL: the doctor pushes back — *"stop rocking the boat. You,
  of all people, know how this works. Go home."* TURN: Ratan answers sideways — a line that lets the change
  in him *leak* without naming it. [The doctor is the mirror in human form: a finished Rajput, what Ratan
  stays if he never lifts the sword. No official channel is left to him now.]

**S19 · Forced off-books → the trail.** No channel he can trust; every one lies, and he's the proof. He
works it the only way left — the people, the field hands, the village.
- WANT: find who she was, where she's from. WALL: the belt's silence; the trade's teeth (questions are
  dangerous). TURN: the trail leads to a house — and he goes to it.

**S20 · The house — it's Sugna's.** He walks in to question the family and it's his estranged sister
**Sugna**; the dead girl was her daughter — **his niece.**
- WANT: question the household over the body. WALL: the door opens on his own blood. TURN: **the knife** —
  he signed his own niece away unseen; *he didn't even read her name.* [Recognition resolves here — Open
  Q1. Bherulal present: the brother-in-law, the opium empire. HELD: Sugna is the grieving mother only —
  nothing of her hand; nothing of the mercy-killing.]

## MOVEMENT 6 — THE TURN (~5 min)
*No channel left but himself. The Jhujhar lifts the sword.*

**S21 · The pressure converges.** Bury it. The trade and **Bherulal** need it shut — an open murder invites
the CBN scrutiny that sinks the new **patta in the son's name** (and the belt's quiet).
- WANT (Bherulal / Mishra / the trade): close the file. WALL: Ratan — wrecked, and awake. TURN: the demand
  goes explicit, with the *"bike that slipped"* threat behind it. [The wall is the empire + the apparatus,
  not the killer or the buyer — #22–24 fixed. HELD: the patta-for-the-son motive surfaces only lightly;
  *अफ़ीम का पट्टा लड़के से बढ़कर है* incarnated.]

**S22 · Ratan alone — the all-night pen.** The doda; the band of pale skin where the ring was; the girl's
face he never looked at.
- WANT: decide. WALL: everything — his life, the threat, twenty-five years, and the *honorable* exit the
  clean PM already handed him. TURN: the refusal finally inverts.

**S23 · The door of no return (chiastic A′).** He caps the pen. He writes a fresh requisition — a real
postmortem at the **district hospital**, out of the bought local doctor's reach — and signs it **Ratan
Singh Pawar**, his own name on the thing that will kill him.
- WANT: the truth, out of the machine's hands. WALL: it is, plainly, suicide. TURN: he signs anyway. **The
  headless body lifts the sword.** Cut toward the saka. Black. [A′ closes the chiasmus; the mirror is never
  stated — the audience makes it.]

## Open questions (lock while scripting)

**Q1 — the recognition mechanic (the one to nail).** How does Ratan sign his own niece's death unseen?
- **Recommended (A+):** the estrangement is old and total — he doesn't know Sugna's married house or her
  children; the file is thin and name-light and he *doesn't read it* (the corruption). At the house (S20)
  the shock is double: *this is where she ended up* **and** *I signed her girl away without reading the
  name.* Preserves the mystery + the knife, and folds in the self-horror.
- Alt (B): he knows the household is Sugna's and signs anyway (peak deadness, #10) — darker, but loses the
  reveal and risks illogic. *I recommend A+.*

**Q2 — runtime balance.** M3 (catalyst) is two big emotional scenes (~8–10 min); M6 (turn) is tight (~5).
If EP1 runs long, S16 (Deva/Manju) is the most compressible.

**Q3 — names still open:** the Parmar ancestor (the ring/the Jhujhar — falls at the 1305 conquest), the
doctor, Jhujhar spelling. Resolve during scripting; not blockers.

**Watch-flags for the gates:** S14 (Bhairav) — confirm the WANT (absolution-he-can't-buy) holds under the
Mamet pass; S5 (pawnbroker) — keep the Jhujhar naming lean, never the mirror stated; S16 — keep the rhyme
unspoken (clear-pane); S1/S20 — no prose spoilers.
