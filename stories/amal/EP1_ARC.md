# अमल — Episode 1: तौल / The Weighing — ARC (the cage)

*Re-envisioned EP1 (supersedes old EP1.md + EP1_BREAKDOWN.md). Ground in MALWA.md. EP1 is
**establishment, not investigation** (Breaking Bad / Last Samurai rule) — live in the "before" so the turn
is earned. He is a **CBN Inspector (a cop)** reduced by 25 years of surrender to a rubber stamp. ~40 min,
~15 scenes. Apply the full craft process (drama in every scene; B-story; heart; iceberg; show-don't-tell;
NO shrink-wrap — let it breathe; clear-pane; consistency; the keyhole; world; weave). Postmortem = LAST
shot.*

## The four things beyond humiliation (not everything in Walt's hour is humiliation)

- **The promise (in medias res).** The weighing cold open gives the *world,* not the *payoff.* So precede
  it with a **flash-forward teaser**: a few electric, context-free seconds of the saka — an old man,
  bloodied, a talwar, fire and headlights, men falling — then ripped away. Our "desert." Promises the
  audience the explosion so they'll sit the slow burn.
- **The diagnosis (mortality catalyst, NOT copied cancer).** A **forgotten peer's funeral** — an old CBN
  man, just retired, dead in a month, buried small, no one from the dept there. Ratan sees his own near
  future: two years, then a forgotten nothing, having been bought, having been no one. The pension date
  sharpens it: now or never.
- **The B-story.** Deva and his sister **Manju's** arranged marriage — the same marriage-machine, in a
  warm, ordinary, loving house. Normal — which is what makes it sit in the stomach later. Gives Deva
  stakes; breathing room out of the A-story.
- **The gift + the heart + the world.** Show his *gift* (the real investigator's eye, once, good at
  something); the *heart* (a flicker of the man Kanta married; the buried love/guilt with his sister); the
  *world* (the belt — fields, amlis, tea stall — texture, so it's a place not a plot). Anti-shrink-wrap.
- **Self-medication = doda (poppy-husk) tea, NOT alcohol** (Algren's bottle, made Malwa + avoids on-screen
  alcohol glamorization). Ratan is a quiet **doda-amli** — the cop who polices opium, numbed by opium, a
  warrior's soul with nowhere to go drowning in the same gum he signs off. It's the cage made physical.
  **Rejecting the tea = the awakening** (back half, not early): the day he pours it out / refuses it is the
  day the bought man dies and the Rajput comes back.
- **Save-the-cat (the redeemable thing):** an old **amli** the trade hollowed out, whom everyone steps
  over. Ratan shares his doda-tea with him, treats him with a dignity no one else bothers to, and when
  someone shoves the old man around, Ratan steps in — the protector flickering one beat, then sits back
  down. Triple duty: buried decency (we root for him), his own addiction (they're the same ruined men),
  the theme (opium eats them).
- **The marker — the Parmar signet ring** (the seal of his line, kings of Malwa; pairs with the talwar;
  full arc in MALWA.md §5b). EP1 shows it *gone* — **he pawned it** in his lowest year (sold his crown for
  tea-money = the spine made physical). Establish via the **pale band on his bare finger**, maybe a glimpse
  of it in a pawnbroker's tray. (Awakening = he redeems it; saka = on the talwar hand; lean = the pawn,
  softer variant = locked away.)
- **The groom/buyer = a sleazy OLD HINDU man (Muslim angle DROPPED — Hindu families only).** Named
  **DHANRAJ** in the pages: old, moneyed, sleazy (gold, a young servant girl), buying a 16-yr-old for money
  because Bherulal's in debt. No snakebite/wives — just old and sleazy. The horror is the **selling**
  (Bherulal trading his daughter for the patta), never religion.

## Names (locked)

**Harsiddhi Mata** = the Kuldevi (Devi Poojan; the goddess of the Malwa kings, Ujjain). **Hamir** = the
Jhujhar ancestor AND his signet ring (the pawnbroker asks to see "Hamir" — never "a ring"). Sword
**unnamed on screen** (one proper noun too many for the audience); reference-only name = Bhairav.

## Scene list (20 scenes, ~50 min — Delhi Crime length)

1. **Flash-forward (the promise)** — a bloodied old hand on an ancient talwar; the heavy signet → reads as
   the **Jhujhar ancestor Hamir,** not Ratan. *(Hook + misdirection.)*
2. **Title — अमल.**
3. **Cold open — the weighing** *(exists)* — govt pittance, then the black brick of cash.
4. **The tip** — Ratan signs off a death as routine, takes the thin *chacha* envelope from a fixer who
   won't get out of the jeep; **Deva** (idolizing the old *Inspector Ratan Singh Panwar*) watches.
5. **The belt (THE WORLD)** — Ratan's drive through the opium country: lanced fields, the bazaar, a tea
   stall (a kid paying off a fat wad — the obscene cash). *(The place breathes; show-the-world rule.)*
6. **The amli + the tea** — *doda* tea shared with a wrecked old amli; he steps in when the old man's
   shoved (the danger flickers), then sits back down. *(Addiction + save-the-cat.)*
7. **The pawnbroker** — over tea, "want to see *Hamir*?"; Ratan refuses; the bare finger. *(Ring pawned +
   misdirection.)*
8. **The rites, and Kanta** — sharpens the family talwar (sharp when every other's rust); Shastra Puja +
   Devi Poojan to **Harsiddhi Mata;** then Kanta, the worn marriage, the pension countdown. *(Buried Rajput
   + heart.)*
9. **The Jhujhar (bloodline conversation)** — at the family shrine / with an elder: the Hamir story, the
   ancestor who fought on after he fell, the sthal. *(Earns the cold-open misdirection; the heritage.)*
10. **The gift, wasted** — his eye catches what everyone missed; the superior waves it off, sign it.
    *(Gift + "classroom" humiliation.)*
11. **The diagnosis** — a peer's small, forgotten funeral; Ratan sees his own end. *(Mortality catalyst.)*
12. **Deva's house (B-story)** — Manju's arranged match talked up in a warm house. *(B-story; rhyme.)*
13. **The women's world** — the belt's women: the Devi temple, a possessed woman at Hussain Tekri, the
    wells & looms. The female register, broadly (not just Sugna). *(World; seeds the women's fate.)*
14. **The body (the keyhole)** — a girl dead at the field's edge at dawn; he means to write it a fall.
15. **His sister's girl** — the dead girl is his estranged sister's daughter; love & guilt under the cold;
    the unsaid — she was being sold to a sleazy old man.
16. **The buyer** — a glimpse of the sleazy old man + the marriage-deal (the fate she was sold into, given
    a face). *(Groom both-versions, Mirzapur rules.)*
17. **On his own ground** — the big man's compound stands on the family's lost land, the **sthal where Hamir
    fell;** kept waiting there by the risen-low, the danger moves and he buries it. *(Deepest humiliation +
    the saka-ground planted.)*
18. **Sign it** — they assume him, the envelope's in his pocket — against the wrongness and his sister's
    face. *(The fight.)*
19. **The rhyme** — Manju's wedding readied, cut against the dead girl who was sold. *(Weave; unspoken.)*
20. **The turn** — the death-by-fall form; instead, he sends her for postmortem. Cut to black. *(Door of no
    return; the vachan + real investigation start EP2.)*
