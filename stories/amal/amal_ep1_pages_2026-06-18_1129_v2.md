FADE IN:

**EXT. — NIGHT**

No establishing. Close, fast, firelit:

An old hand — scarred, slick with blood — closes around the worn hilt of a talwar. The blade is old, a
hundred small nicks. On one finger, a heavy gold signet, its seal rubbed almost smooth.

The blade goes forward, into someone. A wet grunt. A shape folds away.

Fire, off to the side. Men coming. The hand swings the sword again, and again.

For half a breath, wider: an old man on his feet on dark ground, the smoke behind him lit saffron, blood
to the elbow, more of them closing in. He does not go down.

## SCENE 4 — THE TIP

**EXT. A DIRT TURNOUT OFF THE FIELD ROAD — DAY**

A man's body in the dust at the road's edge, a cycle bent under him. Flies. INSPECTOR RATAN SINGH PANWAR —
late fifties, heavy, khaki gone soft at the seams, a grey moustache nobody's twirled in years — crouches
over it on one knee, not really looking. A clipboard. He writes.

Constable DEVA — twenty-four, the uniform still creased from the shop — stands a step behind, watching
Ratan the way you watch a man you've heard stories about.

A Bolero idles on the road. The window comes down. A YOUNG FIXER, sunglasses, doesn't get out.

FIXER: साहब, निपट गया?

Ratan doesn't look up.

RATAN: ट्रैक्टर ने मारा होगा। रात का मामला है।

FIXER: हाँ हाँ, वही।

A folded note flicks out the window, lands by Ratan's knee in the dust.

FIXER: चाय-पानी रख लो, चाचा।

The Bolero is already pulling away before the word lands, dragging a tail of dust over the body.

Ratan looks at the note a moment. Picks it up. Doesn't count it. It goes into his breast pocket. He gets
up off his knee slow, one hand pushing down on the thigh.

Deva is looking at the pocket. Then at the body. Then he catches himself.

DEVA: इसका पोस्टमॉर्टम...?

RATAN: (already turning away) ट्रैक्टर ने मारा है, देवा। पोस्टमॉर्टम में ट्रैक्टर थोड़ी निकलेगा।

He walks toward his own jeep. Deva stays with the body a second, then comes after.

DEVA: साहब... एक बात पूछूँ?

RATAN: पूछ।

DEVA: शहर की पोस्टिंग छोड़ के मैं आपके नीचे आया। लोग बोले — रतन सिंह पँवार के नीचे लग जा, आदमी बन जाएगा।

Ratan stops at the jeep door. Doesn't turn around right away. Something moves across his face — there and
gone, like a fish under ice.

RATAN: (gets in) बन जाएगा।

He pulls the door shut. Through the windshield: the body in the dust, the flies, the place where the
Bolero was. Deva climbs in the other side. Says nothing.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 5 — THE BELT

**EXT. FIELD ROAD, AMARGARH BELT — DAY**

The jeep on the field road, Deva driving, Ratan in the passenger seat, window down. The country goes by:
poppy fields to the horizon, the pods scored, lines of women and men bent over the rows scraping the grey
gum before the sun takes the dew. A government weighment shed, a queue of steel lotas, a hand-painted
board — केंद्रीय नारकोटिक्स ब्यूरो.

Deva reads it all like a book he's only heard about. Ratan watches none of it; he's had forty years of it.

They slow at the edge of the bazaar — a tea stall, a kettle, men on a bench. A BOY, fifteen, in a pressed
shirt, peels a note off a roll thick as his fist, pays for two plates of poha and a tea, doesn't wait for
change. Nobody at the stall looks up.

Deva does.

DEVA: (low) इसके पास इतने पैसे...?

RATAN: किसान का छोरा है।

That's the whole of it. Deva turns it over. Ratan is already looking past him — at the stall, at the old
man on the ground against its leg.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 6 — THE AMLI

**EXT. ROADSIDE TEA STALL — CONTINUOUS**

Ratan eases down onto the bench like a man who's sat here ten thousand times. The stall-owner sets a glass
in front of him unasked — not tea: a cloudy grey water. Doda. Poppy-husk. Ratan drinks.

Against the stall's leg sits an OLD AMLI — sixty going on ninety, the trade chewed through him years back,
a man the bazaar steps around like furniture. Without looking, Ratan tips a little from his own glass into
the old man's cracked cup. The shaking hands take it.

Deva stays standing, apart, watching his inspector drink the grey water.

A SHOPBOY hauling crates kicks the amli's leg aside — casual, not even cruel, just clearing his path.

SHOPBOY: हट, अमली।

Ratan doesn't raise his voice. He doesn't even rise all the way — just enough, a hand closing on the boy's
wrist as he passes, and something comes up in the old inspector's face that stops the boy cold.

RATAN: (quiet, to the stall-owner, not letting go) इसको खाना दे। मेरे खाते में।

He lets the wrist go. The boy's gone, fast. The owner ladles poha onto a leaf for the amli. Ratan sits
back, drinks. The thing that was in his face is gone.

Deva watches all of it — the grey glass, the old man with his leaf, the inspector. He doesn't sit. He
doesn't say anything.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 7 — THE PAWNBROKER

**INT. SETH'S PAWNSHOP, AMARGARH BAZAAR — DAY**

Cramped, a barred window, shelves of other people's lives — brass pots, a transistor, bangles, a stilled
sewing machine. SETH — fifties, soft, careful — counts a stack of notes and slides it across the
glass-topped counter. Ratan pockets it without counting. Through the barred window, the jeep: Deva waits
by it, out of earshot.

The Seth pours two teas — real tea — and pushes one over. Then, easy, the way he says it any other day:

SETH: हमीर देखोगे, साहब? रखा है, सँभाल के। जब बोलो।

Ratan's glass stops halfway to his mouth. A beat too long. He looks at the tea, not at the Seth.

RATAN: नी।

SETH: (a soft shrug, no push) जैसी मर्ज़ी। (a beat) ब्याज चढ़ रहा है, साहब। बढ़ता ही जाएगा।

Ratan drinks his tea. Says nothing. Outside the barred window, the bazaar goes on.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 8 — THE RITES, AND KANTA

**INT. RATAN'S HOUSE — EVENING**

A small, plain house. In a corner, a low wooden shelf: a framed Harsiddhi Mata, vermilion, a dried
marigold, a steel diya. Across two pegs, a talwar — the scabbard worn soft, but when Ratan draws an inch
of it, the steel is bright. Sharp.

Ratan sits cross-legged on the floor with a whetstone and an oily rag, working the edge — slow, practiced,
the one careful thing he does all day. He touches the flat of the blade to his forehead, then a marigold
to the Devi's feet, and says something too low to hear.

KANTA — sixties, a woman who stopped expecting things a long time ago — watches from the doorway, a steel
plate in her hand.

KANTA: फिर वही चाकू-छुरी। खाना ठंडा हो रहा है।

Ratan lays the sword down on the cloth, careful. Doesn't get up.

KANTA: (setting the plate down) पेंशन के काग़ज़ आ गए। दफ़्तर से फ़ोन था। दो साल।

A beat. Ratan looks at the bright edge on the cloth.

RATAN: दो साल।

KANTA: फिर क्या करोगे? सारा दिन यही घिसते रहोगे?

He picks up the plate, eats with his fingers, slow.

RATAN: (not looking up) तेरे हाथ का खाना। बस यही अच्छा बचा है ज़िंदगी में।

Kanta stops. For a second, something of the man she married.

KANTA: मक्खन मत लगा। ठंडा खा अब।

She goes. Ratan eats alone, the Devi on her shelf, the sword on the cloth.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 9 — THE THAN

**EXT. A JHUJHAR THAN AT THE EDGE OF THE FIELDS — DUSK**

A rough stone under a neem, smeared vermilion, a small trident, the ground worn smooth by generations of
feet. Past it, fields; past the fields, the lights of a big walled compound coming on.

An OLD CHARAN — eighties, one eye gone milky, the family's keeper of names — sits by the stone. A small
BOY, six, fidgets next to him. Ratan stands a little apart, a man visiting a grave he's ashamed to visit.

CHARAN: (to the boy, an old rhythm) यहीं गिरा था हमीर। सिर धड़ से अलग, और फिर भी सात को काटा। तलवार हाथ से नी
छूटी। यहीं, इसी माटी में।

The boy's eyes go wide. The Charan's good eye turns, slow, to Ratan.

CHARAN: उसी का खून है ये भी। पँवार। मालवा के राजा।

Ratan says nothing. The Charan looks at the soft belly, the khaki.

CHARAN: राजा का खून। (a tilt of the head at the lit compound) अपनी ही ज़मीन पे।

Ratan looks at the lights a long moment. Then crouches, touches the vermilion stone, the ground, his own
forehead.

RATAN: (quiet, to the stone) राम राम, बाबा।

He stands and goes, back toward the fields and the lights. The Charan watches him.

CHARAN: (to the boy) ये भी हमीर का खून है।

The boy watches the old inspector walk away.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 10 — THE GIFT

**INT. CBN OFFICE — DAY**

Yellowed files, a slow fan, a framed minister on the wall. SUPERINTENDENT MISHRA — fifties, neat, a man
who made his peace years ago — slides a thin file across the desk. Ratan opens it: a weighment register, a
dead grower, an accident.

He reads. His eye stops. He turns a page back, lays two columns side by side — the man's sanctioned area,
the gum declared off it.

RATAN: इतनी क्यारी से इतना माल नी निकले, साहब। आधा भी नी। किसी और का माल इसके नाम चढ़ा है।

Mishra doesn't look up.

MISHRA: तो?

RATAN: तो आदमी मर गया। हिसाब गड़बड़ है, और आदमी मर गया।

MISHRA: (a thin smile) पच्चीस साल हो गए, रतन। अब आके हिसाब सीखेगा? (taps the file) हादसा है। साइन कर।

A beat. Ratan looks at the thing his eye caught, that nobody wants. Then he signs. Pushes it back. Mishra
takes it without a glance, already on to the next thing.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 11 — THE FUNERAL

**EXT. A SMALL CREMATION GROUND — DAY**

A poor cremation. A pyre going. A handful of people, no more than eight. Ratan stands at the edge in plain
clothes. A low word going around tells it: an old CBN man, a "sahab" once, retired three months back, dead
of nothing in particular — the heart, the doda, the empty days. Nobody from the department came.

Beside Ratan, an OLD COLLEAGUE, also near the end, watches the fire.

COLLEAGUE: तीन महीने। रिटायर होके तीन महीने में गया। (a dry sound, not a laugh) चालीस साल नौकरी की। आज दस आदमी
भी नी आए।

Ratan watches the fire take the body.

COLLEAGUE: (not unkindly) तेरे कितने बचे, रतन? दो साल? (back to the fire) फिर तू। फिर मैं।

The pyre cracks and settles. Ratan stands in the smoke. His hand goes to his breast pocket — the note
still there. He takes the hand away.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 12 — DEVA'S HOUSE

**INT. DEVA'S FAMILY HOME — NIGHT**

A small warm house: a hand-pump in the yard, the smell of frying, a TV murmuring. AMMA kneads dough; BAPU
lies on a charpai; MANJU, eighteen, moves between them with the food. Deva comes in, still in uniform.

AMMA: आ गया। (brightly) सुन — गिरधारी का छोरा। मंजू की बात लगभग पक्की है। अच्छा घर है, पट्टा है।

BAPU: छोरा शहर में नौकरी देख रहा है।

Manju sets a bowl down, doesn't look up.

DEVA: मंजू से पूछा?

AMMA: (a beat, not unkindly) पूछने की क्या बात है बेटा। अच्छा रिश्ता है।

Manju's eyes come up to Deva's, then go back to the food. Deva looks at her, at the warm room, at the
thing nobody's asking. He doesn't push it. He sits, eats. The TV murmurs.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 13 — THE WOMEN

**EXT. A SMALL DEVI TEMPLE, A VILLAGE — NIGHT**

An aarti. Women, only women, a press of them — the bells, the smoke, a small fierce Devi behind the flame.
The men are somewhere else. This is the women's hour.

The faces in the diya-light: a girl of fourteen, already married by her bangles; a toothless old woman
with fierce eyes; a young mother with a baby on her hip and a bruise gone yellow under one eye.

Then one of them goes. A WOMAN in the middle of the press — her head starts to roll, her hair comes loose,
her body shaking. माता आ गई. She comes up on her knees, and the voice out of her is not hers — low,
cracked, saying things: half-words, a name, a grief, a curse. The women around her don't flinch; they hold
her, they let it come. This is known. This is what the hour is for.

At the edge of the press, SUGNA, fifties, a closed face. She watches the shaking woman. She does not
shake. She holds the end of her sari and watches.

The bell, the smoke, the woman with the goddess in her, the ones holding her down.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 14 — THE BODY

**EXT. POPPY FIELD, OUTSKIRTS OF AMARGARH — DAWN**

A poppy field before sunrise, cold enough to show breath. Scrapers already at the rows, lifting the grey
gum, not looking over. Where the rows meet the bund, a girl lies on her side in the dirt: sixteen, a
printed kameez stiff with dew, one slipper gone in the furrow.

The CBN jeep. Ratan gets out slow, the clipboard. Deva behind him. A knot of villagers at a distance; a
father a few rows off, not near her, not looking — waiting for the paper to be made.

Ratan crouches by the girl. A routine. He's written हादसा a hundred times; the pen is already moving
toward the box. He glances up at the father coming over — and stops.

He knows that face. Older, wrecked, but he knows it. BHERULAL.

A beat. Two men who haven't spoken in twenty years, a dead girl between them.

BHERULAL: (something crossing his face, then pushed down under the rest) इंस्पेक्टर साहब। (the official word,
so he doesn't have to say the other one) रात को बाहर निकली थी। अँधेरे में पाँव फिसल गया। गिर गई। काग़ज़ बना दो।

Ratan looks down at the girl. His sister's child. He hasn't seen her since she was small.

And his eye — the thing he can't switch off — goes over her. The slipper, too far in the furrow. The way
she's lying.

He doesn't write.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 15 — SUGNA

**INT. BHERULAL'S HOUSE, THE INNER ROOM — DAY**

Keening from inside — हाय लाड़ो, हाय म्हारी लाड़ो — the women. Ratan stands in the doorway of a house he was
never welcome in.

On the floor, a woman with the dead girl's clothes in her lap. SUGNA. His sister. Older, harder, the face
closed like a fist. She looks up. Sees him.

Twenty years in one look. She doesn't get up. She doesn't soften.

SUGNA: (low, flat, so the others don't hear) तू। (back down at the clothes) म्हारी बच्ची मर गई, तब आया।

Ratan has no word for it.

An AUNT bustles in, breaks it without knowing what she's breaking — साहब, बाहर पधारो, जनानी जगह है. Ratan
lets himself be turned toward the door. At it he stops.

RATAN: (quiet) ब्याह किसके साथ तय था इसका?

Sugna doesn't look up. It's the aunt who answers, low, ashamed.

AUNT: एक उमर वाले आदमी से। पैसे वाला। भेरूलाल पे उधार था।

The word उधार sits in the room. Ratan looks at his sister, the folded clothes in her lap. Then he goes.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 16 — THE BUYER

**INT. DHANRAJ'S HOUSE — DAY**

A moneyed grower's house, heavy furniture, a cooler roaring. DHANRAJ — sixty-odd, soft and heavy, gold on
three fingers. A YOUNG SERVANT GIRL, maybe fourteen, sets down tea and goes; his eyes follow her out.

Ratan stands across from him, official, the case in hand. Dhanraj is unbothered, expansive.

DHANRAJ: दुख की बात है। अच्छी छोरी थी। (a sigh) ब्याह की बात तो पक्की थी। अब... (a shrug)

RATAN: (flat) आपकी उमर क्या है? और छोरी सोलह की।

DHANRAJ: (a small smile) उमर का क्या है, साहब... (he doesn't finish it, reaches for his tea) भेरूलाल पे उधार
चढ़ा था। बहुत। (a shrug) बात चल रही थी। हो जाती। अब नी हुई।

The servant girl crosses the far end of the room again. Dhanraj's eyes go to her, then back. Ratan looks
at him, says nothing, and leaves.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 17 — ON HIS OWN GROUND

**EXT. RANA'S COMPOUND — DAY**

A big walled compound, new money: a cream Fortuner, a generator humming, young men with the soft menace of
being protected. This is the land. Near the gate, under a neem, a rough vermilion stone — the than, the
sthal — left standing because even new money won't move a Jhujhar's stone.

Ratan is left to wait in the forecourt. Standing. A long time. The compound's young men pass, glance at
the old khaki, don't bother to greet him.

A FIXER comes at last — the same easy contempt — doesn't take him inside, hands it over in the open.

FIXER: साब ने कहा — भेरूलाल वाली छोरी, हादसा। निपटा दो। ऊपर बात हो गई है। (already turning away) चाय-पानी पहुँच
जाएगा।

He's gone. Ratan stands alone in the forecourt. His hand, at his side, closes slow into a fist. He looks
across the forecourt at the stone under the neem.

Then he opens the fist. On his way out he stops at the stone, touches it — राम राम — and goes.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 18 — SIGN IT

**INT. CBN OFFICE, RATAN'S DESK — NIGHT**

Late, the office empty. Ratan under a single lamp, the girl's thin file open. The form — कारण मृत्यु: हादसा
— half its boxes already ticked. A pen. On the desk, the day's chai-paani folded in a small pile: the
fixer's, the pawnbroker's, all of it.

The boxes are mostly ticked already. A minute's work; he's done it a hundred times.

He looks at the file. A school photo clipped to the corner — the girl grinning crooked at a camera, a
board exam three weeks off. The slipper, too far in the furrow. Sugna's face: तब आया।

The pen hangs over the box. It doesn't move.

DEVA: (from the doorway, two teas in his hands) साहब... घर नी जाएँगे?

Ratan doesn't look up from the form.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 19 — THE RHYME

**INT./EXT. — NIGHT (INTERCUT)**

DEVA'S HOUSE: by lamplight, Manju holds a wedding bangle up to the light. The women fuss; Amma's happy
chatter runs under it. Manju turns the bangle, her face unreadable.

BHERULAL'S HOUSE: the dead girl's clothes, folded by the aunt into a bundle to be given away. Sugna on the
floor, not helping, the bundle filling with her daughter's things.

The bangle catches the light. The bundle fills.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 20 — THE TURN

**INT. CBN OFFICE, RATAN'S DESK — NIGHT**

The desk, the form, the pen over the box. The pile of chai-paani. Deva still in the doorway, the two teas
going cold in his hands.

Ratan looks at the box a long time.

Then he caps the pen. Sets it down. Opens a drawer and takes out a different form — a requisition. Starts
to write. Different boxes.

DEVA: (careful) साहब... वो हादसा वाला...?

RATAN: (writing, not looking up) पोस्टमॉर्टम। ज़िला अस्पताल। कल सुबह।

Deva goes still. The teas in his hands.

Ratan signs the requisition. His name.

He doesn't look up. Outside the window, the dark belt.

CUT TO BLACK.
