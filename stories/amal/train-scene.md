# अमल — The Train (full scene, as directed)

*Supersedes the compressed/sliced version. English sluglines and action with Devanagari dialogue,
1990s Indian Railways. This is the director's cut, where the dread is built through one suspicious
passenger and the horror is shown rather than withheld.*

---

**INT. RAILWAY COMPARTMENT — DAY**

A crowded 1990s general compartment, blue walls, barred windows, ceiling fans turning slow in the
heat. By the window sits a WOMAN, a baby wrapped to the crown in a faded green cloth held against her
shoulder, one hand flat and still on its back.

On the facing bench, SURESH (forties, tired) sits with his wife KAMLA, who has been talking since the
platform. His younger brother RAJESH (twenties) sprawls on the side berth.

KAMLA: मैंने कहा था ना सुबह वाली पकड़ लेते। अब बैठो इस लू में। और वो अचार वाला डब्बा तुमने ऊपर रखा कि नीचे? मुझे तो याद ही नहीं आ रहा।

SURESH: (looking out, half there) रखा है कहीं।

KAMLA: और जीजाजी को फ़ोन कर देना उतरते ही, वरना वो फिर मुँह फुलाए बैठे रहेंगे शादी भर।

Suresh nods at nothing. Kamla flaps a hand at the air near her face.

KAMLA: उई, ये मक्खियाँ कहाँ से आ गईं इतनी। (to the woman, friendly) बहन, तुम्हारे ही ऊपर भिनभिना रही हैं। कुछ मीठा रखा है क्या साथ में?

The woman shakes her head. She says nothing.

KAMLA: कितने महीने का है? लड़का है ना?

A small nod from the woman.

KAMLA: इतनी गरमी में इत्ता लपेट के रखा है बेचारे को, घुटन नहीं होगी उसको? ज़रा खोलो ऊपर से।

WOMAN: सो रहा है। ठीक है वो।

Kamla shrugs and turns back to her husband.

KAMLA: हाँ तो मैं कह रही थी, जीजी का घर स्टेशन से दस मिनट है, पर रिक्शे वाले दिन में लूटते हैं...

But Suresh is not listening to her anymore. He is watching the woman. The flat hand. The cloth drawn
up over the child's head. Something about it sits wrong in him.

The woman feels his eyes. She glances at him, then turns a few degrees toward the window, drawing the
bundle in, making herself smaller.

Suresh stands and stretches, slow, and as he does it he angles for a look down at the baby's face. The
woman rotates the bundle away from him, against her other shoulder.

He sits back down and leans across to Rajesh.

SURESH: (low) राजेश। इधर आ ज़रा।

Rajesh leans in.

SURESH: (low) उस औरत को देख। बच्चे को। इतनी गरमी है और उसने मुँह तक ढक रखा है। और एक बात बता — गाड़ी चली तब से, वो बच्चा एक बार हिला तेरे को?

RAJESH: सो रहा होगा भैया। छोटे बच्चे दिन भर सोते हैं। और ठंड भी जल्दी लग जाती है इनको, इसीलिए ढका होगा। तुम भी ना।

---

**EXT. SMALL STATION — DAY**

The train stands at a low platform. Suresh comes back along it to the barred window with a paper
packet of snacks and holds it up to Kamla inside.

SURESH: ये लो। पानी भी चाहिए?

KAMLA: अरे इतना सब। राजेश को भी देना थोड़ा। नमकीन लाया कि मीठा?

As Kamla takes the packet, Suresh leans in at the bars, and his eyes go past her, to the woman and
the bundle by the window.

SURESH: सब ठीक है ना बहन? बच्चा कैसा है? ... ज़रा एक बार चेहरा दिखा दो।

The woman draws the green cloth closed and turns the bundle in.

WOMAN: ठीक है वो। सो रहा है।

The guard's whistle goes. Suresh climbs back up.

---

**INT. RAILWAY COMPARTMENT — DAY**

Suresh sits, but he keeps looking down the carriage. He can't let it go.

SURESH: मैं कह रहा हूँ कुछ गड़बड़ है। दो बार बोला चेहरा दिखा दो, नहीं दिखाया। मेरा दिल नहीं मान रहा।

RAJESH: भैया छोड़ो। अकेली औरत है, छोटे बच्चे के साथ जा रही है, और तुम घूर-घूर के सवाल कर रहे हो। कोई तमाशा खड़ा कर देगा।

Kamla, who has caught the tail of it, leans in.

KAMLA: क्या हुआ? तुम दोनों किसके बारे में खुसुर-पुसुर कर रहे हो?

RAJESH: भैया को लग रहा है उस औरत के बच्चे में कुछ गड़बड़ है।

Kamla drops her voice to a hush, but the chatter is the same, only quieter now, leaning between them.

KAMLA: मुझे भी ना ठीक नहीं लगा था। मैंने खोलने को बोला तो खोला ही नहीं उसने। और बच्चा एक बार भी नहीं रोया, एक आवाज़ नहीं। और वो मक्खियाँ... राम राम।

RAJESH: तुम दोनों वहम कर रहे हो। ... पर हाँ, मक्खियाँ कुछ ज़्यादा ही हैं।

SURESH: अगले स्टेशन पे मैं सिपाही को बता दूँगा। चेक कर लें, बस।

Across the aisle the woman has gone very still. She is not looking at them, but she is listening to the
shape of it — three heads bent together, a voice that says सिपाही. Her hand presses flat on the bundle.

---

**INT. RAILWAY COMPARTMENT — DAY (NEXT STATION)**

The train slows and stops. Suresh and Rajesh get up and step down to the platform. The woman watches
them go. She leans to the window and looks out along the platform, and sees Suresh standing with a
CONSTABLE, talking, his hand pointing back at the carriage.

She gets up. She leaves her steel trunk where it is, gathers the bundle close, and moves quickly down
the aisle, through the vestibule, into the next bogie.

A moment later the CONSTABLE and a second one come up into the compartment behind Suresh. Kamla half
rises, pointing down the car.

KAMLA: उधर गई, उस तरफ़ वाले डिब्बे में।

---

**INT. RAILWAY COMPARTMENT — NEXT BOGIE — CONTINUOUS**

The constables reach the woman in the crowded aisle. She turns, the bundle high against her.

CONSTABLE: बहन जी, ज़रा रुकिए। दो मिनट बात करनी है।

WOMAN: (loud, agitated) क्या है? मैंने क्या किया? वो आदमी मेरे पीछे पड़ा है तब से। औरत अकेली देखी तो... मैं इसीलिए सीट छोड़ के आई।

She turns it on Suresh, who has come up behind the constables.

WOMAN: गंदी नज़र से देख रहा था मुझे। इसकी घरवाली बैठी है वहीं, पूछ लो। मैं हट के बैठी फिर भी पीछे आ गया।

The constable glances at Suresh, reassessing him. Suresh's face goes hot.

SURESH: मैडम, बस बच्चा एक बार दिखा दो। बस एक बार।

WOMAN: बच्चा सो रहा है मेरा।

CONSTABLE: (to the woman, easy) अरे जगाएँगे नहीं बहन जी। बस एक नज़र, और आप जाओ अपने रास्ते।

The woman is cornered. Slowly she folds the green cloth down from the top. A baby's face shows in the
gap, eyes closed, still. It could be a child asleep.

A beat. The constable lets his breath out and his shoulders drop.

CONSTABLE: सो ही तो रहा है। (turning on Suresh, irritated) क्यों बेकार में परेशान कर रहे हो भाई साहब? चलो।

The constables move to go. Suresh does not move.

SURESH: साहब, रुको। ठीक से देखो ज़रा। पूरा खोल के।

CONSTABLE: (sharp now, at Suresh) बस करो अब! बहन जी अकेली जा रही हैं, और तुम सुबह से पीछे पड़े हो। चलो हटो यहाँ से।

Suresh looks at the woman, at the bundle, at the flies. Then he steps in and takes hold of the child
and pulls it out of her arms.

WOMAN: (screaming) नहीं! मत छू उसको! छोड़! दे दे मुझे!

The green cloth comes away in his hands. The face that looked asleep does not change, and below it
the small body has been opened at the belly and stitched and split again at one end, and from the
split, packed and taped, the opium spills out onto the berth.

For one second nobody in the carriage moves.

Then the woman throws herself at him, clawing for the body, and the constables catch her and hold her,
and she is screaming, a sound with no words left in it, reaching past all of them for what they have
taken.

WOMAN: मेरा बच्चा! दे दो! मेरा बच्चा है वो!

Her screaming fills the carriage and goes on filling it as we pull away.

**CUT TO BLACK.**
