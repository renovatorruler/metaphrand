### 4. Kanta's speech register — a wife wouldn't use the तू / informal imperative.
- In this region/community, a woman addresses a man with the low form (तू, खा, लगा) **only if she's his
  older sister or his mother.** A **wife** speaks respectfully — आप / the **-इए** imperative.
- Fix: e.g. "मक्खन मत **लगाईए**, खाना **खाईए**" — NOT "मक्खन मत लगा, खाना खा". Shift **all of Kanta's lines**
  (Sc12) to the respectful आप/-इए register. (Ratan → Kanta stays informal, as a husband would. Sugna is
  his *sister* so her low/harsh form is fine; re-check every other female→male address in the accuracy
  pass, see #6.)

    in a Malwa town contradicts that.
  - **Ratan is a PARMAR (Paramara); Hamir is a CHAUHAN** — a different clan. A Parmar's ancestor cannot be
    Hamir Chauhan.
- Fix: rename the ancestor + the ring to a **Parmar/Paramara-of-Malwa** figure (the Paramaras *were* the
  kings of Malwa — Bhoj, Munja, etc.) and make the Jhujhar/saka consistent with a Malwa Parmar falling in
  or near the town. Re-verify the **Kuldevi** (currently Harsiddhi Mata — she *is* the Paramara/Ujjain
  kuldevi, so probably fine, but confirm she fits the lineage). Root cause = the ring name "Hamir" + the
  lineage details. (Touches Sc1 cold-open, Sc14 the than, Sc23 the estrangement, the ring/marker arc.)
