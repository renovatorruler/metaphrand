# अमल — The Train (recurring fragment; first appearance: Episode 2 cold open)

*Shown, never explained. No caption, no date, the woman's face never clear, no marker that fixes it in
time, so it reads as now, or long ago, or no time at all. The green cloth, the
flat hand, the hummed tune, the flies: these are the rhymes that return around Sugna in the present, so
the connection is made by the viewer and never by the script. Cut hard out of it. What lies under it is
in `BACKSTORY.md`, and is said nowhere.*

---

## COLD OPEN

**EXT. PASSENGER TRAIN, OPEN COUNTRY — DAY**

Flat country going past, a field of cut stubble that looks the same mile after mile. The train is an
old general compartment, windows barred, packed full, the heat sitting heavy inside it.

**INT. GENERAL COMPARTMENT — CONTINUOUS**

Bodies on wooden benches, tin trunks, a man asleep sitting up. By the barred window a WOMAN holds a
baby high against her shoulder, wrapped to the crown in a faded green cloth. We see her from the side
and from behind, never quite her face. One hand stays flat on the child's back. She hums, low, the
same few notes over and over, and lets the train rock her.

A vendor edges through with tea. The compartment goes on with its small business around her.

The WOMAN PASSENGER across the bench leans in, friendly.

WOMAN PASSENGER: कितने महीने का है?

The woman at the window does not turn. She draws the green cloth up an inch, over the child's head.

WOMAN AT WINDOW: सो रहा है।

The passenger smiles and settles back. The same stubble goes past the window for a long while, and the heat sits in the carriage and does not move.

A fly settles on the green cloth, and then another, and the woman does not lift her hand to brush them
off. Across the bench the friendly passenger has gone quiet and is watching the bundle, because the
child has not stirred once in a long time, not at the whistle, not at the vendor's shout, not at
anything. The unease spreads down the compartment, one face turning and then the
next, people looking and then carefully not looking at the woman by the window, who hums her few notes
and keeps her hand flat on the child's back and watches the stubble go past.

A man near the door gets up. A word goes from him toward the vestibule, and then forward, out of the
compartment.

**EXT. SMALL STATION — DAY**

The train stands at a low platform in the middle of nowhere. Two POLICEMEN come along it at a walk that
turns into a hurry, and swing up into the carriage.

**INT. GENERAL COMPARTMENT — CONTINUOUS**

The policemen push down the aisle through the standing crowd. The passengers press back into each
other to make the lane. We stay close on the woman at the window: the flat hand, the green cloth, the
small still shape inside it. A policeman reaches in for the bundle. Her hand holds it down for a moment,
then lets go.

We never see her face, and we never see inside the cloth.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

**TITLE: अमल**
