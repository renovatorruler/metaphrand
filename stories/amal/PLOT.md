# अमल — Season Plot (working, 6 episodes)

*Ground truth in `BIBLE.md`. The spine: the patta is bigger than a daughter's life — so big that the
only protection a powerless mother can give is to end her, and a father will bury his child's death to
keep the deal. The man investigating finds his own signature in the chain that forced her hand.*

---

## The truth (what happened to Leela)

Everyone in the belt diverts: deliver your quota of gum to the CBN, sell the surplus to the
black-market route for ten times the price. **Bherulal** (the father) diverted too much, so his
weighed deliveries run short. **Khare**, the CBN measurement officer, has covered the short weight for
a cut, signed off year after year by Inspector **Ratan Singh Panwar**. Now Bherulal is trapped two
ways: one audit and his patta is cancelled (civil death), and he owes **Daulatram**, the transporter
who fronted him money against the diverted gum.

The marriage is his escape hatch. Daulatram's nephew **Pratap**, fifty-five, has two pattas and a secured
route. Marry Leela to Pratap and the debt clears, Bherulal's land folds under **Rana**, the MLA who
controls who gets a patta at all, and everyone is safe. Leela is not a bride. She is the contract.

What Bherulal does not weigh is what his wife knows. **Sugna**, the mother, knows Pratap, and knows
his first wife died young under a "snakebite" cover. She lived her own version of that life. When
Leela works it out and runs for the bus and is caught and dragged back and locked in, with the men
coming at dawn and every door bolted, Sugna opens the only one she can. In the night she gives her
daughter **amal** — the opium, the belt's own poison, the drink poured at weddings and at funerals.
Whether Leela, broken, half-chose it with her is the thing no one will ever be sure of.

Bherulal believes it was suicide — girls do take the doda to escape these marriages — and buries it as
a fall, to protect the alliance. He never learns his wife's hand was in it. Sugna carries it alone.

---

## The misdirection (why it is a real mystery, not a clone)

Everyone hunts the father. Ratan builds the case straight at Bherulal: the patta motive, the cover,
the hard man who chose the deal. The audience goes with him. Nobody looks at Sugna — because in this
economy women are objects, not actors, and the lens that runs the belt cannot imagine the mother as
the hand. Ratan cracks it only when he finally sees her as a person who could act. The system's
blindness to its women is what hides the killer in plain sight.

---

## The mirror (Ratan's arc)

Ratan is the system, not its enemy: he signs the short weights for his cut. The wound the bible
plants: his own family's patta was cancelled a generation ago over a "short weight" that Rana's father
engineered to grab their land. He has spent thirty years becoming the exact machine that ate his
father, and it is now eating girls like Leela.

- **Doorway 1 (he can't look away):** in the field at dawn, he stops the lancing (Ep1).
- **Midpoint (the mirror):** the marriage was a debt settled with a daughter, and the debt exists
  because men like him let the weights run short. The case is his own family's ruin, running again.
- **Doorway 2 (no way back):** the thread turns, impossibly, toward Sugna — and he finds his own
  signatures in the chain that trapped Bherulal and forced her hand. He cannot tell the truth without
  indicting himself, and the truth now means a grieving mother hangs while the men who built the trap
  walk.
- **End:** he refuses to hand them the mother. He burns the machine instead — the diversion racket,
  Khare, Daulatram, his own sign-offs — and cracks the cause that can never be charged as her murder.
  He shields Sugna and lets the death stand as the half-suicide it was. Justice stops being "book the
  killer" and becomes "burn the thing that made her one." Rana stays standing; the racket breaks; Sugna
  lives in her own hell; Ratan walks out with nothing, clean for the first time in thirty years.

---

## The weave (so it is not a monorail)

- **B-story, Deva and his sister.** Constable **Deva**, twenty-four, works the case and goes home to
  his own family arranging a match for his sister **Sunita** — the same machinery, gentler, in a house
  we like. As the truth surfaces, the rhyme lands on him, and at the end he refuses to let Sunita be
  "seen." The cycle breaks in one house: the season's only clean light.
- **The mother, Sugna.** The tragic core, not a late reveal. Present and silent the whole season, the
  truth hidden in plain sight because no one — not the belt, not Ratan — looks at a woman as someone
  who could do anything. On a second pass every quiet beat reads differently.
- **Leela, in retrospect.** We never treat her as a prop. We learn her through what she left behind:
  the hidden bus money, the friend she told, the fight she put up in the dirt. She resisted to the end.

## Episode breakdown

1. **तौल (The Weighing).** The body in the field; the harvest won't stop; Ratan stops it and decides to
   look. The family wants it closed before the groom's people arrive. *(Doorway 1.)*
2. **The Viewing.** The groom's people come anyway and keep the alliance alive. The doctor finds opium
   in her — which everyone is glad to read as suicide.
3. **Short Weight.** Ratan and Deva pull the diversion thread; Khare's covered weights, Daulatram's
   route. Rana pushes from above, through Ratan's own CBN superior: close it as suicide, move on.
4. **The Debt.** The marriage decoded — Leela was clearing Bherulal's debt and sealing his land to
   Rana's network. The case points hard at the father. *(Midpoint: the mirror.)* Deva's family names a
   boy for Sunita.
5. **The First Wife.** Pratap's dead first wife surfaces, and the same cover-story; the pattern. The
   thread turns, impossibly, toward Sugna, and Ratan finds his own signature in the chain. *(Doorway 2.)*
6. **अमल.** The truth: Sugna's hand, and why. Ratan refuses to give them the mother, burns the machine,
   shields her, and gives up everything. Bherulal's deal is dead; Deva stops Sunita's viewing; the
   death stays an official suicide. Ratan walks out clean and with nothing.

---

## Held cards (shown, never told)

- **Amal as the weapon** is the title's whole payoff — the economy's own poison as a mother's mercy.
  Shown in image and fact, never spelled out as symbolism.
- **Sugna's act is mercy and love, never madness or honour** — seeded all season, a tragedy, not a
  twist. If it ever reads as a gotcha, it has failed.
- The Jungian frame (the case as Ratan's mirror) and Rana's untouchability are structure, never a line.
