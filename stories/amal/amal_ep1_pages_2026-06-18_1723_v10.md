## SCENE 1 — FLASH-FORWARD (the promise)

FADE IN:

**EXT. — NIGHT**

No establishing. Close, fast, firelit:

A scarred old hand, slick to the wrist with blood, closes around the worn hilt of a talwar — the blade
old, nicked a hundred times over, a heavy gold signet rubbed almost smooth on one finger. It drives
forward into someone we never see, and a shape folds away into the dark with a wet grunt. Off to the side
there's fire, and men coming out of it. The old hand swings the sword to meet them, again and again.
For half a breath the frame pulls wider onto an old man on his feet on dark ground — the smoke behind him
lit saffron, the blood gone to his elbow, more of them closing in — and he does not go down.

CUT TO BLACK.

## SCENE 3 — DEVA ARRIVES

**EXT./INT. AMARGARH CBN POST — DAY**

A low government building gone the colour of dust, the board out front with its paint flaking, a CBN jeep
up on bricks with one wheel missing. DEVA climbs down from a shared tempo with a steel trunk on his
shoulder, his uniform still new, and stands looking at the place.

Inside, a MUNSHI sits behind a register under a slow fan, a glass of tea going cold by his elbow. He
doesn't look up.

DEVA: कांस्टेबल देवा। शहर से ट्रांसफर। रतन सिंह पँवार साहब को रिपोर्ट करना है।

The munshi turns a page.

MUNSHI: साहब राउंड पे हैं।

DEVA: कब तक आएँगे?

MUNSHI: (a shrug) आ जाएँगे। (he looks at Deva now — the new uniform, the trunk) शहर से आया है? यहाँ काहे?

Deva doesn't have an answer ready. He looks around the post — the dead jeep, the cold tea, a calendar two
years out of date on the wall. None of it matches the stories.

DEVA: सुना था... पँवार साहब के नीचे आदमी बन जाता है।

The munshi looks at him a second, makes a sound that isn't quite a laugh, and goes back to his register.

MUNSHI: बैठ जा। चाय पिएगा?

Deva sets the trunk down and sits to wait.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 5 — THE ROUTE

**EXT. A DIRT TRACK BEHIND THE FIELDS — NIGHT**

Two trucks stand with their lights off, men loading sacks under a tarp by torchlight. A FIXER — older than
the one in the Bolero, a ledger in his hand — walks the line, counting. Ratan stands by his jeep with Deva
a step behind, watching the loading.

The fixer comes over and tears a slip from a pad.

FIXER: सब गिना हुआ है, साहब। आप बस...

Ratan takes the slip, glances at it, puts it in his pocket. He doesn't count the trucks.

DEVA: (low, to Ratan) इतना माल... ये सब जाता कहाँ है?

RATAN: (not looking at him) पूछना मत।

The first truck pulls out slow, no lights until it reaches the road. Then the second. Ratan watches them
go.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 6 — THE TIP

**EXT. A DIRT TURNOUT OFF THE FIELD ROAD — DAY**

A man's body lies in the dust at the edge of the field road, a cycle bent under him and the flies already
at work. Crouched over it on one knee, not really looking, is INSPECTOR RATAN SINGH PANWAR — late fifties
and heavy, his khaki gone soft at the seams, a grey moustache nobody's twirled in years — writing on a
clipboard he's balanced against his thigh. A step behind him stands Constable DEVA, twenty-four, the
uniform still creased from the shop, watching everything the older man does.

A Bolero idles on the road, and as its window comes down a YOUNG FIXER in sunglasses leans into the gap
without getting out.

FIXER: साहब, निपट गया?

Ratan doesn't look up.

RATAN: ट्रैक्टर ने मारा होगा। रात का मामला है।

FIXER: हाँ हाँ, वही।

A folded note flicks out the window and lands by Ratan's knee in the dust.

FIXER: चाय-पानी रख लो, चाचा।

The Bolero is pulling away before the word has even landed, dragging a tail of dust across the body.

Ratan looks at the note a moment, then picks it up without counting it and tucks it into his breast
pocket, pushing himself up off his knee slow with one hand braced on the thigh.

Deva's eyes go to the pocket, then to the body, before he catches himself.

DEVA: इसका पोस्टमॉर्टम...?

RATAN: (already turning away) ट्रैक्टर ने मारा है, देवा। पोस्टमॉर्टम में ट्रैक्टर थोड़ी निकलेगा।

He walks off toward his own jeep, and Deva lingers by the body a second before coming after him.

DEVA: साहब... एक बात पूछूँ?

RATAN: पूछ।

DEVA: शहर की पोस्टिंग छोड़ के... मैं खुद आपके नीचे आया, साहब। लोग बोलते थे — रतन सिंह पँवार के नीचे लग जा... आदमी बन जाएगा।

Ratan stops at the jeep door without turning around, and something moves across his face, there and gone,
like a fish under ice.

RATAN: (gets in) बन जाएगा।

He pulls the door shut, and through the windshield there's the body in the dust, the flies, the patch of
road where the Bolero stood. Deva climbs in the other side without a word.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 7 — THE BELT

**EXT. FIELD ROAD, AMARGARH BELT — DAY**

The jeep moves along the field road with Deva driving and Ratan in the passenger seat, his window down.
The country goes by — poppy fields run to the horizon with the pods scored, men and women bent over the
rows scraping the grey gum before the sun can take the dew. They pass a government weighment shed with a
queue of steel lotas outside it and a hand-painted board over the door: केंद्रीय नारकोटिक्स ब्यूरो.

Deva takes all of it in; Ratan watches none of it.

They slow at the edge of the bazaar, where a kettle steams over a tea stall and men sit along a bench. A
BOY of fifteen in a pressed shirt peels a note off a roll thick as his fist, pays for two plates of poha
and a glass of tea, and doesn't wait for the change. Deva's head turns to follow him, the roll vanishing back into his pocket.

DEVA: (low) इसके पास इतने पैसे...?

RATAN: किसान का छोरा है।

Deva turns it over while Ratan is already looking past him, at the stall, at the old man slumped against
its leg.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 8 — THE AMLI

**EXT. ROADSIDE TEA STALL — CONTINUOUS**

Ratan eases down onto the bench. He's sat here most evenings for years. Without being asked, the
stall-owner sets a glass in front of him — not tea but a cloudy grey water, doda, poppy-husk — and Ratan
drinks it down.

Against the stall's leg sits an OLD AMLI. The trade hollowed him out years ago, and the bazaar walks
around him now without looking. Without looking, Ratan tips a little from his own glass
into the old man's cracked cup, and the shaking hands take it.

Deva stays standing, apart, watching his inspector drink the grey water.

A SHOPBOY hauling crates kicks the amli's leg out of the way as he passes, casual about it, just clearing
his path.

SHOPBOY: हट, अमली।

Ratan doesn't raise his voice. He barely comes off the bench — just enough to catch the boy's wrist as he
passes. He holds it, his eyes coming up until the boy's drop.

RATAN: (quiet, to the stall-owner, not letting go) इसको खाना दे। मेरे खाते में।

He lets the wrist go and the boy is gone, fast. The owner ladles poha onto a leaf for the amli while Ratan
settles back and drinks.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 9 — THE TEACHER

**INT. RATAN'S JEEP — DAY (MOVING)**

The jeep on the road, Ratan driving, Deva in the passenger seat. Deva has been quiet a long time. He's
been turning something over since the body.

DEVA: साहब... वो जो आदमी मरा था सुबह। सच में ट्रैक्टर?

Ratan drives.

RATAN: तू क्या समझता है?

DEVA: (careful) मुझे लगा किसी ने मारा होगा।

RATAN: तो?

DEVA: तो रिपोर्ट...

RATAN: (flat) रिपोर्ट में ट्रैक्टर। (a beat) यहाँ हर महीने चार-पाँच लाशें गिरती हैं, देवा। तू हर एक का खून
निकालेगा? किसके खिलाफ — पता भी है किसके खिलाफ?

Deva says nothing.

RATAN: नहीं पता। अच्छा है। यहाँ टिकना है तो दो बात याद रख — देखना मत, पूछना मत। जो हाथ में आए रख ले, और शाम को
ज़िंदा घर चला जा।

A long silence. Deva looks out at the fields going by.

DEVA: (quiet) लोग कहते थे आप अलग हो।

Ratan doesn't answer. He keeps his eyes on the road and drives.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 10 — THE PAWNBROKER

**INT. SETH'S PAWNSHOP, AMARGARH BAZAAR — DAY**

The shop is cramped, a barred window and shelves of other people's lives: brass pots, a transistor,
bangles, a stilled sewing machine. SETH, fifties, soft and careful, counts out a stack of notes and slides
it across the glass-topped counter; Ratan pockets it without counting. Out through the barred window
the jeep waits with Deva beside it, well out of earshot.

The Seth pours two teas — real tea — and pushes one across. Then he asks it, easy:

SETH: हमीर देखोगे, साहब? रखा है, सँभाल के। जब बोलो।

Ratan's glass stops halfway to his mouth and stays there a beat too long, and he looks at the tea instead
of at the Seth.

RATAN: नी।

SETH: (a soft shrug, no push) जैसी मर्ज़ी। (a beat) ब्याज चढ़ रहा है, साहब। बढ़ता ही जाएगा।

Ratan drinks his tea, and outside the barred window the bazaar goes on.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 11 — THE POWERFUL

**INT. RANA'S OFFICE, A GRAIN-TRADING FRONT — DAY**

A clean office behind a grain-trading sign, the air-conditioning humming, a garlanded photo of RANA on the
wall — a heavy man in a white kurta. A MANAGER counts Ratan's slips and a bundle of notes at the desk.
Ratan stands. He isn't offered a chair.

MANAGER: (not looking up) इस महीने दो केस ज़्यादा हैं। ऊपर पूछताछ हुई थी।

RATAN: हादसे थे।

MANAGER: (a thin smile) हादसे ही रखना। (he slides a smaller bundle back across the desk) रानाजी खुश हैं तेरे
काम से। खुश रहें, यही ठीक है।

Ratan takes the bundle. Behind the manager, through a glass partition, the real RANA sits on a phone,
laughing at something. He doesn't look up. Nobody in the room looks at Ratan.

He pockets the money and goes.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 12 — THE RITES, AND KANTA

**INT. RATAN'S HOUSE — EVENING**

It's a small, plain house. In one corner a low wooden shelf holds a framed Harsiddhi Mata in vermilion, a
dried marigold, a steel diya; across two pegs above it lies a talwar, its scabbard worn soft, and when
Ratan draws an inch of the blade the steel underneath is bright and sharp.

He sits cross-legged on the floor with a whetstone and an oily rag, working the edge — slow, practiced. He touches the flat of the blade to his forehead, then a marigold
to the Devi's feet, and says something too low to hear.

KANTA watches from the doorway, sixties, a steel plate in her hand.

KANTA: फिर वही चाकू-छुरी। खाना ठंडा हो रहा है।

Ratan lays the sword down on the cloth, careful with it, and doesn't get up.

KANTA: (setting the plate down) पेंशन के काग़ज़ आ गए। दफ़्तर से फ़ोन आया था, बोल रहे थे दो साल में रिटायर हो जाओगे।

For a moment he only looks at the bright edge lying on the cloth.

RATAN: दो साल।

KANTA: फिर क्या करोगे? सारा दिन यही घिसते रहोगे?

He picks up the plate and eats with his fingers, slow.

RATAN: (not looking up) तेरे हाथ का खाना।

Kanta stops and looks at him a second.

KANTA: मक्खन मत लगा। ठंडा खा अब।

She goes, and Ratan eats alone, the Devi on her shelf and the sword on the cloth beside him.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 13 — THE DODA, ALONE

**INT. RATAN'S HOUSE — NIGHT**

Late, the house dark, Kanta asleep. Ratan sits alone by a low light with a brass tumbler and a small cloth
bundle. He unties the bundle — dark poppy husk — and works it into the water with two fingers, slow, a
thing his hands do on their own. He drinks it down.

Kanta appears in the doorway and looks at him a moment. She has seen this many nights. She goes back to
bed without a word.

Ratan sits a long while with his eyes half shut, the Devi on her shelf and the sharp sword on its pegs
above him.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 14 — THE THAN

**EXT. A JHUJHAR THAN AT THE EDGE OF THE FIELDS — DUSK**

A rough stone stands under a neem, smeared with vermilion, a small trident planted beside it and the
ground around it worn smooth by generations of feet. Past it lie the fields, and past the fields the
lights of a big walled compound are coming on.

An OLD CHARAN sits by the stone — eighties, one eye gone milky, the family's keeper of names — with a
small BOY of six fidgeting beside him. Ratan stands a little apart from them both.

CHARAN: (to the boy, an old rhythm) यहीं गिरा था हमीर। सिर धड़ से अलग, और फिर भी सात को काटा। तलवार हाथ से नी
छूटी। यहीं, इसी माटी में।

The boy's eyes go wide, and the Charan's good eye turns, slow, to Ratan.

CHARAN: उसी का खून है ये भी। पँवार। मालवा के राजा।

Ratan says nothing, and the Charan takes in the soft belly, the khaki.

CHARAN: राजा का खून। (a tilt of the head at the lit compound) अपनी ही ज़मीन पे।

Ratan looks at the lights a long moment, then crouches to touch the vermilion stone, the ground, his own
forehead.

RATAN: (quiet, to the stone) राम राम, बाबा।

He gets to his feet and walks back toward the fields, the lights. The Charan watches him go.

CHARAN: (to the boy) ये भी हमीर का खून है।

The boy watches the old inspector walk away.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 15 — THE SACRED

**EXT. A KAAL BHAIRAV SHRINE — NIGHT**

The aarti — drums, a fierce black image smeared with vermilion under silver-foil eyes, men pressed into
the smoke, liquor poured to the god. The pujari swings the flame. Ratan stands at the back, out of the
press.

He puts a few folded notes in the box — the day's chai-paani — and joins his hands. He doesn't ask the god
for anything. He stands in the noise and the smoke with the rest of them.

Coming down the line with the flame, the pujari reaches Ratan, marks his forehead with ash, and says the
old blessing for the family.

PUJARI: जय भैरव। पँवार के बेटे।

Ratan takes the ash and folds his hands again. The aarti moves on down the line. He stands in the smoke a
while, then goes.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 16 — THE GIFT

**INT. CBN OFFICE — DAY**

The room is all yellowed files and a slow fan, a framed minister on the wall. SUPERINTENDENT MISHRA,
fifties and neat, slides a thin file across the desk, and Ratan opens it onto a weighment register, a dead
grower, an accident.

He reads, and his eye stops; he turns a page back and lays two columns side by side — the man's sanctioned
plot against the gum declared off it.

RATAN: इतनी क्यारी से इतना माल नी निकले, साहब। आधा भी नी। किसी और का माल इसके नाम चढ़ा है।

Mishra doesn't look up.

MISHRA: तो?

RATAN: तो आदमी मर गया। हिसाब गड़बड़ है, और आदमी मर गया।

MISHRA: (a thin smile) पच्चीस साल हो गए, रतन। अब आके हिसाब सीखेगा? (taps the file) हादसा है। साइन कर।

Ratan looks at the file a moment longer, then signs it and pushes it back. Mishra takes it without a
glance, already on to the next thing.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 17 — THE MACHINE, TOTAL

**INT. AMARGARH CBN POST — DAY**

A normal morning. A GROWER waits on the bench with a tin of gum and an envelope; the munshi takes the
envelope, weighs the tin with a thumb resting on the scale, writes down less than the tin weighs.
At the door another CONSTABLE pockets something from a trucker. The fan turns. The queue moves.

Deva stands by the wall and watches it run. Every hand in the room takes something — the munshi, the
constable, the man at the door. Nobody hides it.

The munshi passes Deva on his way to the back and, without breaking step, tucks a few notes into Deva's
shirt pocket.

MUNSHI: नए आदमी का हिस्सा।

He's gone before Deva can answer. Deva stands there with the notes against his chest, not knowing what to
do with them.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 18 — THE FUNERAL

**EXT. A SMALL CREMATION GROUND — DAY**

A poor cremation, the pyre already going, no more than eight mourners. Ratan
stands at the edge in plain clothes, and the low talk says the dead man was an old CBN man, a
sahab once, retired three months back, dead now of nothing in particular — the doda, the empty days.
Nobody from the department came.

Beside Ratan, an OLD COLLEAGUE, also near the end, watches the fire.

COLLEAGUE: तीन महीने। रिटायर होके तीन महीने में गया। (a dry sound, not a laugh) चालीस साल नौकरी की। आज दस आदमी
भी नी आए।

Ratan watches the fire take the body.

COLLEAGUE: (not unkindly) तेरे कितने बचे, रतन? दो साल? (back to the fire) फिर तू। फिर मैं।

The pyre cracks and settles, and Ratan stands in the smoke of it. His hand goes to his breast pocket,
where the note still is, and then he takes the hand away again.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 19 — THE RECKONING

**EXT. THE CREMATION GROUND — DUSK**

The pyre burned down to embers, the mourners gone. Ratan sits in his parked jeep without starting it,
watching the last of the smoke go up thin and grey.

He takes the day's chai-paani out of his pocket and looks at the folded notes in his hand. He looks at
them a long moment, then puts them back.

His thumb finds the band of bare skin on his finger where the ring used to be. He looks at that too. Then
he starts the jeep and drives.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 20 — DEVA'S HOUSE

**INT. DEVA'S FAMILY HOME — NIGHT**

It's a small, warm house — a hand-pump in the yard, the smell of frying, a TV murmuring somewhere. AMMA is
kneading dough, BAPU lying back on a charpai, MANJU at eighteen moving between them with the food, when
Deva comes in still in his uniform.

AMMA: आ गया। (brightly) सुन — गिरधारी का छोरा। मंजू की बात लगभग पक्की है। अच्छा घर है, पट्टा है।

BAPU: छोरा शहर में नौकरी देख रहा है।

Manju sets a bowl down and doesn't look up.

DEVA: मंजू से पूछा?

AMMA: (a beat) पूछने की क्या बात है बेटा। अच्छा रिश्ता है।

Manju's eyes come up to meet Deva's, then drop back to the food. He looks at her, then around the warm
room, and lets it go; he sits down and eats while the TV murmurs on.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 21 — DEVA & MANJU

**INT. DEVA'S HOUSE, THE BACK STEP — NIGHT**

The others asleep. Deva and MANJU on the back step under a low light, she stitching something, he still
half in uniform.

DEVA: गिरधारी का लड़का। तूने देखा है उसको?

MANJU: (not looking up) फोटो में।

DEVA: और?

Manju shrugs and keeps stitching.

DEVA: मंजू। तू मना करना चाहे तो...

MANJU: (quiet) तो क्या? (she looks at him) माँ-बापू को क्या बोलूँगी — कि भैया की तनख्वाह में चार लोग हैं, और मैं
पाँचवीं?

Deva has no answer.

MANJU: (softer, back to the stitching) अच्छा घर है। पट्टा है। ठीक है।

She keeps stitching. Deva sits with her a while, then goes in.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 22 — THE GIRL, ALIVE

**EXT. BHERULAL'S HOUSE, THE YARD — DAY**

LEELA — sixteen, quick and sharp-eyed, the girl we will find in the furrow — grinds masala at a stone in
the yard while her mother SUGNA hangs the washing. A schoolbook lies open on the ledge; Leela glances at
it between turns of the stone.

BHERULAL comes through, agitated, half to Sugna and half to himself.

BHERULAL: धनराज ने फिर आदमी भेजा था। उधार माफ़, और ऊपर से कुछ। बस हाँ करनी है।

Leela's hands stop on the stone.

LEELA: मेरा पेपर तीन हफ़्ते में है, बापू।

BHERULAL: (tired, trapped, not unkind) पेपर से पेट नहीं भरता, बेटा।

LEELA: (flat — past pleading) मैं उस बूढ़े के साथ नहीं जाऊँगी।

Sugna doesn't turn from the washing. Bherulal looks at his daughter, at the trap he's in, and has nothing
to say.

Leela goes back to the stone and grinds harder.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 23 — THE ESTRANGEMENT

**INT. THE OLD PANWAR HOUSE — DAY (FLASHBACK, ~TWENTY YEARS EARLIER)**

A bigger house than Ratan's now, but men are carrying furniture out of it and papers are being signed at a
table by the door. A YOUNGER RATAN, in a crisp uniform, stands apart — not signing, not stopping it. A
YOUNGER SUGNA, fierce, a baby on her hip, takes his arm.

SUGNA: तू वर्दी में खड़ा है! कुछ कर! ये हमारी ज़मीन है।

RATAN: (low, not meeting her eyes) सुगना। जिनके पीछे ये लोग हैं, उनसे नहीं लड़ सकते।

SUGNA: नहीं लड़ सकते, या तू नहीं लड़ेगा?

Ratan says nothing.

SUGNA: (stepping back, quiet and final) कायर। (she looks at him as if seeing him new) बाबा की तलवार रखी है
तेरे पास। उससे सब्ज़ी काट।

She walks out with the baby. Younger Ratan stands in the emptying house while the papers are signed behind
him, and doesn't move.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 24 — THE WOMEN

**EXT. A SMALL DEVI TEMPLE, A VILLAGE — NIGHT**

An aarti is underway, women and only women, a press of them in the bells and the smoke with a small fierce
Devi behind the flame. The men are somewhere else.

In the diya-light the faces come and go: a girl of fourteen already married by her bangles, a toothless
old woman with fierce eyes, a young mother with a baby on her hip and a bruise gone yellow under one eye.

Then the young mother goes. Her head rolls back, her hair coming loose, and another woman lifts the baby
off her hip. माता आ गई। She comes up onto her knees, and the voice out of her is not hers — low, cracked,
saying things nobody answers. The women around her don't flinch; they hold her and let it come.

We stay a moment on the bell and the smoke, on the bruised woman with the goddess in her and the others
holding her down.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 25 — THE DANGER BENEATH

**EXT. A SMALL GROWER'S FARMSTEAD — DUSK**

Rana's men at a poor grower's place — the grower down in the dirt, two men over him, a third holding the
grower's young son back by the collar. The ENFORCER doing the talking isn't shouting. He's almost gentle.

ENFORCER: माल कम था इस बार। (he crouches by the grower) रानाजी समझते हैं, मौसम खराब था। पर हिसाब हिसाब होता है।

He nods to the two men. They do something brief to the grower's hand — off the frame, only the sound of it
and the grower's cry and the boy screaming against the third man's grip. The enforcer stands and wipes his
palms.

ENFORCER: (to the boy, gently) रो मत। बाप से सीख ले।

They walk to their vehicle. Down the road, Ratan's jeep slows. He has seen it. The jeep idles a second.
Then he drives on.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 26 — MANJU, PRESSED

**INT. DEVA'S HOUSE — DAY**

The groom's people visiting — GIRDHARI, his soft city-aspirant son, two relatives — tea and the good
plates out. AMMA radiant, BAPU formal. MANJU brought out in a good sari with a tray of tea, eyes down. The
groom's mother lifts Manju's chin to see her face, turns it, approves.

GROOM'S MOTHER: (to Amma) लड़की ठीक है। रंग साफ़ है।

Amma beams. Manju holds the tray. In the corner, in his uniform, Deva watches his sister be looked over,
and says nothing.

They settle the date over tea. Manju sets the tray down and goes back inside.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 27 — THE BODY

**EXT. POPPY FIELD, OUTSKIRTS OF AMARGARH — DAWN**

A poppy field before sunrise, cold enough to show breath, the scrapers already out at the rows lifting the
grey gum and not looking over. Where the rows meet the bund a girl lies on her side in the dirt — sixteen,
a printed kameez stiff with dew, one slipper gone somewhere in the furrow.

The CBN jeep pulls up and Ratan gets out slow with the clipboard, Deva behind him. A knot of villagers
stands off at a distance, and a few rows from the body a father waits — not near her, not looking at her,
only waiting for the paper to be made.

Ratan crouches by the girl. He's written हादसा a hundred times; the pen finds the box on its own. Then he
glances up at the father coming over, and stops.

The face is older now, and wrecked, but he knows it — BHERULAL.

For a moment neither of them moves. They haven't spoken in twenty years. The girl lies in the dirt
between them.

BHERULAL: इंस्पेक्टर साहब। रात को बाहर निकली थी। अँधेरे में पाँव फिसल गया। गिर गई। काग़ज़ बना दो।

Ratan looks down at the girl — his sister's child, though he hasn't seen her since she was small.

And his eye travels over her — the slipper lying too far in the furrow, the wrong angle of the way she's
fallen.

He doesn't write.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 28 — THE BODY, PROCEDURE

**EXT. POPPY FIELD — DAWN (CONTINUOUS)**

Around the body now: a few villagers at a distance, a local CONSTABLE with a register, Bherulal hovering,
the light coming up. The constable holds the register out to Ratan with the pen ready, the boxes waiting
for हादसा.

CONSTABLE: साहब, लिख दूँ? गिरना-विरना? पंचनामा करके निपटा देते हैं।

Ratan crouches by the girl and doesn't take the pen. He looks at the slipper, the angle she's lying at,
the dew on her clothes.

BHERULAL: (low, urgent) साहब। बना दो। घर में मातम है।

Everyone waits for the same word. Ratan stands up slow. He doesn't take the register.

RATAN: बॉडी को हाथ मत लगाओ।

The constable blinks.

CONSTABLE: साहब?

RATAN: (not loud) सुना नहीं? हाथ मत लगाओ।

At the edge of it, Deva watches the old man refuse the easy word for the first time, and doesn't
understand it yet.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 29 — SUGNA

**INT. BHERULAL'S HOUSE, THE INNER ROOM — DAY**

From inside comes the women's keening — हाय लाड़ो, हाय म्हारी लाड़ो — and Ratan stands in the doorway of a
house he was never welcome in.

On the floor sits a woman with the dead girl's clothes gathered in her lap — SUGNA, his sister, older and
harder than he remembers, her face closed like a fist. She looks up and sees him.

She looks at him a long moment. She doesn't get up, and she doesn't soften.

SUGNA: (low, flat, so the others don't hear) तू। (back down at the clothes) म्हारी बच्ची मर गई, तब आया।

An AUNT bustles in and breaks the moment — साहब, बाहर पधारो, जनानी जगह है — and Ratan lets himself be steered toward the door, where he stops.

RATAN: (quiet) ब्याह किसके साथ तय था इसका?

Sugna doesn't look up, and it's the aunt who answers instead, low and ashamed.

AUNT: एक उमर वाले आदमी से। पैसे वाला। भेरूलाल पे उधार था।

Ratan looks at his sister a long moment, at the folded clothes in her lap, and then he goes.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 30 — THE BUYER

**INT. DHANRAJ'S HOUSE — DAY**

It's a moneyed grower's house, heavy furniture and a cooler roaring in the window. DHANRAJ sits in the
middle of it, sixty-odd, soft and heavy, gold on three fingers, and when a YOUNG SERVANT GIRL of maybe
fourteen sets down the tea and goes, his eyes follow her out of the room.

Ratan stands across from him, official, the case in his hand, and Dhanraj is unbothered, expansive.

DHANRAJ: दुख की बात है। अच्छी छोरी थी। (a sigh) ब्याह की बात तो पक्की थी। अब... (a shrug)

RATAN: (flat) आपकी उमर क्या है? और छोरी सोलह की।

DHANRAJ: (a small smile) उमर का क्या है, साहब... (he doesn't finish it, reaches for his tea) भेरूलाल पे उधार
चढ़ा था। बहुत। (a shrug) बात चल रही थी। हो जाती। अब नी हुई।

The servant girl crosses the far end of the room again, and Dhanraj's eyes follow her there and back.
Ratan looks at him a moment, then leaves.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 31 — THE PRESSURE

**INT. CBN OFFICE, MISHRA'S ROOM — DAY**

Mishra behind the desk, not friendly now. Ratan stands.

MISHRA: भेरूलाल वाली लड़की। तूने पंचनामा रोक दिया।

RATAN: पोस्टमॉर्टम चाहिए उसका।

MISHRA: (almost laughing) पोस्टमॉर्टम? पच्चीस साल में एक नहीं भेजा तूने। आज क्यों?

Ratan doesn't answer.

MISHRA: (quieter, the threat under it) ऊपर से फ़ोन आ चुका है। केस बंद। हादसा। (he slides a paper across) साइन
करके घर जा। दो साल बचे हैं तेरे। पेंशन खराब मत कर।

Ratan looks at the paper, then at Mishra.

RATAN: (quiet) वो किसी की बेटी थी।

MISHRA: (flat) सब किसी की बेटी होती हैं। साइन कर।

Ratan doesn't take the pen. He turns and goes. Mishra watches him go.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 32 — ON HIS OWN GROUND

**EXT. RANA'S COMPOUND — DAY**

A big walled compound, all new money — a cream Fortuner in the drive, a generator humming, young men who
don't make way for anyone. This is the land. Near the gate, under a neem, a rough vermilion stone still
stands — the than, the sthal.

Ratan is left to wait in the forecourt, standing, for a long time, while the compound's young men pass and
glance at the old khaki and don't bother to greet him.

A FIXER comes out at last, with the same easy contempt, and instead of taking him inside he hands it over
right there in the open.

FIXER: साब ने कहा — भेरूलाल वाली छोरी, हादसा। निपटा दो। ऊपर बात हो गई है। (already turning away) चाय-पानी पहुँच
जाएगा।

And then he's gone. Ratan stands alone in the forecourt, his hand closing slow into a fist at his side,
his eyes on the stone under the neem across the open ground. On his way out he stops at it long enough to
touch it — राम राम — and goes.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 33 — DEVA'S RECKONING

**EXT. BEHIND THE POST — NIGHT**

Deva catches up to Ratan in the dark, agitated, the thing pieced together.

DEVA: साहब, वो लड़की — आप जानते हैं वो गिरी नहीं थी।

RATAN: (warning) देवा।

DEVA: आप पोस्टमॉर्टम भेज रहे हैं, मतलब आप भी जानते हैं! फिर बाकी सब क्यों दबा रहे हैं? रानाजी का आदमी —

RATAN: (grabbing him, low and hard) चुप। (he looks around — who's near) एक बार बोल रहा हूँ, सुन। तू इस केस में
नहीं है। तूने कुछ नहीं देखा।

DEVA: पर —

RATAN: (harder) तेरी माँ है, बहन है। शहर वापस जाने की अर्ज़ी लिख। आज।

Deva stares at him.

DEVA: (quiet, wounded) लोग कहते थे आप अलग हो। (a beat) झूठ कहते थे।

It lands on Ratan. He lets Deva go and says nothing. Deva walks off into the dark. Ratan stands alone.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 34 — RATAN, ALONE

**INT. RATAN'S HOUSE — NIGHT**

Ratan alone, late. The doda tumbler made and ready in front of him. In his other hand, the girl's school
photo — he has taken it from the file.

He lifts the tumbler, looks at the photo in his other hand, and sets it down again without drinking.

On the pegs, the sword he keeps sharp and has never once used. He looks at it a long time. Then he turns
off the light.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 35 — SIGN IT

**INT. CBN OFFICE, RATAN'S DESK — NIGHT**

It's late and the office is empty, Ratan alone under a single lamp with the girl's thin file open. The
form is already half filled — कारण मृत्यु: हादसा, most of its boxes ticked — a pen beside it, and the day's
chai-paani folded in a small pile on the desk: the fixer's, the pawnbroker's, all of it.

The rest is a minute's work, and he's done it a hundred times.

He looks at the file instead — a school photo clipped to one corner, the girl grinning crooked at a
camera, a board exam three weeks off. Behind it comes the slipper lying too far up the furrow, and then
Sugna on the floor with the clothes in her lap, looking up at him.

The pen hangs over the box and doesn't come down.

DEVA: (from the doorway, two teas in his hands) साहब... घर नी जाएँगे?

Ratan doesn't look up from the form.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 36 — THE RHYME

**INT./EXT. — NIGHT (INTERCUT)**

DEVA'S HOUSE: by lamplight Manju holds a wedding bangle up to catch the light while the women fuss around
her and Amma's happy chatter runs on underneath. Manju turns the bangle slowly.

BHERULAL'S HOUSE: the aunt folds the dead girl's clothes into a bundle to be given away, while Sugna sits
on the floor and doesn't help, the bundle slowly filling with her daughter's things.

The bangle catches the light; the bundle fills.

CUT TO.

## SCENE 37 — THE TURN

**INT. CBN OFFICE, RATAN'S DESK — NIGHT**

Back to the desk, the form, the pen still over the box, the pile of chai-paani where it was, and Deva
still in the doorway with the two teas going cold in his hands.

Ratan looks at the box a long time.

Then he caps the pen and sets it down, opens a drawer instead and takes out a different form — a
requisition — and starts to write, filling in different boxes.

DEVA: (careful) साहब... वो हादसा वाला...?

RATAN: (writing, not looking up) पोस्टमॉर्टम। ज़िला अस्पताल। कल सुबह।

Deva goes still, the teas forgotten in his hands.

Ratan signs the requisition with his own name.

He doesn't look up, and beyond the window the belt lies dark.

CUT TO BLACK.

END OF EPISODE ONE.
