रेवे वाली ज़मीन (THE RIVER FIELD)
**EXT. RANA'S FARMHOUSE — VERANDAH — DAY**

The world Ratan has not entered yet. RANA on the swing-seat, a line of folded hands before him, tea going round; he grants one man, refuses the next, the same smile for both. Standing apart, not in the line, is KISHAN BORANA — a farmer-elder, straight-backed, white stubble, summoned, not begging. He has not folded his hands. Rana has been working on him a while.

राणा: *(warm, patient)* किशन, बैठ जा भई। कब से खड़ा है? *(Kishan stays standing; Rana lets it go, fond)* अच्छा। वो रेवे किनारे वाली तेरी ज़मीन, चार बीघा। बाज़ार भाव से डेढ़ गुना दूँगा, नक़द। सड़क उधर से निकल रही है, पूरे इलाक़े का भला होगा।

किशन: *(quiet, level)* ज़मीन बाप-दादा की है, साहब। उसमें उनकी हड्डियाँ पड़ी हैं। बिकाऊ नहीं है। माफ़ी चाहता हूँ।

राणा: *(no flicker; he expected it)* हड्डियाँ। *(a respectful nod)* अच्छी बात है। आदमी को अपनी ज़मीन से इतना ही लगाव होना चाहिए। *(he lets it sit, then, gently)* और बेटा कैसा है तेरा? करण? नीमच में फ़ार्मेसी कर रहा है ना।

A small stillness goes through Kishan, and Rana sees it and looks somewhere else.

राणा: सुना है आजकल कुछ ऐसे लड़कों के साथ उठने-बैठने लगा है जो ठीक नहीं हैं। दोड़े के धंधे वाले। *(concerned, sad)* कल को कोई केस बन गया तो बच्चे की पूरी ज़िंदगी ख़राब, लाइसेंस भी गया। *(warm again, a hand to his own chest)* पर तू फ़िक्र मत कर। एसपी अपना आदमी है। मैं कह दूँगा, करण का नाम कहीं नहीं आएगा। बिल्कुल साफ़।

Kishan understands all of it. His back is still straight, but something behind his eyes has gone quiet.

किशन: *(after a long moment, low)* ...काग़ज़ कल ले आऊँ?

राणा: *(lighting up, as if it were Kishan's own idea)* अरे देखो! समझदार आदमी है किशन तो। *(claps once)* काग़ज़ की चिंता तू मत कर, वो मैं बनवा लूँगा, तू बस दस्तख़त कर देना। *(rises, an arm around him, walking him to the steps)* और सुन — करण की बाक़ी पढ़ाई का ख़र्चा अब मेरे ज़िम्मे। अपना ही लड़का है।

He sees Kishan off like an honoured guest. The old man goes down the steps having just signed away his fathers' land to keep his son. Rana turns back, claps for tea, and the line moves up by one.

फोटो (THE PHOTO)
**INT. DEVA'S HOUSE — ITARSI — NIGHT**

A small warm room, one tube-light. AMMA on the cot with her phone, a neighbour leaning in, both of them lit up over a photo. MANJU sits to the side, eyes down, present and absent at once. DEVA comes in late, the day still on him — he has spent it with a dead sixteen-year-old and walks into his sister's marriage being arranged on a phone screen.

अम्मा: *(bright)* आ गया! देख, लड़के की नई फोटो आई है, ट्रैक्टर के साथ खड़ा है। राजस्थान में अच्छी-ख़ासी ज़मीन है इनकी। *(to the neighbour)* मंजू तो वहाँ रानी बन के रहेगी, रानी।

Deva looks at the photo a moment, then at his sister, who has not looked up from her lap.

देवा: मंजू भी देख रही है फोटो?

अम्मा: *(waving it off, fond)* अरे ये क्या देखेगी, शरमा रही है बेचारी। *(beaming)* अच्छा घर है, देवा। ऐसे रिश्ते रोज़-रोज़ नहीं आते।

Manju glances up at her brother, then back down at her lap. Deva says nothing. The girl from the morgue is still behind his eyes, and his sister is sitting three feet away while her life is settled across a phone.

बड़ा घर (THE BIG HOUSE)
**INT. BHERULAL'S HAVELI — KOTESHWAR — DAY**

The house Ratan grew up beside and has never gone into. Big, cool, cash-heavy — a colour TV loud somewhere, a deep-freeze humming, a new tractor's papers on the table. BHERULAL bands notes at a low table: a brick, a rubber band, another brick, the way another man counts grain. His flat-eyed MAN waits.

भेरूलाल: *(not looking up)* अस्पताल वाले को दे आया?

आदमी: दे आया, सेठ। मुंशी, कंपाउंडर, सब सध गए। बस वो पँवार...

भेरूलाल: *(a flicker, sealed)* पँवार को मैं देख लूँगा। अपना ही आदमी है, घर का, समझ जाएगा।

In the inner doorway SUGNA has stopped, a steel tumbler going cold in her hands. She heard all of it and she has not moved. Bherulal feels her there but does not look up.

भेरूलाल: तू अंदर जा। ये मर्दों का काम है।

सुगना: *(flat, not moving)* कितने का सौदा हुआ मेरी बेटी का? *(eyes to the notes)* ज़िंदा थी तब भी भाव लग रहा था उसका, मरी है तब भी।

भेरूलाल: *(stung, then hard)* भाव मैं नहीं लगा रहा, सुगना। दुनिया लगाती है। मैं तो घर बचा रहा हूँ। बेटा है मेरा, चार साल का। उसका नाम, उसका पट्टा। एक के पीछे पूरा घर डुबा दूँ?

Sugna looks at him, then at the money, and goes back into the dark of the house without a word. Bherulal watches the empty doorway. His hands rest on the notes a moment without moving; then he bands the next bundle tighter than the last, and does not look at the doorway again.

आदमी: कोई लड़की के बारे में पूछे तो?

भेरूलाल: *(banding the notes)* गिर गई। मेड़ से पैर फिसला, सिर लगा। चार दिन हो गए, अब तो भगवान भी यही लिखेगा।

मर्ज़ी (WHAT SHE WANTS)
**INT. DEVA'S HOUSE — ITARSI — NIGHT**

Later in it all. Deva home from the district headquarters, the cut still in his eyes — he has stood in a cold room and watched what was done to a sixteen-year-old who said no. AMMA is on the phone in the next room, happy; the Rajasthan match is all but fixed. MANJU sits with schoolbooks she no longer opens. Deva sits down across from her and asks her the thing no one in this house asks.

देवा: मंजू। ये राजस्थान वाला रिश्ता। *(she looks up)* तेरी अपनी मर्ज़ी क्या है? सच बता।

मंजू: *(reflexive)* माँ ने देख लिया है ना। जैसा आप लोग ठीक समझें।

देवा: माँ को छोड़। मैं तुझसे पूछ रहा हूँ। तू जाना चाहती है उस घर में?

Manju opens her mouth and finds she has no answer ready. No one ever taught her to keep a wish of her own, let alone say it out loud. She looks toward her mother's voice in the next room.

मंजू: *(very low)* पता नहीं।

It is the most honest thing said in this house in weeks. Deva looks at his sister, and the girl on the steel table will not leave the room. He does not push her; he has nothing to offer her instead, not yet.
