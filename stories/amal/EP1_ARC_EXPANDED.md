# अमल — Episode 1: तौल / The Weighing — EXPANDED BLUEPRINT (~50 min)

*The 20-scene spine (written, One-Law-clean) + 17 new lean scenes = the body of a Delhi-Crime-length
episode. EP1 stays ESTABLISHMENT (the cage); the only turn is the last shot. Every new scene is a fight
with a turn, written through the One-Law gate, held cards buried (Sugna = killer; "Hamir" = the pawned
ring AND the ancestor; the marriage horror = the SELLING never religion; the saka cold-open reads as the
ancestor, not Ratan). [S] = spine (exists) · [N] = new.*

**Runtime:** 20 spine ≈ 19 min + 17 new (relationship/world/procedure run ~1.5–2.5 min) ≈ ~30 min → **~37 scenes ≈ ~48–50 min.**

---

## ACT ONE — THE CAGE  (~16 min)
*The bought man, the witness who reveres him, the world, the markers — doda, ring, the legend.*

1. **[S] Flash-forward — the promise.** A bloodied old hand on an ancient talwar, mid-saka — staged to read as the legendary ancestor Hamir, not Ratan. Hook + misdirection.
2. **[S] Title — अमल.**
3. **[N] Deva arrives.** Constable Deva reports to the Amargarh post — a city posting left behind to serve under the storied Ratan Singh Panwar — and finds a sleepy, bought backwater that already doesn't match the legend. *Want:* meet the man he idolizes. *Turn:* the myth cracks before he's shaken the hand.
4. **[S] Cold open — the weighing.** The government scale's pittance against the black-market brick of cash — the opium economy in one transaction.
5. **[N] The route.** Ratan waves a consignment through; the gum leaves the belt by truck. He isn't only a man who signs deaths — he's a node in the trade, and the thing above him is huge. *Turn:* the scale of what he serves.
6. **[S] The tip.** Ratan signs a death off as a tractor accident, pockets the chai-paani; Deva watches the legend debased.
7. **[S] The belt.** The drive through opium country; the farmer's boy paying off a fat wad. The world breathes.
8. **[S] The amli.** Doda tea, and the hollowed-out old amli Ratan quietly protects. Buried decency.
9. **[N] The teacher.** Deva presses about the body and the envelope; Ratan teaches him the only lesson that keeps a man alive here — don't see, take the tea-money, go home. The heart begins; the myth cracks; Ratan glimpses his younger self.
10. **[S] The pawnbroker.** "Want to see Hamir?"; Ratan refuses; the bare finger; the mounting interest.
11. **[N] The powerful.** RANA — the MLA risen out of the Chambal ravines on the ruin of families like Ratan's — introduced through his operation. Ratan kept waiting, used like a tool. We see who he really serves, and on whose land.
12. **[S] The rites + Kanta.** The family talwar kept sharp, Shastra Puja to Harsiddhi Mata; then Kanta, the pension, the buried Rajput.
13. **[N] The doda, alone.** Ratan numbs himself on the poppy-husk in private — the opium cop drowning in the gum he signs off. The cage made physical.
14. **[S] The than.** The old Charan tells the boy the Hamir legend — the Jhujhar who fought on headless — and names Ratan as that blood, keeping watch on his own lost land.
15. **[N] The sacred.** A Malwa set-piece — Mahakal's bhasma-aarti, or the Narmada at dusk — the bought man before his own gods with nothing to offer. The belt's faith, and his shame.

## ACT TWO — THE WORLD, THE WOUND, THE DIAGNOSIS  (~20 min)
*The cage proves total; the world and the bonds deepen; the dead girl becomes a person; the estrangement and the mortality land.*

16. **[S] The gift.** Ratan's eye catches a weighment discrepancy in a death-file; Mishra waves it off — "twenty-five years, you'll learn arithmetic now? Sign it." The gift wasted.
17. **[N] The machine, total.** It isn't one bad man — the whole post is on the take: the munshi, the constables, the system. Deva sees there's no clean corner to stand in.
18. **[S] The funeral.** A forgotten peer's small cremation — retired three months, dead of nothing, nobody came. "Then you. Then me." The diagnosis.
19. **[N] The reckoning.** The funeral sits on Ratan — two years, then the same fire and the same empty ground. Now-or-never, unspoken.
20. **[S] Deva's house.** Manju's arranged match talked up in a warm, loving house — the marriage-machine, smiling.
21. **[N] Deva & Manju.** The sibling bond — Deva and the sister he can't protect — before the match swallows her. The B-story's heart, and the rhyme with the dead girl, planted.
22. **[N] The girl, alive.** The niece before the field — her world, the debt selling her to old Dhanraj, and the spine she still has that Ratan lost twenty-five years ago. *(Held: nothing of how she dies, nothing of Sugna's hand.)*
23. **[N] The estrangement.** *(flashback — flag for veto.)* The break between Ratan and Sugna, years back — the day the bought path cost him his sister. The wound that makes the niece's death his.
24. **[S] The women.** The aarti, the bruised young mother taken by the goddess — the belt's women, and the grief that has only the Devi to put it down.
25. **[N] The danger beneath.** The trade shows its teeth — a debt collected hard, a warning made — so we know the machine doesn't just sign papers, it kills. The real stakes of ever saying no.
26. **[N] Manju, pressed.** The match advances over Manju's silence; Deva swallows it. (Rhymes with the girl being sold.)
27. **[S] The body.** A girl dead at the field's edge at dawn; Ratan recognizes Bherulal, then the niece; his eye catches the wrong, and the pen stops. The keyhole.
28. **[N] The body, procedure.** Villagers, constable, father — all wanting "fall, accident, sign." Deva watches Ratan stall over a body for the first time. The first visible crack.
29. **[S] Sugna.** His estranged sister over the dead girl's clothes — "now you come" — and the debt that sold her daughter, said by the aunt because Sugna won't.

---

## ACT THREE — THE PRESSURE, THE TURN  (~14 min)
*The machine demands the rubber-stamp; the Rajput stirs; the door of no return.*

30. **[S] The buyer.** Dhanraj — old, moneyed, the young servant girl, "matches don't look at age, they look at standing" — the fate the niece was sold into.
31. **[N] The pressure.** The machine leans in — Mishra, the fixers, "it's settled up top, close it" — the cage tightening to a single demand: sign.
32. **[S] On his own ground.** Rana's compound on the family's lost land, the sthal where Hamir fell; Ratan kept waiting in his own forecourt, told to sign by a boy who won't take him inside; the fist; the stone.
33. **[N] Deva's reckoning.** The witness can't unsee a murdered girl filed as a fall; he pushes, and Ratan warns him off — hard — to keep him alive. The heart, at its cost.
34. **[N] Ratan, alone.** The doda, the band of pale skin where the ring was, the school photo — the bought man at the lip of the choice, the Rajput stirring under twenty-five years of surrender.
35. **[S] Sign it.** The desk, the half-ticked form, the day's chai-paani, the girl's photo; the pen hangs and won't come down.
36. **[S] The rhyme.** Manju's wedding bangle, intercut with the dead girl's clothes folded into a bundle. The weave, unspoken.
37. **[S] The turn.** He caps the pen, takes out a requisition, writes "postmortem, district hospital," signs his own name. The door of no return. Cut to black.
