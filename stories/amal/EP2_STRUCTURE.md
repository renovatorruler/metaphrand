## Continuity locks (from Ep1 v2.1 — exact)

- At Ep1 S23 ("His Own Name") Ratan **signed, in his own name**, an order for a second postmortem at the
  **district HQ hospital, ~200 km away** — out of Bhanwar's reach, Mishra's, and the big house's. The
  order is **signed but not yet executed.** That is Door 1.
- The body was **never cremated** — Ratan's earlier order (S12) holds Leela in the morgue. The family is
  desperate to burn her fast (a quick burning buries it as a fall). The held body is live pressure.
- **Bhanwar's clean "fall" report already exists, filed** (the same-day stamp Ratan read in S17). It is
  the counter-paper the real incision has to overturn.
- Bherulal's pressure (S21) is on the record: the son's patta needs the house-name clean; don't drag it.

## What Ep2 is for (its job in the season)

1. **Confirm the murder** — the real incision finds what the first PM hid: not a fall, hands. The
   audience now *knows*. (Held: the hand. Never Sugna.)
2. **Build Rana into a person** — the saka's apex stops being a backstory function and becomes a face
   Ratan has sat across from and refused. (Fixes the Ep1-in-isolation gap: you cannot ride a saka at a fog.)
3. **Take Ratan's footing** — the cost of forcing the truth: suspended, pension dangled, cut off the
   inside. The corpse fights on without the department.

## The three changes (Mercurio, episode scale)

- **Plot:** a "fall" becomes a confirmed murder *on the record* — and the man who forced it loses his place.
- **Character (Ratan):** Ep1 he crossed in private (signed an order). Ep2 he refuses the apex *to his
  face*, and pays. The private cross becomes a public stand.
- **Character (Deva):** the fork tilts — he watches Ratan refuse the thing every sane man takes, while
  his own house runs the same marriage-machine that killed Leela.

## Logline (for-loop + exception)

Every body the belt calls a "fall" stays a fall — *except* when a finished man forces a real incision
into one girl, and the cut runs all the way back into his own blood. The exception costs him his place;
the truth it frees saves no one.

## The title — चीरा / The Incision (earned three ways)

- the literal cut of the postmortem into Leela's body;
- the incision Ratan makes into the machine's lie (the second paper against the first);
- the incision into Ratan himself — the old wound Rana reopens with a velvet knife.

## Held cards (stay buried on the page)

- **WHO killed Leela (Sugna; mercy).** The incision says *murder*, never the hand.
- **Leela is Ratan's niece / Sugna is his sister.** (Ep1 let the audience feel it; Ep2 does not name it.)
- **The saka = Ratan's own death.** Untouched.
- **Patta-for-the-son machinery** stays light (Ep3's reveal).

## The movements (structure; scenes broken only after approval)

### M1 — The Order vs. The Machine
*Want:* Ratan wants the signed order to become a real incision — the body moved 200 km to the one knife
out of reach. *Wall:* it is "already closed" — Bhanwar's clean paper is filed; Mishra works to delay,
lose, or soften the requisition; the family wants the body released to burn; the machine can simply
fail to provide the vehicle. *Turn:* Ratan won't withdraw it → the matter goes over Mishra's head, to
Rana. *(Thread: the held body; the obscene-cash texture; Bhanwar's "go home" still hanging from Ep1.)*

### M2 — The Velvet Offer (Rana — the elaboration)
The Rana scene; the heart of the episode. We meet the man: genial, generous, owns the room and the
district ("अपना ही घर है"). He knows Ratan's wound and reaches into it — **not a threat, a gift**: let
the girl be a fall, and the **Pawar patta comes back**, the signet off the sahukar's shelf, the standing
his father lost. The man whose blood broke the fort offers to rebuild it, for one more look-away.
*Want (Ratan):* get out of that room without taking the deal. *Wall:* the deal is everything he's wanted
for 25 years, offered warmly, and refusing it is suicide. *Turn:* he refuses — quietly, the way he seals
under pressure (the card) — and for one instant the warmth shows its ravine. The apex is now an enemy
with a face. *(Deva drives him / waits outside — the fork watches the refusal.)*

### M3 — The Incision (murder confirmed)
Ratan gets the body through to the HQ hospital and the real cut happens — Deva's outsider eye in the
morgue, the horror plain. It finds what the first PM hid: **hands, not a fall** — the marks of smothering
or a grip, a thing a fall does not leave. *Murder confirmed* (the card the audience gets). The body does
not say who. *Turn:* Ratan now holds a paper that calls the machine a liar, and there is no taking it back.

### M4 — The Wheel at Home (the weave; Deva & Manju)
Deva's household: Manju's match across into Rajasthan tightens — the groom's side wants more; the
marriage-machine grinds in Deva's own home the way it ground Leela. Deva, fresh from watching Ratan
refuse Rana, feels a rhyme he can't name. The B-story carries the grief the case won't speak, and banks
the heart for the season's later spend. *(Paces the murder-confirmation; keeps the fork in view.)*

### M5 — The Footing Goes (cost; the episode's door)
The machine answers the murder report: a suspension order, the pension dangled, cut off the inside —
Rana's hand behind it, the personal-political wound now fully live. Ratan, off the department, keeps the
paper and keeps moving. *Door:* the corpse fights on though it should have fallen — and the question
turns to *who*, pointing (wrongly) at the man with motive and opportunity: **Bherulal**. *(Seeds Ep3 —
the wrong-suspect build.)*
