# ANCAPISTAN — World Bible (working title)

**What this document is:** the single consolidated reference for the TV show's universe —
history, geopolitics, the legal-insurance system, Cainland, the two world orders, and the
storytelling functions each piece serves. Every detail stated by the author is preserved here;
items the author may want to refine are marked **[TO CONFIRM]**. The companion technical
reference for the dispute system (the full Texas Hold'em rules) is incorporated into Part IV.

---

## Part 0 — The Genre Guardrail (read first, every session)

**This is not a dystopia.** Every LLM that touches this material assumes collapse-fiction and
plays the world for bleakness. That assumption is wrong and forbidden. The rule, in the
author's own analogy: a show about a magician who uses dark magic to catch criminals is not
dystopian just because dark magic exists in it. The fractured America is the *arena*, not the
*tragedy*. Ancapistan in particular is functional, orderly, and — canonically — **the most
prosperous region on Earth**. Stories set here are stories about people inside a working,
fascinating system, not survivors crawling through a broken one.

## Part I — The Fracture

### The war
The United States of America fractured in a civil war. Two successor entities emerged:

- **The Union States of America** — the continuation state. Culturally, institutionally, and
  technologically, the Union is *contemporary America, continued*: federal government, big
  tech, full internet, a space program, global financial standing.
- **The Free States of America (FSA)** — every state not in the Union. Deliberately NOT
  called "Confederate" — in-world and out, the naming avoids that echo. The FSA is a **loose
  confederation with no general central government**. Each state runs its own system, and the
  systems genuinely differ: some places are tyrannical, some attempt to recreate the old
  United States, and one region built something the world has never seen (Part III).

### How the military split
- **The Army defected roughly 50–50** between Union and Free States.
- **Navy and Air Force personnel also defected** in numbers — but they could not take their
  ships or planes with them. The hardware stayed where it was based or was retained by the
  Union. Result: the **Union holds total naval and air supremacy**.

### How the Union uses that supremacy (ongoing, to the present day)
- A **complete naval blockade** of the Free States.
- A **no-fly zone** over the entire FSA. Nothing flies; FSA satellites would be shot down on
  launch, so there are none.

### How the Free States survived: the Fort Knox theft
The secession succeeded by economics, not by arms. **The gold of Fort Knox was stolen** by
the Free States' side — modeled on the Nationalists shipping China's gold reserves to Taiwan.
The theft sent the dollar, already strained by civil war, into free fall. The dollar's global
status was the Union's most important export; to save its economy and its standing in the
world, **the Union was forced to stop fighting**. That armistice-by-market-panic is what
allowed the Free States to consolidate and, eventually, flourish.

### The gold: the rules of the mystery (canon, settled)
- **Who stole it and where it is will NEVER be shown or told in this series.** (Supporting
  fiction someday, maybe. Not here.) Today — **decades after the second civil war** — nobody
  in the world knows where that gold is.
- For the Union, the theft is **the greatest crime the Free States ever committed**, and the
  Union maintains its grievance and its claim over the FSA the way China does over Taiwan.
- **The bar-scene ritual (canon texture):** put a Union citizen and a Free States citizen at
  the same table with drinks, and sooner or later the Union one accuses the other of stealing
  their gold. Every time. It is the fracture's handshake.
- **The metaphor (author-stated, kept off the page):** the accusation means *"our prosperity
  was stolen by the secessionists"* — a backhanded confession that the Union is less
  prosperous than it believes it should be. When a Union citizen sees Free States prosperity,
  the cope is automatic: *they aren't really rich; they just have our gold.*
- **Why the cope is plausible:** Ancapistan's technological prosperity is invisible — there's
  no internet or silicon to see. What a visitor CAN see is **specie circulating as money**:
  silver coins for the small things (a coffee, a bus fare); gold coins and gold bars for anything
  larger. The everyday is silver; the serious money is gold, and it changes hands in the open — so
  to a Union eye every gold coin looks like Fort Knox.
- **The truth: Ancapistan never saw the Fort Knox gold. Not an ounce.** Its prosperity comes
  from the services it sells the rest of the world (Part VI) and from the plain fact that
  wealth-building is the one thing an anarcho-capitalist society knows how to do. The Union's
  resentment is the resentment of a man certain the billionaire stole it, when the money was
  acquired one voluntary trade at a time. The accusation is wrong twice over: the wealth
  isn't the gold, and the gold was never here.

## Part II — The Information Regime

### The inverse firewall
With no meaningful right wing left inside its borders, the Union turned to policing
misinformation — and found that the largest source of "misinformation" was the one place it
didn't control: the Free States. Its solution: **an inverse firewall — a North Korea enforced
from the outside.**

- Undersea cables serving the FSA: **cut**.
- FSA satellite launches: **prohibited and enforced by shoot-down** (under the no-fly regime).
- Result: **there is no internet in the Free States.**

### What technology the FSA actually has
- Telephony works: cell towers exist, and you can call from Dallas to Louisiana.
- Data moves over **landlines and buried copper** — slow, regional, physical.
- **No AI, no modern internet features, no latest chips.** The Union, by contrast, has
  everything — including its own space program.

### Why (the storytelling function, stated plainly)
A connected world kills stories: everything is searchable, traceable, instantly known. The
inverse firewall is an **arena function** — it restores distance, rumor, paper, couriers,
phone calls, and physical presence to the toolkit of plotting, while keeping one side of the
world (the Union) fully modern. Two information physics, one continent.

### The propaganda gap
The Union lies to its own population about conditions in the Free States. The official
picture: a chaotic, lawless, anarchist wasteland — roving gangs, atrocity folklore
("murdered babies"), continually reinforced. The reality is heterogeneous (Part I), and the
crown jewel of the FSA is the opposite of the propaganda. Two specific canon rules:

1. Union citizens DO know the name **Ancapistan**.
2. **They conflate Ancapistan with Cainland.** What an average Union citizen believes about
   Ancapistan is actually an accurate-enough description of Cainland (Part V). **This is
   never stated in dialogue or text — only shown.**

## Part III — The Economy and the Cartel Route

The blockade left the FSA exactly one trade artery: **the southern border**. Goods flow out
and in along the same routes drugs used for decades — and so **the cartel became the Free
States' biggest trading partner**. The old smuggling infrastructure is now the customs
service, the freight line, and the chamber of commerce, in whatever proportions a given
episode needs.

## Part IV — Ancapistan: Dallas–Fort Worth

### The society
The Dallas–Fort Worth area privatized everything, including all of law and order. There is no
state, no police, no public courts, no prisons. **Participation is voluntary** — and
structured so that opting in is the only practical path: when a person moves in, nobody will
sell or rent them property unless they enroll with one of the **in-network insurance
agencies**. Nothing forces enrollment; everything assumes it. Loss of coverage is civil death.

Despite no internet and no modern chips, **Ancapistan is the most prosperous area of the
whole world.** This is canon, and it is the engine of the show's quiet argument with its
Union characters (and its audience).

### The legal principle
**All criminal matters are civil matters.** There is no criminal law because there is no
state. A robbery is a dispute between the robber and the robbed, intermediated by their
insurance agencies. Justice means restitution, not punishment.

### How a wrong gets righted
- Your agency represents you, investigates, negotiates, and recovers from the party who
  damaged you. **If the agency writes you a check, it's because the agency is doing the
  recovery job** — getting the other side to pay. You must let them run the process; if you'd
  rather hire a specialized outside firm, you may — entirely out of your own pocket. The
  agency route is what your premiums already paid for.
- If YOU caused the damage and it wasn't malicious — no intent — your agency typically pays
  the award, notes your file, and/or raises your premiums, scaled to the event.
- **[TO CONFIRM]:** the author's verbal note phrased the robbery case as "the insurance
  agency of the robber, who cannot be an insured person" — normalized here per the written
  rules to: insured wrongdoer → agency-to-agency resolution; a wrongdoer with no agency is an
  outside-network problem whose refusals point toward Cainland.

### Restitution beyond means — and no prisons
If a person insured to $100,000 causes $5,000,000 in damage, no cage awaits. The debtor
**voluntarily enrolls in a work program**: they work, and their wages are split between them
and the victim until the award is satisfied. The system matches work to talent — a skilled
software developer is not sent to break rocks; their highest-value labor pays the debt
fastest. Refusal of all participation is the one unforgivable act (Part V).

### The dispute system — "Texas Hold'em"
The full procedural reference follows (incorporated from the standing rules document). The
short version first, in the author's framing:

A party claims a fact and **escrows money it is willing to lose if the fact is proven
wrong**. The stake is a confidence signal priced by evidence: an agency holding video footage
plus testimony from fifteen birthday-dinner witnesses might stake **$250,000** on "our client
was at the restaurant"; the same agency holding only a neighbor's driveway-camera clip might
stake **$75,000** on "our client was home." The opposing side can **fold** (the fact resolves
against them), **call** (match), or **raise** (match and add — signaling stronger
counter-evidence, e.g., proof the client's car turned around after passing that camera). A
pre-agreed third-party arbitrator stands behind the whole exchange. At any point a party that
doubts its own claim can back out at a bounded, known cost — **which is the design: the
structure exists to price and encourage settlements.**

The complete mechanics, canon in full:

- **Two layers.** Fact-determination (narrow, stakeable propositions: "John was outside a
  ten-mile radius between 18:00 and 20:00") is separate from dispositive arbitration (given
  the established facts, what does the governing community contract require?). Different
  proceedings, often different arbitrators.
- **Phases.** Filing (named monetary remedy) → Pleading (each side's theory; contested facts
  crystallize) → Fact-determination (the betting rounds) → Dispositive arbitration → Award,
  agency-to-agency payment, premium adjustment. Settlement is available at every stage and is
  the normal outcome.
- **The round.** Filing fees fund the arbitrator pool. The asserting agency posts an opening
  surety and discloses evidence **metadata only** ("timestamped community-entrance footage,"
  "sealed third-party attestation from a discreet-witness firm") — a price signal and a
  shape, not the evidence. Fold / call / raise follows, with escalating sureties and
  escalating metadata. Sureties cap at multiples of the claim's value; after the cap, either
  side may force **showdown**, where evidence is presented openly TO THE OPPOSING PARTY (not
  merely the arbitrator — this is the anti-corruption guarantee). Loser's surety forfeits to
  the winner.
- **Three outcomes, not two:** *established*, *established-against*, or *unestablished* —
  the arbitrator is allowed to say "I don't know," sureties return, and the record stays
  honest. Arbitrators who never say it lose reputation and business.
- **Forfeit-with-seal:** at any point, a party may concede the fact, pay the full surety, and
  have its evidence destroyed or returned unexamined — the privacy valve. The married man
  with a mistress for an alibi takes the adverse finding and keeps the secret.
- **Sealed by default:** standard policies seal whole categories (whereabouts,
  communications, finances, household composition) automatically — so sealing signals
  nothing. Going OPEN on a normally-sealed category is the marked move ("I have nothing to
  hide"). Deviations from a person's own sealing baseline are the investigator's thread.
- **Discreet-witness firms and reputation:** sealed testimony is priced by the firm's public
  track record in the fraction of cases that DO go to showdown. A firm validated 197 of 200
  reveals commands a premium on everything it seals. Occasional revelation underwrites
  widespread secrecy; a lying firm is eventually caught by a forced reveal it cannot control,
  and dies commercially. (Institutional incentive, not cryptography.)
- **The standing factual record:** every showdown is public — parties, stakes, arbitrator,
  ruling. Established facts persist and carry into all future disputes; agencies' libraries
  of established facts are commercial assets. Soft case law accumulates.
- **Standard of proof:** preponderance, ~51%. There is no criminal standard because there is
  no criminal law. The system accepts that innocents sometimes lose, and bounds the error
  three ways: proportionality (money, never liberty or life), recoverability (findings reopen
  on new evidence), and aggregate reputation (patterns price people, not single findings).
  Exile is **never** a consequence of losing — only of refusing to play at all.
- **"Hands":** agencies' fact-staking specialists are called hands, and the best command
  star salaries. (Casting note: the show's professional ecosystem — hands, arbitrators,
  discreet-witness partners, community-contract lawyers — is its precinct house.)

### Communities (corrected canon)
Neighborhoods, towns, and developments each maintain a **community contract** — the
governing law for disputes arising inside, specifying recognized agencies, procedures, and
penalty schedules. Buying or renting in means accepting the contract.

Do not exoticize this. Communities are geographically real places, and people shop for them
exactly the way people shop for communities today — by wealth, comfort, and kind. There are
rich communities and poor communities; there is the community where the fresh-off-the-boat
immigrants land and the community where the rich live. Ordinary residential sorting, not a
gimmick.

And do not imagine wall-to-wall HOAs: **roughly as many areas have no community association
as have one.** The non-HOA areas put no association on your property — but they belong to a
**loose consortium** among themselves ("liberalized HOAs, in a way"): the minimal mutual
arrangement that keeps them inside the dispute network without governing anyone's paint
colors.

## Part V — Cainland

Named for the biblical Cain, cursed to roam. The one mechanism of expulsion in a system with
no prisons: a person or entity that **refuses to participate** — drops coverage, refuses an
award, recognizes no community contract — is pushed out of every property in Ancapistan and
into Cainland.

- It is the boogeyman: lawless, coverage-less, recourse-less, where criminals run everything.
- The threat of it is what gives every other mechanism its bite — and it is gated entirely on
  **refusal**, never on losing a dispute. You can lose every case you ever enter and remain a
  citizen in good standing, paying out, covered.
- **The secret (major reveal, held for later in the story): Cainland has structure.** Despite
  its boogeyman reputation, something organized exists in there. What that structure is, who
  runs it, and what it wants are deliberately unset in this document — to be developed as the
  reveal arc. **[TO CONFIRM: the author holds this card.]**
- And the standing dramatic irony (Part II): what Union citizens think Ancapistan is, is
  actually Cainland. Shown, never said.

## Part VI — The Two World Orders

### The superficial order: the United Nations
The world believes the UN is the world order. The Union States, post-fracture, has warmed to
the UN considerably. The third world loves it: a toothless body where every nation's vote
counts the same is a free megaphone.

And it has devolved into spectacle. Because one-nation-one-vote rewards persuasion of the
many over argument to the few, **member states replaced speeches with staged performances —
Eurovision diplomacy.** Instead of a Ukrainian president's plea about Russian aggression:
Ukrainian performers enacting the aggression itself — staged atrocities, fake blood, props,
choreography, production design — played to the general assembly for the vote. The show
treats this deadpan: it is simply how the UN works now, and everyone behaves as if it were
normal. (The author's intent: the audience should get a kick out of every UN scene.)

### The real order: Ancapistan as the world's Delaware
Because anything can be incorporated in Ancapistan and any dispute can be resolved there,
**the world's organized crime registered itself in Ancapistan.** Historically, organized
crime fights because it has no dispute-resolution mechanism — the sit-down among family
heads was the best it ever had. Now there is a formal legal system that takes their cases,
exactly the way every American corporation registers in Delaware. So:

- The families don't fight each other anymore. They litigate — or rather, they stake.
- **Turf disputes that do go physical are conducted as rule-based engagements — treated like
  UFC cage fights** with agreed rules, because at the end of the day these people are
  criminals, and the system prices even violence rather than pretending it away.
- Beyond crime: **third-world dictatorships and paramilitary groups register in Ancapistan
  too**, for the same reason. The pariahs of the superficial order are clients of the real one.

### The signature plot pattern (canon, and meant to be VISIBLE on screen)
A live interstate war — Russia–Ukraine is the named example; Israel–Palestine works the same —
runs through both orders in sequence:

1. The **Union States president** hosts a summit to "resolve" the conflict (the superficial
   order performing itself).
2. The parties appear **at the UN** to play to their third-world constituencies — the
   Eurovision performance.
3. Then they **fly to Dallas**, where the actual, binding dispute resolution happens —
   stakes, sureties, arbitrators, an award.

The audience watches world history get decided in a Texas conference room while the world
watches the theater. This juxtaposition is a recurring engine, never explained, always shown.

## Part VII — Storytelling Functions (why each piece exists)

| World element | What it buys the show |
|---|---|
| No internet in the FSA | Restores mystery, distance, couriers, paper trails, phone calls, physical presence — plot physics that connectivity kills |
| Union = contemporary US | One foot in the audience's world; contrast engine; fish-out-of-water traffic in both directions |
| Insurance-law system | A procedural genre nobody has seen: the cop show, the courtroom drama, and the poker movie fused — with "hands" as its gunslingers |
| Texas Hold'em fact-betting | Scenes with built-in stakes (literally), bluffing, tells, folds — drama mechanics native to the legal system itself |
| Sealed mode / discreet witnesses | Secrets with prices on them; privacy as a tradable asset; the investigator's anomaly-hunting |
| Cainland | The boogeyman with a hidden structure — a long-arc reveal held by the author |
| The Ancapistan/Cainland conflation | Standing dramatic irony about propaganda, never spoken |
| Cartel as trading partner | Frontier commerce texture; the old smuggling world gone semi-legitimate |
| UN-as-Eurovision | Recurring satirical set-piece; the superficial order as theater |
| Dallas as the world's Delaware | The kick: global wars settled by Texas arbitration while the UN performs |
| No prisons / work-program restitution | The system's moral signature; an ethic of recoverability over punishment |
