# ANCAPISTAN — The Galápagos of Texas (Material Culture)

**The principle.** Two real-world keys. **Iran / Russia / North Korea under sanctions:** a resource-rich
state cut off from goods doesn't collapse — it *diverges.* It perfects what it has and grows its own
idiosyncratic substitutes. **Japan:** a place of strong character grows product categories that exist
nowhere else (the washlet, the kei car, the konbini) — a material *dialect* only spoken there. Ancapistan
is both, but **prosperous.**

**The tech level — get this right (it's not Victorian).** Ancapistan **makes its own chips.** Calculator,
appliance, controller, and drone silicon is mature, easy technology — Texas fabs it locally and well. The
*only* thing it can't get is the **frontier: roughly 3-nanometer and below — the AI, datacenter, and
modern-phone silicon — which the US embargoes** and which trickles in only by smuggling, scarce and dear.
Add the severed internet (cables cut, satellites shot down, ARENA Part II). So: **computers, yes;
calculators and washing machines and drones, yes; advanced math, yes. The cloud, frontier AI, and the
internet, no.** The divergence is what forty years of *build-it-to-last, build-it-yourself, no
upgrade-treadmill, no network* actually grows.

**Deployment rule (non-negotiable).** Texture, not a tour. None of this is ever explained. A character
just *does* the alien-normal thing for a second and we move on; the audience absorbs the world by
accumulation. If a line ever explains an item, cut the line. (See the clear-pane law.)

## The inventions (each a one-second touch)

**The immortal truck.** What Iran did to the Paykan, Texas did to a pickup: one model, built and refined
for forty years, mechanically simple, repairable forever, handed down. A worn, immaculate thirty-year-old
one is the ultimate old-money counter-signal — you can't buy heritage. The master drives that; the
arriviste drives a flashy new one; we read both men in a beat. (ECONOMY.md §7.)

**The hundred-year good.** China made everything cheap and unfixable; this economy went the opposite way.
You buy a **hundred-year fridge** — *guaranteed for a century*, by a maker whose name is on it and who
services it for that century — even though the country itself is only forty years old. The guarantee
outliving the nation is the point: it's a society declaring it intends to *last.* So a major appliance is
bought **once in a life, or inherited** — a considered, near-ceremonial purchase, like a house, not a
disposable. No planned obsolescence, no e-waste, no upgrade churn (nothing gets obsoleted by a software
update, because nothing's chasing the frontier). The Union throws its world away and rebuys it; here the
stove is eighty years old and perfect. *Touch:* a serial plate reads a date older than the buyer; a
grandmother's range still runs.

**Fast roads and drones.** No internet to order things and no patience for slow — so logistics is
*physical and quick.* The roads are private, so there are **express courier lanes** where licensed
runners move fast, and **simple delivery drones** (Ukraine-grade, no frontier chips) fly the metroplex.
You don't click; you **call** (landline or local cell) and a drone or a runner has it to you within the
hour. The Texan Amazon is a phone number and a drone. *Touch:* a delivery drone drops a parcel on a porch
mid-scene; nobody looks up.

**Outdoor cooling.** Energy is nearly free, so they spend it on comfort the way Manhattan spends gas on
sidewalk heaters in winter — **outdoor air-conditioners** over patios, porches, market stalls, ballpark
seats. Not the whole outdoors refrigerated; just *cooled where people sit,* in August, casually. *Touch:*
a patio is comfortable in obvious heat and no one remarks on it.

**The unrecognizable shelf.** Autarky plus the Mexico door means **no global brands at all** — the store
is full of well-made Texan and Latin goods and the Union man recognizes *nothing.* That blankness tells
him how far from home he is better than the jets did. *Touch:* he scans a shelf and finds not one familiar
label.

**The assayer's world.** Money is physical metal, so a whole craft surrounds it — a little brass coin
scale on every good counter, assay marks, the ring test, Texas-leather money goods heavy with silver.
*Touch:* change is *weighed,* never improvised from a cigar box; a man feels a coin's weight without
looking.

**Live and on the air.** No internet means **nothing streams** — so music is live or broadcast, and
**radio drama is prime-time mass media.** (Which quietly makes the audio dramas we build an *in-world
artifact*, not just our method.) *Touch:* a radio play murmurs under a kitchen scene; a dance hall is full
on a Wednesday.

**The Latin under the Texan.** Trade and labor are Latin, so the dialect is Tex-Mex-pan-Latin fused —
conjunto under the country, saint's-day dances in the calendar, Spanish humming beneath the English. The
white-Hispanic culture (ECONOMY.md §5, Sod) lives here, in texture only. *Touch:* a blond rancher named
Reyes leads a saint's-day dance; never a word of why.

## What this is for

The Watson doesn't learn this world from a lecture; he learns it the way you learn a foreign country — a
hundred small wrongnesses that are right to everyone but him, until one day they're right to him too.
One or two touches a scene, glancing. Overused, it's the theme-park tour we are not making.
