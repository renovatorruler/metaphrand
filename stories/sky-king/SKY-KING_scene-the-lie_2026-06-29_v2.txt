INT. TOWER — REAR CORRIDOR — NIGHT

A dead-end hall behind the cab. Fire-exit lighting. HOLT stands close
to REYES, voice low, the cab noise muffled behind a door.

HOLT
He likes you. He's been talking to you for ten minutes.

REYES
He's been talking to the controller. I'm new to him.

HOLT
Then he's not bored yet. Use it.

REYES
Use it how.

HOLT
You tell him what gets the gear down. Whatever that is. I don't care
what shape it takes.

Reyes looks at him. Holt doesn't look away.

REYES
And if he asks me straight. Am I charged, what happens when I land.

HOLT
Then you be persuasive. You're good at that, it's the job.

REYES
That's not a yes.

HOLT
I don't need it pretty. I need it down, away from the houses. He made
his choice going up. He can make a better one coming down.

Reyes is quiet. Holt holds out the spare headset.

HOLT (CONT'D)
Clock's the clock. You want the seat or not.

Reyes takes the headset.

CUT TO:

INT. TOWER CAB — CONTINUOUS

BISHOP at the scope, headset on, steady. REYES settles the headset over
his ears. HOLT stands a step behind him, arms folded. Out the glass:
black, and the city burning orange far below.

BISHOP
(into mic, low)
Birdy. Got somebody here wants a word. He's all right.

A beat of static.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(over radio, dry, gentle)
Hi, somebody.

REYES
(into mic)
My name's Reyes. I work for the county.

BIRDY (V.O.)
You drive out here this time of night, you must want something.

REYES
I want you on the ground. That's all. And I want you to hear me on
what's waiting.

BIRDY (V.O.)
Go ahead.

Reyes glances at the notes in his hand. Holt watches the back of his head.

REYES
You bring it down now, nobody hurt, no damage — there's no case to
make. You taxi in, you walk to your car. We let it go.

A long silence on the radio. Engine drone underneath.

BIRDY (V.O.)
You're a prosecutor.

REYES
I am.

BIRDY (V.O.)
Then you're the one who'd make the case. And you're telling me there
isn't one.

Reyes doesn't answer. Holt shifts his weight.

BIRDY (V.O.) (CONT'D)
You been straight with me, or you reading?

Reyes lowers the notes. He looks at the orange light on the glass, not
at Holt.

REYES
I'm reading.

Holt's jaw tightens. He steps in.

HOLT
(hard whisper, off mic)
Reyes.

REYES
(into mic, even)
They handed me a thing to say and I said it. The walking-to-your-car
part, that's not mine to give. I asked. They won't put their name on it.

HOLT
(low)
That's enough.

Reyes keeps the key pressed.

REYES
(into mic)
So I'm not going to promise you a thing I can't pay. I don't know what
happens when you land. I'd be lying if I said I did.

Static. The cockpit hum. BISHOP doesn't move, eyes on Reyes.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(quiet)
Okay.

REYES
That's it. That's all I've got that's true.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(after a beat)
Thanks for telling me.

Reyes lets the key up. He pulls the headset down to his neck. He turns.

HOLT
You just gave him a reason to stay up there.

REYES
I gave him the only thing he'd believe.

Holt holds his eyes a moment. Then keys his own radio to the room behind
them, done with Reyes. Reyes stays where he is, headset around his neck,
looking at the back of the man's head.

SMASH CUT TO BLACK.
