## 1. THE FEELING (what the first time felt like)
Falling in love with a voice, in real time, while doing math you don't want to
do. The register the mourners themselves used: "I feel like I'm trying to mourn
someone I never met." / "His story hurts me in a way that only losing the
closest of friends and family have hurt me." Strangers grieved him like kin —
BECAUSE the tapes made them his intimates for 73 minutes. The film must
reproduce that intimacy first (the voice, unescapable, funny, apologetic) and
the math second (fuel, jets, the ending they think they know).

## 2. THE SUMMIT MOMENTS (rank-ordered feeling targets)
1. **"I'll just bring this nose down and call it a night."** (user-verified as
   the comment sections' most-cited heartbreak) — the gentlest death sentence
   ever spoken: the end of a life in the language of the end of a shift. OUR
   TRANSFORM: he says it — the audience that knows the tapes braces — and the
   nose comes down onto a runway. The line that meant death becomes the line
   that means going home tired. The whole he-lives wager in one sentence.
2. **"Just a broken guy, got a few screws loose I guess. Never really knew it,
   until now."** — the self-elegy; the dead man supplying his own eulogy.
3. **The barrel roll** + the shoreline videos — awe and grief in the same
   image; "dreamlike stunt execution" celebrated like a fallen athlete's
   highlight.
4. **The job exchange** (the dream sayable only as a joke) and its detonating
   coda-line — see HANDLE WITH CARE.
5. **The controllers' kindness / the pilots not firing** — the machine's people
   going gentle one hour too late.
6. NEW FIND — asked his NAME by ATC, the real man said: **"That's a question
   I've been asking my whole life. Identity issues."** Our KEEP-scene name
   deflection ("Aw. It's, uh. — Everybody just calls me Birdy.") has a
   real-world twin. The film's instinct is verified by the tapes.

## 3. THE GRIEF-REGISTERS (how the mourning sounds — the film's tonal palette)
- **Intimate-stranger grief**: kin-level mourning for a man never met.
- **Working-class recognition**: "a human cog in the airport machinery who
  suddenly revolted… a final dogged display of the human spirit. True
  Americana." The audience sees THEIR unlived life — the mirror.
- **Protectiveness**: the mourners guard him ("so much more than his final
  moments"); they de-emphasize the death, refuse ghoulishness, police mockery.
  → The audience will PROTECT Birdy; scenes can lean on that (they'll forgive
  him anything; they will not forgive US cheapening him).
- **Deadpan eulogy**: meme-grammar used tenderly; jokes AS grief. → The film's
  humor must never signal "comedy"; it is how these people cry.
- **The projection surface**: Icarus, blank slate — he holds everyone's
  meaning. → Birdy must stay slightly under-explained (never diagnose him, no
  wound-flashback that "solves" him); the space IS the appeal.

## 5. THE HULU DOC — WHAT'S TAKEN, WHAT'S EMPTY (differentiation)
"#Skyking" (Gillespie, Hulu 2026-04-14) chose to DE-MYTHOLOGIZE: it counters
the folk-hero lore, grounds him in wages/hours/isolation, and is strongest
where it is most unadorned; critics dinged its middle for lost momentum and its
one late musical flourish for "falling prey to the online mythologizing."
CONSEQUENCES FOR US:
- **The ballad lane is EMPTY.** The doc refused the folk-song on purpose. Our
  film IS the folk-song — the responsible version: the myth WITH the man inside
  it, the crowning WITH the price.
- **Their weakness is our form**: momentum is what drama has and vérité
  doesn't. The bridge grammar (ember → witness ladder → debt reversal → armor →
  kneeling → reprieve) is the momentum machine.
- **Their lesson stands for us too**: unadorned beats hit hardest. Our
  plainness laws are the same discovery from the other side; earn every
  flourish (the one flourish they were punished for is the exact reach our
  doctrine bans).
- **And ours ends differently**: they could only mourn. We answer the mourning
  — pre-grief turned to reprieve. He lives.

## 6. HANDLE WITH CARE (hard flags from the record)
- **The "white guy" line was appropriated by alt-right communities in
  reality.** If Birdy's version is used, the lampshade design (the film's own
  world flinches; observation, never thesis) is MANDATORY armor, and the beat
  must stay deadpan-thrown-away, never a speech. The film must be impossible to
  quote as ammunition without visible dishonesty.
- **The real widow asked for privacy.** Our fictionalization (Birdy, Maya, no
  real names, invented biography) is a duty. The film honors the FEELING of the
  event, never re-enacts the man's private life.
- **"He is certainly no hero" is a real voice** (his own faith adviser). The
  film should hold that pressure somewhere honest (the machine's cold read; the
  cost visible) — worship without gravity would betray both the man and the
  audience's intelligence.
