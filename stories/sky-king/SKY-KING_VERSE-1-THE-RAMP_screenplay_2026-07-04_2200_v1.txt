ACTION: The holding room is a windowless office off the command post, one desk lamp on, a folding chair pulled out.
ACTION: DEZ sits in the folding chair, twenty-three, a hi-viz vest still on over his ramp shirt, both hands wrapped around a paper cup of coffee he hasn't touched.
ACTION: His hands haven't stopped shaking since they walked him in.
ACTION: REYES, the agent from the door, forties, pulls a second chair around and sits close, not across.
REYES: You want more in that? It's cold by now, I think.
DEZ: No. No, I'm okay. I'm good.
ACTION: Reyes lets the quiet sit. She doesn't write anything down.
REYES: What was he like. Birdy.

DEZ: I don't. He was just. He was a good dude, he was.
DEZ: Like the other day, right, we're doing the morning push and the app kicks out the board, the assignments, and it gave the widebody to somebody else. Which is his. That's his turn, he'd earned that turn.
ACTION: The lamp holds on his face and the room goes with him.
ACTION: The ramp at Sea-Tac, full daylight, an ordinary shift, the noise of engines and tugs everywhere.
ACTION: BIRDY, late thirties, a headset around his neck, works a bag belt fast and clean, stacking bins two at a time without looking.
ACTION: DEZ, younger, in the same vest, stands next to him holding a tablet, thumbing at the schedule screen.
DEZ: Man, this is wrong. It's got you on the regional, that's a walk to C, that's the worst one.
DEZ: You had the widebody. That was yours, everybody knows that's yours.
ACTION: Birdy sets a bin down and glances at the tablet, then goes back to the belt.
BIRDY: It's fine. It's the app, it does what it does.
DEZ: You gonna say something?
BIRDY: Nah. Regional's easier anyway, half the bags.
ACTION: Birdy hooks the tug and starts walking the cart toward the regional gate.
ACTION: Dez stands there with the tablet a second, then follows him.
ACTION: A cart of bags sits half-loaded on the ramp, and one net is thrown wrong, tangled.
ACTION: TANNER, nineteen, brand-new vest still creased, jogs over from the tug, all elbows.
TANNER: Was that mine? The net, was that the one I did?
ACTION: WARD, the ramp lead, older, a clipboard against his hip, comes across fast without slowing down.
WARD: Whose was this. This cart.
ACTION: Ward stops at the tangled net and looks at it, then at the three of them.
BIRDY: That's me. I rushed it, my bad.
ACTION: Ward makes a mark on the clipboard and is already turning away.
WARD: Watch it. That's a write-up if a bag walks.
BIRDY: Yeah. Won't happen again.
ACTION: Ward's gone toward the next gate.
ACTION: Tanner exhales and grins.
TANNER: Thanks, man. I owe you.
BIRDY: You don't owe me nothing. Go grab the last two.
ACTION: Tanner jogs off. Birdy pulls a turkey sandwich out of his vest pocket, still in the deli plastic, and eats it standing up by the cart.

DEZ: You keep doing that. Taking the kid's stuff.
BIRDY: He's green. He'll get there.

BIRDY: Maybe. We'll see.
DEZ: I'm serious, man, put in. You're better than half these guys with the stripes.
ACTION: Birdy smiles at him and tosses the plastic in the cart bin.
BIRDY: We'll see. Come on, they're calling the push.

DEZ: I told him. I kept telling him.
