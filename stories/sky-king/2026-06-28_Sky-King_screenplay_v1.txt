SKY KING
A fictional story inspired by true events. No real names. He lives.
Working draft — 28 June 2026

================================================================
THE SHAPE — 19 beats across three acts
(★ = written in full below; the rest are one-line beats, ready to write)
================================================================

ACT ONE — THE GROUND  (the cage)

  1. Cold open / sky tease — Birdy in the cockpit at gold hour, Bishop on
     the radio. Birdy wants one more minute of sky and puts off landing
     with a joke that lands like a goodbye. Hard cut before we understand it.

  2. The ramp — Dez wants Birdy to march in and demand his raise. Birdy
     takes the smaller hit and waves a jet out into a sky he never gets to fly.

  3. Home — Maya wants one true sentence from him. She gets "I'm fine."
     The gap widens.

  4. The simulator — Maya gets into the one room where he's still alive, and
     glimpses the man she married fly for a few seconds before the shutter drops.

  5. The kindness — Birdy presses cash he can't spare on Dez. Dez, taking it,
     finally senses something is wrong.

  6. The discovery — Maya, having found the pills, tries to say it and ask him
     to stay. The words die. She says goodnight, and hates herself for it.

  7. The last ordinary morning — Birdy gives Maya a perfect day she won't
     recognize as goodbye. She almost holds him here. The weight wins.

  8. The eve — Birdy sends Dez home with a warm lie and turns to the empty
     plane in the dark.

  9. DOORWAY OF NO RETURN — the hijack. The tower catches the rogue taxi;
     Bishop orders him to stop; Birdy lifts off, airborne and alone.

ACT TWO — THE SKY  (the hour)

  10. First contact — Bishop refuses to bark and talks to him like a person.
      Birdy, startled, answers.

  11. The freedom begins — Birdy asks where the orca is. Bishop breaks a rule
      and gives him the heading. The beauty starts to loosen something he
      thought was dead.

  12. The escalation — Maya is brought to the tower and begs for the radio.
      Bishop keys her in. "I left the porch light on." The stakes turn unbearable.

  13. ★ THE ROLL — the sky peak. (written below)

  14. ★ THE HONEST DEAL — the DA negotiation. (written below)

  15. DOORWAY 2 — the decision to land. A man with no training decides to try.
      Voss, watching live, publicly offers him a job — a future to come down to.

ACT THREE — COMING HOME

  16. The descent — untrained, terrified, fighting the airplane and his own
      despair, talked toward the ground inch by inch by a stranger's voice and
      his wife's, the whole city looking up.

  17. Touchdown — he lands it. Rough, and alive.

  18. Aftermath — the honest reckoning: the arrest, the court. Reyes keeps her
      word; the suspended sentence holds; the cage is avoided.

  19. Coda — the recordings of his gentle voice become the thing strangers pass
      around: a living folk hero this time, not a tragic one. He and Maya begin,
      slowly, to actually talk. A changed man choosing the ordinary life he
      almost left.

13.  THE ROLL — THE SKY PEAK   (the "M83 moment")
----------------------------------------------------------------

INT./EXT. TURBOPROP COCKPIT — GOLDEN HOUR (AIRBORNE)

The plane is small in a wide gold sky. Below, the city is hazed and
far down.

BIRDY sits at the controls. Headset on. His face is slack. His hands
move on instinct, no more.

The fuel gauge sits in the low quarter.

A burst of static.

          BISHOP (V.O.)
     Still with me?

Birdy wets his lips. Takes a second to find his voice.

          BIRDY
     Yeah.

He looks out the side window.

The sun sits low on the horizon. It fills the cockpit. The light is
on his hands, his knees, the dash.

Far below, the city is coming on. One light, then more, then a whole
grid of them in the blue.

Birdy watches the lights come up.

He pulls back on the yoke. The nose lifts. The plane climbs.

The haze drops away under him. Above it, the gold is clean.

Birdy's eyes are wet now. He keeps climbing.

His hand goes to the yoke. He banks left a little. Levels. Banks
again. He watches the wing dip and come back.

He holds the yoke. He doesn't move.

Then he turns it all the way over.

The horizon rolls. Gold swings up the windshield, across the top,
down the other side. The city wheels overhead and comes back under him.

The plane comes level.

Birdy lets out a breath that turns into a laugh. A short one, surprised.

Then another. It keeps coming and he can't stop it, and it isn't only
laughing anymore. His face is wet, his shoulders are going.

He laughs and cries at the same time, both hands over his mouth, the
yoke loose between his knees.

          BISHOP (V.O.)
     You okay up there?

Birdy nods. Then remembers to speak.

          BIRDY
     Yeah. Yeah. I'm —

He stops. Wipes his face with the back of his wrist.

          BIRDY (CONT'D)
     Okay.

He sits a moment. Breathing. The gold is going to dusk on his face.

He puts his hands back on the controls.

He banks the nose around, toward the field, toward the lights coming
up below.

The sun drops under the horizon. The gold leaves his face.

----------------------------------------------------------------
14.  THE HONEST DEAL — THE DA NEGOTIATION
----------------------------------------------------------------

INT./EXT. TURBOPROP COCKPIT — DUSK (AIRBORNE)

The gold has gone orange in the windscreen. BIRDY, mid-forties, ramp
coveralls, one hand loose on the yoke. Out the left window, a fighter
jet sits off the wingtip, close enough to see the pilot's helmet.

The fuel gauge needle rests low. Birdy taps the glass with one finger.
Watches the needle not move.

INTERCUT — TOWER CAB

BISHOP at the console, headset on, eyes on the radar. The slow blip
crawls between two fast ones. REYES stands beside him, a hand already
out for the spare mic. Bishop looks at her. Holds the mic a second
before he gives it over.

          REYES
     (into mic)
     Birdy. My name's Reyes. I work for the county.

          BIRDY
     County.

          REYES
     I'm going to make this simple. You bring it down now, right now,
     and there's no charges. Clean. You walk.

Birdy looks out at the jet. The pilot looks back.

          BIRDY
     You can do that.

          REYES
     I'm telling you I can.

A long beat. Birdy lets out a breath through his nose. Almost a laugh.

          BIRDY
     No.

          REYES
     No what.

          BIRDY
     I'm not — I'm slow, but I'm not stupid.

Reyes' jaw tightens. Bishop keeps his eyes on the radar.

          BIRDY (CONT'D)
     I took an airplane. I know I did something.

          REYES
     Birdy —

          BIRDY
     Don't. Please.

Birdy's thumb moves on the yoke.

          BIRDY (CONT'D)
     Just tell me the real one. What it actually is.

Reyes lowers the mic. Looks at Bishop. Bishop doesn't help her. She
brings it back up.

          REYES
     Okay.

She sets her feet.

          REYES (CONT'D)
     You're going to be charged. That's true no matter what I say up
     here. I can't make that go away and I'm not going to sit here and
     pretend.

INTERCUT — COCKPIT

Birdy goes still. Listening.

          REYES (V.O.)
     So here's what's real. You put this thing down. Nobody on the
     ground gets hurt.

          BIRDY
     Nobody. I picked the airplane that was empty. I checked.

          REYES (V.O.)
     I believe you.

INTERCUT — TOWER CAB

          REYES
     You land it clean, and I go in front of a judge myself. I argue
     it. Suspended sentence. You'd answer for it. There'd be rules,
     people checking on you. But not a cell. That's the one I'd fight for.

A pause on the line. The blip holds.

          REYES (CONT'D)
     I can't promise the judge. I can promise I'll be standing there.

INTERCUT — COCKPIT

Birdy is quiet a moment. He looks at the orange going out of the glass.

          BIRDY
     Rules.

          REYES (V.O.)
     Yeah.

          BIRDY
     Somebody checking on me.

          REYES (V.O.)
     Probably for a while.

Birdy nods to himself. Wipes his nose with the back of his hand.

          BIRDY
     That's the part that scares me. More than the —
     (he stops)
     The rest of it.

          REYES (V.O.)
     I know.

          BIRDY
     You don't, but. Thanks.

He puts his other hand on the yoke. Both hands now.

          BISHOP (V.O.)
     Birdy. You still with me?

          BIRDY
     Yeah, Bishop.

          BISHOP (V.O.)
     That offer she made. That's a true one.

Birdy watches the fighter pilot off his wing. Lifts two fingers off
the yoke. The pilot, after a moment, lifts a gloved hand back.

          BIRDY
     Okay.

          REYES (V.O.)
     Okay what.

          BIRDY
     Tell me where you want me to put it.

He levels the wings. The needle sits low and steady. Out the
windscreen, far off, the city starts to come on, light by light.
