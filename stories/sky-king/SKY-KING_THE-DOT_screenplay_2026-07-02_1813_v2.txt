SKY KING — INT. SEATTLE APPROACH - RADAR ROOM - NIGHT

@@SCENE@@
ACTION: The radar room runs half-dark and quiet. Most of the scopes are dimmed, and a coffee goes cold at Bishop's elbow.
ACTION: Bishop leans into his scope and works the trackball, following a slow return that crawled off the Sea-Tac ground a minute ago.
BISHOP: Price. You got anything working off the field down there?
ACTION: Price rolls his chair a few inches and looks at Bishop's scope without much interest.
PRICE: Off Sea-Tac? Nothing of mine. Everything I've got is up north.
BISHOP: There's no ID on him. No altitude coming off him either, just the blip. Tower never called me anything.
PRICE: Then it's not really a thing. Somebody's ground equipment, probably.
BISHOP: It's moving, though. Came off the ground a minute ago.
ACTION: At the desk behind them, Kemp caps a pen and signs the last page in a thin folder. His visitor badge sits clipped crooked, and his jacket is already over one arm.
BISHOP: Aircraft off Seattle-Tacoma, this is Seattle Approach. Identify, and say your intentions.
ACTION: The frequency keys. Under the carrier hiss there's a breath and the drone of an engine, and then the mic clicks off.
BISHOP: Aircraft off Sea-Tac, Seattle Approach, you're coming in with your mic but I'm not hearing any words. Say again.
ACTION: The mic keys a second time. The same breath comes through with the same engine behind it, and then it clicks off.
ACTION: Bishop half-turns from the scope and lifts a hand toward the desk.
BISHOP: Hey. Can you come look at this a second. I got somebody keying up on me and not saying anything.
ACTION: Kemp sets his pen down and drifts up behind the two chairs with the jacket still over his arm.
KEMP: That a thing that happens? Somebody keys the radio and doesn't talk?
BISHOP: It happens. Not usually off a target with nothing on it.
KEMP: Because if it's routine I don't put it in the writeup. If it's not routine, I do.
ACTION: The supervisor comes up behind Bishop's chair and leans a hand on the seatback.
SUPERVISOR: Where'd he come off.
BISHOP: The Sea-Tac ground. Nobody filed a plan for him. He's just out there with no tag on him at all.
SUPERVISOR: Hit him again. Let me hear it.
BISHOP: Aircraft off Sea-Tac, Seattle Approach. Say your call sign, tell me what you're doing.
ACTION: The frequency opens, and a soft voice comes through under the engine.
BIRDY (RADIO): Hey, uh. Sorry to bug you guys.
ACTION: Nobody in the room moves. The blip crawls one notch across Bishop's scope.
BIRDY (RADIO): I, uh. I kind of did a bad thing here. I got one of the airplanes up and it's, it's not really mine to have. So. Real sorry about it.
ACTION: The supervisor straightens off the seatback and looks at the badge on Kemp's shirt.
SUPERVISOR: You're still on the property.
KEMP: I was signing out.
SUPERVISOR: Soon as it left the ground it's a federal thing. You're the only federal I've got standing here. So it's yours.
ACTION: Kemp moves the jacket to his other arm and looks at the phone on the wall by the desk.
KEMP: Okay. What is he. Somebody give me what he actually is, because I've got to say it to the watch floor and it has to hold up.
BISHOP: No ID on him, no altitude. He came off Sea-Tac, and there's a guy on the radio telling me the airplane isn't his.
KEMP: So it's just him. Not a crew flying it.
BISHOP: One voice on there. Sounds like just the one.
ACTION: Kemp lifts the phone off the hook and holds it against his chest without dialing.
KEMP: A civilian took an aircraft off the field. No. A guy off the ground got an airplane up and he's...
ACTION: Kemp thumbs the number and puts the phone to his ear, and his eyes stay on the blip.
KEMP: This is Kemp, DHS, out here at Seattle Approach. I've got a ground worker who took an airliner off Sea-Tac, and he's up flying it right now.
ACTION: Nobody at the scopes says anything. The blip crawls off the coastline on Bishop's screen and out over the black of the water.
