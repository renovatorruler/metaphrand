# SKY KING — Scene: The Roll (the sky peak / "M83 moment", ≈ beat 13, Act 2)

*The hardest test: a near-wordless grace scene with no conflict to lean on — exactly where
AI prose usually slides into purple slop. Written through one lean model call (tools off,
dynamic context off). UNEDITED engine output, so you can see what it produced on the hard case.*

**The engine here is CONTRAST, not conflict:** the gap between how small/finished Birdy feels
and the vastness of the grace he stumbles into. The turn from wanting to die to wanting to live,
carried entirely by what his body does — the hesitation-then-commit on the roll, and the laugh
that breaks into crying — not by anything he says. The decision/"what happens if I land" is the
next scene (SCENE-da-negotiation.md); this scene is the FEELING.

*Residual tell to note (taste call): a couple of fragment-rhythm beats ("A short one. Surprised.";
"One light. Then more. Then a whole grid.") — mild, and arguably earned as filmable beats, but
it's the one place the AI cadence still shows. Left in for your call.*

INT./EXT. STOLEN TURBOPROP - COCKPIT - GOLDEN HOUR

The plane is small in a wide gold sky. Below, the city is hazed and far down.

BIRDY sits at the controls. Headset on. His face is slack. His hands move on instinct, no more.

The fuel gauge sits in the low quarter.

A burst of static.

                    BISHOP (V.O.)
          Still with me?

Birdy wets his lips. Takes a second to find his voice.

                    BIRDY
          Yeah.

He looks out the side window.

The sun sits low on the horizon. It fills the cockpit. The light is on his hands, his knees, the dash.

Far below, the city is coming on. One light. Then more. Then a whole grid of them in the blue.

Birdy watches the lights come up.

He pulls back on the yoke. The nose lifts. The plane climbs.

The haze drops away under him. Above it, the gold is clean.

Birdy's eyes are wet now. He keeps climbing.

His hand goes to the yoke. He banks left a little. Levels. Banks again. He watches the wing dip and come back.

He holds the yoke. He doesn't move.

Then he turns it all the way over.

The horizon rolls. Gold swings up the windshield, across the top, down the other side. The city wheels overhead and comes back under him.

The plane comes level.

Birdy lets out a breath that turns into a laugh. A short one. Surprised.

Then another. It keeps coming and he can't stop it, and it isn't only laughing anymore — his face is wet, his shoulders are going.

He laughs and cries at the same time, both hands over his mouth, the yoke loose between his knees.

                    BISHOP (V.O.)
          You okay up there?

Birdy nods. Then remembers to speak.

                    BIRDY
          Yeah. Yeah. I'm —

He stops. Wipes his face with the back of his wrist.

                    BIRDY (CONT'D)
          Okay.

He sits a moment. Breathing. The gold is going to dusk on his face.

He puts his hands back on the controls.

He banks the nose around, toward the field, toward the lights coming up below.

The sun drops under the horizon. The gold leaves his face.
