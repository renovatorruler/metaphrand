# SKY KING
### A feature treatment — *(working title; everything here is yours to change)*

A fictional film, no real names, inspired by the 2018 "Sky King" event. It exists to do one thing: make a person who feels invisible and finished believe — through love of a gentle man who reached the very edge and turned around — that they don't know the beauty they carry, and that they shouldn't go.

**Responsible-handling notes I'm holding throughout:** depression is shown as an illness, never a weakness or a choice; the method is never romanticized or shown as a doorway; the rescue is *connection* (a stranger's kindness, a wife's voice, a city looking up); and — the whole point of our version — **he lives.**

---

## Logline

A gentle, overlooked airport ramp worker who has quietly decided to disappear steals an empty plane meant to be his goodbye — and in one impossible hour above the city, met with unexpected kindness and shown unbearable beauty, discovers he is not nothing, and chooses, at the very edge, to come home.

## The man, and the people around him

- **BIRDY** (Eli) — the protagonist. A ramp agent: he pushes other people's planes onto runways and watches them lift off without him. The crew nicknamed him *Birdy* years ago — the man who works around airplanes and has never once flown. Soft-spoken, self-deprecating, helplessly kind, drowning behind a practiced "I'm fine." His one private joy is a homemade flight simulator. His one buried secret is that he means this flight to be the end.
- **MAYA** — his wife. Warm, grounded, frightened. She has found the pills he hid and cannot find the words.
- **BISHOP** — the air traffic controller who, when the impossible happens, refuses to treat him as a threat and just talks to him like a person. The steady voice that stays.
- **REYES** — a prosecutor, patched into the tower. Sharp, fair, and — when it counts — honest enough to respect him.
- **VOSS** — a tech founder watching the whole thing unfold live, who bets on Birdy in public.
- **DEZ** — Birdy's ramp buddy. Loud, loyal, the first to sense something is wrong.

## The transformation (the spine)

From *"I am a broken nobody, and the kindest thing I can do is disappear quietly"* → to *"I am capable of grace, I am held by people I never knew saw me, and that is worth coming back for."* The film admires everything people loved about the real man — the gentleness, the humor in the worst hour of a life, the hunger for beauty — and adds the thing this version earns: **the courage to choose to live.**

## The plotline

### ACT 1 — THE GROUND *(the cage, ~25 min, ending on the Doorway of No Return)*
Birdy's small, erasing life: the underpaid grind on the ramp, the debt he hides from Maya, the daily indignities he swallows with a smile. The love that can't reach him — Maya trying for one true sentence and getting "I'm fine"; Maya finding the pills and being too afraid to speak. His one secret aliveness: the flight simulator, where for a few seconds he is capable and free. His gentleness even as he sinks: pressing cash he can't spare on a friend worse off than him. Then the unbearable tenderness of a man saying goodbye to the world without anyone recognizing it — a perfect ordinary morning, a near-miss where Maya almost holds him here and the weight wins anyway. **Doorway of No Return:** alone on the night ramp, he boards an empty turboprop and — the simulator's hours suddenly, terribly real — takes off. Airborne. Past the point of return.

### ACT 2 — THE SKY *(the hour, ~50 min)*
The world erupts — alarms, fighter jets scrambled — and against the pressure to treat him as a target, **Bishop** insists on reaching the man, not the threat. Birdy, braced to be shouted at, is undone by kindness, and answers. The terror loosens into wonder; he asks where the famous orca is, and Bishop, breaking every rule, gives him the heading. **Maya** is brought to the tower and begs for the radio; Bishop keys her in. And then **the sky peak** — the gold light, the city tiny and shining beneath him, the orca, one clean impossible roll — pure freedom and grace, the thing no one watching will ever forget. In the middle of his own ending, he discovers he wants to *live.* *"I wasn't planning on landing… but maybe I could."*

But down to *what?* A cage? Birdy is gentle, not naïve. **Reyes** offers a too-clean "we won't charge you" deal to get him down fast — and he refuses it: *"I'm not stupid. I know I did something. Tell me realistically what it would be."* His honesty disarms her, and she levels with him: land it safe, no one hurt, and she'll fight for a suspended sentence — consequences, but not a cell. And the city is watching — **Voss** publicly offers him a job, a future, if he comes down. For the first time, Birdy has somewhere to come back *to.* **Doorway 2:** a man with no training decides to try to land.

### ACT 3 — COMING HOME *(~25 min)*
The descent is the hardest thing he's ever done — not the dying, the *living*: untrained, terrified, fighting the airplane and his own despair, talked toward the ground inch by inch by a stranger's voice and his wife's, the whole city looking up. He lands it — rough, and alive. The aftermath is honest, not saccharine: real consequences, the court, the arrest — but Reyes keeps her word, the suspended sentence holds, the cage is avoided; Voss's offer is real; he and Maya, the pills and the silence between them, begin, slowly, to actually talk. The coda is the folk hero *alive*: the recordings of his gentle voice become the thing strangers pass around — not a tragic meme this time but a living one — *he was at the very edge, and he turned around.* It ends not on a death but on a changed man choosing the ordinary life he almost left.

## Act 1 — scene by scene *(what the engine is fleshing now)*

1. **Cold open / sky tease.** Birdy (cockpit, gold hour) and Bishop (radio); Birdy wants one more minute of sky and puts off landing with a joke that lands like a goodbye — hard cut before we understand it. *(hook)*
2. **The ramp.** Birdy and Dez in the rain; Dez wants him to march in and demand his raise; Birdy takes the smaller hit and waves a jet out into a sky he never gets to fly. *(the cage + the underpay)*
3. **Home.** Maya wants one true sentence; gets "I'm fine"; the gap widens. *(the love that can't reach him)*
4. **The simulator.** Maya wants into the one room where he's still alive; glimpses the man she married fly for a few seconds before the shutter drops. *(the secret aliveness)*
5. **The kindness.** Birdy presses cash he can't spare on Dez; Dez, taking it, finally sees something's wrong. *(what people admire)*
6. **The discovery.** Maya, having found the pills, tries to say it and ask him to stay — and the words die; she says goodnight and hates herself. *(the near-miss)*
7. **The last ordinary morning.** Birdy gives Maya a perfect day she won't recognize as goodbye; she almost holds him here; the weight wins. *(the unbearable tenderness)*
8. **The eve.** Birdy sends Dez home with a warm lie and turns to the empty plane in the dark. *(the decision hardens)*
9. **Doorway of No Return.** The tower catches the rogue taxi; Bishop orders him to stop; Birdy lifts off, airborne and alone. *(the hijack — Act 1 break)*
