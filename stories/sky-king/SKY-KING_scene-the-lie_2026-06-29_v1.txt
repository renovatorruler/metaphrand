SKY KING — Scene: "THE LIE"  (top of Act 3 — where the real conflict starts)
29 June 2026 — v1   |   unedited engine output

[ Function: the system moves to bring him down by lying to him. Birdy catches
  it and withdraws — and we (knowing he'd just secretly started to want to
  live) feel that fragile thing almost go back out. Plants Reyes's turn.
  Holt = institutional indifference, the wall. ]

----------------------------------------------------------------

INT. TOWER CAB — NIGHT

Dark room. Console glow. Through the glass, the city is coming on light
by light below the black sky. BISHOP at the radio. MAYA stands behind a
strip of tape on the floor, a coat over her shoulders. HOLT stands at
the center, reading a clipboard. REYES holds a headset, not yet on.

HOLT
He's clear of the city now. Two-forty heading. I want him over water or
over field when this ends.

REYES
When it ends how?

HOLT
On the ground. That's the only number I have. Put it on him.

REYES
And the charges.

HOLT
Tell him there aren't any. Tell him he sets it down and walks to his
car. Whatever moves him.

REYES
That's not true.

HOLT
It doesn't have to be true at altitude. It has to get the gear down.

Maya looks at Holt. She doesn't speak. Bishop looks at Reyes. Reyes puts
the headset on.

INTERCUT — COCKPIT / TOWER CAB

Instrument glow on BIRDY's face. Out the side window, a fighter jet sits
off his wingtip, running lights steady. City lights slide under the nose.

REYES
Eli? My name's Reyes. I work for the county.

BIRDY
County.

REYES
I came up here to help you out of this. You bring it down now, tonight,
there's no case. No charges. I give you my word. You land, you walk away.

A beat. Birdy watches the jet off his wing.

BIRDY
Walk away.

REYES
To your car. Home. That's it.

Birdy's thumb moves on the yoke. The warmth that was in his voice earlier
is gone. Flat now.

BIRDY
You're a prosecutor.

REYES
I am.

BIRDY
And there's no case.

Pause. Reyes doesn't answer fast enough.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
I'm not stupid.

In the cab, Holt makes a small circle with his finger — keep going.
Bishop watches the strip readout. Maya's hand is at her mouth.

REYES
Eli —

BIRDY
Tell me the real one.

REYES
The real one.

BIRDY
The one where I land. What happens. Say that one.

Reyes looks at Holt. Holt shakes his head once. Reyes covers the mic.

REYES (off mic)
Give me something I can stand on.

HOLT
Get the plane down.

Reyes uncovers the mic. Nothing comes. In the cockpit Birdy waits, eyes
on the dark ahead.

BIRDY
Yeah.

He reaches up. Turns the volume knob down a notch. The cab radio thins.

REYES
Eli. Stay with me.

No answer. The carrier hum, then quiet. Birdy's hand comes off the radio
panel and back to the yoke. He doesn't look at the jet anymore. He looks
straight out, at the lights.

INT. TOWER CAB — NIGHT

Reyes pulls the headset half off one ear. He looks at it. Bishop is
already leaning into his own mic.

BISHOP
Eli, it's Bishop. I'm still here. Same as before.

Static. Maya is crying without sound, watching the back of Holt's head.

HOLT
He's still flying it. Heading's holding.
(to Reyes)
You did the job.

Reyes sets the headset on the console. He doesn't pick it back up.

HOLT (CONT'D)
He made his choice a long time before tonight.

Reyes looks at Maya across the tape line. He doesn't have anything to say
to her either.

INT. COCKPIT — NIGHT

Birdy, alone in the glow. The city below, fully lit now. He breathes out
slow. His hand stays on the yoke, steady.

FADE OUT.
