SKY KING — INT. SEATTLE APPROACH - RADAR ROOM - NIGHT

@@SCENE@@
ACTION: The radar room sits low and dark, and the scopes glow amber down the row of chairs.
ACTION: Bishop leans into his with the headset on one ear and a paper coffee cup going cold by his elbow.
ACTION: A slow primary return crawls up off the Sea-Tac surface, a soft blip with no data block beside it.
ACTION: Bishop watches it take a couple of sweeps, then he taps the glass next to it.
BISHOP: Hey, Price. You got anything working off the field down here, the southwest corner.
ACTION: Price is turned toward his own scope with a hand on his headset, talking to his airplane.
PRICE: Alaska twenty-six forty, descend and maintain eight thousand.
ACTION: Price rolls his chair a few inches toward Bishop without really looking over.
PRICE: That's not me. I got nothing down that low.
BISHOP: I don't have a strip on it. Nobody handed it to me either.
ACTION: Bishop pulls a strip out of the bay, turns it over, and sets it back.
BISHOP: You didn't take a handoff on something coming off the ground down there.
PRICE: Off the ground? No. Tower didn't ship me anything tonight.
ACTION: The blip crawls another sweep to the southwest, steady, with nothing hanging next to it.
BISHOP: It's squawking nothing. I don't even have an altitude on it.
PRICE: So it's just a primary.
BISHOP: Yeah. And it's moving.
ACTION: Bishop keys his mic and holds it a second.
BISHOP: Aircraft off the Seattle-Tacoma surface, southwest bound, this is Seattle Approach, ident and say your intentions.
ACTION: The frequency opens with a hiss, a mic keys somewhere out there, and a slow breath comes through it with an engine droning behind.
ACTION: Bishop waits. The carrier holds open. The breathing stays on the line.
ACTION: Then the mic clicks off and the frequency goes flat.
ACTION: Bishop drops his voice toward Price without turning his head.
BISHOP (WHISPER): He keyed up. He's just not saying anything back.
ACTION: Price finally looks over at Bishop's scope.
PRICE: He answered you?
BISHOP: He keyed the mic. There was somebody breathing on it.
ACTION: Bishop keys the mic again, flat and even.
BISHOP: Aircraft southwest of Sea-Tac, Seattle Approach, if you read me, go ahead.
ACTION: The carrier breaks open again, a breath, the engine under it, and then the click of the mic dropping.
ACTION: Bishop pulls the headset off his ear and turns toward the supervisor's desk across the room.
BISHOP: Hey. You want to come take a look at this one.
ACTION: The supervisor gets up from his desk without any hurry and comes across to stand behind Bishop's chair.
SUPERVISOR: What've you got.
BISHOP: Primary target, no data block, came up off the Sea-Tac surface. No strip, no flight plan on it. I hailed him twice, and he keys up and you can hear him breathing on it and then it just drops off again.
SUPERVISOR: Nobody worked a handoff.
BISHOP: Nothing. Price doesn't have him either.
PRICE: Not mine.
ACTION: The supervisor leans down closer to the glass and watches the blip take a sweep.
SUPERVISOR: Try him one more time.
ACTION: Bishop keys the mic once more.
BISHOP: Aircraft southwest bound off Sea-Tac, this is Seattle Approach. Go ahead and tell me what you're doing out there.
ACTION: The frequency opens. A breath comes first, close on the mic, and a soft voice finds its way onto it.
BIRDY (RADIO): Yeah, hi. Sorry to bug you here.
BIRDY (RADIO): I, uh... I kind of did a bad thing.
BIRDY (RADIO): I'm real sorry about it. This airplane's not... it's not really mine to have.
ACTION: Nobody in the room says anything. Price has both hands on his headset and he isn't talking to his airplane anymore.
ACTION: The supervisor straightens up and looks at the blip a second longer.
ACTION: He steps back to the desk and lifts the landline off its cradle.
SUPERVISOR: This is the one for DHS.
ACTION: He starts dialing without looking anything up.
ACTION: On the scope the amber blip crawls past the coastline and out over the black of the water, no data block beside it.
