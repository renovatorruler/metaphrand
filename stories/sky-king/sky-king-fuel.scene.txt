@@SCENE@@
ACTION: Gus drags the fuel hose toward the Q400's wing and snaps the nozzle into the underwing port.
ACTION: Birdy walks over with a coil of bonding cable already in his hand and a clip on the end.
BIRDY: Hold your start a sec, I'll get the bond on before you flow.
GUS: It's on. Front bumper, same as always.
ACTION: Birdy crouches at the truck's frame and follows the cable down to the clip.
BIRDY: Want it on bare metal, not paint. Takes ten seconds.
GUS: I'm two turns behind, Birdy. It's fine.
ACTION: Gus thumbs the deadman and the pump kicks over with a low whine.
ACTION: Birdy reaches under the truck and lifts the clamp; it slides free off a painted bracket with no bite at all.
BIRDY: Gus. This is sitting on paint and it shook loose. You're flowing with no bond.
GUS: It's always fine. Forty years that hose never lit up.
BIRDY: Fuel's moving right now. Stop it for me.
GUS: I am not stopping my turn for a ramper. Clip it back and quit hovering.
ACTION: Birdy steps to the deadman in Gus's fist and presses it; the pump winds down and the hose goes slack.
ACTION: Gus stares at the dead nozzle.
GUS: Are you kidding me.
ACTION: Birdy works the clamp open, scrapes it onto the bare steel of the frame rail, and clicks it tight.
BIRDY: There. Good metal now. You can go again.
ACTION: A baggage cart driver near the cargo door has stopped to watch.
ACTION: Gus keys his radio without looking at Birdy.
GUS (RADIO): Yeah, ramp, fueler at gate eleven. Your handler shut my pump down in the middle of a turn. I'm gonna blow the slot.
ACTION: Tanner crosses the ramp with a tablet, eyes on the screen.
TANNER: Who stopped the fueling.
BIRDY: That was me.
TANNER: You hit the deadman on an active fuel.
BIRDY: Bond was off the metal. I reset it.
TANNER: We're nine minutes over. When there's a problem you flag the fueler and let him handle it. That's a write-up.
ACTION: Tanner taps the tablet and turns it for Birdy to sign.
GUS: Hose was fine.
ACTION: Birdy signs with one finger and hands the tablet back.
BIRDY: Well. Never a dull day at the gas station.
TANNER: Don't do it again.
BIRDY: Yes sir.
ACTION: Tanner walks off reading the clock.
ACTION: Gus thumbs the deadman and the pump spins back up, fuel running through the clamped bond.
ACTION: Birdy picks up his bag tags and heads back to the cart.
