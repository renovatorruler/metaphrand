The plane drifts toward the sun.
BIRDY: It is like the air decided already, you know, you push the stick and you think you are doing it, but the wing was always going where the wing wants... anyway. I told everybody I would call when I landed.
BISHOP (V.O.): So call.
BIRDY does not answer.
He tips the yoke to the left.
The horizon rolls. The gold light sweeps across his face and floods the glass.
The plane comes through the full barrel and levels off.
BIRDY laughs out loud.
BISHOP (V.O.): What was that.
BIRDY: I just rolled it. The whole sky went under me and then it came back, and I felt it in my teeth, I felt my whole self get loud for one second... I have never felt that. Not once. Anyway.
BISHOP (V.O.): Roll it level. Stay level.
BIRDY: I wasn't planning on landing.
Silence on the headset.
BISHOP (V.O.): Say that again.
BIRDY: I wasn't planning on landing... but maybe I could.
He wipes his eyes with the back of his hand.
BISHOP (V.O.): I have the field lit. Bring it home.
BIRDY: Okay.
BIRDY: Okay. I want to.
He turns the plane out of the sun, toward the dark line of the ground.
The gold light stays on his face a moment longer, then lets him go.
