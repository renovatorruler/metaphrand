# SKY KING — Scene: The Honest Deal (≈ beat 14, late Act 2)

*Written through one lean model call (tools off, dynamic context off). The "...anyway"
verbal tic that flattened the earlier 13 scenes is gone; three distinct voices
(Reyes / Bishop / Birdy). One manual fix marked below.*

**Want/Wall/Turn:** Birdy wants the *truth*, not comfort, and would rather die than be
managed with a lie. Reyes wants him on the ground now and reaches for a clean lie to do it.
The lie nearly loses him. The turn: she throws out the script and tells him the unvarnished
truth — and being treated as a man owed the facts is what reaches him.

---

INT. TURBOPROP — COCKPIT — DUSK

The gold has gone orange in the windscreen. BIRDY, mid-forties, ramp coveralls, one hand loose on the yoke. Out the left window, a fighter jet sits off the wingtip, close enough to see the pilot's helmet.

The fuel gauge needle rests low. Birdy taps the glass with one finger. Watches the needle not move.

INTERCUT — TOWER CAB

BISHOP at the console, headset on, eyes on the radar. The slow blip crawls between two fast ones. REYES stands beside him, a hand already out for the spare mic. Bishop looks at her. Holds the mic a second before he gives it over.

REYES
(into mic)
Birdy. My name's Reyes. I work for the county.

BIRDY
County.

REYES
I'm going to make this simple. You bring it down now, right now, and there's no charges. Clean. You walk.

Birdy looks out at the jet. The pilot looks back.

BIRDY
You can do that.

REYES
I'm telling you I can.

A long beat. Birdy lets out a breath through his nose. Almost a laugh.

BIRDY
No.

REYES
No what.

BIRDY
I'm not — I'm slow, but I'm not stupid.

Reyes' jaw tightens. Bishop keeps his eyes on the radar.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
I took an airplane. I know I did something.

REYES
Birdy —

BIRDY
Don't. Please.

Birdy's thumb moves on the yoke.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Just tell me the real one. What it actually is.

Reyes lowers the mic. Looks at Bishop. Bishop doesn't help her. She brings it back up.

REYES
Okay.

She sets her feet.

REYES (CONT'D)
You're going to be charged. That's true no matter what I say up here. I can't make that go away and I'm not going to sit here and pretend.

INTERCUT — COCKPIT

Birdy goes still. Listening.

REYES (V.O.)
So here's what's real. You put this thing down. Nobody on the ground gets hurt.

BIRDY
Nobody. I picked the airplane that was empty. I checked.

REYES (V.O.)
I believe you.

TOWER CAB

REYES
You land it clean, and I go in front of a judge myself. I argue it. Suspended sentence. You'd answer for it. There'd be rules, people checking on you. But not a cell. That's the one I'd fight for.

A pause on the line. The blip holds.

REYES (CONT'D)
I can't promise the judge. I can promise I'll be standing there.

COCKPIT

Birdy is quiet a moment. He looks at the orange going out of the glass.   <!-- manual fix: was "looks at the porch-light gauge that isn't there" (unfilmable) -->

BIRDY
Rules.

REYES (V.O.)
Yeah.

BIRDY
Somebody checking on me.

REYES (V.O.)
Probably for a while.

Birdy nods to himself. Wipes his nose with the back of his hand.

BIRDY
That's the part that scares me. More than the —
(he stops)
The rest of it.

REYES (V.O.)
I know.

BIRDY
You don't, but. Thanks.

He puts his other hand on the yoke. Both hands now.

BISHOP (V.O.)
Birdy. You still with me?

BIRDY
Yeah, Bishop.

BISHOP (V.O.)
That offer she made. That's a true one.

Birdy watches the fighter pilot off his wing. Lifts two fingers off the yoke. The pilot, after a moment, lifts a gloved hand back.

BIRDY
Okay.

REYES (V.O.)
Okay what.

BIRDY
Tell me where you want me to put it.

He levels the wings. The needle sits low and steady. Out the windscreen, far off, the city starts to come on, light by light.
