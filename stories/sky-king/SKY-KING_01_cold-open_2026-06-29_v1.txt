COLD OPEN

EXT. SKY — DUSK

A small white turboprop, alone. It hangs in a sky going gold, very
high, very slow. Below it the world is soft and far — dark stands of
fir, an inlet gone copper, the long shadow of a mountain laid out flat
across the water.

No other planes. No sound but the engines, even and patient.

INT. TURBOPROP — COCKPIT — CONTINUOUS

A man at the controls. Thirty, maybe. An ordinary face. He sits easy,
one hand light on the yoke, the other in his lap. The low sun comes
straight through the windscreen and lays gold across him — his jaw, the
side of his neck, the worn knee of his work pants.

He isn't doing much. He's looking out at it.

The instruments glow. A headset crackles soft against his ear.

BISHOP (V.O.)
(calm, kind)
How you doing up there.

The man keys the mic. When he speaks it's quiet, almost shy.

BIRDY
Doing okay. It's real pretty up here.

He banks the plane a few degrees, slow, just to keep the light on his
face a little longer. The horizon tilts. The water swings under the
nose and steadies.

BISHOP (V.O.)
You're gonna want to start bringing her down soon. While you've still
got the light.

BIRDY
Yeah.
(a beat)
Couple more minutes.

He smiles a little. Out the side window the mountain has gone pink at
the top, the snow catching the last of it.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
You seeing this? The mountain.

BISHOP (V.O.)
I'm on the ground, partner. I'll take your word for it.

A small laugh from the man. He shakes his head, looking at it.

BIRDY
Man. I didn't think it'd look like this.

The sun touches the horizon. The gold deepens, goes orange, starts to
leave the cockpit one inch at a time. His face slides slowly into shadow.

He's quiet a while. Watching it go.

BISHOP (V.O.)
You still with me?

BIRDY
Still here.

He breathes out. He sets his free hand flat on the dash for a second,
then puts it back on the yoke.            <!-- fix: cut the "...something you were leaving" simile; let the gesture carry it -->

BIRDY (CONT'D)
(soft, easy)
Hey — thanks for staying up with me. Sorry to keep you so late.

BISHOP (V.O.)
That's what I'm here for.

The man nods to himself. He looks out one more time at the last of the
light on the water.

BIRDY
Okay.

HARD CUT TO:

BLACK.
