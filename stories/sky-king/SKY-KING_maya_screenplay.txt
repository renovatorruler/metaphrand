SKY KING — THE PATIENT WHO SEES HER (Act 1) — Doris

INT. CARE HOME - NIGHT

ACTION: The corridor light is off. A lamp burns in Doris's room at the end of the hall.
ACTION: Doris sits in a hard chair turned to face the door. She has her coat on. Her handbag is on her knees.
ACTION: A small radio plays low on the sill.
ACTION: Maya comes in with a mug, sets it on the table, and stops when she sees the coat.
MAYA: You've still got your coat on.
DORIS: It's cold in here.
ACTION: Maya crouches and folds back the corner of the bed. The chair does not move from the door.
MAYA: She said seven, didn't she. On the card, it said seven.
DORIS: It doesn't matter what it said.
ACTION: A radio host laughs at something. The line under it is not clear.
RADIO: —and that's the wrong number again, sorry love, third time tonight—
ACTION: Maya straightens. She looks at the handbag on Doris's knees, at the coat, at the door.
MAYA: I can call her. Two minutes. Just to see she got home all right, that's all.
ACTION: Maya reaches for the phone on the table.
DORIS: Leave it.
ACTION: Maya's hand stops over the phone.
MAYA: It's no trouble, Doris. It's a phone call.
DORIS: I said leave it. I don't want you calling anyone on my behalf.
ACTION: Maya lifts the phone anyway, slow, thumb over the buttons.
DORIS: You go home to nothing.
ACTION: Maya's thumb stops.
DORIS: I've watched you. You stay and you stay. You do the rounds twice. There's no one waiting up for you either, is there. That's why you're standing in my room at this hour offering to call my daughter.
ACTION: Maya looks at her. She does not answer.
DORIS: No. Thought not.
ACTION: The radio plays. Neither of them speaks.
ACTION: Maya sets the phone back down on the table. She does not dial.
ACTION: She takes off her cardigan, folds it over the back of the second chair, and sits.
DORIS: Your shift's done. I heard the cart go.
MAYA: I know.
ACTION: Maya reaches over and turns the radio up a little, so the room isn't quiet.
ACTION: Doris looks at the door. Then she looks at Maya. She doesn't tell her to go.
ACTION: They sit. The mug goes cold on the table between them.
ACTION: After a while Doris takes her handbag off her knees and puts it on the floor.
ACTION: Maya stands, unhurried. She takes Doris's coat by the collar and eases it off her shoulders.
MAYA: I'll leave the lamp on for you.
DORIS: Do what you like.
ACTION: Maya hangs the coat on the hook. She turns the chair, the one facing the door, so it faces the bed instead.
ACTION: At the door she stops, a beat longer than she needs to, her hand on the frame.
MAYA: Night, Doris.
ACTION: Doris doesn't answer. Maya goes. The lamp stays on. The radio plays to the two empty chairs.
