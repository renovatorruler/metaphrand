EXT. SKY — DUSK

A turboprop airliner — a twin-engine Bombardier Q400, no airline markings —
sails alone through a sky going gold. High, smooth, unhurried.

Below: dark forest, an inlet gone copper, a snow mountain pink at the top.

INT. Q400 COCKPIT — CONTINUOUS

BIRDY, around 30, in ground-crew clothes and an orange hi-vis vest, alone at
the controls. One hand easy on the yoke. He isn't doing much. He's looking
out at it.

A headset crackles soft against his ear.

BISHOP (V.O.)
(radio, calm)
How's it look up there.

BIRDY
...Real something. You ought to see this mountain. Pink all over the top.

BISHOP (V.O.)
I'll take your word for it. Stuck down here.

A small smile. He banks the plane a few degrees, slow, keeping the light on
his face. The horizon tips and steadies.

BIRDY
Water's like glass. You can see the whole way down.

BISHOP (V.O.)
Yeah?

BIRDY
Didn't think it'd look like this.

The plane sails on, smooth, golden. He's easy. A man enjoying a flight.

Then — out the left window — a shape slides into frame. Holds there.

A FIGHTER JET. Grey, armed, close enough to see the pilot's helmet turned
toward him.

And out the right window: ANOTHER one, riding his wing.

Birdy glances at them. Unbothered. Like they've been there a while.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Your boys are still out here with me.

BISHOP (V.O.)
They're just keeping you company.

A beat. The jets hold their stations off each wingtip. The little airliner
between them, drifting through the gold.

BISHOP (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Birdy. I need you to start thinking about bringing her down. While you've
still got the light.

Birdy watches the gold lying flat on the water, far below.

BIRDY
Yeah.
(gentle, almost apologetic)
I'm not ready to do that just yet.

He flies on. The jets ride his wings. The mountain holds, pink, in the glass.

BLACK.
