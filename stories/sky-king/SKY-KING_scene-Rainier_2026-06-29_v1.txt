INT./EXT. TURBOPROP COCKPIT — GOLDEN HOUR (AIRBORNE)

The plane is small in a lot of sky. Gold light comes in flat through
the windshield.

Out the left window: MOUNT RAINIER. Huge. Pink on the top. The bottom
is going blue. Far past it, the Olympic mountains, smaller, dark.

BIRDY sits at the controls. Headset on. He's looking at the mountain.
Both hands easy on the yoke. He banks the plane a little, slow, and the
mountain swings across the glass.

BIRDY
Look at that.

The radio is quiet a second.

BISHOP (V.O.)
You seeing Rainier?

BIRDY
Yeah. I'm seeing it.

He keeps the bank. The mountain holds in the window.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Loaded bags right under this thing for nine years. Never looked up.

BISHOP (V.O.)
Looking now.

BIRDY
Now I'm looking.

A small smile. He levels the wings, then eases them over again, lazy,
circling. The light is going.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Couple people are gonna be real disappointed in me when this is over.

A beat.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
That's just true. Not crying about it. Just — they will.

BISHOP (V.O.)
Okay.

BIRDY
You're not gonna argue?

BISHOP (V.O.)
No.

BIRDY
Good man.

He checks a gauge. Light, like it's any other night.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
This is jail, right? Like — the rest of it. They don't let you out
for this.

BISHOP (V.O.)
I don't know about all that.

BIRDY
I do a little. It's jail.

He laughs, short, no fear in it.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Long time to think about a pretty mountain.

The Olympics out past Rainier are nearly black now. The top of Rainier
is still pink.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Hey. Real question.

BISHOP (V.O.)
Go ahead.

BIRDY
If I set this thing down nice. Greaser. Don't bend it.

He pats the yoke, easy.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Think somebody'd give me a flying job after? I'm basically flying it.

A second. He's half-grinning, waiting.

Static. Bishop doesn't answer.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Yeah. No.

He nods to himself. Looks out at the mountain.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Not after this. Not me.

He says it the same easy way he said everything else. Then he taps the
glass with one knuckle, like changing the subject.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Worth a shot.

BISHOP (V.O.)
You're doing fine up there.

BIRDY
I got a couple screws loose. Always have.

He says it plain, like a man reading a weather report.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Just so it's on the record.

BISHOP (V.O.)
It's on the record.

BIRDY
Thanks.

He banks again. The mountain comes back around through the windshield,
gold gone soft on it.

BISHOP (V.O.)
I'm right here, Birdy.

BIRDY
I know it.

He flies. The pink is leaving the top of the mountain now. He keeps the
slow turn going, around and around, the small plane and the big dark
mountain, the last of the light.

He doesn't say anything else for a while.

Neither does Bishop.

The plane keeps circling.
