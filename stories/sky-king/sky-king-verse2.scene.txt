@@SCENE@@
ACTION: The command post is a windowless room lit blue by monitors, and REYES sits across a folding table from WARD, a heavyset man in a reflective ramp vest, his badge still clipped to it.
ACTION: WARD keeps his eyes down on the table where his hands are folded.
REYES: I'm not asking you to say anything you don't want to say. I just want to get a picture of him.
WARD: He worked for me nine years. Good with the equipment.
REYES: What about lately. Last couple weeks, last month. Anything going on with him that you noticed.
WARD: Not that he told me.
REYES: I don't mean told. I mean anything change for him. At work, I don't know, something come up that didn't go his way.
ACTION: WARD moves a paper clip a half inch and does not look up.
WARD: It's a ramp. Same thing every day.
REYES: Sure.
WARD: I don't know what you want me to say.
ACTION: WARD looks past her at the wall, and the room goes to daylight.
ACTION: The ramp office is a plywood booth with a window onto the tarmac, sun coming in hard, a jet parked out on the concrete beyond the glass. WARD stands at a corkboard with a printed sheet in his hand, younger by the two weeks.
ACTION: MARQUEZ leans in the doorway, twenties, a lanyard and a clean vest, a coffee going cold in his fist.
MARQUEZ: You put the lead posting up yet, or.
WARD: Going up now.
ACTION: WARD pins the sheet to the board and steps back from it.
MARQUEZ: I put my name on it. The other day. I don't know if that's, if you saw.
WARD: I saw it.
MARQUEZ: Okay. I just didn't want it to seem like I was, you know.
WARD: You're the one that wants it. That's the sheet.
ACTION: WARD turns and BIRDY is at the fuel truck out the window, late thirties, hearing protection around his neck, coiling a hose without hurry.
ACTION: WARD steps out onto the ramp and the noise comes up, and BIRDY looks over.
WARD: Got a minute.
BIRDY: Yeah.
WARD: I gave the lead to Marquez. Signed it this morning.
ACTION: BIRDY sets the hose end down and wipes his hands on his pants.
BIRDY: Oh. Good. He'll be good at that.
WARD: You didn't put in.
BIRDY: No. No, it's alright. He wanted it.
ACTION: WARD stands there a second longer than he needs to.
WARD: I need somebody to bring him up on the whole thing. The board, the turns, all of it. You know it better than anybody out here.
BIRDY: Sure. No problem.
WARD: You don't have to.
BIRDY: No, it's fine. Kid's sharp. Won't take long.
ACTION: MARQUEZ comes out from the booth and stops a few feet off, hands in his pockets.
MARQUEZ: Hey. I don't want it to be weird, or.
ACTION: BIRDY steps over and puts out his hand, and MARQUEZ takes it.
BIRDY: Congratulations. You earned it, putting your name down.
MARQUEZ: Thanks. I mean it, I'll take whatever you got.
BIRDY: We'll start Monday. It's easy once you see it.
ACTION: BIRDY picks the hose back up and goes back to the truck, and WARD watches him do it.
ACTION: The light drops out and the monitors are blue again, REYES still across the table, waiting.
REYES: Anything?
ACTION: WARD keeps his eyes on his hands.
WARD: He was a good worker. Steady.
ACTION: A radio on the table breaks up with a controller's voice, low under the room.
CONTROLLER (RADIO): Post, we still got him west of the field, no change.
ACTION: REYES lets it sit and does not write anything down.
REYES: Steady.
ACTION: WARD nods once and does not look at her.
