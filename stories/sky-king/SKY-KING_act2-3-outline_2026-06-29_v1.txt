================================================================
THE CLOCK  (the timing thing you flagged)
================================================================

The whole flight is ONE continuous stretch — about an hour, late
evening. The light runs in a single unbroken arc and never resets:

    golden hour  ->  SUNSET (the roll, the peak)  ->  dusk  ->  lit night

Two rules from that:

  • The sun sets ONCE. After the roll, the gold is gone — from there
    it's dusk, then night. No later scene goes back to golden light.

  • It never "just goes dark." Night here is LIT: the city below is a
    field of lights, the runway lights ahead, the instrument glow, the
    last blue in the sky. We always keep seeing.

(Act 1 — his ground life — happens earlier, over ordinary days. The
continuous clock starts only at takeoff, the end of Act 1.)

    for the first time he wants to live. This is the visual peak — the
    last blaze floods the cockpit; as he levels, the sun is gone.
