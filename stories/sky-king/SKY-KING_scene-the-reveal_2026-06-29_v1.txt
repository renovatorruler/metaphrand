INT. INCIDENT COMMAND POST — NIGHT

A converted conference room gone to war. Folding tables shoved into an L.
Maps weighted down with coffee cups. Three laptops, a wall monitor split
between a sectional chart and a radar repeater. On the repeater: one slow
blip, dragging a tail of dots. Two faster blips ride its flanks.

Phones ring and go unanswered. A man in a suit murmurs into a cell with a
finger in his other ear. A MILITARY LIAISON — flight suit, sleeves
rolled, headset around his neck — stands at the wall, watching the track,
very still.

HOLT, fifties, tie at half mast, sits at the head of the L with a legal
pad he is not writing on. He's been here since dark and it shows in the
gray of him.

The door bangs. REYES comes through fast from the radar room, still
wearing the spare headset, the cord swinging loose where he tore it from
the jack. He doesn't sit.

REYES
He's not coming down.

HOLT
I heard the floor.

REYES
He asked me what he's coming down to. I told him the truth, because you
told me to tell him the truth. So now I need something that isn't.

Holt looks at him. Says nothing.

REYES (CONT'D)
Give me a number. Years. A facility near his mother. Anything I can hand
the man so he believes the ground is better than the air. He'll take it.
He wants to take it — I heard it tonight, he's reaching for it.

HOLT
There's nothing to authorize, Reyes.

REYES
Then authorize something.

HOLT
It's not at my table.

A phone rings. Nobody moves. Holt lets it ring twice, three times, before
a young AGENT lifts it and steps away.

REYES
Whose table.

HOLT
(a small lift of the chin — upward, the ceiling, the floors above)
The call's been made.

Reyes stands very still. Behind him the liaison shifts his weight, eyes
never leaving the screen.

REYES
Made how.

Holt sets the pad down square with the table edge. Lines it up.

HOLT
He's away from people. That's the first good thing all night. The
aircraft's contained — it's got two escorts and a fuel gauge, and the
gauge is honest. Nobody's storming anything. Nobody's deciding anything.
(beat)
A man flies a plane he can't land. That tends to find its own bottom.

A beat. Reyes doesn't move.                  <!-- fix: cut Reyes's echo "Its own bottom." -->

HOLT (CONT'D)
We let the situation resolve.

Reyes takes that in. Out on the wall, the slow blip crawls a quarter inch
across the chart. The two fast ones stay glued to it.

REYES
You don't want him on the ground.

HOLT
I didn't say that.

REYES
A trial. Cameras. Some kid who carried suitcases, in a box, telling a
jury how easy it was to walk onto a ramp and take a plane. That's the
thing you can't have. So you'd rather the gauge.

Holt holds his eyes. Doesn't blink. Doesn't speak.

REYES (CONT'D)
Say it's not that.

The liaison's radio crackles low against his hip. Holt glances at it,
then back. Picks up his coffee, finds it cold, sets it down.

HOLT
He made his choice when he started the engines.

REYES
(quiet, flat, laying it on the table)
You're going to fly him until the tanks are dry, or you're going to put
something in him if he turns the wrong way — and either one of those is
cleaner to you than a courtroom.

Nothing from Holt. He looks at the legal pad. Squares it again, a half
inch it didn't need.

That is the whole answer.

The LIAISON presses the headset to his ear. Listens. His face doesn't
change.

MILITARY LIAISON
(into the boom, clipped)
Copy. Reposition the flight to trail. In behind him, line astern.
(listens)
Master arm. Hold. Cleared to engage if he comes off heading toward the
city.

He says it the way a man reads a checklist.

On the wall monitor: the two fast blips, riding the slow one's wings,
peel back. Slide down its flanks. Settle into the dead air directly
behind it — line astern. Two hunters dropped into trail, matching the
slow thing's heading, waiting on it.

The room doesn't react. Phones ring. The suit murmurs. The agent reads a
number off a sticky note. Ordinary night.

Reyes stares at the screen. The blips. The geometry of it — three dots in
a row now, the back two faster than the front. He understands exactly
what he's looking at.

REYES
(barely)
They're behind him.

HOLT
Standard.

Reyes turns from the screen. His eyes go around the room, fast, looking
for a door, a phone, a man, anything — and snag.

In the corner, mounted high, a muted television. Local news. A reporter
on a dark hillside, mouth moving, no sound. Behind her, civilians — a
dozen, two dozen — standing in a parking lot with their heads tipped
back, phones up, all of them aimed at the same patch of sky. Watching.
Faces lit blue by their own screens.

A chyron crawls under them. Reyes reads it.

He looks at the muted faces a beat longer than he should.

When he turns back to Holt, something in him has changed gears — not
calm, the opposite of calm, but pointed now.

REYES
(not to Holt, already moving)
Okay.

HOLT
Reyes.

But Reyes is already at the door, pulling the spare headset off, the cord
dragging. He doesn't look back at the table, at Holt, at the cold call
that's been made.

On the wall, the slow blip drags its quarter inch. Behind it, in trail,
the two fast ones hold their place, weapons hot, waiting for the gauge to
be honest.
