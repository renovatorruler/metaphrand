EXT. SKY — DUSK

A twin-engine Bombardier Q400, no airline markings, flies alone through a sky
turning gold. High and steady.

Below are dark forest, an inlet the color of copper, and a snow mountain pink
at the top.

INT. Q400 COCKPIT — CONTINUOUS

BIRDY, around 30, sits alone at the controls in ground-crew clothes and an
orange hi-vis vest. One hand on the yoke. He isn't doing much. He's looking
out the window.

A headset crackles soft against his ear.

BISHOP (V.O.)
(radio, calm)
How's it look up there.

BIRDY
...Real something. You ought to see this mountain. Pink all over the top.

BISHOP (V.O.)
I'll take your word for it. Stuck down here.

He smiles a little. He banks the plane a few degrees, slow, so the light stays
on his face. The horizon tips, then steadies.

BIRDY
Water's like glass. You can see the whole way down.

BISHOP (V.O.)
Yeah?

BIRDY
Didn't think it'd look like this.

The plane flies on. He's easy, a man enjoying a flight.

Then a shape slides into view outside the left window and stays there.

A FIGHTER JET. Grey, armed, close enough to see the pilot's helmet turned
toward him.

Out the right window, another one rides his wing.

Birdy glances at them. He doesn't seem surprised, like they've been there a
while.

BIRDY (CONT'D)
Your boys are still out here with me.

BISHOP (V.O.)
They're just keeping you company.

The jets hold their stations off each wingtip, the little airliner between
them.

BISHOP (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Birdy. I need you to start thinking about bringing her down. While you've
still got the light.

Birdy watches the light flat on the water, far below.

BIRDY
Yeah.
(gentle, almost apologetic)
I'm not ready to do that just yet.

He flies on. The jets ride his wings. The mountain's still in the glass, pink
at the top.
