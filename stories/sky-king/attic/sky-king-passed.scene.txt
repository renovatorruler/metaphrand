ACTION: Ward stands in the office doorway and waves Birdy in past the time clock.
WARD: Shut that, would you. Wind's blowing the roster all over.
ACTION: Birdy pulls the door to and stays standing by the filing cabinet.
BIRDY: You want me to sit, or.
WARD: No, this is quick. Sit if you want.
ACTION: Birdy sits on the edge of the folding chair.
WARD: You saw the board went up.
BIRDY: Saw Marquez's name on the chief line. Yeah.
WARD: Yeah. That's, uh. That's what this is.
ACTION: Ward moves a stack of paper from one side of the desk to the other.
WARD: I gave it to Marquez. It's his as of Monday.
BIRDY: Okay.
WARD: I sat on that slot better part of two weeks. Longer. Kept it open on the roster with nobody in it, which the schedulers hate, believe me, they called me twice about it.
BIRDY: I bet.
WARD: I was waiting on it to sort itself. Waiting on a guy to come in and, I don't know. And it didn't, and I couldn't keep it hanging anymore, so.
BIRDY: No. You can't leave a line open like that.
WARD: No.
ACTION: Ward looks at Birdy and waits.
WARD: You got nothing on that.
BIRDY: Marquez'll do fine. He's steady.
WARD: He's steady, that's it?
BIRDY: He shows up. That's most of it.
ACTION: Ward taps the pen twice against the desk and stops.
WARD: You're the best hand I got out there. You know that. I'm not — I'm just saying it because it's true, not to, you know.
BIRDY: That's a nice thing to say.
WARD: It's not a nice thing, it's the roster. I put you on the hard turns because you don't drop stuff. That's not me being nice.
BIRDY: Well. Thanks anyway.
ACTION: Ward sets the pen down and rubs the back of his neck.
WARD: Here's the other part. Marquez has run a crew maybe twice, and not the way we do it, not the de-ice cards, not the weight sheets. He's gonna be lost his first week.
BIRDY: Probably his first two.
WARD: So I want you to bring him up on it. The chief stuff. Walk him through the closeouts, the paperwork, the way you square the bravo when it backs up. You know it cold.
ACTION: Birdy looks at the floor for a second.
BIRDY: Yeah. I can get him up to speed.
WARD: You'd do that.
BIRDY: It's not a big thing. I'll ride with him on the closeouts a few shifts till he's got it.
WARD: A few shifts, huh?
BIRDY: However long it takes. He'll get it.
ACTION: Ward sits with that and doesn't pick the pen back up.
WARD: Alright.
WARD: I mean it, that's — that's a good thing you're doing there.
ACTION: Birdy stands and squares the folding chair back under the desk.
BIRDY: Start him Monday?
WARD: Monday.
BIRDY: Okay.
ACTION: A radio on the desk crackles.
VOICE (RADIO): Ramp, we got the inbound off the bravo early, need a set of hands down here.
BIRDY: That's me.
ACTION: Birdy opens the door and the wind takes the roster off the desk.
WARD: Leave it, leave it, I got it.
ACTION: Birdy walks out onto the ramp and does not look back, and Ward stands over the scattered paper without picking it up.
