SKY KING — THE CALL (Act 2)

INT. FBI CAR / Q400 FLIGHT DECK - NIGHT

ACTION: The back of a federal car at the curb, dome light off, blue and red sliding across the ceiling.
ACTION: Maya sits with a phone pressed to her ear, an agent crouched in the open door, another one behind the glass watching her.
ACTION: A headset on the console, a hand holding a card up to the window with words on it she isn't reading.
ACTION: The cockpit of the Q400, panel glow green on Birdy's face, the black water below, a fighter's nav light steady off the wing.
BISHOP (RADIO): Ma'am, I've got him on the line now. Go ahead. He can hear you.
ACTION: A click. Static, then the hum of an engine come through the phone.
BIRDY (RADIO): There she is. Hi.
ACTION: Maya turns her face away from the agent in the door.
MAYA: ...Sam.
BIRDY (RADIO): Yeah. It's me. I know, it's late, I'm sorry.
MAYA: Where are you.
BIRDY (RADIO): I'm okay. That's the thing, I want you to know I'm okay, I didn't mean to scare everybody like this, it kind of got away from me.
MAYA: They put me in a car. There's a whole — they've got a car and a guy telling me stuff to say.
BIRDY (RADIO): Left throttle, there we go. Sorry. You don't have to say anything.
MAYA: I'm not gonna say the thing on the card.
BIRDY (RADIO): No. Okay.
ACTION: The agent in the doorway leans in, points at the card. Maya doesn't look at it.
MAYA: The spare room's still got your boxes in it. I never did anything with it.
BIRDY (RADIO): I'll get to those.
MAYA: You said that.
BIRDY (RADIO): I did say that. Altitude's fine, hang on. ...Yeah.
MAYA: I stopped, you know. Waiting up. A while ago. I'd just go to bed.
BIRDY (RADIO): You should. It's real late.
MAYA: That's not — I'm not telling you so you'll feel bad. I don't know why I'm telling you.
BIRDY (RADIO): It's alright.
ACTION: Off the wing, the fighter drifts closer, then holds. Birdy watches it.
BIRDY (RADIO): There's a couple planes up here with me. They're being real nice about it.
MAYA: Sam.
BIRDY (RADIO): It's fine, they're just keeping me company. I got it kind of figured, honey. I didn't figure anybody'd — it's a lot of fuss for nothing.
MAYA: Quit talking like that.
BIRDY (RADIO): I'm just a guy who loads bags. I always thought if I, you know. It'd be quiet. Nobody'd have to do all this.
MAYA: I came.
BIRDY (RADIO): ...What?
MAYA: I got in the car. I'm here. They called and I got up and I came, so.
ACTION: Silence on the line, just the engine drone.
MAYA: I'm mad at you. I want you to know I'm real mad at you.
BIRDY (RADIO): I know.
MAYA: But I came.
ACTION: Birdy's hand rests on the throttle, not moving. The water below, the dark.
BIRDY (RADIO): Yeah.
ACTION: He wipes his face with the back of his wrist, keeps his eyes on the panel.
BIRDY (RADIO): Fuel's, uh. Fuel's getting there. It's getting down there.
MAYA: So then come back.
BIRDY (RADIO): I don't know how that goes now. I kind of already — I let it go, is the thing.
MAYA: Okay but the boxes.
BIRDY (RADIO): I ain't forgot about 'em.
MAYA: I'm not moving 'em. They're yours. You come get your boxes.
ACTION: A long breath comes through the phone, uneven.
BIRDY (RADIO): I got something to tell you. It's dumb. It's about up here.
MAYA: Tell me.
BIRDY (RADIO): Not on the — I want to tell it to you. Like sitting down.
ACTION: Maya presses the phone harder to her ear, her other hand flat against her mouth.
BIRDY (RADIO): I don't want to die. I didn't — huh. I think I want to come down.
MAYA: Okay.
BIRDY (RADIO): I don't know if I can. I'm just saying it.
MAYA: Okay. Okay, then say it again.
ACTION: The panel glow on his face, the fighters holding, the black water sliding under the nose.
ACTION: Maya in the back of the car, the door open, the lights turning over her, the dark sky somewhere above all of it.
