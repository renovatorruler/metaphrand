INT. TRACON RADAR ROOM — NIGHT

A long room with no windows. The only real light comes from the scopes —
twelve of them in a row, washing the faces above them in a sick
green-gray. Headsets murmur. Somewhere a printer ticks out a strip and
nobody tears it off.

A SUPERVISOR walks the row slow, hands behind his back, stopping at each
station, moving on. He has been doing this for an hour. It shows in his
shoulders.

At the third scope: BISHOP, fifties, headset on, posture easy. A paper
coffee cup sits by his right hand with a skin on it, cold. On his screen
one slow PRIMARY RETURN crawls west, and snugged to either flank of it,
two faster blips that hold their distance and never drift.

In the corner, mounted high, a TV with the sound off. A news-chopper
feed: a small turboprop, far away, black against the last of the dusk
over water. Nobody in the room looks at it anymore.

REYES, forties, stands apart by the back wall. Suit jacket still on. A
county lanyard at his sternum, out of place. A cell phone pressed hard to
his ear, a finger plugging the other one.

REYES
(low, into the phone)
I'm here. I'm in the room.

He listens. His eyes go to the main scope. To the two blips pinned on
the slow one's wings.

REYES (CONT'D)
No. I haven't said a word to him.

HOLT (V.O.)
(filtered, even, far away)
He's been on that radio an hour. He likes the sound of a kind voice.
You've got a kind voice, Reyes. It's why you win juries.

Reyes turns his back to the room.

REYES
What is it exactly you want me to tell him.

HOLT (V.O.)
I want him on the ground. Whatever gets him there. You're better with
people than the rest of these men. That's the whole reason you're
standing where you're standing.

REYES
That's not really an answer.

HOLT (V.O.)
It's the only one I've got for you. Get him down. I'll be right here.

The line stays open. Holt does not hang up. Reyes lowers the phone, looks
at it, slides it into his jacket pocket with the call still live.

He crosses to Bishop's station.

REYES (CONT'D)
They told you what they want.

BISHOP
They told me to let you on the frequency.

Bishop comes up with a spare headset on a coiled cord. Reyes takes it,
puts it on crooked. Bishop straightens the band with two fingers, like
dressing a kid for the cold.

BISHOP (CONT'D)
Boom mic at the corner of your mouth. Not in front of it. This lights
amber, you're keyed up and he hears you. You let off, you hear him.
There's a half-second lag, you'll want to rush it.

Reyes nods. Bishop looks at him, a long plain look.

BISHOP (CONT'D)
He's polite. Whatever you came in here thinking he is — he isn't that.

Reyes presses the amber key. It lights.

REYES
(into the mic)
This is Reyes. I've come down to the radar room to talk with you a
minute. How're you doing up there.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(filtered, warm)
Evening. Real nice of you to come down. Sorry to keep everybody up so
late on my account.

REYES
Nobody's upset with you. We just want to get you down safe. That's all
we want.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(gentle)
You don't sound like you mean it, friend. That's alright. I'm not sore.
Somebody asked you to come say it, and you came and said it. You did
good. I just don't want you catching heat over me.

Reyes glances at the back of Bishop's head. Bishop doesn't turn. Reyes
lets off the key. A beat. He keys it again.

REYES
Can I ask you what you do, down on the ground.

BIRDY (V.O.)
I move bags. Ramp side. Tow the carts, load the belly, all that. Twelve
years I've been throwing other folks' luggage.

REYES
Then you know I'm not from the airline.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(a soft laugh)
No, sir. You're the only fella tonight who came on sounding like he
reads things for a living. What is it you do.

REYES
I'm a prosecutor. County. They walked me in here because I'm supposed to
be good at talking.

A long lag. The slow blip crawls. Bishop's eyes don't leave the scope.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(slow, pondering)
A prosecutor. Huh. Well. Then maybe you're the right one to ask.

REYES
Ask me.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(quiet, almost apologetic)
I keep doing the arithmetic up here. What I took. Where I took it. And I
keep landing on the same place. That's the part I keep landing on. They
gonna put me in a cell for the rest of my life? My whole life? You can
just tell me. I'd rather a man tell me than not.

Reyes opens his mouth. Nothing comes. His thumb stays on the key. The
amber holds.

In his jacket pocket, the live phone. He doesn't look at it.

REYES
(quiet)
I was sent in here to tell you it won't be what you're afraid of.

BIRDY (V.O.)
And?

REYES
And I'm not going to tell you that.

He lets off the key. Wipes his mouth with the back of his hand. Keys it.

REYES (CONT'D)
You took a federal aircraft. That's not a county thing, not a fine, not
a bad couple of years. That's the United States on the other side of the
table, and they will charge it heavy. Aircraft, the airspace, all of it.
I'd be lying if I sat here and told you the top of that isn't very bad.
It is. The worst of it is about as bad as it gets short of someone
getting hurt.

A silence on the band. The half-second lag, and then more than the lag.

REYES (CONT'D)
And here's the part I won't dress up. I came down here with nothing in my
hands. Nobody gave me a thing to offer you. No number. No paper. I can't
stand here and promise you how it comes out, because no one upstairs has
let me promise you anything. I won't make one up. You asked a man to tell
you. That's me telling you.

A long beat. When Birdy comes back, his voice is lower, and there's
something near gratitude in it.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(gentle)
Well. There it is. Thank you. Everybody keeps telling me it's gonna be
fine. All night. Real kind people, every one. Gonna be fine, gonna be
fine. You're the first one who wouldn't say it. I think I needed one
fella who wouldn't say it.

REYES
I'm not trying to scare you down. I'm telling you the truth because
you're owed it.

BIRDY (V.O.)
I know you are. And you see now why I'm not in any hurry to put her on
the deck. You just laid it out plain. Down to what, friend? Down to
that? The rest of my whole life in a box, and nobody's even got a piece
of paper for me. A man'd have to be in an awful hurry to come down to
that.

Reyes closes his eyes. Keys it.

REYES
I hear you.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(warm again)
Don't you carry it. You did your part fine. Go on back up and tell the
man who sent you that you said it true. Don't let him put it on you. I
don't want you catching heat over me. None of this is your doing.

Reyes stands there with the amber lit and nothing left to send. His hand
is shaking just slightly on the key.

Bishop, without turning from his scope, reaches over and gently lifts
Reyes's finger off the key with one of his own. The amber dies.

BISHOP
That's enough. Don't make him comfort you. He's got enough to do.

Reyes pulls the headset down around his neck. He's left standing in the
green-gray with empty hands.

In his jacket pocket, muffled through the cloth, a voice. Patient.
Waiting.

HOLT (V.O.)
(through the jacket)
Reyes... Well?

Reyes doesn't reach for it. Lets it talk to the inside of his coat.

On the scope, the slow primary return crawls west, two blips pinned to
its flanks, holding, never drifting.

Bishop turns back to his screen.
