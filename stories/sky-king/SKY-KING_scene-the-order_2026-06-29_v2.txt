INT. TRACON — NIGHT

Banks of dark glass. A single radar return crawls the screen, two
smaller blips pinned to its sides. HOLT stands behind BISHOP's chair,
arms folded. REYES, suit jacket gone, tie loosened, hangs back by the door.

Holt doesn't turn around.

HOLT
I want him on the ground.

REYES
What am I telling him.

HOLT
Whatever puts him down.

Reyes waits. Holt watches the blip.

HOLT (CONT'D)
You're better with people than I am.

Bishop holds up the spare headset without looking. Reyes takes it. Puts
it on slow.

REYES
(into the mic)
Eli. My name's Reyes. I'm with the county.

Static. Then —

BIRDY (V.O.)
(warm, far off)
Evening. Sorry to drag you out so late.

Reyes almost smiles. Looks at the floor.

REYES
You didn't drag me anywhere. Listen — I want you to bring it down.
Whatever you're carrying tonight, that's an illness. Nobody's looking
to bury you in it. We work it out on the ground. People help with this.
That's all it is.

He swallows on the last word. His eyes go off the glass, to the dark
window.

BIRDY (V.O.)
That's real kind.

REYES
I mean it.

A beat. The blip drifts.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(gentle)
You don't sound like you mean it, friend.

Reyes goes still. Holt's head turns, just slightly.

REYES
Eli —

BIRDY (V.O.)
Naw, it's all right. I'm not sore. You did good. You said it nice.

Reyes opens his mouth. Nothing.

BIRDY (V.O.) (CONT'D)
I'm just a broken guy, got a few screws loose. You're doing your job. I
don't want you catching heat over me.

REYES
(low)
You don't have to look after me.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(a small laugh)
Habit, I guess.
(softer)
I'm not quite ready to bring it down just yet. But thank you. Honest.

Reyes stands there. The hedging gone out of him. Nothing left to sell.

He slides the headset off. Holds it in both hands, looking at it.

Holt watches him.

HOLT
Well?

Reyes doesn't answer. Bishop quietly takes the headset back.

On Reyes — eyes off the glass, somewhere past the room.
