INT. TRACON RADAR ROOM — NIGHT

A long room with no windows. The only real light comes from the scopes —
twelve of them in a row, washing the faces above them in a sick
green-gray. Headsets murmur. Somewhere a printer ticks out a strip and
nobody tears it off.

A SUPERVISOR walks the row slow, hands behind his back, stopping at each
station, moving on. He has been doing this for an hour. It shows in his
shoulders.

At the third scope: BISHOP, fifties, headset on, posture easy. A paper
coffee cup sits by his right hand with a skin on it, cold. On his screen
one slow PRIMARY RETURN crawls west, and snugged to either flank of it,
two faster blips that hold their distance and never drift. He watches it
the way you watch a kettle.

In the corner, mounted high, a TV with the sound off. A news-chopper
feed: a small turboprop, far away, black against the last bruise of dusk
over water. A scroll runs under it. Nobody in the room looks at the TV
anymore.

REYES, forties, stands apart by the back wall. Suit jacket still on. A
county lanyard hangs at his sternum, out of place, the laminate catching
scope-light. He has a cell phone pressed hard to his ear and a finger
plugging the other one.

REYES
(low, into the phone)
I'm here. I'm in the room.

He listens. His eyes go to the main scope across the dark. To the two
blips pinned on the slow one's wings.

REYES (CONT'D)
No. I haven't said a word to him.

HOLT (V.O.)
(filtered, even, far away)
You don't have to make it complicated. He's been on that radio an hour.
He likes the sound of a kind voice. You've got a kind voice, Reyes. It's
why you win juries.

Reyes turns his back to the room. Lowers his own voice further.

REYES
What is it exactly you want me to tell him.

HOLT (V.O.)
I want him on the ground. Whatever gets him there. You're better with
people than the rest of these men. That's the whole reason you're
standing where you're standing.

A beat. Reyes works his jaw. Across the room a CONTROLLER laughs once at
something, quiet, and is hushed.

REYES
That's not really an answer.

HOLT (V.O.)
It's the only one I've got for you. Get him down. I'll be right here.

The line stays open — a soft electric nothing. Holt does not hang up. He
waits, somewhere, in Reyes's ear.

Reyes lowers the phone. Looks at it. Doesn't end the call. Slides it
into his jacket pocket with the call still live, the way you'd carry
something you didn't want to touch.

He crosses to Bishop's station. Stands at his shoulder. Bishop doesn't
look up from the slow return.

REYES (CONT'D)
They told you what they want.

BISHOP
They told me to let you on the frequency.

Bishop reaches under the console, comes up with a spare headset on a
coiled cord. Holds it out without ceremony. Reyes takes it. Turns it
over once, finds nothing about it familiar, puts it on crooked. Bishop
reaches over and straightens the band on his head with two fingers, like
dressing a kid for the cold.

BISHOP (CONT'D)
Boom mic sits at the corner of your mouth. Not in front of it.

He guides the little arm down. Taps a backlit key on the panel.

BISHOP (CONT'D)
This lights amber, you're keyed up and he hears you. You let off, you
hear him. Don't talk over the top of him. There's a half-second lag,
you'll want to rush it.

Reyes nods. His hand hovers near the key. Doesn't press it.

Bishop finally looks at him. A long, plain look. Behind it, the slow
blip crawls another pixel west.

BISHOP (CONT'D)
He's polite. Whatever you came in here thinking he is — he isn't that.
Just so you know going in.

Reyes swallows. Looks at the scope, at the lone return, then down at the
amber key. Presses it. It lights.

REYES
(into the mic, careful)
Aircraft, ah — this is — my name's Reyes. I've come down to the radar
room to talk with you a minute. How're you doing up there tonight.

He lets off the key. The half-second yawns. Then —

BIRDY (V.O.)
(filtered, warm, unhurried)
Evening. Real nice of you to come down. Sorry to keep everybody up so
late on my account.

A few heads at the other scopes turn, just slightly, listening without
seeming to. The supervisor stops his pacing at the end of the row.

Reyes keys up. His eyes aren't on the glass anymore. They're on the
floor. On the lanyard swinging at his chest.

REYES
Don't worry about that. We're — nobody's upset with you. We just want to
get you down safe. Get your feet on the ground. Everybody down here,
that's all we want for you.

He says it the way you read a statement into a record. Even, correct,
hollow. He lets off the key.

Silence on the frequency. The lag, and then more than the lag. Bishop's
eyes move from his scope to Reyes's face.

BIRDY (V.O.)
(gentle)
Can I say something, friend, and you won't take it wrong?

Reyes keys up. Nothing comes. He lets off.

BIRDY (V.O.) (CONT'D)
You don't sound like you mean it. That's alright. I'm not sore about it.
Somebody asked you to come say that, I expect, and you came and said it.
You did good.

Reyes's hand comes off the key entirely. He stands there with his mouth
a little open. Across the dark room, the muted TV holds its far shot of
the little plane against the dusk.

BIRDY (V.O.) (CONT'D)
I just don't want you catching any heat over me. You go on and tell 'em
you tried. You did. I heard you.

Bishop has gone very still beside him. The cold coffee. The two blips
holding their stations.

Reyes lifts his hand. Presses the amber key. When his voice comes it's
lost the courtroom out of it.

REYES
You don't have to do anything for me. You understand? Forget what they
told me to say. I'm —
(a breath)
I'd just like it if you came down. That's me asking. Not the room.

He lets off. The longest lag yet.

BIRDY (V.O.)
That was better. Thank you. Honest.
(a soft, apologetic beat)
I'm just not quite ready to bring it down just yet. I'm sorry. You've
been kind about it and I won't forget that.

The key is still lit amber. Reyes doesn't speak into it. He just stands,
keyed up, breathing, sending the man nothing but the open channel and
the sound of the room.

Bishop reaches over, gently, and lifts Reyes's finger off the key. It
goes dark.

The frequency closes. The murmur of the room comes back up around them —
the printer, a vector being read off down the row, the supervisor
resuming his walk.

Reyes pulls the headset off slow. Holds it in both hands at his waist
like something handed to him at a funeral. He looks at the main scope.
The slow return crawls. The two fighter blips ride its wings, patient,
going nowhere.

On the muted TV, the little plane banks once, far off, and levels.

From inside Reyes's jacket pocket — small, tinny, still live this whole
time —

HOLT (V.O.)
(muffled, through the cloth)
Reyes. Reyes, you there? ... Well?

Reyes doesn't reach for it. He sets the headset down on the edge of
Bishop's console, careful not to knock the cold coffee. His eyes stay on
the glass.

The phone goes on talking in his pocket, a small voice asking for
something he doesn't have.

Bishop keys his own mic, quiet, turning back to his scope and his slow
lone return, leaving the prosecutor standing in the green-gray light
with nothing in his hands.
