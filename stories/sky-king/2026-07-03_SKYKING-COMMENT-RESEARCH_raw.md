## A. VERBATIM VOICES

**The mourners (Rolling Stone, tribute-page devotees):**
- "It's been haunting me because I feel I understand him and have experienced
  the same pain… I feel like I'm trying to mourn someone I never met."
  (founder, Richard "Beebo" Russell SkyKing Tribute Page)
- "His story hurts me in a way that only losing the closest of friends and
  family have hurt me." (tribute-page devotee)
- "His story's true Americana." (Rolling Stone framing of the folk read)
- "a modern-day Icarus whose dramatic dying act has become a blank slate for
  others to project meaning upon" (Tim Dickinson, Rolling Stone)
- The projection, per Rolling Stone: "a working man, broken down by indifferent
  bosses — a human cog in the airport machinery who suddenly revolted, stole an
  airplane, buzzed a volcano, sassed air traffic controllers, and performed
  airshow stunts in a final dogged display of the human spirit."
- 4chan mourning register (Know Your Meme): the "odd, empty feeling of mourning
  over someone we never met"; a long anonymous post reasoning that he "had come
  to the conclusion that from this point on, his life would be one of
  mediocrity" — and honoring that he harmed no one but himself.

**The folk canon (already minted by the crowd):**
- Merch: **"Better to live one day as a pilot than 100 years as a baggage
  handler."** (the constitution, in the folk's own words)
- Merch: "Puget Sound Flight Club: Fly It Like You Stole It."
- Wallpaper text: "I'm not going there to die. I'm going to find out if I'm
  really alive."
- Tribute songs exist (Sam Shepard's tribute song "utilizing parts of his final
  discussion"; whole YouTube tribute playlists; anniversary tributes on
  multiple platforms). ABC News framed its tribute: "A moment frozen in time…
  and the people who loved him listening as it unfolds."

**The people who knew him (skyk.ing / Ted K Archive compilation):**
- "He was so much more than his final moments." (site's recurring assertion)
- "kindhearted, adventurous, and deeply thoughtful"; a "brother bear"; "He just
  tried to get everybody on the straight and narrow with laughter and kindness"
  (close friend); hosted "non-alcoholic bonfires"; deep faith; a Hebrew "BUT"
  tattooed on his wrist.
- His mother: "I still can't believe he chose the path he chose, I didn't see it."
- Sister: "He was a bundle of joy"; "calm" amid "chaos."
- Coworker: "He seemed like a levelheaded person… Richard was right behind me.
  He reassured me."
- The countervoice (his faith adviser, Rolling Stone): "He is certainly no
  hero" — rejecting that "stealing a plane, barrel rolling, and crashing it"
  was "awesome."
- The family, on publicity: "Out of respect for Beebo's widow, we have agreed
  as a family to not speak on this."

**From the tapes, newly surfaced in this research:**
- Asked his name by ATC: **"That's a question I've been asking my whole life.
  Identity issues."** (the real man's name-deflection — twin of our KEEP beat)

## B. THE MOMENTS PEOPLE CITE (most-quoted lines/beats)
- Know Your Meme's most-quoted list: "I wouldn't know how to land it / I wasn't
  really planning on landing it"; "Sorry, my mic came off, I threw up a little
  bit"; "I'm sorry about this, I hope this doesn't ruin your day"; the orca
  coordinates joke; "sweet nothings."
- USER'S FIRSTHAND (YouTube comments, 2026-07-03): **"I'll just bring this nose
  down and call it a night"** reads as the single most moving line in the
  comment sections. (Tool-fetch could not reach raw YT comments to
  quantify; treated as ground truth by direct observation.)
- Established canon moments across sources: "just a broken guy, got a few
  screws loose"; the barrel roll (with shoreline video); the job exchange +
  "Nah, I'm a white guy"; the controllers' kindness; the pilots not firing.

## C. RECURRING GRIEF-REGISTERS
1. Mourning a stranger as intimately as kin (the two tribute-page quotes).
2. Working-class recognition — "he was all of us" / the cog who revolted.
3. Protectiveness — communities defending his memory, de-emphasizing the
   suicide ("so much more than his final moments"), family-privacy respect.
4. Grief fused with meme-grammar — jokes AS eulogy (Chad Sky King formats,
   dark humor that reads tender in context, "Fly High, Skyking").
5. The projection surface — Icarus / blank slate: everyone sees their own
   unlived life in him.

## D. TONE TAXONOMY
Deadpan + sincere simultaneously; meme formats used tenderly; celebration of
competence ("dreamlike stunt execution") braided with grief; his OWN register
(apologetic, self-mocking) adopted by the mourners as the voice of mourning.
No anger at him anywhere mainstream; anger reserved for "the machine" (wages,
invisibility) — and by some, for the mythologizers.

## E. THE HULU DOCUMENTARY (confirmed)
"#Skyking", dir. Patricia Gillespie. Premiered SXSW 2026-03-12; Hulu/Disney+
2026-04-14. Reception mixed-positive (Decider: "harrowing live audio journey…
effective examination"; Culture Mix: "empathetic"; Daily Campus 4/5: "turns an
internet sensation into a quiet tragedy"). POSITIONING (decisive for us): it
DE-MYTHOLOGIZES — "redirects focus toward his actual circumstances: long hours,
relatively low pay and an increasing sense of isolation"; the Inlander
criticized its one late musical flourish as "falling prey to the online
mythologizing." Its strongest moments per reviewers: the UNADORNED ones ("no
need for added narration or dramatic music… they force you to sit with the
reality"). Its weakness: pacing/momentum in the middle. Viewer thread: "very
fair and balanced, with some incredibly moving moments."

## F. HANDLE-WITH-CARE FLAGS
- The "white guy" line was seized by alt-right communities (Rolling Stone).
  Any in-film use must be built so the film observes the detonation without
  becoming ammunition — the lampshade design is not optional.
- The real man was married; the family asked for privacy for his widow. Our
  fictionalization (Birdy/Maya, no real names) is a duty, not just a choice.
- A real countervoice exists ("certainly no hero") — the film should contain
  that pressure honestly (the machine's view; the mother's-eye grief) rather
  than pretend unanimous adoration.
