# SKY KING — Climax & Ending (user vision, 2026-07-01)

This is the WHAT (the director's intent). The engine writes the final lines from a
seed; the exchanges below are the target beats, not hand-typed final dialogue.

## The engine of the turn — RECOGNITION, not (just) beauty; motif = STORIES

He is a coward, so he has no stories — nothing dared, nothing lived. Up there the
world finally sees his gift and is AWED by it, and that recognition is what turns
him toward life: if ONE day of nerve brings this much aliveness, what would a life
of it bring? He chooses to live because he wants MORE of it. (Beauty can stay as
texture; the DRIVER is recognition → pride → wanting to live.)

## The two fighter escorts — one SERIOUS, one FUNNY

The funny one voices the awe the audience feels; because it's inappropriate
(military decorum) the serious one reprimands him — then the funny one keys Birdy
anyway.

- After the BARREL ROLL, funny pilot → Birdy (target beat): *"You son of a bitch,
  you did it. You have any idea how hard that is in that airplane? There are guys
  who can't pull that."*
- The other pilot radios GROUND CONTROL, describing for the transcript what Birdy
  actually did — the achievement made official.
- BIRDY hears it. We show ONLY his face: a look of PRIDE. **No line from Birdy.**

## The dive — sustain "will he die?" to the last second

It looks like he's about to smash it into the ground — like that's what he really
means to do.
- SERIOUS pilot: *"Don't do it, brother. Don't do it."*
- FUNNY pilot: *"I don't know, man. But if you live through this, I'd buy you a
  drink and hear your stories."*
- The plane dives behind the mountain / the far side of the island. **TRANSMISSION
  CUTS OUT.**
- SERIOUS pilot: *"I think we lost him."* (he might be dead)
- Then Birdy EMERGES on the radio from the far side: *"...but I don't have any
  stories."* (answering the funny pilot — a coward's whole life = nothing to tell)
- FUNNY/junior pilot: *"If this is the way you choose to live, you'll always have
  stories. Where you headed?"*
- BIRDY: *"SeaTac."* (he has chosen to live, and to land)
- Then minimal — just him landing the plane.

## The ending — the consequence = he must keep living as brave

- Cut to Birdy in a JAIL CELL. BISHOP (the controller, the human thread) finally
  sees him in person.
- REYES: no jail time — but he'll never work in this industry again.
- Options are dangled without cheese: he could sell a memoir ("One Day of Freedom"),
  make real money. Birdy scoffs: *"I don't want to talk about it. I want to live
  it."*
- As he walks out, a PUBLISHER'S rep: *"I represent a publishing house — we assign
  you a writer, you sell the memoir, you make a lot of money."* Birdy says no. To
  all of it.

## Coherence notes

- The "this is the day" trigger = a FINAL HUMILIATION, but keep it SMALL and quiet
  so it fits the rock-bottom ("comfort is the horror") — the last drop in a full
  glass of numbness, not a melodrama.
- Rock-bottom = his small life is comfortable ENOUGH, and that is the death.
- No villain. The pilots/tower/Reyes are the world reacting AFTER he dares —
  consequences, never causes. Their AWE is the point.
- Pay off "stories": coward = no stories → bravery = stories → choosing to live =
  choosing to keep having them → the ending refusal = he'd rather LIVE stories than
  sell them. = "must keep living as brave."
