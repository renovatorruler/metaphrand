# कुकु और अक्षर — World Bible

*A warm preschool series that teaches Hindi one letter at a time, in the register (not the plot) of* Sesame Street *and* Bluey *— gentle, playful, ritualized, safe. The teaching rides on story and character, never on flashcards.*

## Part 0 — The register (read first, every session)

**The bar:** a child aged 3–5, who speaks little or no Hindi, laughs at a small dragon, waits for his breath, and by the end can say **क** and three words that start with it. Warmth and ritual over plot. The letter is *earned by a story beat*, never announced cold.

**What this is NOT.** Not an app, not a drill, not a worksheet read aloud. No jeopardy, no villain, no scares, no sarcasm, no adult wink over the kids' heads. Nobody monologues the lesson; the grandmother *plays* it.

**The craft laws bind here too, but bend for the age:** heavy repetition and call-and-response — a defect in the adult stories — is **required pedagogy here.** The engine's echo/repeat instincts are deliberately overridden for dialogue (see Part VI).

## Part I — The Place

**अक्षर घाटी (Akshar Ghaati — "Letter Valley").** A soft green Himalayan valley of friendly little dragons. Warm light, round hills, a stream, Dadi Maya's stone house with a small oil lamp (दीया). Every dragon here breathes fire — it is how they light lamps, warm tea, toast the morning. It is ordinary and cozy, not fearsome. Into this ordinary world, one dragon breathes something no one has seen: **letters.**

## Part II — The engine (fire → letters)

The youngest dragon, **कुकु**, cannot make flame. When he breathes — especially when he is excited or brave — a glowing **अक्षर** floats up instead: a letter of light and smoke, hanging in the sky. Each episode, one letter. This is the show's whole machine: **a story beat makes Kuku breathe, the letter appears, and the valley (and the child at home) learns it.**

## Part III — The child's journey (the real curriculum)

Under the fun, a real spine for the viewer: recognize the shape → make the sound → hear it starting familiar words → say it back → find it in the hero's own name. "Most-useful-first" letter order (`LETTER_MAP.md`) means the child is building *real, high-frequency words* (family, body, animals, food) from episode one, not abstract glyphs.

## Part IV — The cast (see `VOICE_CARDS.md` for the voice spec)

The core is a **trio** (Super-Kitties team shape) — every episode's problem needs all three gifts:

- **कुकु** — hero, the heart + the power; breathes letters; wants to be a "real" fire-dragon.
- **फ़ूरिया (Furia)** — the spark. First at everything (catchphrase «मैं पहले!»), kind but fiercely
  competitive with her brother; mothers anything small; dreams of a shiny red कार; writes each
  day's letter in her किताब; owns the recurring Ф→टोपी gag. *(Mirrors the author's daughter.)*
- **वैस्पर (Vesper)** — the dreamer, Furia's younger brother. Kind, sweet, lost in imagination
  (must be called 4–5 times); drifts into English and is reminded «हिंदी में, वैस्पर!» (the show's
  in-character translation device); notices what everyone misses; **falls asleep before everyone,
  anywhere — every episode ends on where.** *(Mirrors the author's son.)*
- **पापा** — Kuku's father; kind-but-firm; never a villain.
- **दादी माया** — grandmother, mentor, bilingual host; leads the lesson.
- **चीकू** — the toddler dragon Furia & Vesper look after (their real-life caretaking, in-show).
- **भोलू** — big gentle older dragon (recurring, optional).
- **सूत्रधार / Narrator** — offscreen bilingual framing (lighter now — Vesper carries most English).
- **Friends-of-the-show:** अन्ना & माशा (the kids' real best friends) — referenced from Ep2 (माशा's
  letter → म), guest appearance later.
- **Future segment idea (from the family's real ritual of writing letters to the dragon):** Kuku's
  mailbox — episodes can open with a real letter from a viewer-kid.

## Part V — The spine and the underlayer

**Spine (on the page):** the littlest dragon can't do what the big dragons do — and discovers he can do something wonderful instead, a new one each day.

**Underlayer (never on the page — the Sod):** your difference is your gift; learning comes *out* of you when you dare to try. Stated once in `PITCH_v1.md`, never in a line of dialogue.

## Part VI — Craft laws (non-negotiable)

1. **English sluglines & action, Devanagari dialogue.** Standard Devanagari only; one line per paragraph; no em-dashes, no emoji inside dialogue.
2. **Preschool register.** Short present-tense sentences; ~300 most-common spoken-Hindi words; तू/तुम among the little dragons; Dadi warm, never stern.
2a. **PROPER HINDI, complete sentences (author's law, 2026-07-21).** The kids LEARN syntax from
   this show. Every dialogue line is a complete, grammatically correct Hindi sentence — short and
   simple, never chopped staccato fragments (the «काला कुत्ता. मैंने बनाया.» fragment-append style
   is the megalist's Tier-3.6 fragment-drama tell AND bad pedagogy — banned). Model line:
   «पापा, पापा, देखो वो काला कुत्ता। आप एक कुत्ता मेरे लिए घर ले आओ ना।» Warm colloquial, not
   stiff formal. Exceptions: toddler Cheeku's babble; single-letter call-response («क!»).
2b. **Pronoun register (author's law).** Children address elders (Papa, Dadi, any grown-up) with
   **आप** — never तुम or तू. Elders → children use तू/तुम. Kids among themselves and to the puppy
   use तू. (आप + warm imperative «ले आओ ना» is the house register, per the author's example.)
3. **Repetition is required, not a smell.** Call-and-response, the recurring "कुकु का क" button, the same ritual each episode — all intentional. (The mechanical Craft gate is English-token-based and will not fight this on Hindi lines; the model-judged lift is steered by director's notes to *preserve* the simplicity — see below.)
4. **The letter is earned by a beat, never announced cold.** Story first, then the breath, then the lesson.
5. **No fear.** No villain, no peril, no shaming Kuku. Failure is gentle and brief and turns into wonder.
6. **The Sod is never stated.** No character explains the moral. It lives only in what happens.
7. **Native-speaker check before any episode ships.** Register/spelling/idiom verified by a Hindi native.

## Part VI.5 — The टोपी (hat) principle — the disambiguation engine

Every Devanagari letter hangs from a horizontal headline (शिरोरेखा) — a "hat." **No Latin or
Cyrillic letter has it.** This is the single most reliable "this is a Hindi letter" cue, and it
works for EVERY letter, so it is a **series-wide signature**, taught once with क and paid off
forever.

**Why it's here:** a specific, real confusion — क reads like the Cyrillic **Ф** (the first letter
of Furia's name): both are "a round thing with a vertical line." The fix is not to explain the
foreign letter; it is to point at the hat.

**Show device (recurring, every episode):** when Kuku breathes a letter, it first appears
**hatless** — a plain circle-on-a-stick that is not yet a letter (and happens to look like Ф).
Then the **टोपी snaps on with a "pop"** and it becomes the अक्षर.
- Dadi: «टोपी बिना, ये अक्षर नहीं… टोपी लगी — अब ये है, क!»

**Secondary cue (क vs Ф, optional / say-it-to-Furia):** Ф is a ball on a stick, the same on both
sides; क wears a hat and has a little leg — lopsided, like a tiny person, not a ball.

**General rule:** whenever a Hindi letter resembles a foreign one, we disambiguate with the hat,
never by teaching the foreign letter.

## Part VII — How prose is made (respect the engine)

Per repo `CLAUDE.md`: dramatized scene prose is **not hand-typed.** It comes from `studio/` — a `Seed.sceneSeed` (structure only) → `Write.writeScene` → the mandatory `Write.liftDialogue` pass → an emitted scene with a **receipt** `Write.verify` can check. For this series the lift is handed **director's notes that protect the preschool register** (keep it simple/repetitive; add no subtext), because the default dialogue doctrine pushes toward adult subtext we do NOT want here. The letter-lesson call-and-response and songs are pedagogical templates (`LETTER_MAP.md`, per-episode design), not dramatic scenes.
