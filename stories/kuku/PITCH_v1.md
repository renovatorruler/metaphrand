**Working title (OPEN):** कुकु और अक्षर — *"Kuku and the Letters."* English handle: *Kuku the Letter-Dragon.* Alt: **आग-अक्षर** (*Aag-Akshar*, "Fire-Letters").

**One line:** In a green valley of little dragons, the smallest one can't breathe fire — he breathes glowing **letters** — and every episode the whole valley learns the letter he makes.

---

## The hook (why this teaches, and why a kid stays)

Every dragon in the valley breathes fire. **कुकु** (Kuku), the littlest, breathes an **अक्षर** — a glowing letter of light and smoke — instead. What looks like his flaw is the engine of the show: each episode, something Kuku loves makes him breathe, and **the day's letter floats up into the sky.** Then दादी माया (the grandmother-dragon, our Sesame "grown-up") turns to camera: **"आज का अक्षर है — क!"** and teaches it.

Two things make it sticky for a 3–5-year-old learning from zero:
- **The hero IS the letter.** Episode 1's letter is क — and **कुकु's own name starts with क.** The child bonds to Kuku, and the letter rides in on that bond. Every letter we can, we tie to a character or a beloved word.
- **The letter is a physical, magical event**, not a flashcard. It comes *out of the dragon*. The kid waits for the breath.

---

## The letter-of-the-day mechanism (the show's engine)

1. A tiny problem/want in the valley (story).
2. Kuku gets excited and **breathes → the day's letter appears, glowing, in the sky** (the hook moment).
3. **आज का अक्षर** — Dadi Maya, to camera: names it, makes the sound, air-traces the shape, **call-and-response** ("बोलो मेरे साथ — क!"), then **three picture-words** that start with it (Hindi-first, letter emphasized, a one-word English gloss for the from-scratch kids), then the **letter song**.
4. Back to the story — the letter's word pays off the tiny problem.
5. **Recap + goodbye song** ("आज हमने सीखा — क!").

## The cast (want vs. want; the secret that powers each)

- **कुकु (Kuku)** — the hero, the child-surrogate (~4). Round, eager, tries hard. Breathes letters, not fire. *Want:* to be a "real" dragon like the big ones. *Secret (never said):* what he calls his failure is the best thing about him.
- **दादी माया (Dadi Maya)** — grandmother-dragon, mentor + host. Warm, patient, playful. The **one bilingual bridge**: leads call-and-response and gives the tiny English gloss. *Want:* to let Kuku discover himself, not to fix him.
- **चीकू (Cheeku)** — Kuku's best friend, small and fast and funny; gets things adorably wrong; drives repetition ("फिर से! फिर से!"). *Want:* fun, right now.
- **भोलू (Bholu)** — big, gentle, clumsy older-brother dragon; makes huge flames; kind. Contrast, not a bully. (Recurring, optional per episode.)
- **सूत्रधार / Narrator** — light offscreen bilingual voice for framing + English helper narration (amal's *sutradhar* convention: reads the action, never the dialogue).

Names are chosen so **most of the cast seeds a future letter**: **क**ुकु, **म**ाया, **च**ीकू, **भ**ोलू.

## The letter order — "most-useful-first" (full map in `LETTER_MAP.md`)

Season 1 front-loads letters that (a) name the cast, (b) start the highest-frequency *picturable* preschool words — family, body parts, animals — so kids build real words fast. Opening run: **क → म → र → न → प → त → च → ब → ग → भ …** Vowels/मात्राएँ enter as a woven parallel track once words demand them (flagged OPEN below).

## Gates check (conception — adapted for preschool)

- **Spine:** a want (Kuku: to be a real fire-dragon) vs. a wall (he can't make fire) with a small clock each episode (the valley's little problem). But/Therefore, not And-then.
- **Doorway / turn:** Kuku's "failure" turns into the letter — the same turn every episode, which is a *feature* for this age (ritual = safety).
- **No-luck rescue:** the letter is *caused* by Kuku daring to try again, not by accident.
- **Likability:** Kuku wants something and is kind; no villain, no scares.
- **Repetition:** heavy call-and-response and recurrence is **required pedagogy**, not a craft flaw — this deliberately overrides the adult "echo" instinct.
- **Parable can lose:** the small story has a real (tiny) stake — if Kuku gives up, the problem stays unsolved.

## Language / register protocol (binds every episode)

- **English sluglines & action; Devanagari dialogue** (the amal law). Standard Devanagari characters only; one line per paragraph.
- **Register = preschool-from-scratch.** Dialogue: short, present-tense, the ~300 most common spoken-Hindi words; warm; no irony, no subtext on the surface, no sophistication, nothing scary.
- **तू/तुम** among the little dragons (intimate, natural); Dadi is warm-गुरु, never stern.
- **English helper glosses** live with Dadi/Narrator only, kept to one or two words, never inside a dragon's in-story line.
- **Dialect:** neutral Khari-boli Hindi (not Malwi like amal, not Sanskritized, not Urdu-heavy) — clean, national, teachable. *Native-speaker check before any episode ships.*
