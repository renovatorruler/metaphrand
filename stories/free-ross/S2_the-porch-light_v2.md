# FREE ROSS DETECTIVE AGENCY
## Season Two — "THE PORCH LIGHT"
*A complete ~8-minute case. One turn, resolved. The held card is at the bottom.*
*v2 — humanizer + clear-pane + dialogue-realism + Mamet drama pass + Texan layer.*

---

**COLD OPEN.**

**INT. FREE ROSS DETECTIVE AGENCY — DAY**

A converted storefront off a Dallas access road. Mismatched desks, a wall of index cards, a coffee machine somebody babies. ROSS UNDERWOOD (50s, ex-NYPD, dressed like the laundry's a week behind) sits across from a NERVOUS MAN folding a receipt into a smaller and smaller square.

NERVOUS MAN: — and it was in the drawer when I left and gone when I got back. My brother-in-law was the only one in the house.

Ross isn't looking at the receipt. He's looking at the hands.

ROSS: How many times'd you say that on the drive over?

NERVOUS MAN: ...What?

ROSS: You got it smooth. "Drawer when I left, gone when I got back." (beat) Nobody tells a robbery that smooth unless he's been telling it to himself.

NERVOUS MAN: I'm just trying to —

ROSS: He's broke. You're not. And you'd rather lose the watch than sit across from him and ask. (slides the receipt back) Go on home. Ask him. He's waiting for you to.

The man stays a second. Then he goes. RUTH MARROW (40s, the agency's arbitrator) sets a file on the desk.

RUTH: You could've charged him.

ROSS: For what.

RUTH: For this one. Thursday. We're the defense.

---

**INT. AGENCY — CONTINUOUS**

Ross opens the file. A photo on top: SADIE VANCE, sixteen, in a wheelchair. Not smiling. Not looking away either.

RUTH: Cole Vance, claiming against his next-door neighbor. His girl went onto the neighbor's lot at night. In-ground pool, half-dug, no fence around it. She went in. Her back.

ROSS: And the neighbor's ours.

RUTH: Tomas Reyes. Left the hole open. On the facts he's finished. Vance is asking eleven thousand grams and lifetime surety.

ROSS: Reyes can cover that?

RUTH: About a tenth of it. He loses, he's in the work-program the rest of his life. (beat) He could fold today, save himself the hearing. He won't.

ROSS: Why not.

RUTH: He says she didn't fall.

ROSS: They all say that.

RUTH: He won't say anything else. Just that. (reads off a note) "I know that hole, Mr. Ross. Nobody falls in that hole."

Ross looks at the girl's photo. A beat too long.

---

**INT. REYES HOUSE — DAY**

A kitchen kept up thirty years. TOMAS REYES (60s, a contractor's forearms, reading glasses pushed up onto his head) can't stay in his chair.

TOMAS: I should've fenced it that night. A week early, costs me nothing, and — (stops) you can't go back and close a hole. So.

ROSS: Walk me through it.

TOMAS: I was asleep.

ROSS: Hear anything?

TOMAS: No. (a finger up — this is the part) That's the thing I keep — no. A girl goes off the lip into a pit that deep, there's a sound. Concrete. I'd have heard it.

ROSS: Would you.

TOMAS: I sleep with the window open. Forty years, that window. (quiet) I heard a dog. I heard the highway. I did not hear a girl.

Ross doesn't write it down. He keeps it.

---

**INT. VANCE HOUSE — DAY**

Spotless. Everything squared away. COLE VANCE (50s), pressed shirt, pours coffee he won't drink.

COLE: She slipped out. We didn't — it wasn't a fight, she's sixteen, they go. She went across in the dark and that man had a grave open in his yard.

ROSS: Your porch light on?

COLE: ...No.

ROSS: Why not.

COLE: (half a second quick) Bulb went. We kept meaning to.

Ross nods. Lets it sit there.

Down the hall, SADIE has rolled to her doorway. She heard it — her father, "the light was off." Her jaw sets. She backs the chair out of sight.

Ross saw.

---

**INT. AGENCY — NIGHT**

Ruth, Ross. Across the bullpen a WOMAN works a binder like it owes her money — ENYA SHORE (30s, Union-crisp, the posture of a badge in a place that outlawed badges).

RUTH: Claimant's investigator. Vance hired up.

ROSS: She's Union.

RUTH: Best procedural read in the Metroplex. She built the whole file. Pool unsecured, no fence, the medicals. (beat) On paper your man's already in the program.

Enya crosses. Even. Not unkind.

ENYA: Mr. Underwood. I've read about how you work.

ROSS: Nothing to read.

ENYA: A hole in the ground. A man who left it open. A child who won't walk. (beat) Every word of that is true and on the table. Thursday you're going to stand up and try to make a feeling weigh more than three true things. (almost a smile) I came a long way to watch that.

ROSS: You built the cleanest file I've seen.

ENYA: I did.

ROSS: That's the part that bothers me.

He leaves her with it.

---

**INT. AGENCY — LATE**

Ross alone, two photos in front of him — Sadie, and Cole at his clean counter. Ruth in the doorway, coat on.

ROSS: Cole told me the light was off three times. Reyes told me his was off once and didn't care, because why would he. (taps Cole's photo) A man tells you twice it was only the bulb, and you never asked about a bulb —

RUTH: People let porch lights go.

ROSS: Not the night their kid's out in it. You leave it on so she can find the step coming home. (beat) Unless you don't want to watch her come home yet.

RUTH: (slow) ...They fought.

ROSS: She didn't slip out. He put her out. Or close enough he can call it slipping and still get to sleep. She went into the dark mad, and the dark had one hole in it.

RUTH: You can't establish any of that. No witness. There's a porch light, that's all there is.

ROSS: I'm not going to establish it. I'm going to stand him somewhere he can't bet on it. (beat) Seal the hearing. Bring the girl.

---

**INT. ARBITRATION HALL — DAY. THE HEARING.**

A plain room. A felt-topped table. A SURETY-MASTER (60s, Texan, unhurried) lays the claim out like a hand of cards. Cole and Enya on one side; Ross and Tomas on the other. Ruth neutral at the head. Sadie at the back, in her chair.

SURETY-MASTER: Vance against Reyes. Eleven thousand grams, lifetime. Reyes' answer.

ROSS: We don't argue the hole. We don't argue the girl's hurt. We argue why. (to Enya) You laid a path. Steps, street, the lot. You proved every inch of it but the piece that pays — that she fell because Tomas left it open. (to Cole) So stake it. One gram. On "my daughter fell."

ENYA: He doesn't have to stake a fact you can't disprove.

ROSS: He staked eleven thousand on it without blinking. I'm asking him for one. (beat) One gram, Mr. Vance. On "she fell."

Cole's hand is near his stack. It doesn't move.

ROSS (CONT'D): You keep telling the room the light was off. The bulb. (no cruelty in it) You turned it off, Cole. She wanted out, you let the door shut behind her, and you killed the light so the house would feel like something she walked away from. You've told it so clean there's no door left in it and no dark. Just a hole, and a man who left it open. (beat) So stake the part where she fell. You can't. Last you saw of her she was walking — fast, away from you, into a yard you'd warned her about a hundred times.

COLE: (barely) Stop.

ROSS: One gram.

Cole doesn't move. From the back of the room:

SADIE: I jumped.

The room goes still.

SADIE (CONT'D): (to her father, only him) I wasn't drunk. I wasn't lost. The light — Daddy, the light was never it. We fought, and I went out there, and I sat on the edge of that stupid hole because it was the one place you'd never come looking. (beat) I don't even know if I meant it. But I didn't fall.

Cole isn't holding the seams anymore. Enya looks at her file like the words moved while she wasn't watching.

---

**INT. ARBITRATION HALL — MOMENTS LATER**

SURETY-MASTER: On cause — unestablished. Reyes is released from the claim.

Tomas lets out a breath he's held two weeks. He doesn't celebrate. He looks at the girl.

TOMAS: (to Cole, low) The care still happens. (to Ruth) Surety covers a child who needs it. Doesn't need a guilty man to pay for it to work.

RUTH: Community surety. She's covered. Nobody goes in the program.

Cole crosses to Sadie. Gets down to where her eyes are. For once he hasn't got a clean sentence ready.

COLE: I turned off the light.

SADIE: I know, Daddy. (beat) I always knew.

---

**TAG.**

**EXT. ARBITRATION HALL — STEPS — DAY**

Heat coming off the lot. Ross on the steps, not doing anything in particular. Enya finds him.

ENYA: You didn't establish a thing. A girl broke and you took the credit.

ROSS: She didn't break for me. She broke for him. I made the room quiet enough to hear it.

ENYA: That's not detective work. You decided he was guilty and you bent a hearing till a kid couldn't stand it. Next time you're wrong, somebody who didn't do a thing goes to Cainland on your read.

ROSS: I'm not wrong.

ENYA: (and there it is — not anger, something steadier) That's the part of you that scares me. (beat) I'm staying in Dallas, Mr. Underwood. Every case you touch, I'll be across the table, and I'll show the room that what you call knowing is a nicer word for deciding. (offers her hand) We're going to know each other a long while.

Ross looks at the hand. Doesn't take it. Not cruel. Careful.

ROSS: You read everything in that file but the one thing that mattered. You'd have put an innocent man away on the best case you ever built. (beat) Be scared of that.

He goes. Enya watches him go. Not angry. Decided.

**END.**

## THE HELD CARD — *(if this pops, here's the season)*

- **The recurring engine:** every episode is one self-contained claim Ross cracks by reading the lie people tell *themselves* — complete in 8 minutes, House-format (a case plus a quick cold-open micro-read).
- **The Sod we didn't resolve:** **Enya Shore.** She stays in Dallas to prove Ross's method is bias in a good suit — and across S2 she's *sometimes right* (the countertransference cases, where Ross's grief reads into a file and an innocent person nearly pays). She's Peshat with a badge; the tragedy is she's a true believer, not a villain.
- **The deeper card (held for the finale):** the one case Ross's read *fails* on is the one the caged killer composed from inside the work-program — and Enya is the one who "wins" it, handing Ross his first wrong call. That's where S2 turns into S3.
- **The heart ledger:** Ruth and Ross — banked, not spent; Mabel's notebook in the drawer he doesn't open on screen.
