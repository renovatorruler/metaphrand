# FREE ROSS DETECTIVE AGENCY
## Season Two — "THE PORCH LIGHT"
*A complete ~8-minute case. One turn, resolved. The held card is at the bottom.*

---

**COLD OPEN.**

**INT. FREE ROSS DETECTIVE AGENCY — DAY**

A converted storefront. Mismatched desks, a good coffee machine, a wall of case-cards. The underdog shop. ROSS UNDERWOOD (50s, ex-NYPD, dresses like a man who stopped caring and it suited him) sits across from a NERVOUS MAN clutching a receipt.

NERVOUS MAN: — and I'm telling you the watch was in the drawer when I left and gone when I came back. My brother-in-law was the only one in the house.

Ross doesn't look at the receipt. He looks at the man's hands.

ROSS: How long you rehearse that?

NERVOUS MAN: What?

ROSS: "In the drawer when I left, gone when I came back." Clean. No ums. You said it to the mirror.

NERVOUS MAN: I'm just trying to be —

ROSS: Your brother-in-law's broke and you're not, and you'd rather lose a watch than have a Tuesday where you ask him to his face. (slides the receipt back) Go home. Ask him. He'll tell you. He already wants to.

The man sits there. Then goes. RUTH VANCE-no — RUTH MARROW (40s, the agency's in-house arbitrator, sharp, warm, the only one who can read Ross back) drops a case-file on his desk.

RUTH: You could've taken his money.

ROSS: He didn't have a case. He had a grudge with a coat on.

RUTH: This one's a case. Showdown's Thursday. We're the defense.

---

**INT. AGENCY — CONTINUOUS**

Ross opens the file. A photo: a teenage girl, SADIE, in a wheelchair, eyes that have decided not to need anyone.

RUTH: Cole Vance is claiming against his neighbor. Sixteen-year-old daughter went onto the neighbor's lot at night — empty in-ground pool, mid-construction, no fence. Fell in. Spinal. She'll need care the rest of her life.

ROSS: And the neighbor's our client.

RUTH: Tomas Reyes. He left a hole in the ground unsecured. On the facts, he's done. Cole's asking eleven thousand grams, lifetime surety. (beat) Tomas couldn't cover a tenth of it. He folds, he's in the work-program till he dies. He fights and loses, same thing, plus the seal.

ROSS: So why'd Tomas hire us instead of just folding.

RUTH: Because he says he didn't do it.

ROSS: Everybody says that.

RUTH: He says the girl didn't fall. (beat) He won't explain. He just keeps saying it. "She didn't fall, Mr. Ross. I know that hole. You don't fall in that hole."

Ross looks at the photo of Sadie a beat too long.

---

**INT. REYES HOUSE — DAY**

TOMAS REYES (60s, a contractor's hands, a careful man) at a kitchen table. Won't sit still.

TOMAS: I fence it tonight, I fence it a week early, none of this — but you can't un-leave a hole open, so. (presses his palms flat) I'm not saying the girl's lying. I'm saying somebody is, and it isn't the hole.

ROSS: Walk me through the night.

TOMAS: I was asleep. Porch light off, whole street dark. I heard the — (stops) I heard nothing. That's the thing. A kid goes into a pit that deep, there's a sound. I'd have heard it. I sleep with the window open.

ROSS: You sleep with the window open.

TOMAS: Forty years.

Ross writes nothing. Files it.

---

**INT. VANCE HOUSE — DAY**

Modest, immaculate. COLE VANCE (50s, ironed, contained, a man holding himself together with both hands) makes coffee he doesn't drink.

COLE: She snuck out. We don't — we didn't fight about it, she just, kids. She went over there in the dark and that man left a grave open in his yard. (steadies) The porch light was off. Ours. She couldn't see the step. That's how it started. Dark house, she goes down the steps wrong, by the time she's at his lot she's already —

ROSS: Whose porch light.

COLE: Ours. It was off.

ROSS: Why.

COLE: (a half-second too fast) Bulb. We'd been meaning to.

Ross nods. Lets it sit. Down the hall, SADIE in the doorway of her room. She's heard her father's voice say "the porch light was off." Her face does something. Ross catches it. She wheels back out of sight.

---

**INT. AGENCY — NIGHT**

Ruth and Ross. A WOMAN waits across the bullpen — ENYA SHORE (30s, Union-crisp, a badge-holder's posture in a world with no badges), reviewing a binder like it owes her money.

RUTH: That's the claimant's investigator. Cole hired up. She's good.

ROSS: She's Union.

RUTH: She's the best procedural read in the Metroplex. She built Cole's whole file. Pool unsecured, attractive nuisance, the medicals — it's airtight. On paper Tomas is already in the program.

Enya comes over. Even, not unkind. A true believer.

ENYA: Mr. Underwood. I've read about your method.

ROSS: There's no method.

ENYA: There's a hole in the ground, a man who left it open, and a child who can't walk. Three facts, all established, all on the table. (beat) You're going to try to make a feeling outweigh that. I want to watch you try.

ROSS: You build the cleanest case I've seen, you said.

ENYA: I did.

ROSS: That's what bothers me. (he leaves)

---

**INT. AGENCY — LATER**

Ross alone with the two photos — Sadie's face, and Cole at his spotless counter. Ruth in the doorway.

ROSS: Cole told me the porch light was off three times. Tomas told me his was off once, like it didn't matter, because it didn't. (taps Cole's photo) A man tells you the broken thing was the bulb, twice, when you didn't ask — the bulb's not broken. He turned it off.

RUTH: People turn off porch lights.

ROSS: Not the night their kid's out. You leave it on so she can find the step. (beat) Unless you don't want her coming back in yet.

RUTH: ...They fought.

ROSS: She didn't sneak out. He put her out. Or close enough that he can call it sneaking and sleep at night. She went into the dark mad, in the dark, and she found the only hole on the street. (quiet) He knows. He's known since the sound he didn't hear either.

RUTH: You can't establish that. There's no witness. There's a porch light.

ROSS: I don't have to establish it. I have to make him unable to bet on the lie. (beat) Seal the showdown. And put Sadie in the room.

---

**INT. ARBITRATION HALL — DAY. THE SHOWDOWN.**

Spare, almost sacred. A felt-topped table. A SURETY-MASTER deals the claim like a hand. Cole and Enya on one side; Ross and Tomas on the other. Ruth observes as the neutral. Sadie at the back, in her chair.

SURETY-MASTER: Vance claims against Reyes. Eleven thousand grams, lifetime. Reyes' position.

ROSS: We don't contest the hole. We don't contest the injury. We contest the cause. (to Enya) You established a path. Steps, street, lot. You established everything except the only thing that pays — that she fell because Tomas left it open. So I'm going to ask the claimant to stake that. Just that. (to Cole) Raise on it. Put grams on "my daughter fell."

ENYA: He doesn't have to stake a fact you can't disprove.

ROSS: He raised eleven thousand on it sight unseen. I'm asking him to raise one. One gram, Cole. On "she fell."

Silence. Cole's hand near his stack. Not moving.

ROSS (CONT'D): You keep telling everyone the porch light was off. The bulb. (gentle) You turned it off, Cole. She wanted out and you let the door close behind her and you turned off the light so the house would feel like a thing she chose to leave. You've told that story so clean there's no fight in it, no door, no dark. Just a hole, and a man who left it open. (beat) Stake the part where she fell. You can't. Because the last thing you saw was her walking, fast, away from you, into a yard you'd warned her about a hundred times — and going in anyway.

COLE: (barely) Stop.

ROSS: One gram.

COLE doesn't move. From the back of the room:

SADIE: I jumped.

Everything stops.

SADIE (CONT'D): (to her father, not to the table) I wasn't drunk, I wasn't lost, the light being off — Dad, the light was never the thing. We fought, and I went out there, and I sat on the edge of that stupid hole because it was the one place you'd never look, and I — I don't even know if I meant it. (beat) But I didn't fall.

Cole has stopped being a contained man. Enya looks at her airtight binder like it's in a language she suddenly can't read.

---

**INT. ARBITRATION HALL — MOMENTS LATER**

SURETY-MASTER: On the cause — unestablished. Reyes is released from the claim.

Tomas exhales a breath he's held for two weeks. He doesn't gloat. He looks at Sadie.

TOMAS: (to Cole, quiet) The care still happens. (to Ruth) Coverage covers a child who needs it. Doesn't need a guilty man to pay for it. That's the whole point of the thing.

RUTH: (nodding) Community surety. She's covered. No one's in the program.

The system did not punish anyone. It just stopped a man from buying a lie with his neighbor's life.

Cole crosses to Sadie. Goes down to her eye level. For the first time, doesn't have a clean sentence.

COLE: I turned off the light.

SADIE: I know, Dad. (beat) I always knew.

---

**TAG.**

**EXT. ARBITRATION HALL — STEPS — DAY**

Ross, lighting nothing, just standing. Enya finds him.

ENYA: You didn't establish anything. A girl confessed and you took credit for a feeling.

ROSS: She didn't confess to me. She confessed to him. I just made the room quiet enough.

ENYA: That's not detection. That's — you decided he was guilty and you bent a hearing until a child broke. If you're wrong next time, somebody innocent goes to Cainland on your *mood*.

ROSS: I'm not wrong.

ENYA: (a small, certain smile — the smile of a true believer who has found her life's work) That's exactly what frightens me about you. (beat) I'm staying in Dallas, Mr. Underwood. I'm going to take every case you touch and prove that what you do is just a prettier word for prejudice. (she offers a hand) I think we're going to know each other a long time.

Ross looks at the hand. Doesn't take it. Not cruel — careful.

ROSS: You read everything in that file except the one thing that mattered, and you'd have buried an innocent man on the cleanest case you ever saw. (beat) You should be more frightened of *that.*

He goes. Enya watches him, and we hold on her face — not anger. Resolve. The nemesis, born.

**END.**

## THE HELD CARD — *(if this pops, here's the season)*

- **The recurring engine:** every episode is one self-contained claim Ross cracks by reading the lie people tell *themselves* — complete in 8 minutes, House-format (a case + a quick clinic-duty micro-read in the cold open).
- **The Sod we didn't resolve:** **Enya Shore.** She stays in Dallas to prove Ross's method is bias dressed up — and across S2 she's *sometimes right* (the countertransference episodes, where Ross's grief reads into a case and an innocent person nearly pays). She is Peshat with a badge; the tragedy is she's a true believer, not a villain. The season-long question: *is "everybody knows" wisdom, or is it just a man who can't stop being certain?*
- **The deeper card (held for the finale):** Enya doesn't know it yet, but the one case Ross's read will *fail* on is the one the caged killer composed from inside the work-program — and she'll be the one who "wins" it procedurally, handing Ross his first wrong call. That's where S2's war turns into S3.
- **The heart ledger:** Ruth (the rope) and Ross — banked, not spent; Mabel's notebook in the drawer he doesn't open on screen.
