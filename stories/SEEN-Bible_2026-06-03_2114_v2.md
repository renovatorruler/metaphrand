# SEEN — Character Bible (the iceberg)

*Working title.* An undercover cop joins a cult that promises to make its members
famous artists, and can't tell anymore which of his selves is the cover.

Reference, not script. **Surface** = shown, never told. **Submerged** = informs
only. **Deep** = no possible use in the film, kept anyway, because real people are
mostly made of things that never come up. Most of this is Deep. Each person gets a
**natal chart** as a fixed temperament anchor — a wheel to check them against so
they never drift, and a generator of directions the plot didn't plan.

The buried engine of the whole film: **what really happened to Eli.** Everyone is
organized around what they believe happened to him, and the truth would free them
and can't be said.

---

## THE FORMAL QUESTIONS — the material life and the essence

Three questions, answered for everyone first, or they go one-dimensional: **what do
they do for money, and is it enough? what is the pressure on it right now? what is
their essence — the one engine under all the behaviour, embodied, never named?**

**DANNY (Det. Daniel Cole).** A city detective, undercover specialist, twelve
years; a department salary he barely touches because he's never home to spend it.
Lives out of a sublet that was never his, under a cover identity — "Danny," a
broke aspiring painter — that the department built him a fake past for. — *Pressure:*
he's been under too long, too many times; his lieutenant is starting to talk about
him the way you talk about a tool that's gone soft; and the cover life is the only
one with anyone in it. — *Essence:* emptiness wearing competence. He made himself no
one so there'd be nothing left to hurt, and he is the best in the unit because there
is nothing underneath to leak. The one question that stops him cold is *but what do
you want.*

**MARSH (born Gary Pruitt).** Lives entirely off the cult — "the Collective." Tuition
for the workshops, the members' savings and inheritances signed over as "patronage,"
a gallery and a retreat that launder it into the look of a real institution, and the
quiet engine: the work the members make under his roof, sold under his name. —
*Pressure:* the Collective runs on a death it has to keep a suicide, and on a
promise (fame) it has never once delivered to anyone but the dead boy. — *Essence:*
a man who turned his own failure into other people's faith. He has a true gift for
*seeing* the hunger in a person and no gift at all for the thing he sells; he is the
emptiest believer in his own church, and half of him thinks he's saving them.

**ELI (deceased).** Made art and nothing else; the Collective fed and housed him for
four years in exchange for everything he made. — *Pressure (past tense):* he was the
proof — the one who was going to be famous, the catalog the others were sold on — and
he found out the catalog had someone else's name on it. — *Essence:* belief, all the
way to the floor. He needed a family and to be special and the Collective sold him
both at once; he was the realest believer in the room, which is why the truth, when
it came, was fatal.

**NORA (Eli's sister).** Night-shift x-ray tech at a county hospital; spent her
savings and a year of sleep refusing to let the suicide ruling stand, and badgering
the city until a detective got assigned. — *Pressure:* everyone she knows wants her
to accept the comforting version and grieve like a normal person, and she can't. —
*Essence:* the one who refuses the comforting story. Love that looks like stubbornness.

**LT. REYES.** Runs the undercover unit; twenty-two years on the job, a pension she
can taste. — *Pressure:* she's running an officer she can feel slipping, on a case
the brass wants closed cheap. — *Essence:* faith in the function. She believes the
badge is a real self the way Marsh's people believe the Collective is — the law is
its own cult, and she is its truest member, and the film never lets her off the hook
for it.

## DANIEL "DANNY" COLE — the cop with no one home

**Natal chart.** Born 3 March 1979, just before midnight. **Pisces sun** — no fixed
shore, the self that takes the shape of whatever it's poured into, the natural actor
and the natural drowner. **Gemini moon** — feeling that won't sit still, a dozen
masks and not one underneath. **Scorpio rising** — the watcher at the edge of the
room, the one who learns you before you learn his name. The spine of it is **Sun
conjunct Neptune:** a man who isn't quite *there* — deception as a birthright, and a
self that keeps dissolving when he reaches for it.

**The body.** Unremarkable on purpose — the face that's hard to describe an hour
later. Good hands he keeps still. A way of going so quiet in a room that people
forget he's in it, which he learned and now can't switch off. A scar on the
hairline he gives a different origin to depending on who's asking, and no longer
remembers the true one.

**Childhood & the parts that go nowhere.** Shuffled through four foster placements
between nine and fifteen; learned to read a new house in an afternoon and become the
kid it wanted — quiet here, funny there, invisible mostly — and to never unpack the
bag, because the bag was the only thing that stayed his. Could do every voice on
television by twelve. Stole nothing but library cards, one per town, in names that
weren't his, just to have a card with a name. Cried the first time someone called
him by a cover name and meant it kindly, and has never told anyone that.

**Tastes, habits, superstitions.** Black coffee he lets go cold; eats standing up.
Can't sleep in a bed he owns and sleeps fine in a stranger's. Keeps a cheap
disposable camera in the cover apartment and has never developed a roll. Orders
whatever the person across the table orders, automatically, and has no idea what he
likes.

**People who never appear.** *Maria*, the ex-wife, who left because she said living
with him was like living with a very good house-sitter. *Brennan*, his first
handler, dead three years, the closest thing to a father, who told him the gift was
also the wound and he'd have to choose, and he hasn't. *The kid he was at nine*,
before the houses, whose name on a report card he can still see and can't feel.

**Things he can do that never matter.** Walk into any trade and pass — carpenter,
dealer, deacon — for a week. Disappear in a photograph. Sit a polygraph flat,
because there's no anxiety where there's no self.

**The plot core.**
- *(Submerged)* He took undercover work *for* the self-erasure, not despite it; being
  no one is the only safe thing he's found. He suspects there is nothing to go back
  to under the covers, and the suspicion is the thing he can't look at.
- *(Surface)* The competence; the stillness; the cover life that has the only people
  in it.
- **Want vs. need:** wants the next legend, clean, in and out / needs to find out
  whether there is a man under the masks at all — and the only place that answer
  lives is the one place that will lie to him about it.

**The houses (deeper).** *The Dolans*, nine to eleven — a good-enough couple who
wanted a quiet boy and got one; he was so quiet they forgot his birthday twice and
he let them, because being forgotten was a skill and skills were the only currency he
carried between houses. *The Reaches*, twelve to thirteen — a man who hit, and Danny
learned to be so exactly what the man wanted in the doorway that the hand stayed down;
that was the first cover he ever ran, and the man's approval the best review he ever
got. *The Okonkwos*, thirteen to fifteen — the one kind house, where a foster mother
named *Grace* taught him chess and meant to keep him, and he ran away the week before
the adoption hearing, because being kept was the single thing he had no cover for. He
has never told this to a living soul, and has given three different therapists three
different reasons he left.

**The drawing (submerged, load-bearing).** Between houses, in the back of every
caseworker's car, he drew — never pictures of anything, just the same dense
crosshatch that swallowed a page and a half-hour and let him be nowhere. An art
teacher once held up his notebook and said, *this boy disappears into a page like
it's a door,* meaning it as worry; Danny took it as the only true thing anyone ever
saw in him and stopped drawing the next day, because being seen was worse than the
hand in the doorway. The cover — "Danny, aspiring painter" — is going to put a
pencil back in his hand for the first time in thirty years, and the department thinks
it's a prop.

**The covers.** Eleven legends in twelve years. A dock foreman; a hospice volunteer;
a man who sold knockoff insulin to people who couldn't afford the real thing and was
loved for it. *Wendell* — a cover so complete he kept the apartment six weeks after
the arrests because he'd forgotten how to be no one again. And the one that wakes
him: *Petey,* a kid he ran against a crew, nineteen, who trusted Danny like a brother
and is doing eleven years on Danny's word, and who writes him — to the cover name —
twice a year, *Danny, when you getting out?,* and Danny reads them and can't answer,
because there is no Danny to get out.

**Tastes, fears, contingencies.** Can't sit in a room with a ticking clock and won't
say why (it isn't trauma; a clock is just the one thing that was the same in every
house). Tips exactly twenty percent, always — the one fixed value he owns. Has never
finished a book. Fears being *recognized* — actually known, by someone who knew the
boy — more than any gun. Still plays Grace Okonkwo's chess opening, the Caro-Kann,
against a phone app, and loses on purpose.

## MARSH (GARY PRUITT) — the prophet who can't paint

**Natal chart.** Born 12 August 1958, noon exactly, which he has always taken as
proof. **Leo sun** — the center, the warmth, the unbearable need to be adored.
**Pisces moon** — the dreamer and, when it sours, the manipulator who feels his way
into your wound. **Sagittarius rising** — the preacher, the big sky, the man who
makes a room believe the horizon is closer than it is. **Sun square Neptune:** the
grand illusion he sells and is the first to fall for.

**The body.** Big, soft, beautiful hands he uses like a conductor and never to make
anything. A voice he can drop to a register that makes a person feel chosen. Paint
under the nails he puts there in the morning like other men put on a tie.

**The wound (submerged).** Thirty years ago he was a real young painter with one
real show, and a critic ended him in four sentences that he can still recite, and a
rival who couldn't draw as well as him got the cover and the career, and Gary Pruitt
understood, completely and forever, that the gate was rigged and he was on the wrong
side of it. He could not be let in. So he built a room of his own and made himself
the one who decides who's great — and discovered he was a genius at *that.*

**The gift and the lie (submerged).** He can look at a frightened person and see,
exactly, the shape of the hole in them, and name it, and offer to fill it. That is
real, and it is the only real thing he has. He cannot paint. So he takes what his
people make and signs it, and tells them their fame is *coming*, that the work has
to ripen, that the world isn't ready — and half of him believes he's giving them
something truer than fame: a reason to make the work at all.

**Childhood & the parts that go nowhere.** A sign-painter's son who could letter
beautifully and was told it wasn't art. Afraid of exactly one thing: a blank
canvas, which he has not touched alone in three decades. Collects other people's
rejection letters, his own among them, in a locked flat file. Genuinely weeps at
other people's openings, and means it.

**The plot core.**
- *(Submerged)* He has been selling Eli's work — and others' — under his name for
  years, and tells himself it's tuition.
- *(Submerged)* He recognizes Danny the instant he walks in — *another empty one,
  another man with no self, the only kind who really understands the offer* — and
  the recognition is the seduction. He is not conning a mark. He is courting a mirror.
- **Want vs. need:** to be the one who confers greatness, since he was denied it / to
  be seen for the small failed man he is, by one person, and survive it.

**The four sentences (the wound, deeper).** The critic was *Howard Hollis,* dead now,
whom Marsh has outlived and never forgiven for it. The show was eleven paintings in a
borrowed space, decades back; Hollis came, and wrote that the work had "the terrible
competence of a man who studied feeling the way other men study for the bar," and
that "Mr. Pruitt can render anything and has discovered nothing," and two sentences
worse that Marsh can still recite at four in the morning. The rival was *Dana Frame,*
who couldn't draw a hand and got the magazine cover and the teaching chair, and who
once, twenty years on, didn't recognize him at a benefit — and *that,* not Hollis, is
the wound the Collective is built on: not the cruelty of the gate, but being
un-rememberable at it.

**How he sees (the gift, in action).** He clocks the wound in thirty seconds — the
way a person says *I just dabble,* the way they pull the room's attention and then
flinch from it, the photo on the phone of the thing they made at sixteen. Then he
says the sentence no one has ever said to them: *you were the most alive the day you
made that, and you've been dying ever since, and you know it.* It is true. It is the
truest thing he owns. He spends it like a man spends a counterfeit bill he is almost
sure is real.

**The doctrine, in his own mouth.** The world's galleries are "a death-cult of the
already-dead." Fame is "a late and lesser confirmation" of a greatness the Collective
grants *now.* Your work must "ripen out of the light" — which means leave the building
unsigned. The Seeing is "realer than any review." He believes about sixty percent of
all this, which is the single most dangerous percentage there is.

**The daily ritual & the parts that go nowhere.** Puts paint under his nails each
morning from a tube he never otherwise opens. Keeps a studio at the top of the loft
no member may enter; inside stands one primed six-foot canvas he set up decades ago,
has never touched, and dusts himself. Can't sleep without the radio tuned to the hiss
between two stations. *Loved Eli* — actually loved him, the way you love the part of
yourself you killed — and after, alone, went down to the river at the hour the boy
went in, every week for a month, and told no one, and it is the nearest thing to
prayer he has.

## ELI — the proof (the buried engine)

**Natal chart.** Born 1 July 1999, dawn. **Cancer sun** — the one who needs a family,
a shell, a place that's his. **Leo moon** — and needs, just as badly, to be *seen* as
special. **Pisces rising** — the dreamer who takes the dream straight. **Sun conjunct
Neptune:** built on a vision, and undone when the vision dissolved.

**Surface / what the case shows.** A genuinely gifted painter, twenty-three, no
family but the Collective, found dead in the river; ruled a suicide; Marsh's people
all say the same gentle, rehearsed thing about how *fragile* he was, how *the gift
is heavy.*

**The buried engine (submerged).** Eli was Marsh's chosen one, the catalog the
others were sold on. For four years his paintings left the building and came back as
money with Marsh's name on the wall text, and Eli was told his hour was coming. Then
a buyer at a fair called him "Mr. Marsh's assistant," kindly, and the floor went out.
He had not been becoming famous. He had been the machine that fame ran on, and the
self the Collective built him — Eli-the-coming-genius — was a thing they wore. When
that self died, the boy did. *(Whether by his own hand in the collapse, or by hands
that needed it to look that way, is the spine of the investigation — and the thing
Danny is walking straight toward, because the gift that's seducing him is the exact
gift that killed Eli when it turned out to be a lie.)*

**The parts that go nowhere.** Hummed while he worked, always the same four bars, no
one knew of what. Kept a goldfish named after the critic who'd ended Marsh, a joke
only he and Marsh shared. Wrote his sister letters he never mailed, found in a coffee
can. Couldn't whistle. Believed, all the way down, and that was his whole genius and
his whole death.

## NORA — the one who won't be comforted

**Natal chart.** 4 September 1994. **Virgo sun** (the one who checks the work, who
will not sign off on a lie), **Capricorn moon** (grief turned to grind), **Scorpio
rising** (won't look away from the body). Saturn on the Sun: built to carry.

**The life that goes nowhere.** Night-shift x-ray tech; reads bones for a living and
trusts what's actually there over what people swear happened. Raised Eli after their
mother left — eight years older, more parent than sister — and signed him into the
art class that became the Collective, a thing she will not forgive herself for.
Keeps his unmailed letters in the coffee can and has read each one a hundred times.
*(Submerged:* she is the only person in the film who loved Eli for himself and not
for the promise, and she is the proof that being seen plainly by one person is the
thing the whole cult is a forgery of.)*

She becomes Danny's secret tether to the real world — the one face that wants
*nothing performed* from him — which is exactly why she's the most dangerous person
in the film to his cover, and to him.

## LT. REYES — the law as its own faith

**Natal chart.** 19 January 1971. **Capricorn sun** (the structure, the chain of
command, the pension as a creed), **Aquarius moon** (the rules over the person),
**Capricorn rising**. Saturn rising: the institution wearing a face.

**The parts that go nowhere.** Twenty-two years; a daughter at the academy she's
proud of and frightened for; a drawer of commendations she never framed. Believes,
without ever examining it, that the badge is a real self you can put on and have it
be true — which is the cult's exact claim, in a cleaner uniform, and the film makes
her and Marsh rhyme on purpose. She will be the one to order Danny to wear the wire,
and she will be right, and it will cost him the only self he ever wanted.

## THE COLLECTIVE — three of the strands

- **AUGUST, ~55**, a dentist who sold the practice and gave the proceeds, because at
  fifty he could not bear to be a man who'd only ever fixed teeth. Wants, more than
  air, to not have been ordinary. The face of the hunger as vanity.
- **PRIYA, ~20**, the new chosen one — genuinely gifted, exactly where Eli was four
  years ago. Danny watches the machine start on her in real time, which is the
  ticking clock. The face of the hunger as youth.
- **COWAN, ~40**, the believer-enforcer who handles "members in crisis" and the
  paperwork no one's supposed to see. All-in, and therefore dangerous. The face of
  the hunger as belonging, gone hard.

---

## THE COLLECTIVE — the world, the money, the doctrine

*The loft.* A converted ribbon factory at the edge of the arts district. Four floors:
the **workshop** (open, paint-smelling, always lit, no clocks); the **dormitory**
(members give up their leases and move in, "to remove the friction of the outside");
the **gallery and café** at street level (the legitimate face, where the public buys
the work — signed *Marsh*); and the **top floor,** Marsh's, off-limits.

*The money.* Three streams the front launders into one: **tuition** for the public
workshops; **"patronage"** — members signing over savings, inheritances, the proceeds
of sold houses, framed as freeing themselves of the world's claims; and the engine,
**the members' own work** leaving as Marsh's and coming back as money. Cowan keeps two
sets of books. The gallery makes the whole machine look like an institution with a
mission, which is exactly what a good front is for.

*The language.* You don't join, you "come inside." Your old life is "the noise." Your
family is "the first audience that taught you to perform instead of make." Money is
"friction." Leaving is "going back to the dark." Being chosen is "being Seen." Marsh
is never "the leader" — just *Marsh,* said the way you'd say a kind of weather.

*The Seeing (the central rite).* Once a week the Collective circles one member and
tells them, one by one, exactly what they see in them — the gift, the wound, the
greatness coming. It is the most loving thing most of them have ever felt, and it is
the hook: nothing in the outside world will ever do that for them again, and they know
it, and that knowledge is the bars.

*The rebirth (the deep rite — and the film's mirror).* Rare, and only for the chosen:
you "burn the man who couldn't." You destroy something of your old self and make, in
front of everyone, the truest thing you have, and you are renamed. Eli went through
it. Danny will be offered it. It is where the mirror of the film lives — the night he
is meant to *perform* sincerity and discovers, maybe, he has none left to perform.

*The rules no one calls rules.* No clocks on the workshop floor. No phones above the
café. You don't ask where the money goes. You don't ask about Eli.

## THE STRANDS THAT GROW FROM THIS

1. **Danny's strand** — the empty man being handed a self (the cover that becomes
   the truth; the art he starts, against the job, to make for real).
2. **The case strand** — what happened to Eli (Nora, the letters, the buyer, the
   river): the engine that turns the seduction into a countdown.
3. **Marsh's strand** — the prophet and his failing church (Priya being chosen; the
   money; the thing under the suicide), and Marsh courting his mirror.
4. **The law's strand** — Reyes and the wire: the old hollow self calling Danny home
   to a self that was never any realer than the cult's.

They braid at the mirror — the rebirth ritual, where Danny is meant to perform
sincerity and instead makes something true, and can't tell afterward whether he
conned them or confessed.

## METHOD NOTE — why most of this can never appear

The goldfish named for the critic, Danny's undeveloped roll of film, Nora reading
bones, Marsh's locked drawer of rejection letters, the natal charts entire — none of
it will be on screen, and that's the point. It's here so the people are *people* —
known by a thousand things never said — and so the film has somewhere to surprise
itself. Still the second pass at most. A bible is soil; grow more of it than the
story can ever spend.
