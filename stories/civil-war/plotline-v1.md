# (untitled — the second-war novel) — plotline v1

> Drawn 2026-06-10 with the full acquired toolkit (THE HEIR: promise/clock/antagonist-with-agency/resist-ladder/the-break/the-lie/weave-law · PETRICHOR-WORKSONG: the blend-vs-the-sort mirror, love-as-erasure, the suppression props, the petrichor-fact). Honors the bible and both accepted scenes; closes the bible's open threads. Voice law unchanged: Leonard, PaRDeS per scene, sermon discipline — nobody names the theme, the flag never waves.

## The spine, in one breath

America is sorting itself into single-color boxes, and seven families who don't fit any box pay a dead-inside pilot to smuggle them OUT of the country the world used to die to sneak into. The exits keep costing more; some make it out and discover the ache; and when the network is finally betrayed and burned, the ones left don't run for the next exit — they stand on the one piece of ground where their mixed, improvised, bickering caravan has accidentally become the thing they were all fleeing the death of. *You can't escape America by wagon train. The wagon train is America.*

## The engine rules (applied, never stated)

- **Two layers.** Peshat: a book about leaving America. Derash: every act of leaving — the pooled money, the shared watch rotas, the potluck table, the compact — re-founds it. The melting pot reconstitutes itself inside the escape line. The narrator NEVER says this; at most three characters get one oblique naming line (the pilot, Okafor, Mother).
- **The antagonist is the engine's recurring one: love-as-erasure.** The Settling, the Sealing — and now the Sort. **Mother** believes she is healing America by giving everyone "home among their own." She must be rootable: competent, gentle, sincere — and wired in (see ledger). The deeper hand behind her stays shadow at street level but costs arrive only along built edges (weave law).
- **The promise (cold open, en medias res):** the accepted estate scene IS it — a Black family in period clothes bluffing riders off a plantation museum, a shot man hidden in the root cellar. The reader's questions — *why period clothes, who's the wounded man, how is this the future* — are the book's promise. Then: "Eighteen months earlier," Dallas (accepted scene 2). **The wounded man is the pilot** (open thread #1, closed).
- **The clock, in three turns of the screw:** Registration Day (everyone files an Origin Declaration) → Transfer windows (the buses to the zones) → the Airspace Seal (the announced date after which nothing small flies). The pilot's calendar is the book's metronome.
- **The resist-ladder (the for-loop):** for each tightening, the families try one more way out — legal exit visas (denied: "retention is national healing") → the overland convoy (turned back at the state line) → the gulf boat (sold out, the dock raid) → the flights. Every rung costs more and binds the strangers tighter. The audience starts wanting them to stop running — that's the trick.
- **The naming-tell:** officially it's "Community Restoration"; the street calls it **the Sort**. The registry's word for the ensemble — mixed marriages, hyphens, the unboxable — is **"Pending."** Who uses which word is the loyalty-tell, never explained.
- **Props over speeches (Pixar discipline, adult-sized):** the Mizrahis' unused El Al tickets pinned under the motel front-desk glass · Okafor's framed naturalization certificate (and his Nigerian passport, in a drawer, until he burns it) · Desmond's axe (the man who only ever split logs with sed) · the period clothes · the motel ledger that becomes the manifest · Mother's lanyard.
- **The petrichor-fact:** the Mayflower Compact — people *fleeing* a country, signing themselves into a people aboard the escape vehicle. The book restages it without naming it: a handwritten page in a motel office, signed by everyone who stays. The second fact underneath: the naturalization oath ("I absolutely and entirely renounce...") — run in reverse by the Authority's exit visas, and refused.

## The ledger (weave law — every family load-bearing in another family's arc; no cost from offstage)

- **DESMOND / DELLA / NIA (the anchor — Black, St. Louis → Dallas → the estate).** Oldest claim in the cast: no boats to burn; they were the cargo once. Desmond's systems lens (failure modes, redundancy, single points of failure) is the railroad's hidden architecture — he becomes its dispatcher without ever volunteering. Arc shape: *dread vindicated → redundancy as love → learning the one failure mode he never modeled (going it alone).* Della: the hope to his dread, rifles clean. Nia: 12 in Dallas, 16 at the estate; her thread with the Kim boy is the melting pot's first act (accepted scene 2) and the book's young spine.
- **THE PILOT — RAY COLE (the native, the inverse Rick).** Crop-duster turned charter ghost; sells exits for gold and means it about nothing. Arc shape: *merchandise → meaning;* the man who sold the way out ends up the one who can't board. He is the wounded man in the cold open — shot at the dock raid, hauled to the estate by the family he was paid to fly out. His Bonasera-ledger: every family aboard once did him a kindness he priced at zero.
- **THE KIMS (Korean — the collaborators, and the Carlo run in reverse).** Dallas neighbors from accepted scene 2; the mother drew the first line in a driveway. The father buys safety as a registry block-warden — *comply, comply, comply* — until the Sort zones his own mixed-blood niece as "Pending." The turn: his registry access becomes the railroad's manifest. The betrayal beat inverts Carlo: the network is blown not BY him but by the Authority *through* him (his logins audited — the collaborator's tools point both ways), so his guilt is structural, and his redemption is the climax's key (he knows the Sort's own choreography). Min, the son: crossed the yard to Nia once; crosses everything else after.
- **THE MIZRAHIS (Israeli — somewhere to go).** The standing exit: tickets, relatives, a state that will take them tomorrow. Arc shape: *the unused ticket* — a resist-ladder of their own, refusing their own exit one crisis at a time, never able to say why. The grandfather plays chess with Haddad and won't concede games or arguments.
- **THE HADDADS (Arab — nowhere to go).** Home is rubble; America is the only address left, and America keeps asking them to prove it. Arc shape: *being seen.* The Authority zones the Mizrahis and Haddads apart early as a "tension pair" — Exhibit A that mixing fails — which is exactly why they end as one household at the motel. The keystone pair: one can leave and won't; one can't and isn't believed.
- **THE SHARMAS (Indian — thought they'd made it).** Affluent, assimilated, donor-class — and the Sort doesn't care what you built. They own the **WAYFARER MOTEL** outside Dallas (the bible's "third space" — difference, chosen, alive — given a roof and a ledger). Mother personally grants the Wayfarer a "model business" exemption early — her one mercy, and the debt that makes the whole railroad possible under her nose (the villain's gift enables the resistance; the spared-believer debt, inverted). Arc shape: *from passing to choosing* — the innkeepers who stop charging.
- **THE OKAFORS (Nigerian — the true believers, the moral engine).** Naturalized eight years; the father can recite the oath and does, badly timed, at length; Lagos success waits for them with open arms. Arc shape: *faith on trial* — the only family with a genuinely good elsewhere, and the only man in the book who burns his own boat ON PURPOSE: the passport, in the motel parking lot, at the all-is-lost, with no speech.
- **THE REYES (Hispanic — here first).** Six generations in the Valley; the border crossed *them*. The Sort assigns them a "heritage community" across the river, for a country that was never theirs. Arc shape: *rage into stewardship.* They carry the book's bitterest image and its proudest: the second time the family crosses the Rio Grande, it's northward, at night, coming HOME.
- **MOTHER (the Inquisitor).** Soft-voiced director of Community Restoration, Texas region. Rootable: she ended an actual massacre once with a sorting order, and never recovered from being right. Her naming line (one, late): "People heal among their own. You're bleeding because you're mixed together." Wired in: the Sharma exemption; the Kim handler chain; she interviews Nia once, kindly, and gets the book's flattest child-stare. The deeper hand (the bloc that profits) stays off-page — she is its lanyard.
- Deliberately unwired texture: the estate's living-history wardrobe (why the period clothes exist — it's a plantation museum; the family hides as the exhibit), the motel's permanent residents, the radio preacher.

## The heart ledger (added 2026-06-10 — Part One is the BANKING act)

The law (`metaphrand/heart.py`, `docs/05-PASSES.md` §2): deposits are banked early, in passing, in
scenes about something else, BEFORE any wound spends them; nobody is ever argued warm; cold blocks
plot; deposits can sour; tragedy's move is the almost-thaw. Part One's job is the bank. What each
bond banks, and where it pays:

- **Desmond↔Della** — *the two mugs*: the porch ritual, fifteen quiet minutes at dusk, her mug on
  the rail, his on the step, nothing said that matters. (Pays: on the run, a gas-station coffee
  handed over in silence re-opens a conversation neither could start; sours late if dread wins.)
- **Desmond↔Nia** — *the checklist game*: she "runs diagnostics" with him on the truck, calling
  items off his hand-written card, deadpan, both pretending it's chores. The lens leaking as love.
  (Pays: the evacuation drill that saves the motel in Part Three runs on her voice reading a card.)
- **Min↔Nia** — *the fence trade*: after the yard-crossing (accepted Ch.2, already banked), the
  standing swap over the back fence — Grace's banchan tubs for Della's cornbread, contraband both
  directions, never discussed at either dinner table. (Pays: the manifest warning crosses the same
  fence in Part Two; sours when the Kims register the street.)
- **Avram (Mizrahi)↔Sami (Haddad)** — *the chessboard, mid-game*: watch-repair shop and bakery,
  adjacent doors; the standing Saturday game that has never once finished; each keeps a photo of
  the board's position on his phone. (Pays: reassembled at the Wayfarer mid-game at the midpoint
  potluck; the board stays set forever after Part Three. BANK IT HARD — the grandfather falls.)
- **The Kim household** — *the Sunday car wash*: Howard and Min, buckets, the radio, the one hour
  Grace lets the line in the driveway mean nothing. (Pays: the confession at the potluck table
  lands on a man we watched be gentle; the audit sours the same driveway.)
- **Ray↔his own ledger** — *the stencil*: a boy's name stenciled under the cockpit rail of the
  Cessna, never explained in Part One, his hand touching it on preflight like a switch he has to
  throw. (Pays: Part Three, once, in one sentence. The reader earns it by then.)
- **The Sharmas↔the Wayfarer** — *Room 9*: the standing habit of giving 9 free to stranded
  truckers, against Vik's own ledger-keeping instincts, because Asha started it the week they
  bought the place. (Pays: the habit of vacancy becomes the book's last line.)
- **The Okafors** — *the oath at the birthday*: Emeka recites the naturalization oath at Chidi's
  birthday every year, full length, family groaning, candles burning down. Cringe and love in one
  ritual. (Pays: the megaphone scene in Part Three — the same recitation, weaponized against him,
  recited anyway. Sours never: this one is bedrock.)
- **The Reyes** — *the riverbank table*: the June asado on the Rio Grande bank, same spot six
  generations, the river just scenery. (Pays: the northward crossing in Part Four is a return to
  a picnic spot. The angriest image in the book and the warmest, same coordinates.)
- **Mother's anti-deposit** — *the exemption*: her one mercy to the Sharmas, given graciously,
  on camera. (Pays: revoked on camera; and the railroad runs under it the whole time.)

Per-chapter rule for the outline (Phase B): every chapter banks at least one deposit OR spends one;
no chapter does neither. Wounds begin only after beat 6 (the buses).

## Beatline (the braid; ~30 beats; the railroad is the monorail all arcs hang on)

### Part One — the sorting (the lie-world)
1. **COLD OPEN = accepted scene 1.** The estate, the riders, the bluff, the shot man in the root cellar. *Which plantation did you run away from.* They shoot clear. — "EIGHTEEN MONTHS EARLIER."
2. **= accepted scene 2.** Dallas; the house tour; Desmond's compound joke that isn't; the Korean mother's line in the driveway; Min crosses the yard to Nia. (Sod detonates: same people, now.)
3. The shock arrives sideways: prices, the bank holiday, the atrocity on every screen pinned to one group, the reprisal nobody claims. The neighborhood watch becomes a checkpoint in eight weeks.
4. **Registration Day.** The Origin Declaration forms: Desmond reads his ("African-American — Zone assignment pending") in the systems voice he uses for failure reports. The Kims file early and correctly. Okafor staples his naturalization certificate to the form, beaming, missing it entirely.
5. World-flesh: the Wayfarer Motel at its own business — long-stay rooms, the Sharma teenagers running towels, the ice machine politics. Mother's visit: the model-business exemption, graciously given. (The debt planted.)
6. The first buses. The Haddads' street zoned east; the Mizrahis' west — the "tension pair" separated for everyone's safety, the grandfather's chessboard left mid-game in a moving box.
7. **RAY COLE introduced doing the thing:** a night flight out of a county strip — gold taken, no names wanted, a family delivered to a Mexican tarmac like freight. Dead inside, competent, paid. (The inverse Rick, established in commerce.)
8. The Reyes notice their assignment is across the river. Old man Reyes laughs once, wrong, and starts cleaning a rifle older than the state of Texas.

### Part Two — the ladder (every exit costs more)
9. **Rung 1 — the legal way.** Exit-visa office; the Authority denies en masse ("retention is healing"). The families first see EACH OTHER here, in the same line, holding the same Pending stamps.
10. **Rung 2 — the convoy.** Overland to the border, turned back at a state line by a militia that takes the trucks. Cost: the Okafors' savings; the first night all seven families sleep under one roof — the Wayfarer. Nobody calls it anything yet.
11. **D1 — they hire the pilot.** Desmond negotiates (systems lens: "what's your failure rate"); Ray prices them like freight; the families pool gold across lines that two months ago were walls. *The doorway: the night they become criminals together.*
12. The motel becomes the station without a vote: Sharma stops writing names in the ledger and starts writing weights (the manifest). Kim's registry access starts steering bus schedules around flight nights. Della teaches the Mizrahi kids to shoot; the Haddad father fixes the well.
13. **The first flight out works.** The Okafor cousins lifted to Monterrey. Triumph — then the midpoint's first half arrives by letter: Lagos is warm, generous, and *foreign*; "you've become American, we can hear it." The elsewhere doesn't fit. (The ache, planted early and from off-stage — along a built edge.)
14. **MIDPOINT MIRROR — the potluck.** One table in the motel lot: seven cuisines, one argument about brisket, the chessboard reassembled mid-game, Min and Nia on the roof keeping watch like it's a porch. The reader sees it plainly: this parking lot is the country they're trying to reach. The families misread it exactly: "we only hold together because we're leaving." The truth, seen and refused — the lie reinforced with evidence.
15. **Rung 3 — the boat.** The gulf option for the big group; the dock raid (sold out from inside the harbor, not the motel — costs arrive on built edges only); Ray takes a round dragging the Reyes boy clear. Cost: the boat money, the coast route, and the pilot's blood — the cargo carries HIM now.
16. Mother, closer: the Kim audits begin. The block-warden's logins have patterns. The father knows what the patterns spell and says nothing, sick.

### Part Three — the burn (the break)
17. The Airspace Seal announced: a date. The metronome goes loud. The remaining families split the last flights by lottery — and quietly, each rigs it for someone else (the Mizrahis palm their own numbers; Okafor "loses" his slip). The exits everyone fought for start being given away, and nobody discusses it.
18. **The flight that leaves.** Half the cast at the strip at dawn. The Mizrahis, at the stair door, with real tickets in a real plane for the first time — don't board. The grandfather: "We're mid-game." (The unused ticket pays; the keystone pair stays one household.) Who actually leaves: the Sharma elders with the grandchildren; the Reyes mother with the youngest. The plane gets small. The ache is now inside the cast.
19. **The manifest blown.** The Kim audit closes its loop; the Authority reads the railroad out of the registry. The father confesses at the potluck table BEFORE the raid lands — the inverse-Carlo: the betrayal announced by the betrayed instrument himself. The motel empties in ninety minutes on Desmond's drilled redundancy plan (the systems payoff: there was always a backup site — the estate).
20. **The raid / ALL IS LOST.** The Wayfarer taken and stripped; the exemption revoked on camera (Mother, gently: "We protected this place once"); Okafor arrested ON the framed certificate; Ray shot at the airstrip torching his own plane so it can't be flown for them. The night ends with the burning motel sign and Okafor's passport already ash in the lot — he burned the boat an hour before they took him.
21. **The estate.** The survivors fold into the plantation museum: period clothes off the costume racks, the exhibit as camouflage — America's future hiding inside its oldest wound. (Open thread closed; the cold open's question answered in full.) Ray bleeding in the root cellar: the riders come. **= the cold open, replayed from inside, two beats longer:** after the shots clear, Desmond stands in the yard in an overseer's coat he refuses to button and says the book's quietest line to no one: "Only logs I ever split, I split with sed."
22. **The break.** Not a speech — a logistics meeting. Desmond at the museum's smokehouse table with the manifest ledger: the next exit options read aloud — another boat, a desert line, a bribe schedule. Long silence. Della cleans nothing, for once. Nia: "Where would we even be FROM?" Nobody answers her, which is the answer. Desmond closes the ledger. *Flight becomes fight on a child's grammar question.*

### Part Four — the stand (the test)
23. They take the Wayfarer back before the Authority re-lets it — not as a station now; as an address. The repainting beat (each family's color argument = the modulation valley).
24. **The lawyer's-baptism, theirs:** one coordinated week, all on paper and patience — Kim's choreography knowledge stalls the zone buses; Sharma's exemption appeal ties Mother's office in its own procedure; Reyes files six generations of land title; the Mizrahi-Haddad household registers as ONE family and dares the form to object. The Sort fought with its own instruments.
25. Okafor's trial-by-megaphone: marched out to recite the oath for the cameras as humiliation — and he recites it the whole way through, slowly, like a man counting his money, and the clip runs everywhere the Authority didn't intend. (The moral engine fires; no narrator comment.)
26. **The Battle of the Wayfarer.** Mother sends the enforcement she always had. The defense is the weave paying off in one sequence: Desmond's redundancy (the second well, the second generator, the second exit), Kim reading the assault's bus-schedule logic, Reyes ground-craft, Della's range discipline, the Haddad-Mizrahi pair holding the roof like a chessboard. Ray, half-healed, flies exactly once more — empty, low, loud: the distraction pass. They don't win a war; they make the cost of one motel absurd, in front of cameras the Authority can't sort.
27. Mother's withdrawal — and her one naming line at the fence with Desmond: "People heal among their own." Desmond, flat: "These are my own." She looks at the seven-family roofline a long beat, and the book lets her almost see it.
28. **The compact.** The motel office, that night: a handwritten page — house rules, watch rota, the well schedule, who teaches whom what — signed by everyone who stays, kids included, Min and Nia last. Nobody calls it anything. It goes under the front-desk glass NEXT to the Mizrahis' unused tickets. (The Mayflower under glass; not one word of theme.)
29. **The exit visas arrive** — the Authority's last move: expulsion dressed as mercy, renunciation paperwork enclosed. The stack sits on the desk a day. Then Sharma uses them as scratch paper for the towel inventory. (The oath, refused in inventory ink.)
30. **Spring.** The sign repainted. The Reyes mother and the Sharma elders come BACK north across the river with the kids, on foot, at night, laughing wrong and crying right. A new family pulls in at dusk asking — the way people now ask — not for a room: for the way out. Desmond looks past them at the lot: the chess game, the towel line, Nia and Min painting Room 9. "We can do better than that," he says. "We got vacancies." — END.
