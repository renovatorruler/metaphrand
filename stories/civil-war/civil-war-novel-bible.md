# (working title — untitled) — Story Bible

Companion to the manuscript (`civil-war-novel.md`). Holds the design: the world, the structure, the cast, the method, the metaphors, and per-scene notes. Voice and scene discipline live in `docs/03-VOICE_GUIDE.md`.

## What it is

A novel. A second American civil war, told through an **ensemble of families** who'd never have chosen each other. The subject is **American identity** — whether "American" is something you're born or something you become — *not* the left/right culture war.

**The aim (the transformation):** the reader closes the book with their love for the American idea rejuvenated — earned, never declared. The melting pot, proven under fire by the people with the least reason to believe in it. Hold every page to the sermon discipline: nobody names the theme, the flag never waves, the feeling is paid for in concrete acts and real graves.

**The engine of decline (backstory, never exposition):** old immigrants burned their boats — no way home, so they *became* American. New immigrants never burned anything: cheap flights, a phone that's always home, a second passport in the drawer. One foot out the door, loyalty never fully poured. The war's pressure burns the boats for them.

## The world

**Why the war.** Not two armies. A country that came apart: the shared "we" thinned over a generation → a hard economic shock (money crisis, the safety net fails) → an atrocity pinned on one group → reprisals → fragmentation. Power drops to militias, enclaves, private security. Bosnia in American sprawl.

**The enemy — the Authority.** It ends the open killing the only way it can imagine: by keeping everyone with their own kind. Not jackboots — the captured *helping* institutions (emergency management, public health, the universities, the NGOs) fused into a bureaucracy of care pointed at separation. A lanyard, not a uniform. Fronted by **the Inquisitor** ("Mother") — soft-voiced, righteous, certain she's saving everyone. Behind her, a power bloc that profits from a permanently sorted, dependent, frightened population.

**The zones.** The Authority registers everyone by origin and moves them into "safe communities" for their protection. The metaphor cast in concrete: forced urbanization / the already-segregated American city with the polite fiction stripped off — the diverse metro where a hundred languages never share a table, made mandatory. A gilded replica of the old country: a velvet cell. Three spaces, three meanings — the **zone** (sameness by force), the **home country** they flee to (sameness by choice), the **building** (difference, chosen, alive = the real America).

**Sealed borders.** Leaving is criminalized. The wall faces inward: a refuge people are paying to flee is a prison, so the lie requires the wall. (Legitimacy; labor/brain-drain; population-as-power in a faction war; the hidden hand guarding its captive economy.)

## The structure

**The inverse-coyote story.** America is the place the world dies to sneak *into*; this is a book about families paying a smuggler to sneak *out*. *Casablanca* run backwards. The image is the thesis — no one has to say a word.

**Flight becomes fight.** They spend the book trying to burn their *American* boats (flee) and find they won't burn — home isn't home. What burns is the home-country fantasy. They become American the moment they turn and choose to stay and fight for the place.

**The braid.** The escape is the shared spine (the cage); the families are the different animals — six distinct arcs and reasons, braided by the escape and converging at the stand. **Order: anchor → reveal → fan out.** Don't fan to the other families until the reader has the frame and a family they'd bleed for.

## The cast

**The native anchor — the pilot.** A disillusioned native who smuggles people out for money, dead inside about the country (the inverse Rick). Through these families he gets his country back; the man who sold the escape ends up the one who can't leave. The reader's surrogate (the upset native).

**The six families** — each a different answer to "what makes an American":
- **Israeli** — somewhere to go (a real, waiting exit; stays anyway).
- **Arab** — nowhere to go (home is rubble); fights to be *seen* as American. *(Keystone pair with the Israelis: one can leave and won't, one can't and isn't let in.)*
- **Indian** — thought they'd made it (assimilated, affluent); the sort doesn't care.
- **Nigerian** — true believers (love the country harder than any native); faith on trial. The moral engine.
- **Hispanic** — here first (six generations; the border crossed them); the angriest.
- **Asian / Korean** — the collaborators (buy safety by helping the Authority); guilt, then the turn.

**The anchor family (first chair) — Desmond's family.**
- **Desmond** — a Black systems engineer who ran a data center (kept ten thousand machines breathing at 3 a.m.). His systems mind is his **lens**, kept submerged: he reads the world in failure modes, single points of failure, redundancy, backups — shown in behavior, never named, until the post-reveal payoff. *(The "logs/sed" callback: in ch. 1 he can't split a wood **log**; later, once we know him — "Only logs I ever split, I split with sed. On a machine the size of this house. Never once needed an axe.")* The broader device: every family's pre-collapse self leaks through as the lens they can't help seeing by.
- **Della** — his wife; gentle once, a schoolteacher type, now cleans the rifles without thinking. The hope to his dread.
- **Nia** — the daughter; a watchful kid who already knows hard things. ~12 in Dallas, ~14 and hardened at the estate (the ch.2 marker — eighteen months — is canon).
- **Path:** St. Louis (the old life) → a Dallas suburb (the move, as it starts going bad) → later, on the run, a colonial-era estate where they end up in period clothes = the opening.
- **The Korean boy** (next door in Dallas) — first crossing of a line his mother drew; a thread that pays off later.

## Method

**Period texture (user law, 2026-06-10):** the world must be built rich enough to READ as
slavery-era South without a single explainer — clothing shown in use (brogans split at the welt,
homespun gone soft, linen boiled in the yard pot), work shown mid-task (troughs, oats, harness),
and the language given its allegiance to shock: the riders speak their period's ugliness — the
slur belongs in THEIR mouths, used sparingly so it lands, and never in the narration's. The
narrator never borrows the world's words for people; the world convicts itself.

Voice: **Elmore Leonard** — lean, dialogue-forward, flat, invisible. Every scene is built and checked on **Peshat / Remez / Derash / Sod** + the **Mamet** drama, then run through the slop pass. All of it in `docs/03-VOICE_GUIDE.md`.

## Scene notes

**Scene 1 — the estate.**
- *Drama:* the patrol's MOUTH (Moses) wants the fugitive given up or disproven; its AUTHORITY (Silas, who barely speaks) wants the bounty without losing a man; Desmond wants them gone believing there's nothing here — a bluff with his family's lives on it. *Now:* they've tracked the man here, he's shot, he can't run. Turns when the bluff nearly works and the saddle betrays it.
- *The first shot is DELLA'S* (rev. 2026-06-10, user note): she drops the young one mid-shout from her window while Desmond still believes he's winning the parley. Desmond's anger after ("I had them leaving") runs into the evidence she read and he didn't — the muscle's rein-hand drifting, the quiet one counting windows; the leaving was a performance. The marriage's poles have swapped since Dallas (his dread/her hope → her colder, better eye), and nobody says so.
- *The wounded man = RAY (canon) — and in ch.1 he is NEVER on the page* (layer-card invariant;
  the concealment is the scene). The reader gets only: the riders' description, the bay + the
  silver-cantled saddle (his fee, worn under him, from the world after the Seal killed flying),
  the good linen boiling on a Tuesday (silent), Nia's unanswered WHOSE, and the bounty — forty
  dollars gold (re-read: "forty" is Ray's number, planted in the PATROL'S mouth). The
  fever-counting / answers-with-a-number clue is SAVED for the Part Two reveal, where it does the
  identifying. One quiet anachronism only — the laminated recovery paper — detonated by ch.2.
- *Threads opened, held for Part Two+:* Silas survives and the dead boy is his blood — the patrol returns; what the family does with a man who can't ride; why a man that age is worth that much gold.
- *Peshat:* a Black family bluffs four armed riders off their farm; the bluff breaks on the fugitive's horse; they shoot clear.
- *Remez:* the slave patrol — the hunt, the harbored fugitive, "which plantation you run off from," and the gut-turn that the hunters are his own.
- *Derash:* America's oldest sin doesn't stay buried; a man is measured by who he'll shelter when it comes back.
- *Sod:* it isn't the past — it's the future. (Kept fully off the page.)

**The Grace rule (user law, 2026-06-10): no flat racists in the weave.** When a scene reads as
bigotry and opens a thread, the bigotry is a VENEER over a second, specific, private cause that
re-keys the scene later. Ch.2's fence scene: Grace isn't walling her kids off from the Black girl —
her mother-in-law (halmoni, newly moved in, wandering) got out the front door while the kids were
in the yard; the fury is a caregiver's terror, in Korean, and her eyes never land on Nia because
Nia isn't in her crisis at all. The veneer stays real for NIA (she's earned the read; that ache is
true and stays) — the world is just more specific than the read. Plant in ch.2: a thin voice inside
calling one name twice; Min's non-explanation ("She's like that since my halmoni came"). Re-key in
the tubs section: the recipe is halmoni's; "she got out the front door that first day — that's what
the yelling was." Halmoni then load-bears ch.9/Howard (the heart medication, the stipend).

**Scene 2 — Dallas (the reveal).**
- *Drama:* Desmond wants Della to see the danger he sees; Della wants him to let them have a normal life; the realtor wants the sale. Outside: Nia wants in with the neighbor kids; the Korean mother shuts her out; the boy crosses anyway. *Stakes* (invisible to them): Desmond is right. *Now:* they're about to sign.
- *Peshat:* a family tours a Dallas house; the husband frets and floats a compound, the wife teases him down; the daughter is rebuffed by the Korean mother, and the boy crosses the yard to her.
- *Remez:* the war in seed — the mother walling her kids off is the sort in miniature; Desmond's dread is the first tremor; the boy crossing the line is the melting pot's first act.
- *Derash:* how thin the ice under the ordinary dream is; division and decency both start in a driveway; a person is measured by which line they'll cross.
- *Sod (detonates here):* it isn't 1870 — it's now, and these are the same people. The dream is where the nightmare came from.

## Arena rules — how the Authority actually works (never on the page as exposition)
- **The model:** not jackboots — the captured *helping* institutions (emergency management, public
  health, university compliance, the big NGOs) fused into the **Office of Community Restoration**.
  Lanyards, intake forms, wellness language. Enforcement is contracted security and federalized
  Guard remnants; the OCR never carries the gun itself, it *schedules* the gun.
- **The registry:** Registration Day → every adult files an **Origin Declaration** (ancestry,
  faith, language, "community of belonging"). The form has no box for *mixed* — mixed files get
  stamped **PENDING**. Pending means: travel restricted, accounts flagged, assignment deferred —
  the unsortable live in administrative limbo, which is the point.
- **The zones:** "Heritage Communities" — urban sectors emptied and refilled by category, sold as
  protection from the militias (and the militias are real; the fear isn't fake, which is why it
  works). Transfer windows move people by bus, by street, with movers and counselors and zero choice.
- **The clock:** Registration Day → Transfer windows (rolling, by county) → the **Airspace Seal**
  (announced date; after it, nothing small flies without OCR clearance). Leaving is "abandonment
  of community" — a crime against healing.
- **Money:** accounts tied to registry status; Pending = withdrawal caps. Hence gold, barter,
  and the price of a seat on Ray's plane.
- **The naming-tells (never explained):** officials say *Community Restoration* and *Heritage
  Communities*; the street says **the Sort** and **the zones**; the registry's word for the
  unboxable is **Pending**, and people have started using it as a noun for themselves, with
  exactly the pride you'd expect Texans to find in it.

## Cast detail (iceberg · chart · voice · architecture — the per-work pass)

### Desmond (anchor POV)
**Iceberg:** ran reliability for a St. Louis data center; the man paged at 3 a.m. for a decade,
who learned that everything fails and only design decides what happens next. His grandmother's
stories of Mississippi were *operational* — exits, cash, names of safe towns — and he absorbed
them as architecture, not history. Never states any of this. **Chart:** Virgo Sun · Saturn rising ·
Moon in Cancer (the dread is love wearing armor) · Mercury in Virgo. **Voice/architecture:**
complete, precise sentences; exact qualifiers ("probably," "twice, maybe three times"); never
profane; asks questions he already knows the answers to when he's frightened. His lens leaks
(failure modes, redundancy, single point of failure) — facts never do. Epigram budget: the sed
line, and almost nothing else.

### Della
**Iceberg:** third-grade teacher, twenty years; the one who stayed calm for other people's
children through two lockdowns, which is where the steadiness was forged that now cleans rifles
without looking down. Her father hunted; she hasn't told Desmond how good she is. **Chart:**
Taurus Sun · Moon in Leo (warmth that organizes a room) · Mars in Capricorn (the slow fuse).
**Voice/architecture:** short declaratives wrapped warm; teacher-cadence when she's managing fear
("we're going to do this first, then this"); names people by name when it matters. Never
rhetorical questions.

### Nia
**Iceberg:** twelve in Dallas, fourteen at the estate; the kid who reads rooms because she's been
the only Black girl in three different ones. Keeps a notebook nobody's seen. **Architecture:**
kid-blunt, present tense, questions adults pretend not to hear ("Where would we even be FROM?").
Her sentences get shorter as the war gets longer.

### Ray Cole (the pilot, second POV)
**Iceberg:** Army aviation, two tours flying medevac; came home to a county that hollowed out
quietly — the mill, then the pharmacy, then his boy, whose name is stenciled under the cockpit
rail and never explained until Part Three. Flies freight, then people, prices everything because
pricing is the only arithmetic that never lied to him. **Chart:** Capricorn Sun · Mars in
Taurus · Moon in Scorpio, 12th (the grief in the basement) · Saturn conjunct Sun. **Voice/
architecture:** laconic, transactional, weather-and-load vocabulary for everything including
feelings ("that's more weight than this conversation lifts"); answers questions with numbers.
The only character allowed sentence fragments by design — and even his go subject-verb when he's
moved, which is how the reader learns to read him.

### The Nguyens — Thanh, Hoa, the Toronto son (the dispatchers; POV ch.3)
**Iceberg (Thanh):** strip-mall pharmacist, twenty-one years behind the same counter; son of 1979
boat people who were themselves routed out by a chain of strangers' kindnesses he was raised on
like scripture — he is paying a forty-year-old debt forward and has never once said so. Reads the
war in prescriptions: formulary changes, cash-only scripts, which refills suddenly need a registry
number. **Chart:** Cancer Sun · Saturn in the 4th (the shop is the house) · Mercury in Virgo.
**Voice/architecture:** gentle, unfinished sentences for small things that turn complete and exact
when it matters; drug names pronounced like psalms; never says "escape," says "filled." **(Hoa):**
the ledger of the marriage; wants Toronto (the son, a resident at St. Mike's) and files her case
weekly without ever raising her voice. The standing argument is the chapter's valley engine.
**Thread:** they stay. The shelf of other people's medicine is why, and it is never said.

### The Mehtas — Arun, Priya, Veda (the flown family, ch.6)
**Iceberg (Arun):** cardiologist; privileges suspended by category audit the morning of ch.3; his
father's Omega from Bombay on his wrist, his parents in a frame in the duffel. Toronto sponsor =
his sister. **(Priya):** the one who asks about the dog because somebody has to say it out loud.
**(Veda):** eight; the dog wears her belt for a harness; she has already taught it to ride a
bicycle basket, which is why it takes the airplane personally. **Voice:** Arun precise, grateful
in a way he keeps trying to make contractual; Priya practical-soft; Veda all questions.

### The patrol (ch.1) — four riders, named in the bible, never on the page
Black men hunting Black men for the regime — the Sort making collaborators of everyone is the book's
machine, and the patrol is its oldest gear (Howard Kim's lanyard, eighteen months earlier, is this
crew in beta). Structure: the mouth is not the authority — Desmond's lens finds the single point of
command by watching who the muscle looks at.
- **Moses Tatum (the mouth).** Front-rides, does all the talking in plantation-courtesy forms; freedman
  cadence learned as armor and kept as trade. Iceberg: bought his own family's standing with this work
  and tells himself the ledger balances. Chart: Gemini Sun, Mercury in Leo — performance as survival.
  Voice: cordial menace, never profane, quotes prices like scripture. Takes one high and lives.
- **Silas Crane (the authority).** Rear-rides, oilcloth over the good rifle, spends words like coins
  ("Mount up" is a speech). Iceberg: thirty years a hunt-camp foreman; reads ground, wind, and men, in
  that order; the only one Della can't get a clean line on, which both of them register. Chart:
  Capricorn Sun, Scorpio Moon — patience as violence deferred. SURVIVES UNTOUCHED; the dead boy is his
  sister's son; he will come back. (Part Two+ thread.)
- **Eli Crane (the eyes).** Seventeen, Silas's nephew, first season; sharp-sighted and green — spots
  the silver cantle and dies mid-sentence for being right. Chart: Aries Sun, all throttle. His death is
  the chapter's bill: the family's safety now costs a boy.
- **Dab Ferris (the muscle).** Dismounts, ambles, spits; glances past Moses to Silas before every move
  (the glance is the org chart). Iceberg: plantation-bred bare-knuckler who found the work easier than
  the field. Dies in the yard when it goes.

### The Kims — Howard, Grace, Min
**Iceberg (Howard):** came at nine, anglicized his own name at nineteen the week before a job
interview, has been paying that debt quietly ever since; block-warden because the man with the
clipboard never gets the knock. **(Grace):** her mother rebuilt twice — Seoul to Inchon to LA —
and taught her that lines drawn early are cheaper than walls built late; the driveway line was
love, by her arithmetic. **(Min):** fifteen; crossed the yard once and never quite came back.
**Charts:** Howard — Libra Sun, Saturn in the 10th (safety through correctness); Grace — Capricorn
Sun, Moon in Virgo (love as logistics); Min — Aquarius Sun (the line-crosser). **Architecture:**
Howard speaks in policy ("the guidance says"), softening only off-record; Grace in imperatives and
food; Min in the flat ironies of a teenager translating between two houses.

### Avram Mizrahi & the Mizrahis
**Iceberg:** watch repairman; Haifa-born, Houston for forty years; buried his wife here, which is
the entire answer to "why won't you use the tickets" and he will never say it. Daughter Dalia

late; argues by anecdote that lands two beats after he's left the room; in the chess games, speech
drops to single words ("So." "Again." "Mid-game.").

### Sami Haddad & the Haddads
**Iceberg:** baker; Homs until Homs ended; rebuilt the bakery from a photograph of his father's.
America is the third country to ask him for papers and the first he loved. Wife Rima; daughter
Noor (pre-med, the family's English). **Chart:** Cancer Sun · Mars in Leo (the warmth that won't
duck) · Moon in Taurus. **Architecture:** generous, interruptive, feeds people mid-sentence;

quiet and exact — the only times his English is perfect.

### The Sharmas — Vik & Asha
**Iceberg:** bought the Wayfarer off I-35 in '09, raised two teens (Rohan, Tara) in the back

the free Room 9 the first week; Vik logs it as a loss every month and has never once stopped her.
**Charts:** Vik — Capricorn Sun, Mercury in Aquarius (the ledger and the argument); Asha — Leo
Moon (the open hand). **Architecture:** Vik in inventory and percentages; Asha in hospitality

### The Okafors — Emeka & Ngozi
**Iceberg:** Lagos-credentialed engineer and nurse; naturalized eight years ago; Emeka has the

Kids Chidi and Ada; the Lagos cousins' success is real and waiting. **Charts:** Emeka — Leo Sun,
Jupiter rising (the believer); Ngozi — Virgo Sun (the auditor of the belief). **Architecture:**
Emeka quotes — founding documents, proverbs, his father — always attributed; Ngozi answers the
quote with the grocery list. The gap between is the marriage.

### The Reyes — Nacho, Marco, Lupe, Danny
**Iceberg:** six generations in the Valley; the border crossed *them* in 1848 and the family has
outlasted every government since. Nacho (patriarch) keeps the original land grant in a tube in
the gun safe — not as proof for courts; as proof for himself. Son Marco (diesel mechanic), Lupe,
grandson Danny (the boy Ray drags clear at the dock raid). **Chart (Nacho):** Aries Sun · Saturn
in Taurus (rage with a foundation). **Architecture:** Nacho bilingual-terse, switches to Spanish
for tenderness and legal English for threats; Marco talks torque and schedules; Danny narrates
everything like the YouTube videos he misses.

### Mother — Director Ellen Mara, Office of Community Restoration (Region 6)
**Iceberg:** ER administrator during the collapse year; ended an actual massacre in Kessler Park
by sorting a crowd onto separate buses, and has never recovered from being right once. Soft-voiced,
tireless, remembers everyone's children's names. The street calls her Mother and she has decided
to take it as affection. **Chart:** Scorpio Sun · Moon in Capricorn · Neptune square Mercury (the
mission fog). **Architecture:** wellness language run at governmental scale ("we're going to get
you home"); never threatens — schedules; the one naming line late in the book and no other
self-revelation. Her mercy (the Sharma exemption) is sincere. That's the horror.
