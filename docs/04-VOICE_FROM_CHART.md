# Voice From The Chart

**System: match the chart to the story's world.** Use **Vedic / Jyotish** (the nine *graha*) for Indian
stories, **Western / tropical** for Western ones. The six voice-axes below read the same either way —
only the inputs change. Graha → axis key: **Shani (Saturn)** clips/grounds/colds; **Guru (Jupiter)**
swells/preaches; **Budh (Mercury)** is the core mind-and-language; **Chandra (Moon)** colors with feeling;
**Mangal (Mars)** sharpens/speeds/heats; **Shukra (Venus)** warms; **Rahu** jolts, obsesses, loads,
intoxicates (no Western equal — the insatiable head); **Ketu** dissolves, severs, detaches (the headless
body, the elliptical voice); **Surya (Sun)** sets the bearing. Build a FULL chart (ascendant + the aspects
on Budh + the running *dasha*), not just a sun-sign.

A character's natal chart is already a voice generator; we just have to read it.
**Mercury** is the core — how the mind forms and fires language. The **Moon** colors
it with feeling. Planets that aspect Mercury (or sit strong in the chart) bend it:
**Saturn** clips and grounds, **Jupiter** swells and preaches, **Neptune** dissolves
the edges, **Mars** sharpens and speeds, **Uranus** jolts, **Pluto** loads, **Venus**
warms. The **Sun/Ascendant** set the overall bearing.

Read a voice along the axes below; each chart pushes the character toward poles. Then
write, and check, against where they land. The acid test stays the same: strip the
character cues and you should still know who's talking (blind attribution).

---

## The axes, and what pushes each pole

**1. Sentence length and shape — long/expansive ↔ short/clipped**
- *Long, rolling, over-explaining, preaching, run-on:* Jupiter (esp. Jupiter–Mercury),
  Sagittarius or Gemini Mercury, fire and air.
- *Short, clipped, terse, controlled:* Saturn (esp. Saturn–Mercury), Capricorn Mercury,
  Mars, Scorpio (withholds).
- *Many quick clauses, fragmentary, fast:* Gemini Mercury, Mercury–Mars, Mercury–Uranus.

**2. Emotion ↔ reason ↔ balanced**
- *Soaked in feeling:* water Mercury (Cancer/Scorpio/Pisces), a strong or aspecting Moon,
  Moon–Mercury; Mars adds heat/anger.
- *Reasoned, detached, argued:* air Mercury (Gemini/Aquarius/Libra), Mercury–Saturn, earth.
- *Balanced, weighing, "on the other hand":* Libra (Sun or Mercury), Venus.

**3. Vocabulary — difficult/abstract ↔ simple/concrete**
- *Complex, abstract, jargon, reaching for the big word:* strong Mercury, air and fire,
  Aquarius/Sagittarius, Mercury–Jupiter or –Uranus.
- *Plain, concrete, sensory, few syllables:* earth (Taurus most of all), a water Moon, an
  unaspected Mercury.
- *Big words used slightly wrong — the autodidact's armor:* Mercury–Saturn or –Jupiter in a
  fixed sign carrying a wound.

**4. Precision ↔ diffusion — sharp picture ↔ Piscean**
- *Precise, sharp, defined, factual, names the exact thing:* Virgo or Capricorn Mercury,
  Mercury–Saturn, earth, Mars.
- *Diffuse, impressionistic, poetic, boundaryless, won't hold an outline:* Pisces Mercury,
  Mercury–Neptune, a strong Neptune, water. (The "Piscean" voice: it gestures at the thing
  instead of naming it, and dissolves at the edges.)

**5. Direct ↔ indirect**
- *Blunt, declarative, says it to your face:* Aries, Mars, fire; Aquarius (contrarian-blunt).
- *Oblique, hedged, sideways, says it by saying something else:* Cancer and Pisces (water),
  Libra (diplomacy), Scorpio (withholds and loads).

**6. Pace · warmth · tics**
- *Fast:* Gemini, Mercury–Mars, Mercury–Uranus. *Slow, deliberate, weighted:* Taurus,
  Capricorn, Saturn.
- *Warm:* Venus, Leo, a water Moon. *Cool, dry, detached:* Saturn, Aquarius, Capricorn.
- *Tics by aspect:* Mercury–Uranus blurts and contradicts and leaps (non-sequiturs);
  Mercury–Neptune trails off, drifts, "you know," evades; Mercury–Pluto probes, asks, loads
  the silence, controls; Mercury–Saturn feels unheard and over-builds the case.

## How to use it

1. **Read the chart into a voice card.** For each character, take Mercury (sign + aspects),
   the Moon, and the strongest modulators, and place them on the six axes. Write the card:
   sentence shape, emotion/reason, vocabulary, precision/diffusion, directness, pace/warmth,
   one or two tics, and the hard line — *what this voice never does.*
2. **Write to the card.** A line is in voice when it could only sit on those axes.
3. **Reserve eloquence for who earns it.** The writerly aphorism belongs to the chart built
   for oratory (Sagittarius/Jupiter, the preacher); strip it off everyone else.
4. **Gate it: blind attribution.** Cut the cues, read cold, name the speaker. Any line that
   could be two people fails and gets re-voiced until it can only be one.

The point: voice stops being something the writer improvises (and so makes uniform) and
becomes something the data already specifies, differently for every chart.
