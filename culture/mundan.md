# Mundan / first tonsure — मुंडन संस्कार (चूड़ाकर्म)

READ before writing/rendering any mundan / tonsure / first-haircut scene. *(v1 draft — correct me.)*

## Rules
- A child's **FIRST head-shave**, usually in the **1st or 3rd year (an odd year)** — the birth-hair (माता के
  गर्भ के बाल) is removed as impure / for the child's health and long life.
- Done at a **temple / before the family deity (कुलदेवी–कुलदेवता)** or a powerful shrine — a Mata temple,
  **Kaal Bhairav (Ujjain)**, Mehandipur Balaji, Salasar/Khatu Shyam, Tirupati, a local देवी. The **shorn hair
  is OFFERED to the deity.**
- A **celebration**: family gathers, the **नाई (barber)** shaves the head, prasad, sometimes **कर्णवेध**
  (ear-piercing) the same day.
- **NOTE:** the *same act* — head-shaving — is also done by a **son in mourning** (see cremation.md). Totally
  different context; don't conflate a joyful mundan with a mourning tonsure.

## Dramatic hooks (AMAL)
A vow / mundan at Kaal Bhairav or the kuldevi; a son's mundan as the family's pride (vs the daughters); a
promised offering for a child's wellbeing.
