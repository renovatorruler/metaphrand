# Hindu wedding — विवाह संस्कार

READ before writing/rendering any wedding, baraat, phere, engagement, or vidaai scene. North-India / Malwa
Hindu practice. *(v1 draft — correct me.)*

## Non-negotiable rules
1. **The binding rite is the फेरे, not the garland or sindoor.** The couple is married by the **सात फेरे /
   सप्तपदी** — seven circumambulations / seven steps around the sacred **fire (अग्नि), the witness**. The
   जयमाला (garland) and सिंदूर alone don't marry them.
2. **The bride leaves for the GROOM's home** (patrilocal) — विदाई → गृहप्रवेश. Not the reverse.
3. **A married Hindu woman wears सिंदूर (vermilion in the hair-parting) + मंगलसूत्र + चूड़ियाँ + bichhua (toe-rings)
   + bindi** — the marks of a **सुहागन**. A **widow removes all of these** (white, no sindoor). Don't render a
   married woman without them, or a widow with them.
4. **कन्यादान** — the bride's **father** gives her away; if he's gone, a male guardian (uncle/brother) does it.
5. **मुहूर्त** — the date/time is set auspiciously by a pandit. A death in the family makes the house impure →
   no wedding (sutak).

## Sequence (North India / Malwa)
रिश्ता (see matchmaking.md) → रोका / सगाई (engagement) → तिलक → हल्दी → मेहंदी → महिला संगीत → गणेश पूजा/मंडप →
**बारात** (groom's procession, on a mare/घोड़ी, wearing the सेहरा) → मिलनी (the families meet) → तोरण (groom
strikes the toran) → **जयमाला/वरमाला** → **कन्यादान** → गठबंधन (garments tied) → **सात फेरे + सप्तपदी** (the rite)
→ **सिंदूरदान + मंगलसूत्र** → आशीर्वाद → **विदाई** (bride's tearful departure, throws rice back over her shoulder)
→ **गृहप्रवेश** (kicks over the rice-कलश at the threshold of the marital home).

## Dramatic hooks (AMAL)
The marriage as a transaction for हैसियत or to clear debt (Leela→old Dhanraj; Manju to Rajasthan); the girl
with no say; the सुहागन-vs-widow marks; a wedding shadowed/cancelled by a death; the dowry "gifts" that come
with it (see matchmaking.md).
