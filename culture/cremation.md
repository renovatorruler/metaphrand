# Hindu death & cremation rites — अंत्येष्टि / दाह-संस्कार

READ THIS before writing or rendering ANY death, funeral, mourning, cremation, ghat, 13th-day, or
ash-immersion scene. North-India / Malwa (MP) Hindu practice. *(v1 draft — user is the authority; correct me.)*

## The non-negotiable rules (breaking these breaks the scene)
1. **Hindus CREMATE; they do NOT bury.** The body is burned on a pyre (चिता). There are no graves, no buried
   bodies, **no "ancestors' bones in the land."** Ancestors are honoured through श्राद्ध, not graves.
   *(Exceptions only: sādhus/sanyāsis are buried or water-immersed; very young infants are sometimes
   buried/immersed; a few communities differ. Default Hindu = cremation.)*
2. **WOMEN DO NOT GO to the cremation ground (श्मशान / घाट / चिता).** Only **men** carry the body and attend.
   The mother, wife, daughters — every woman — stay at **HOME** and grieve there. The wailing, the **रुदाली**
   (hired Marwari/Rajasthani mourner-women), the keening — all at the **house**, never the ghat. → NEVER
   render or describe women at the श्मशान/घाट/pyre.
3. **Don't say "जलाना / burn the body."** The act is **दाह-संस्कार / अंतिम संस्कार / अग्नि संस्कार** (last rites,
   consigning to the fire) — not casual "burning."
4. **Cremation happens fast** — ideally same day before sunset, or by next morning. A body kept for days is
   itself a scandal / a sign something is wrong (usable dramatically — e.g. a withheld body).

## The process (men only)
- Body washed, wrapped in a white shroud. A married woman who predeceases her husband (**सुहागन**) is dressed
  in **red / wedding finery** and adorned — her death is "auspicious." An unmarried girl, a widow → plain white.
- Laid feet-first on a bamboo bier (**अर्थी / बैकुंठी**), carried by four male kin, chant **"राम नाम सत्य है।"**
- Taken to the **श्मशान घाट**, usually by a river.
- Chief mourner — normally the **eldest son** (for an unmarried girl, her father/brother) — performs **मुखाग्नि**
  (lights the pyre). He has his **head shaved (मुंडन)**, wears plain white.
- Pyre is wood + ghee + samagri. **कपाल क्रिया** — the skull is ritually broken to release the soul.
- The men bathe before returning (the ghat is ritually impure).

## After
- **अस्थि-संचय** (collect the bones/ash, a day+ later) → **अस्थि-विसर्जन** (immerse in a sacred river: Ganga ideally;
  locally in Malwa the **Narmada/Reva** or the **Shipra at Ujjain**). NOT kept, NOT buried.
- **सूतक / शोक — 13 days** of mourning impurity: family doesn't cook (kin/neighbours bring food the first days),
  no festivals, house "unclean," men shaved.
- **तेरहवीं (13th day)** ends mourning: **पगड़ी रस्म** (turban tied on the eldest son / heir — he is now head of
  the house), a **मृत्यु-भोज / ब्रह्मभोज** (feast), **पिंडदान / श्राद्ध**.
- Annual **श्राद्ध / पितृ पक्ष** for the ancestors (पितर).

## Dramatic hooks (AMAL)
A young unmarried girl's death → quick, quiet, faintly shameful; the family wants it over fast. The mother and
women grieve at the **house**; that's where Ratan would face Sugna, not at the ghat. A body uncremated for days
= obstruction/scandal. The पगड़ी going to the "wrong" heir, a man refusing to light the pyre — all loaded.
