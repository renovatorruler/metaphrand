# Amal / Kasumba — the ceremonial opium ritual (अमल / कसूम्बा)

READ before writing/rendering any scene with the ceremonial opium-water offering. *(v1 — the user is the
cultural authority; correct the Malwa specifics.)*

## What it is
The ceremonial taking of **opium dissolved in water** — a centuries-old Rajput / Malwa custom. Opium here is
woven into **hospitality, honour, grief and peace-making**, not (only) vice. Malwa is India's legal opium belt
(CBN-licensed poppy, the तौल / weighing), so the flower is the region's livelihood *and* its ritual. The show's
title, अमल, is this.

## The words (NOT interchangeable)
- **अमल (amal)** — the opium-water, and the practice of taking it (*अमल लेना*).
- **कसूम्बा / कसुम्बो (kasumba)** — the *ceremonial* offered opium-water (amber-red tint). The formal version.
- **मनुहार (manuhar)** — the courteous, insistent *urging* to accept (the host presses, the guest demurs).
- **डोडा (doda)** — poppy *husk*, the cheap coarse form for heavy addicts (doda amlis) — the low end, opposite
  the ceremonial kasumba.

## Rules / etiquette
- The host rubs/dissolves a small lump of opium in water, strains it through cloth; in the binding form he
  **pours it into his own cupped palm and the guest drinks from the host's hand.**
- **Refusing is a grave insult** — it declines the host's respect, reads as hostility or a refusal to be bound.
- **Precedence = status** — who is served first, and by whom, encodes the hierarchy in the room.
- It **seals things**: weddings, condolences, and especially **settling feuds/disputes** (two enemies drink
  together → the quarrel is over, binding), and sealing deals/alliances.
- **Obligation** is the core: to take it from a man's hand is to enter a bond/debt with him — *"इस हाथ से ली गई
  चीज़ इसी हाथ को लौटानी पड़ती है."*
- A **men's** ritual (gatherings of men) — keep women out of it (consistent with the gender lines).
- A **dying** custom (younger people reject it, the law restricts it) — wielded by old-world power-men
  *because* it is fading: *"आजकल के लोग ये सब भूल गए, मैं नहीं भूलता."*

## Dramatic hooks (AMAL)
The show's metaphor made physical: one flower is licence, honour, hospitality **and** addiction, leash, ruin —
*"सब इसी एक फूल में से निकलता है."* Hospitality as control; a gift as a debt; a courtesy you cannot refuse.
Rana weaponises it to bind Ratan — velvet over iron (Ep2 Sc15). Its cruel twin is the **तौल (the weighing)** —
where a licensed farmer is made or ruined, and whoever holds the scale holds the man.
